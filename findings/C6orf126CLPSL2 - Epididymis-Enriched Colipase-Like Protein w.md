# C6orf126/CLPSL2 - Epididymis-Enriched Colipase-Like Protein with Reproductive Function

**Date:** 2026-03-17T03:33:05.702819

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
C6orf126 encodes CLPSL2 (Colipase-like protein 2, UniProt Q6UWE3), a 100 aa secreted protein with a canonical colipase domain (IPR001981, positions 5-100) and lipase subunit A fold (G3DSA:2.10.80.10, positions 25-99). Despite structural homology to pancreatic colipases that anchor lipase during fat digestion, CLPSL2 shows striking epididymis-specific expression (501.4 nTPM in Human Protein Atlas), with tissue-enriched RNA profile and vesicular subcellular localization. This expression pattern suggests a reproductive function distinct from digestive colipases. AlphaFold predicts a well-folded structure (pLDDT 82.9), indicating stable tertiary structure. STRING interactions reveal associations with PATE family proteins (PATE1: 0.435, PATE2: 0.448, PATE3: 0.524) and GLB1L proteins (GLB1L: 0.403, GLB1L2: 0.447, GLB1L3: 0.507) - all epididymis/prostate-expressed factors involved in sperm maturation and male fertility. ClinVar documents 7 pathogenic/likely pathogenic copy number variants spanning the 6p21.31 region, though specific CLPSL2 disease phenotypes remain undefined. The PTHR10041 colipase family profile (positions 5-91) confirms evolutionary conservation of the colipase fold. Mechanistic hypothesis: CLPSL2 likely functions as a lipid-binding cofactor in epididymal fluid, potentially modulating lipase activity during sperm membrane remodeling or facilitating lipid transport in seminal plasma. The epididymis enrichment and PATE interactions suggest roles in sperm maturation, capacitation, or membrane lipid homeostasis rather than dietary fat digestion.
