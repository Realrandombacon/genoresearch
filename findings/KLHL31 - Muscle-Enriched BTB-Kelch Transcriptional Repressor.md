# KLHL31 - Muscle-Enriched BTB-Kelch Transcriptional Repressor with CUL3 Ubiquitin Ligase Adaptor Function

**Date:** 2026-03-17T08:40:32.015903

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
KLHL31 (UniProt Q9H511) encodes a 634aa BTB-Kelch family protein on chromosome 6p12.1 with established function as a transcriptional repressor in the MAPK/JNK signaling pathway. InterPro analysis reveals canonical BTB-Kelch architecture: N-terminal BTB/POZ domain (IPR000210, residues 64-167) for protein dimerization and CUL3 binding, central BACK domain (IPR011705, residues 172-273) linking BTB to Kelch, and C-terminal Kelch-type beta-propeller (IPR015915, IPR006652, residues 301-627) with 4 Kelch repeats forming a 6-bladed beta-propeller for substrate recognition. AlphaFold structure prediction shows very high confidence (pLDDT 89.9), indicating a stable, well-folded scaffold typical of functional BTB-Kelch E3 ubiquitin ligase adaptors. HPA data demonstrates striking muscle-enriched expression (skeletal muscle 136.8 nTPM, heart muscle 47.2 nTPM, tongue 32.6 nTPM) with tissue-enhanced specificity and intracellular localization, consistent with roles in muscle differentiation or contractility regulation. STRING interaction network reveals 10 partners with high-confidence binding to NTMT1 (0.883, N-terminal methyltransferase), RPL23A (0.798, ribosomal protein), and critically CUL3 (0.753, cullin-3 E3 ligase scaffold), alongside medium-confidence links to PRODH (0.682, proline dehydrogenase), RCC1 (0.607, Ran GEF), TMEM214 (0.600), TMEM38A (0.589, TRP channel), and paralogous KLHL family members KLHL3 (0.518), KLHL40 (0.498, muscle-specific), and MYL2 (0.506, myosin light chain). This interactome strongly supports mechanistic function as a CUL3-based E3 ubiquitin ligase substrate adaptor targeting JNK pathway transcription factors or muscle-specific proteins for proteasomal degradation. ClinVar contains 9 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, exclusively chromosome 6 copy-number alterations (deletions and duplications spanning 6p12.1-q27), implicating the locus in genomic instability disorders though no specific monogenic KLHL31-related disease is yet defined. The combination of muscle-enriched expression, canonical BTB-Kelch E3 ligase architecture, CUL3 interaction, JNK pathway repression activity, and high structural confidence supports a mechanistic hypothesis where KLHL31 functions as a muscle-specific CUL3 adaptor that ubiquitinates and degrades JNK pathway transcription factors or contractile apparatus regulators, potentially linking epigenetic regulation (via NTMT1 interaction) to muscle gene expression programs and sarcomere maintenance.
