# CCDC50 (C3orf6) - Cilia-Associated Ubiquitin Ligase Adapter with Deafness Pathogenesis

**Date:** 2026-03-16T18:14:35.616903

**Quality Score:** 9/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
CCDC50 (Coiled-coil domain-containing protein 50, formerly C3orf6) is a 306 amino acid intracellular protein with strong clinical significance (95 pathogenic/likely pathogenic ClinVar variants). The protein contains N-terminal coiled-coil domains (PF15295, IPR029311, residues 5-128) that mediate protein-protein interactions. HPA localizes CCDC50 to the basal body and cytosol, with immune cell enrichment in blood and ubiquitous tissue expression. Critically, HPA associates CCDC50 with non-syndromic deafness, consistent with basal body dysfunction affecting auditory cilia. STRING interactions reveal a functional network: CCDC192 (0.890, another coiled-coil protein suggesting oligomerization), UBC (0.615, ubiquitin conjugation enzyme indicating ubiquitin pathway involvement), MAP1LC3B (0.490, autophagy marker), and EGF (0.432, growth factor signaling). The high-confidence CCDC192 interaction suggests CCDC50 forms coiled-coil homo/heterooligomers. UBC binding positions CCDC50 as a potential ubiquitin ligase adapter or substrate recruiter. Basal body localization combined with deafness phenotype strongly implicates CCDC50 in cilia maintenance or ciliary protein turnover via ubiquitin-mediated degradation. Pathogenic variants (including p.Lys303del, p.Gln92Pro, p.Gly163Arg) likely disrupt coiled-coil folding, protein interactions, or basal body targeting, leading to ciliary dysfunction and sensorineural hearing loss. This represents a mechanistically tractable dark gene where structure (coiled-coil), localization (basal body), interactors (UBC/MAP1LC3B), and disease (deafness) converge on a testable hypothesis: CCDC50 recruits ubiquitin machinery to basal body substrates for cilia protein homeostasis.
