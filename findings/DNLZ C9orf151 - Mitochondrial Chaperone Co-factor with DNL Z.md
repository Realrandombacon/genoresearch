# DNLZ C9orf151 - Mitochondrial Chaperone Co-factor with DNL Zinc Finger Domain

**Date:** 2026-03-17T04:37:05.309630

**Quality Score:** 9/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
DNLZ (C9orf151) encodes a 178-amino acid DNL-type zinc finger protein (UniProt Q5SXM8) that functions as a mitochondrial co-chaperone. The protein contains a conserved DNL zinc finger domain (IPR007853, PF05180, residues 64-161) and belongs to the TIM15 mitochondrial import protein family (IPR024158). AlphaFold predicts a partially structured conformation (pLDDT 67.5), consistent with chaperone co-factor flexibility. High-confidence STRING interactions reveal functional coupling to HSPA9/mortalin (score 0.944), a mitochondrial Hsp70 chaperone prone to self-aggregation, plus TIMM44 (0.639), GRPEL1 (0.564), and PAM16 (0.557) - all components of the mitochondrial protein import machinery. HPA localizes DNLZ to nucleoplasm and mitochondria with low tissue specificity, detected across many tissues. ClinVar reports 75 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly copy number losses at 9q34.3, indicating haploinsufficiency causes disease. The DNL zinc finger likely coordinates zinc ions (GO:0008270) to stabilize HSPA9 substrate binding during mitochondrial protein import. Mechanistically, DNLZ acts as a TIM15 ortholog that prevents HSPA9 aggregation while facilitating translocation of nuclear-encoded proteins through the TIM23 complex into the mitochondrial matrix. Disease variants disrupt this chaperone co-factor activity, impairing mitochondrial proteostasis and respiratory chain assembly.
