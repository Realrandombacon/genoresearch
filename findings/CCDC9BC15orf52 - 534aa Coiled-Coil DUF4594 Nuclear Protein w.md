## Description

HPA expression data shows tissue-enhanced RNA pattern with highest expression in skeletal muscle (57.0 nTPM), detected in all tissues, and cell type-enhanced specificity, indicating housekeeping nuclear functions with muscle enrichment. ClinVar contains 8 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants predominantly consisting of 15q copy number gains and losses (15q11.2-26.3 region), demonstrating clinical significance in chromosomal rearrangement syndromes affecting the 15q14-15.1 locus. Mechanistic hypothesis: CCDC9B functions as a nuclear scaffold protein that associates with the THO/TREX mRNA export complex through THOC7 interactions, potentially coupling transcription elongation to mRNA processing and nuclear export. The DUF4594 domain may mediate protein-protein interactions or RNA binding within nuclear speckles or transcription factories. Coiled-coil regions likely facilitate oligomerization or recruitment of additional regulatory factors. Pathogenic 15q CNVs likely disrupt dosage-sensitive mRNA export regulation and nuclear organization, contributing to tissue-specific phenotypes particularly in skeletal muscle where expression is highest.
