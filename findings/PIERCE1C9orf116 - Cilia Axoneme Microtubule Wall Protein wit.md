# PIERCE1/C9orf116 - Cilia Axoneme Microtubule Wall Protein with Dynein Arm Attachment Function

**Date:** 2026-03-16T02:57:45.265465

## Description
PIERCE1 (C9orf116) encodes a 136 aa protein (UniProt Q5BN46) belonging to the PIERCE1/2 family (IPR026507, PF14892). Now functionally characterized as a microtubule inner protein (MIP) involved in attachment of outer dynein arms (ODAs) to dynein-decorated doublet microtubules (DMTs) in cilia axoneme, required for motile cilia beating. Shows tissue-enhanced expression with top expression in fallopian tube (91.4 nTPM), testis (59.7 nTPM), and choroid plexus (57.7 nTPM) - all ciliated tissues. Localized to nucleoplasm, cytosol, and mid piece (sperm flagellum region). Exceptionally high-confidence protein interaction network with cilia/axoneme components: C15orf65 (0.886), EFHC2 (0.875), CFAP53 (0.834), TEKT1 (0.832), CFAP161 (0.830), TEKT3 (0.825), CFAP126 (0.817), FAM166B (0.809), C5orf49 (0.809), MNS1 (0.805). All interaction partners are cilia/flagellum-associated proteins, confirming functional role in axonemal structure. GO annotations include axoneme assembly (GO:0035082) and axonemal microtubule (GO:0005879). This represents a successful 'dark gene' characterization - formerly uncharacterized ORF now known to be essential for ciliary motility and sperm flagellum function.
