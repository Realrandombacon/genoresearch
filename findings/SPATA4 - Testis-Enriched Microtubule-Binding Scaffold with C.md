# SPATA4 - Testis-Enriched Microtubule-Binding Scaffold with CH Domain and Ciliary Protein Interactions

**Date:** 2026-03-17T08:43:10.701259

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
SPATA4 (UniProt Q8NEY3) encodes a 305aa spermatogenesis-associated protein on chromosome 4q34.2 with predicted microtubule-binding activity and role in cytoskeleton organization regulation. InterPro analysis reveals a central calponin homology (CH) domain (IPR001715, PF06294, residues 49-155) characteristic of actin/microtubule-binding proteins, embedded within a sperm-specific CH-like domain architecture (IPR010441, residues 55-150), and membership in the Spermatogenesis and Ciliary Microtubule-associated Protein family (IPR052111, PTHR12509, residues 47-302), strongly suggesting cytoskeletal scaffolding functions in spermatogenic cells or ciliated epithelia. AlphaFold structure prediction shows confident folding (pLDDT 74.7), indicating a stable CH domain fold typical of cytoskeletal regulators. HPA data demonstrates striking testis-enriched expression (130.4 nTPM) with tissue-enriched specificity and nucleoplasmic/vesicular/cytosolic localization, consistent with roles in spermatid differentiation, manchette microtubule organization, or sperm flagellum assembly. STRING interaction network reveals 10 partners with high-confidence binding to WDR90 (0.754, WD40-repeat protein implicated in centriole/cilium assembly), SPATA17 (0.738, paralogous spermatogenesis factor), and medium-confidence links to PGK2 (0.625, testis-specific phosphoglycerate kinase), ASB5 (0.562, ankyrin-SOCS box ubiquitin ligase), FANK1 (0.559, forkhead/ankyrin transcription factor), PLAAT5 (0.543, phospholipase), ELMOD2 (0.535, ciliary GTPase regulator), SPAG6 (0.515, sperm-associated antigen 6, flagellar protein), SPATA20 (0.489, spermatogenesis factor), and ZMYND10 (0.422, zinc finger ciliopathy protein). This interactome strongly supports mechanistic function in ciliary/flagellar microtubule organization and spermatid cytoskeletal remodeling. ClinVar contains 77 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly chromosome 4q copy-number alterations (deletions and duplications spanning 4q32.1-q35.2) with some splice-site mutations, indicating disease relevance through genomic instability though no specific Mendelian SPATA4-related phenotype is yet defined. The combination of testis-enriched expression, CH domain microtubule-binding architecture, ciliary protein interactions (WDR90, SPAG6, ZMYND10), confident 3D structure, and clinical variant burden supports a mechanistic hypothesis where SPATA4 functions as a testis-specific microtubule crosslinking scaffold that stabilizes the spermatid manchette or sperm flagellum axoneme during spermiogenesis, potentially coordinating cytoskeletal dynamics with metabolic regulation (via PGK2 interaction) and ubiquitin-mediated protein turnover (via ASB5 interaction).
