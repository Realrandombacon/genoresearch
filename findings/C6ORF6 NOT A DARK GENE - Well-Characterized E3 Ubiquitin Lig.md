## Description
C6ORF6 (Gene ID: 10756), historically named "chromosome 6 open reading frame 6", is actually a WELL-CHARACTERIZED E3 ubiquitin ligase now officially named TRAF3IP2 (TRAF3 Interacting Protein 2), also known as Act1 (Activator 1). This represents a critical finding: C6ORF6 is NOT a dark gene but rather a fully annotated adaptor protein with established function in IL-17 receptor signaling and NF-kappaB activation. UniProt accession O43734 encodes a 574-amino acid cytoplasmic protein. These interactions place TRAF3IP2/Act1 as a central adaptor bridging IL-17 receptors to TRAF ubiquitin ligases and IKK complex. Human Protein Atlas (ENSG00000056972) classifies TRAF3IP2 as disease-related gene, enzyme, potential drug target, predicted intracellular protein with Golgi apparatus/vesicle localization, low tissue specificity (detected in all tissues), immune cell enhanced expression in blood, and cell type enhanced single-cell specificity. ClinVar contains 41 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including nonsense mutations (c.395C>G p.Ser132Ter, c.847C>T p.Arg283Ter, c.973G>T p.Glu325Ter), frameshift deletions (c.1392del p.Lys465fs, c.488_492del p.Ser162_Leu163insTer), splice variants (c.1201+1G>A, c.1202-2A>G), and copy number alterations (6q21-22.31 deletions, 6q21-23.2 duplications), indicating strong association with autoimmunity and inflammatory disease. Functional role: TRAF3IP2/Act1 encodes a cytoplasmic adaptor protein that recruits TRAF ubiquitin ligases to activated IL-17 receptors, catalyzing K63-linked polyubiquitination of signaling intermediates and activating NF-kappaB/MAPK pathways. Mechanistic proposal: Upon IL-17 binding, IL-17RA/RC heterodimers recruit TRAF3IP2 via SEFIR-SEFIR homotypic interaction, enabling TRAF3IP2 to ubiquitinate TRAF6 and recruit IKK complex (IKBKG/IKBKB), leading to IkappaB degradation, NF-kappaB nuclear translocation, and proinflammatory gene transcription (IL-6, CXCL1, CXCL8). TRAF3IP2 also negatively regulates TLR signaling by promoting TRAF3 degradation. Dysregulated TRAF3IP2 contributes to psoriasis, lupus, and inflammatory bowel disease. This gene should be SKIPPED from dark gene analysis - it represents historical nomenclature (C#orf#) superseded by functional annotation (TRAF3IP2/Act1).
