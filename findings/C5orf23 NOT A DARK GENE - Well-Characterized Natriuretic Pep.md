## Description
C5orf23 (Gene ID: 79614), historically named "chromosome 5 open reading frame 23", is actually a WELL-CHARACTERIZED transmembrane receptor now officially named NPR3 (Natriuretic Peptide Receptor 3). This represents a critical finding: C5orf23 is NOT a dark gene but rather a fully annotated clearance receptor with established function in cardiovascular and renal physiology. UniProt accession P17342 encodes a 541-amino acid single-pass transmembrane protein. Human Protein Atlas (ENSG00000113389) classifies NPR3 as receptor, predicted membrane protein, with cytosol localization, tissue enhanced RNA specificity, detected in many tissues, top expression in kidney (100.5 nTPM), cancer enhanced, and immune cell enriched in blood. ClinVar contains 36 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including copy number gains/losses (5p13.3-11), frameshift deletions (c.1229del p.Gly410fs, c.658dup p.Val220fs, c.691_692del p.Thr231fs), missense variants (c.382C>T p.Pro128Ser, c.398G>T p.Gly133Val, c.382C>G p.Pro128Ala), and splice variants (c.1059+1G>A), indicating strong association with cardiovascular and renal disease. Functional role: NPR3 encodes a clearance receptor that binds and internalizes natriuretic peptides (ANP, BNP, CNP) without generating cGMP, thereby regulating peptide bioavailability and half-life. Mechanistic proposal: NPR3 likely functions as a homodimeric single-pass transmembrane receptor with large extracellular ligand-binding domain (residues 42-422) that captures circulating natriuretic peptides, followed by clathrin-mediated endocytosis and lysosomal degradation, controlling systemic peptide concentrations and modulating NPR1/NPR2 guanylyl cyclase signaling. Elevated NPR3 expression may contribute to natriuretic peptide resistance in heart failure and hypertension. This gene should be SKIPPED from dark gene analysis - it represents historical nomenclature (C#orf#) that has been superseded by functional annotation (NPR3).
