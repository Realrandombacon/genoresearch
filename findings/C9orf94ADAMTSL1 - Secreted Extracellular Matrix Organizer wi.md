# C9orf94/ADAMTSL1 - Secreted Extracellular Matrix Organizer with TSP1-Ig Domain Architecture and 91 ClinVar Variants

**Date:** 2026-03-17T04:52:25.446504

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
ADAMTSL1 (C9orf94) encodes a 1762 aa secreted protein with complex domain architecture characteristic of extracellular matrix (ECM) organizers. InterPro analysis reveals 3 thrombospondin type-1 repeats (IPR000884, positions 33-82, 301-360, 376-438), 3 immunoglobulin I-set domains (IPR013098), ADAMTS spacer region (IPR010294, 188-296), and C-terminal PLAC domain (IPR010909, 1726-1762). AlphaFold predicts a confident folded structure (pLDDT 70.9). HPA shows endometrium-enriched expression (12.5 nTPM) with vesicular localization. STRING interactions identify high-confidence partners: B3GLCT (0.739) and POFUT2 (0.717) - both glycosyltransferases that modify EGF-like and TSP1 repeats, plus FBN2 (0.451) - a fibrillin ECM component. ClinVar contains 91 pathogenic variants including copy number gains/losses. The TSP1-Ig domain combination suggests ADAMTSL1 functions as an ECM scaffold protein that organizes basement membrane assembly through glycosylation-dependent protein-protein interactions. B3GLCT/POFUT2 interactions indicate O-fucosylation and O-glucosylation of TSP1 repeats are critical for proper folding and secretion. Disease variants likely disrupt ECM integrity in endometrial and connective tissues.
