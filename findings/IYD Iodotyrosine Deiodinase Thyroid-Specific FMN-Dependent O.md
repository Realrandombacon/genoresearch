# IYD Iodotyrosine Deiodinase: Thyroid-Specific FMN-Dependent Oxidoreductase for Hormone Recycling

**Date:** 2026-03-18T06:05:10.581179

**Quality Score:** 9/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
C6orf71 (IYD) encodes a 289 amino acid iodotyrosine deiodinase (UniProt Q6PHW0) with high-confidence AlphaFold structure (pLDDT 88.1). InterPro analysis identifies FMN-dependent nitroreductase domains: IPR000415 (nitroreductase-like superfamily, aa 72-288), PF00881 (nitroreductase family, aa 98-267), and cd02144 (iodotyrosine dehalogenase catalytic domain, aa 93-285). These domains confer oxidative dehalogenation activity for cleaving carbon-iodine bonds. HPA shows extreme thyroid gland enrichment (442.1 nTPM) with plasma membrane localization, matching its role in thyroid follicular cells. STRING interactions reveal functional coupling to thyroid hormone synthesis machinery: DUOXA2/DUOX2 (0.930-0.952) for H2O2 generation, TPO (0.854) for iodination, SLC5A5 (0.800) for iodide uptake, and SLC26A4 (0.910) for anion transport. ClinVar contains 31 pathogenic variants including nonsense (p.Arg246Ter) and frameshift mutations (p.Ile190fs, p.Lys175fs) causing congenital hypothyroidism. IYD catalyzes reductive deiodination of monoiodotyrosine (MIT) and diiodotyrosine (DIT), salvaging iodide for reuse and preventing loss of this limiting nutrient. This enzyme is essential for thyroid hormone homeostasis—defects cause goiter and hypothyroidism despite normal hormone synthesis capacity.
