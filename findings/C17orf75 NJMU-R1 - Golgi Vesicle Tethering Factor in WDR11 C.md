## Description
C17orf75 encodes Protein Njmu-R1 (UniProt Q9HAS0), a 396 amino acid cytoplasmic protein localized to the trans-Golgi network and cytosol. InterPro analysis identifies the Njmu-R1 family domain (IPR028280, PF15053, residues 38-390) conserved across vertebrates. AlphaFold predicts a well-folded structure (pLDDT 76.7) indicating stable tertiary conformation.\n\nSTRING interaction network reveals C17orf75 functions within the WDR11 complex machinery for Golgi vesicle capture. High-confidence partners include VPS25 (score 0.896), TBC1D23 (0.832), VPS4A (0.787), SNF8 (0.753), and VPS36 (0.744) - all ESCRT pathway components. Medium-confidence interaction with WDR11 (0.647) confirms membership in the WDR11 complex that facilitates AP-1-mediated vesicle tethering to Golgi membranes.\n\nHPA shows ubiquitous expression across all tissues with Golgi apparatus localization. ClinVar contains 11 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly copy number variations (gains/losses) at 17q11.2, suggesting dosage sensitivity and disease relevance when disrupted.\n\nFunctional hypothesis: C17orf75 acts as an adaptor protein bridging TBC1D23-Rab GTPase complexes with WDR11-ESCRT machinery to capture COPI/clathrin-coated vesicles at the trans-Golgi network. The Njmu-R1 domain likely mediates protein-protein interactions with golgin tethers. Disease-associated CNVs may disrupt vesicle trafficking causing developmental phenotypes.
