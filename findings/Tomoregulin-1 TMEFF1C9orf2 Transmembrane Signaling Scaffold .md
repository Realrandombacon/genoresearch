# Tomoregulin-1 (TMEFF1/C9orf2): Transmembrane Signaling Scaffold with Kazal Protease Inhibitor Domains

**Date:** 2026-03-18T06:52:21.874400

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
TMEFF1 (C9orf2) encodes Tomoregulin-1, a 380 amino acid transmembrane protein functioning as a developmental signaling scaffold. The protein contains two Kazal-type serine protease inhibitor domains (IPR002350, PF07648, residues 98-145 and 189-237) and one EGF-like domain (IPR000742, residues 271-311), suggesting dual functionality in protease regulation and growth factor signaling. HPA expression shows brain-enriched transcription (12.9 nTPM in brain, 3.8 nTPM in testis) with group-enriched tissue specificity, indicating roles in neural development. STRING interactions reveal high-confidence partnerships with ZDHHC5 (0.785), a palmitoyltransferase regulating membrane protein trafficking, and TDGF1 (0.695), a cripto family co-receptor for Nodal signaling. Additional interactions with EGF (0.566) and FGF2 (0.511) support growth factor pathway involvement. ClinVar documents 33 pathogenic variants, predominantly copy number alterations on chromosome 9, implicating TMEFF1 dosage sensitivity in disease. AlphaFold structure (pLDDT 69.0) shows medium confidence with ordered extracellular domains and potentially disordered cytoplasmic regions. The Kazal domains likely inhibit extracellular proteases (e.g., trypsin-like enzymes) while the EGF-like domain mediates receptor dimerization. I hypothesize TMEFF1 functions as a bipartite signaling modulator: (1) protease inhibition via Kazal domains regulates extracellular matrix remodeling and growth factor availability, (2) EGF-like domain facilitates transmembrane signal transduction during neurodevelopment. Copy number variants suggest haploinsufficiency causes developmental disorders through disrupted neural patterning.
