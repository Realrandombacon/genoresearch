## Description
ZNF99 is an 864 amino acid C2H2 zinc finger transcription factor (UniProt A8MXY4) encoded at 19p13.11. The protein contains a canonical N-terminal KRAB repression domain (aa 3-75, PF01352/IPR001909) followed by multiple C2H2-type zinc finger DNA-binding motifs (aa 173-335, PF00096/IPR013087) arranged in tandem repeats. AlphaFold predicts a confident folded structure (pLDDT 72.4) with the KRAB domain forming a compact helical bundle and zinc fingers adopting characteristic beta-beta-alpha folds. HPA data shows testis-enriched expression (1.5 nTPM) with classification as a transcription factor involved in DNA-binding and transcription regulation. STRING interactions reveal 8 partners including TRIM28/KAP1 (0.522) - the canonical KRAB co-repressor that recruits heterochromatin machinery - plus other zinc finger proteins (ZNF716: 0.455, ZNF492: 0.450) and surprisingly several olfactory receptors (OR4K15, OR10S1, OR5W2). ClinVar documents 8 copy number gain variants of uncertain significance spanning chromosome 19 regions containing ZNF99. The KRAB-ZNF architecture suggests ZNF99 functions as a sequence-specific transcriptional repressor, recruiting TRIM28/KAP1 to silence target genes through histone methylation (H3K9me3) and heterochromatin formation. Testis enrichment implies potential roles in germ cell development or meiotic gene silencing.
