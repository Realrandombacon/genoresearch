## Description
SMIM20 (alias C4orf52, MITRAC7, PNX) encodes a 67 amino acid mitochondrial inner membrane protein (UniProt Q8N5G0) containing the MITRAC7/Phoenixin family domain (IPR027917, PF15061 residues 2-58, PTHR34923 residues 1-67) with GO annotations for mitochondrial respiratory chain complex IV assembly (GO:0033617) and membrane localization (GO:0016020). AlphaFold predicts very high-confidence structure (pLDDT 90.8) indicating a well-folded transmembrane helix architecture characteristic of small integral membrane proteins that function as assembly factors in oxidative phosphorylation complexes. HPA data reveals nucleoplasm, nucleoli, and cytosolic localization with low tissue specificity (detected in all tissues) and cell-type enhanced expression, suggesting ubiquitous mitochondrial functions with cell-type specific regulation. STRING interactions identify 10 partners including G-protein coupled receptor GPR173 (0.883, high confidence, phoenixin ligand-receptor pair), cytochrome c oxidase assembly factor COA3 (0.785, MITRAC complex), myosin light chain MYL1 (0.738, muscle contraction), troponin I isoforms TNNI2 (0.729), TNNI1 (0.662), TNNI3 (0.432, sarcomere regulation), troponin C TNNC2 (0.682, calcium binding), cytochrome c oxidase subunits COX4I1 (0.594) and COX14 (0.565, complex IV assembly), and dynein assembly factor DNAAF3 (0.562, ciliogenesis), implicating SMIM20 in mitochondrial respiration, muscle function, and receptor signaling. The high-confidence interaction with GPR173 (0.883) identifies SMIM20 as the peptide hormone phoenixin, which activates GPR173 to regulate energy homeostasis, food intake, and reproductive function. ClinVar documents 31 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly chromosome 4p copy number gains and losses encompassing the SMIM20 locus, indicating dosage sensitivity in disease etiology, potentially affecting mitochondrial function and metabolic regulation. The MITRAC7 domain combined with transmembrane topology and mitochondrial inner membrane localization positions SMIM20 as a cytochrome c oxidase assembly chaperone that coordinates early stages of complex IV biogenesis through interactions with COA3 and COX14 within the MITRAC (mitochondrial translation regulation assembly intermediate of cytochrome c oxidase) complex. Dual functionality as both mitochondrial assembly factor and secreted peptide hormone (phoenixin) suggests tissue-specific proteolytic processing determines subcellular fate—full-length membrane-anchored form functions in mitochondria while cleaved signal peptide acts as endocrine regulator. Disease-associated CNVs indicate haploinsufficiency or triplosensitivity mechanisms affecting neurodevelopment and metabolism, potentially through combined disruption of oxidative phosphorylation and GPR173 signaling pathways.
