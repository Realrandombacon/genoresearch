# C12orf75/OCC1 - Vesicle-Associated Oncogenic Adaptor with BTRC/IRS4 Interactions and Pathogenic CNVs

**Date:** 2026-03-16T18:30:56.514784

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
C12orf75 (OCC1/Overexpressed in Colon Carcinoma 1) encodes a 63aa small protein (UniProt Q8TAD7) belonging to the OCC1 family (IPR029133, PF15506). AlphaFold predicts a partially structured conformation (pLDDT 68.4), suggesting mixed ordered/disordered regions typical of signaling adaptors. HPA reveals vesicle localization with tissue-enhanced expression peaking in blood vessel (129.2 nTPM) and seminal vesicle (102.9 nTPM), plus cancer-enhanced RNA expression and immune cell enrichment. STRING identifies 8 interaction partners including BTRC (beta-transducin repeat-containing E3 ubiquitin protein ligase, score 0.557), IRS4 (insulin receptor substrate 4, 0.555), ELAVL1 (RNA-binding protein, 0.468), and UNC119 (0.422). ClinVar documents 10 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly copy number gains/losses across 12q23-24, indicating dosage sensitivity. The BTRC interaction suggests involvement in ubiquitin-mediated proteasomal degradation pathways, while IRS4 linkage implies growth factor signaling modulation. Cancer overexpression combined with vesicle localization and BTRC/IRS4 interactions supports a hypothesis that OCC1 functions as a membrane-associated signaling adaptor that recruits E3 ubiquitin ligases to growth factor receptors, promoting receptor turnover and oncogenic signaling. Pathogenic CNVs likely disrupt this regulatory balance, contributing to colorectal and other epithelial cancers.
