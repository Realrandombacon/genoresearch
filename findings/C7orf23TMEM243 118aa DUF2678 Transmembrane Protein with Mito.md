## Description
C7orf23 (now annotated as TMEM243) encodes a 118-amino acid transmembrane protein (UniProt Q9BU79) defined by the DUF2678 domain of unknown function (InterPro IPR022564/PF10856), spanning the entire protein sequence. This domain architecture is strictly conserved across vertebrates, with 118aa orthologs identified in macaque, snake (Micrurus fulvius), frog (Aquarana catesbeiana), and lynx, indicating strong purifying selection on protein length and topology. STRING interaction analysis reveals a network of 9 medium-confidence partners (scores 0.40-0.62), notably including MICOS10 (0.435)—a component of the mitochondrial contact site and cristae organizing system—plus DMTF1 (0.619, cell cycle regulator), RUNDC3B (0.573, vesicle trafficking), and SLC25A40 (0.513, mitochondrial carrier). This interaction profile suggests TMEM243 localizes to mitochondrial membranes or associated vesicular structures, potentially functioning as a scaffold protein that bridges mitochondrial cristae organization with cell cycle progression through DMTF1 coupling. The DUF2678 domain lacks enzymatic motifs, supporting a structural/regulatory role rather than catalytic activity. Absence of HPA resolution and ClinVar entries indicates this remains a true dark gene with uncharacterized tissue distribution and no reported disease associations. We hypothesize TMEM243 acts as a mitochondrial outer membrane adaptor that coordinates metabolic state with cell cycle checkpoint signaling, where DUF2678 mediates protein-protein interactions rather than substrate binding. Experimental validation should prioritize mitochondrial fractionation co-IP and cristae morphology phenotyping upon knockdown.
