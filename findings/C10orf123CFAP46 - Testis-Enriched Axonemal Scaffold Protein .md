## Description
C10orf123 (Gene ID: 100129244, chromosome 10q26.3) encodes CFAP46 (Cilia- and Flagella-Associated Protein 46), a massive 2715 amino acid structural protein (UniProt Q8IYW2) essential for sperm flagella assembly. The protein contains N-terminal tetratricopeptide (TPR) repeats (InterPro IPR057466, Pfam PF25439, residues 5-150) that mediate protein-protein interactions, and belongs to the CFAP46 family (IPR039586, PTHR15977) with GO annotations for axoneme assembly (GO:0035082) and cilium movement (GO:0060294). AlphaFold structure prediction shows high confidence (pLDDT 88.9) for the N-terminal region (residues 1-247), indicating a well-folded TPR domain scaffold. Human Protein Atlas reveals testis-specific enrichment (39.9 nTPM) with moderate skeletal muscle expression (29.3 nTPM), and subcellular localization to sperm flagella structures: mid piece, principal piece, and annulus. STRING interaction network identifies 10 cilia/axoneme-related partners including PIH1D2 (0.605), CXorf58/CFAP298 (0.584), CFAP74 (0.486), ARMC3 (0.485), CFAP54/WDR16 (0.446), C19orf81/CFAP126 (0.446), CFAP221 (0.440), and HYDIN (0.425), suggesting CFAP46 functions as a scaffold within the axonemal dynein regulatory complex. ClinVar documents 99 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly copy number losses and gains across 10q26, plus at least one splice site variant (c.307-1G>T), yet no specific disease phenotype is defined—indicating CFAP46 dosage is critical but the clinical spectrum (likely male infertility/asthenozoospermia) requires phenotypic correlation. The combination of testis-restricted expression, axonemal protein interactions, and high structural confidence positions CFAP46 as a specialized ciliary scaffold whose precise role in dynein arm assembly and sperm motility awaits mechanistic dissection.
