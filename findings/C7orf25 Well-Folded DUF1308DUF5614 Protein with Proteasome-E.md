## Description
C7orf25 encodes a 421 amino acid uncharacterized protein (UniProt Q9BPX7, UPF0415 family) with two domains of unknown function: DUF5614 (InterPro: IPR041076, PF18474, residues 1-217) at the N-terminus and DUF1308 (InterPro: IPR010733, PF07000, residues 248-409) at the C-terminus. The protein belongs to the PTHR13379 uncharacterized DUF1308 family. The PSMA2-SEC61G connection suggests involvement in ER-associated protein degradation (ERAD) or co-translational quality control.\n\nHPA expression data shows nucleoplasmic and cytosolic localization with low tissue specificity but immune cell enhancement, consistent with housekeeping functions in protein homeostasis. The protein is detected across many tissues, suggesting constitutive expression.\n\nClinVar contains 24 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly large copy number gains and losses spanning 7p14.1-qter regions. While no monogenic disease is definitively established, the variant burden indicates dosage sensitivity and potential contribution to chromosomal instability syndromes or developmental disorders.\n\nMechanistic hypothesis: C7orf25 functions as a bifunctional adaptor bridging the proteasome machinery with ER translocon complexes. The N-terminal DUF5614 domain may anchor C7orf25 to SEC61G at the ER membrane, while the C-terminal DUF1308 domain recruits ubiquitinated substrates to PSMA2-containing proteasomes. This positions C7orf25 as a coordinator of ER-associated degradation (ERAD), facilitating extraction and degradation of misfolded secretory proteins. The well-folded structure enables precise molecular recognition, while immune cell enhancement suggests specialized roles in antigen processing or MHC class I presentation. Pathogenic copy number variants likely disrupt ERAD stoichiometry, causing proteotoxic stress and contributing to developmental or immunological phenotypes.
