# PRR11 - Cell Cycle Progression Scaffold with Helical Domains and Kinesin Interaction Network

**Date:** 2026-03-17T08:44:33.553678

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
PRR11 (UniProt Q96HE9) encodes a 360aa proline-rich protein on chromosome 17q22 with established critical role in cell cycle progression. InterPro analysis reveals two PRR11-specific helical domains: first helical domain (IPR059983, PF28521, residues 69-102) and second helical domain (IPR059984, PF28520, residues 104-135), plus membership in the Actin Dynamics and Cell Cycle Regulator family (PTHR23330, residues 157-359), suggesting structured scaffolding functions despite proline-rich sequence composition. AlphaFold structure prediction shows medium confidence (pLDDT 66.2), indicating a partially structured protein with ordered helical domains embedded in potentially disordered proline-rich regions, consistent with intrinsically disordered protein (IDP) behavior typical of cell cycle regulatory scaffolds. HPA data demonstrates tissue-enhanced expression with highest levels in lymphoid tissue (19.1 nTPM) and bone marrow (17.0 nTPM), immune cell-enhanced specificity, and endoplasmic reticulum localization, consistent with roles in proliferating hematopoietic/immune cells. STRING interaction network reveals 10 partners enriched for mitotic spindle and cell cycle machinery: SKA2 (0.617, spindle microtubule attachment complex), GTSE1 (0.513, G2/mitotic-specific tubulin-glutamylase), CKAP2L (0.513, cytoskeleton-associated protein-like), KIF14 (0.472, kinesin-3 mitotic motor), KIF23 (0.445, kinesin-13 spindle midzone protein), KIF18B (0.440, kinesin-12 microtubule plus-end regulator), plus H1-1 (0.564, linker histone), PRDM15 (0.460, histone methyltransferase), SORD (0.443, sorbitol dehydrogenase), and STAU1 (0.423, RNA-binding protein). This interactome strongly supports mechanistic function as a mitotic scaffold coordinating spindle assembly, chromosome segregation, and cell cycle checkpoint signaling. ClinVar contains 18 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly chromosome 17q22 copy-number alterations (deletions spanning 17q22-q23.2) with some 3UTR and missense mutations (e.g., p.Arg70Lys in first helical domain), indicating disease relevance through genomic instability though no specific Mendelian PRR11-related phenotype is yet defined. The combination of lymphoid/bone marrow expression, helical domain architecture, extensive kinesin/mitotic protein interactions, partial structural order, and clinical variant burden supports a mechanistic hypothesis where PRR11 functions as a proliferating cell-specific scaffold that recruits kinesin motors (KIF14, KIF23, KIF18B) and spindle attachment factors (SKA2, GTSE1) to mitotic chromosomes or spindle microtubules, potentially linking actin dynamics to mitotic spindle assembly and ensuring faithful chromosome segregation in rapidly dividing hematopoietic and immune progenitor cells.
