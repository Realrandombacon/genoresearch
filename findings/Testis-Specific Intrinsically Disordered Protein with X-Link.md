## Description
CXorf49C encodes a 518 amino acid X-linked uncharacterized protein belonging to the CXorf49 family (IPR027822, PTHR31866) containing a domain of unknown function DUF4641 (PF15483: aa85-509). AlphaFold predicts low confidence structure (pLDDT 47.7) indicating the protein is predominantly intrinsically disordered, consistent with non-globular scaffold or regulatory function. HPA reveals striking testis-specific expression (8.1 nTPM) with tissue-enriched RNA pattern, detection in single tissue only, and cancer-enhanced expression, strongly implicating role in spermatogenesis and potential oncogenic function in germ cell tumors. No STRING interactions identified, reflecting limited characterization of this X-linked paralotog. ClinVar documents 54 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly Xp22.33-q28 copy number gains/losses affecting the locus, demonstrating clear clinical significance despite no specific disease annotation. The testis-restricted expression combined with X-chromosome location, intrinsically disordered architecture, and copy number variant pathogenicity suggests CXorf49C functions as a disordered scaffold protein in spermatogenic cells, potentially regulating chromatin organization, meiotic recombination, or sperm maturation. DUF4641 domain may mediate protein-protein interactions with other X-linked testis factors. The higher expression level (8.1 vs 2.0 nTPM for CXorf49B) suggests CXorf49C may be the dominant paralog in testis. Copy number alterations disrupt dosage-sensitive expression required for normal spermatogenesis, potentially contributing to male infertility or X-linked developmental disorders. Cancer-enhanced expression pattern warrants investigation in testicular germ cell tumors.
