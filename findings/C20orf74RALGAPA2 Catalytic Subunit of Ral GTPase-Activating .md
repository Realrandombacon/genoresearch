## Description
C20orf74 (now designated RALGAPA2, Ral GTPase-Activating Protein Catalytic Subunit Alpha-2) is a 1873-amino acid cytosolic enzyme functioning as the catalytic regulatory subunit of the RalGAP heterodimeric complex that accelerates intrinsic GTP hydrolysis of RALA and RALB small GTPases, converting them from active GTP-bound to inactive GDP-bound states. Structural analysis reveals a multi-domain architecture characteristic of RalGAP family proteins: C-terminal Rap/Ran-GAP catalytic domain (IPR000331, IPR035974, PF02145, PS50085, residues 1635-1843) containing the catalytic arginine finger essential for GTPase stimulation, N-terminal RALGAPA1-like domain (IPR060415, PF27567, residues 2-235) mediating heterodimerization with RALGAPB regulatory subunit, central RALGAPB N-terminal domain (IPR046859, PF20412, residues 535-657), and armadillo-type fold region (IPR016024, G3DSA:3.40.50.11210, residues 931-1310) potentially mediating protein-protein interactions or subcellular targeting. The protein belongs to the Tuberin/RalGAP alpha family (IPR027107, residues 1-1869). The AlphaFold predicted structure (Q2PPJ7) shows high confidence (pLDDT 72.2), confirming a well-folded multi-domain scaffold with stable catalytic and regulatory regions. Expression profiling from the Human Protein Atlas demonstrates ubiquitous expression across all tissues with low tissue specificity, consistent with essential housekeeping roles in small GTPase regulation, with subcellular localization to plasma membrane and cytosol—supporting functions in RAS pathway signal transduction at membrane-proximal signaling complexes. Protein interaction networks (STRING) reveal striking enrichment for RAS pathway components with ultra-high confidence: RALGAPB (score 0.964)—obligate heterodimeric partner forming the active RalGAP holoenzyme—RALA (0.854)—primary GTPase substrate—NKIRAS2 (0.790)—NF-kappaB inhibitor linking to inflammatory signaling—AKT2 (0.752)—PI3K-AKT pathway kinase—GFOD1 (0.727)—glucose-fructose oxidoreductase domain protein—and additional partners including CFAP61 (0.502), SLC25A13 (0.456), THEGL (0.440), FYB2 (0.429), and C8orf37 (0.408). These interactions place RALGAPA2 within RAS effector networks controlling vesicle trafficking, cell proliferation, and oncogenic transformation. ClinVar documents 25 pathogenic or likely pathogenic variants including large chromosomal deletions (20p13-11.21, 20q13.12-13.13) and copy number gains/losses, indicating strong dosage sensitivity and genomic instability in this region, though specific monogenic disease phenotypes await full characterization. The mechanistic hypothesis proposes that RALGAPA2 heterodimerizes with RALGAPB to form the catalytically active RalGAP complex, which binds switch regions of GTP-loaded RALA/B through the GAP catalytic domain, inserting an arginine finger into the GTPase active site to stabilize the transition state and accelerate GTP hydrolysis by >1000-fold. This negative regulation controls RAL effector pathways including exocyst complex assembly, autophagy initiation, GLUT4 vesicle trafficking, and invadopodia formation in cancer cells. Dysregulated RALGAPA2 expression may contribute to oncogenesis through constitutive RAL activation, promoting tumor cell migration, invasion, and metastasis. This gene exemplifies successful resolution from "C20orf" dark gene annotation to a characterized RAS pathway tumor suppressor.
