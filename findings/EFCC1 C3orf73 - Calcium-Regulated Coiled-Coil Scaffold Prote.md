# EFCC1 (C3orf73) - Calcium-Regulated Coiled-Coil Scaffold Protein

## Description
EFCC1 (EF-hand and coiled-coil domain-containing protein 1, formerly C3orf73) is a 598 amino acid intracellular protein encoded on chromosome 3q21.3 (UniProt Q9HA90). The protein architecture is dominated by an extensive coiled-coil domain spanning nearly the entire sequence (residues 18-598, PF15799, IPR031601), suggesting structural roles in protein oligomerization or scaffolding. Critically, EFCC1 contains a canonical EF-hand calcium-binding motif (residues 54-89, IPR002048, PS50222) with the characteristic helix-loop-helix fold that confers calcium ion binding capability (GO:0005509). This domain combination positions EFCC1 as a calcium-regulated scaffold: the coiled-coil mediates protein-protein interactions while the EF-hand acts as a calcium sensor that may induce conformational changes upon calcium binding. The extensive coiled-coil (580aa) suggests EFCC1 forms homo-oligomers or heterodimers with other coiled-coil proteins, potentially creating higher-order scaffolding complexes. Calcium binding at the N-terminal EF-hand could regulate these interactions, making EFCC1 a switchable scaffold responsive to intracellular calcium flux. Potential cellular roles include: (1) organizing signaling complexes at membranes where calcium transients occur, (2) stabilizing cytoskeletal structures in a calcium-dependent manner, or (3) recruiting effector proteins to calcium-rich microdomains. Without interaction data (STRING), expression profiles (HPA), or variant information (ClinVar), the precise tissue context and disease relevance remain unclear. However, the domain architecture strongly predicts a mechanoregulatory function where calcium signals control EFCC1's scaffolding activity through conformational switching at the EF-hand domain.
