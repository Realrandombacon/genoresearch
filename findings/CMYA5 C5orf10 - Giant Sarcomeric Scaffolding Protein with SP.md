## Description

InterPro analysis reveals multiple protein-protein interaction domains including fibronectin type III repeats (IPR003961, positions 3704-3898) and a C-terminal B30.2/SPRY domain (IPR001870, positions 3880-4065), characteristic of proteins that organize multi-protein complexes. STRING interaction data shows high-confidence binding to sarcomeric structural proteins: TTN/titin (0.844), CAPN3/calpain-3 (0.791), PRKAR1B/PKA regulatory subunit (0.756), DTNA/alpha-dystrobrevin (0.674), MYOZ2/myozenin-2 (0.437), and ACTN2/alpha-actinin-2 (0.430). This interaction network places CMYA5 at the muscle M-line/Z-disc interface where it likely anchors thick filaments and recruits signaling complexes. HPA expression data confirms muscle specificity with highest expression in tongue (747.0 nTPM) and skeletal muscle (573.2 nTPM), plus cancer-enhanced expression pattern. ClinVar contains 7 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including copy number gains/losses spanning 5q13-14, consistent with dosage sensitivity in muscle tissue. The SPRY domain suggests E3 ubiquitin ligase activity or protein turnover regulation, potentially modulating sarcomeric protein stability. No AlphaFold structure available due to extreme length, but domain architecture predicts an extended scaffolding conformation.
