## Description
TMEM253 (C14orf176) encodes a 217 amino acid transmembrane protein localized to the membrane with confident AlphaFold structure (pLDDT 72.1). The protein belongs to the Transmembrane protein 253 family (IPR038874, PTHR37359) spanning residues 1-199. HPA data reveals strong intestine-enriched expression at 91.3 nTPM with tissue-enriched RNA specificity and cancer-enhanced expression pattern, suggesting epithelial cell function. STRING interactions identify 10 medium-confidence partners including WDR49 (0.583), NOXRED1 (0.580), ARFGEF3 (0.541), TMEM116 (0.539), and multiple TMEM family members (TMEM50A, TMEM151A/B), indicating potential membrane complex formation. ClinVar documents 30 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including copy number gains/losses and deletions at 14q11.2, demonstrating clinical relevance though specific disease associations remain uncharacterized. The well-folded structure (72.1 pLDDT) combined with intestine-specific expression and pathogenic variants suggests TMEM253 functions as an intestinal epithelial membrane protein, potentially involved in nutrient absorption, barrier integrity, or ion transport. Interaction with ARFGEF3 (ARF guanine nucleotide exchange factor) hints at vesicular trafficking role. Pathogenic CNVs affecting this locus may disrupt intestinal epithelial function, contributing to gastrointestinal disease phenotypes.
