# Aminopeptidase O (AOPEP/C9orf3): M1 Metalloprotease with Cell Junction Localization and Dystonia Association

**Date:** 2026-03-18T06:54:44.560027

**Quality Score:** 9/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
AOPEP (C9orf3) encodes Aminopeptidase O, an 819 amino acid M1 family metalloaminopeptidase that catalyzes N-terminal amino acid cleavage from peptide substrates in a zinc-dependent manner. The protein contains multiple catalytic domains including Peptidase M1 membrane alanine aminopeptidase domain (IPR014782, residues 434-651), Leukotriene A4 hydrolase/aminopeptidase C-terminal domain (IPR015211, residues 672-818), and an Armadillo-type fold (IPR016024, residues 684-818) for protein-protein interactions. GO annotations confirm metalloaminopeptidase activity (GO:0070006) and nucleolar localization (GO:0005730). HPA expression shows ubiquitous tissue distribution with low specificity but cell junction enrichment, suggesting roles in intercellular adhesion and signaling. STRING interactions reveal high-confidence partnerships with AGT (0.769), angiotensinogen substrate for blood pressure regulation, DENND1A (0.673), a Rab guanine nucleotide exchange factor, and SUOX (0.621), sulfite oxidase implicated in neurological disorders. Additional interactions with FSHR (0.543) and LHCGR (0.486) suggest endocrine pathway involvement. ClinVar documents 417 pathogenic variants including splice site mutations (c.797+2T>G) and copy number losses on 9q22.32, with disease associations including dystonia. AlphaFold structure (pLDDT 82.9) shows high confidence with well-ordered catalytic HEXXH zinc-binding motif and C-terminal regulatory domains. I hypothesize AOPEP functions as a cell junction proteostasis regulator: (1) the M1 catalytic domain cleaves N-terminal residues from junctional proteins (e.g., cadherins, connexins) modulating adhesion complex turnover, (2) the armadillo fold mediates scaffolding interactions with DENND1A for vesicular trafficking coordination, (3) AGT interaction suggests renin-angiotensin pathway modulation at barrier tissues. Dystonia phenotypes may arise from disrupted basal ganglia synaptic protein processing, while copy number variants cause haploinsufficiency affecting epithelial barrier integrity.
