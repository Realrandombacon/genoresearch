# C1orf36 (RD3) - Retina-Specific GUCY2D Regulator with Leber Congenital Amaurosis Links

**Date:** 2026-03-17T02:08:50.135483

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
C1orf36 encodes RD3 (Retinal Degeneration 3), a 195aa retina-enriched protein (43.8 nTPM in retina) that regulates guanylate cyclase GUCY2D activity in photoreceptors. The protein contains a conserved RD3 domain (IPR028092, PF14473, residues 6-133) with high-confidence AlphaFold structure (pLDDT 81.0). RD3 forms a high-confidence interaction network with phototransduction proteins including PDE6B (0.917), SPATA7 (0.892), LCA5 (0.872), AIPL1 (0.871), RPGRIP1 (0.860), RDH12 (0.851), RPE65 (0.795), and GUCY2D (0.721), positioning it as a scaffold protein in the photoreceptor outer segment. ClinVar documents 38 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including nonsense (p.Gln80Ter, p.Glu132Ter), frameshift (p.Glu27fs, p.His89fs, p.Gln116fs), and missense (p.Leu104Pro) mutations causing Leber congenital amaurosis. The mechanism likely involves RD3 binding to GUCY2D to prevent premature cyclase activation during photoreceptor dark adaptation, with loss-of-function mutations leading to constitutive cGMP elevation, photoreceptor degeneration, and early-onset blindness. Mouse ortholog shows strong conservation (Q8BRE0, 195aa, 100% length identity).
