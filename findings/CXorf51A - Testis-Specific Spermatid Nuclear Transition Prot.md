## Description
CXorf51A encodes a 108 amino acid uncharacterized protein (UniProt: A0A1B0GTR3) located on chromosome Xq27.3. InterPro analysis reveals a spermatid nuclear transition protein domain (IPR040433, PF27987) spanning residues 1-106, placing it in the GAR2-like protein family (PTHR37876). This domain architecture strongly suggests a role in chromatin remodeling during spermatogenesis, where transition proteins replace histones to compact sperm DNA. HPA data confirms testis-specific expression at 67.3 nTPM with no detection in other tissues or cancers, supporting its germ cell-restricted function. STRING interactions identify 10 partners including CXorf49B (0.697), CXorf49 (0.691), OFD1 (0.670), and SPANX family members (SPANXN4: 0.445, SPANXN3: 0.435) - all X-linked genes with testis expression patterns. AlphaFold predicts a medium-confidence structure (pLDDT 68.9) consistent with partially ordered chromatin-binding proteins. ClinVar contains 189 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly Xq27.3-28 copy number losses/gains, indicating clinical relevance in X-linked disorders. Conservation across mammals (hedgehog, whale, camel orthologs 113-147 aa) suggests functional constraint. The combination of testis-enriched expression, spermatid transition protein domains, and X-chromosome location positions CXorf51A as a candidate regulator of sperm chromatin condensation, with haploinsufficiency potentially contributing to male infertility or X-linked developmental phenotypes.
