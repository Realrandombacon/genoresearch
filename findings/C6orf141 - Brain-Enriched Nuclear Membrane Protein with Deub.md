# C6orf141 - Brain-Enriched Nuclear Membrane Protein with Deubiquitinase Association

## Description
C6orf141 (UniProt Q5SZD1) encodes a 244-amino acid uncharacterized protein containing the conserved C6orf141 family domain (InterPro IPR037739, residues 1-200; PTHR36880 family, residues 1-200). This protein-specific domain family suggests C6orf141 represents a distinct structural fold without homology to characterized protein families. HPA data shows brain-enriched expression (8.0 nTPM) with tissue-enhanced pattern and nuclear membrane localization, indicating potential roles in neuronal nuclear envelope function or chromatin regulation. STRING interactions reveal medium-confidence associations with OTUD7B (0.492), MEIS2 (0.466), ZNF586 (0.446), CRBN (0.400), and MBOAT7 (0.400). The OTUD7B interaction is mechanistically significant - OTUD7B is a deubiquitinase regulating NF-kappaB signaling and endothelial barrier function. MEIS2 is a homeobox transcription factor involved in neuronal development and limb patterning. ZNF586 is a zinc finger DNA-binding protein. The nuclear membrane localization combined with deubiquitinase and transcription factor interactions suggests C6orf141 may function as a nuclear envelope scaffold regulating protein stability or transcriptional complexes in neurons. Brain-enriched expression aligns with Alliance of Genome Resources annotation suggesting roles in blastocyst hatching (early developmental process). ClinVar documents 8 pathogenic copy number variants (losses and gains) on chromosome 6p, demonstrating clinical relevance despite limited functional characterization. Disruption could impair nuclear envelope integrity or deubiquitinase recruitment in neuronal cells, potentially contributing to neurodevelopmental disorders.
