## Description
ILRUN (alias C6orf106, FP852) encodes a 298 amino acid intracellular protein (UniProt Q9H6K1) containing an N-terminal UBA-like ubiquitin-binding domain (IPR009060, PF14555, residues 23-64, IPR039517 residues 23-64) and a central NBR1-like FW domain (IPR032350, PF16158, residues 71-180, IPR013783 immunoglobulin-like fold) characteristic of ubiquitin-binding autophagy adapters and protein-protein interaction scaffolds. HPA data reveals nuclear speckle, centrosome, and cytosolic localization with tissue-enhanced expression pattern, highest in skeletal muscle (202.1 nTPM), detected in all tissues, and cell-type enhanced status, with GO annotations for innate immunity, suggesting specialized roles in immune regulation and nuclear RNA processing. The UBA-NBR1 domain architecture combined with nuclear speckle localization and interaction with SPDEF (transcription factor) and SNRPC (spliceosome) positions ILRUN as a regulatory scaffold that may coordinate transcriptional elongation with co-transcriptional RNA processing through transient interactions with ubiquitin-modified chromatin factors and spliceosomal components. High-confidence interaction with SPDEF (0.597) suggests functional coupling between innate immune gene regulation and epithelial differentiation programs. Interaction with HSPBP1 (chaperone regulator) and NUDT3 (mRNA decapping enzyme) indicates potential roles in stress response and mRNA turnover. ClinVar documents 5 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly chromosome 6p copy number gains and losses encompassing the ILRUN locus, indicating dosage sensitivity in disease etiology, potentially affecting immune function and lipid metabolism. The innate immunity annotation combined with skeletal muscle enrichment and nuclear speckle localization positions ILRUN as a tissue-specific transcriptional co-regulator that may modulate interferon-stimulated gene expression or proinflammatory cytokine transcription through ubiquitin-dependent chromatin remodeling mechanisms. Disease-associated CNVs suggest haploinsufficiency or triplosensitivity mechanisms disrupting nuclear speckle integrity or transcriptional co-regulation, potentially leading to immunodeficiency or metabolic dysregulation.
