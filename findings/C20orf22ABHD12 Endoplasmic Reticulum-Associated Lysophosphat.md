## Description
C20orf22 (now designated ABHD12, Alpha/Beta Hydrolase Domain-Containing Protein 12) is a 398-amino acid membrane-associated serine hydrolase functioning as a lysophosphatidylserine (lysoPS) lipase that hydrolyzes lysophospholipids to regulate endocannabinoid tone, neuroinflammation, and immune cell activation. Structural analysis reveals a canonical alpha/beta hydrolase fold (IPR000073, IPR029058, G3DSA:3.40.50.1820, residues 143-391) with the catalytic triad embedded within the hydrolase domain (PF00561, residues 169-274), characteristic of the ABHD enzyme family (PTHR12277, residues 84-391). The AlphaFold predicted structure (Q8N2K0) shows high confidence (pLDDT 84.8), confirming a well-folded catalytic domain with the conserved GXSXG lipase motif and oxyanion hole for substrate binding. Expression profiling from the Human Protein Atlas demonstrates ubiquitous expression across all tissues with low tissue specificity, consistent with housekeeping lipid metabolic roles, with subcellular localization to endoplasmic reticulum membranes—supporting its function in intracellular lipid processing. Protein interaction networks (STRING) reveal strong enrichment for lipid metabolic enzymes: ABHD16A (score 0.855, phosphatidylserine lipase), NAPEPLD (0.803, N-acyl phosphatidylethanolamine phospholipase D producing anandamide), ABHD4 (0.754, lysophospholipase), MGLL (0.747, monoacylglycerol lipase degrading 2-AG), ABHD6 (0.690, 2-AG hydrolase), GPR55 (0.676, lysophosphatidylinositol receptor), DAGLA/DAGLB (0.636/0.621, 2-AG synthesis enzymes), NAAA (0.620, N-acylethanolamine hydrolase), and FAAH (0.410, anandamide hydrolase). These interactions place ABHD12 within the endocannabinoid lipid signaling network. ClinVar documents 127 pathogenic or likely pathogenic variants including missense SNVs (p.Gln118His, p.Trp243Gly, p.Arg2Lys), splice site mutations (c.191+2T>A, c.787+1G>A, c.317-2A>G), frameshifts (p.Arg41fs), and copy number losses (20p11.21), with strong disease association to PHARC syndrome (Polyneuropathy, Hearing loss, Ataxia, Retinitis pigmentosa, Cataract)—a rare neurodegenerative disorder. The mechanistic hypothesis proposes that ABHD12 functions as an ER membrane lipase hydrolyzing lysoPS and related lysophospholipids to prevent toxic lipid accumulation, modulate microglial activation, and maintain neuronal lipid homeostasis. Loss-of-function causes lysoPS accumulation, triggering neuroinflammation, demyelination, and photoreceptor degeneration.
