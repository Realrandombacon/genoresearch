## Description
C20orf17 (now designated TSHZ2, Teashirt zinc finger homeobox 2, also known as OVC10-2) is a 1034-amino acid nuclear transcription factor functioning as a sequence-specific DNA-binding repressor with critical roles in developmental patterning and cell fate determination. Structural analysis reveals a multi-domain zinc finger homeoprotein architecture: N-terminal teashirt family region (IPR027008, PTHR12487, residues 1-1033) spanning the full protein, three N-terminal C2H2 zinc finger clusters (IPR013087, PS00028, PS50157, SM00355, residues 215-239, 275-304, 380-404) for DNA recognition and protein-protein interactions, and a C-terminal homeodomain (IPR001356, cd00086, SM00389, IPR058631, PF26094, residues 840-914) containing the helix-turn-helix DNA-binding motif characteristic of homeobox transcription factors. The AlphaFold predicted structure (Q9NRE2) shows medium confidence (pLDDT 54.5), indicating a partially structured protein with ordered DNA-binding domains embedded within intrinsically disordered transcriptional regulatory regions—typical of eukaryotic transcription factors that function through dynamic protein-protein interactions. Expression profiling from the Human Protein Atlas demonstrates expression in many tissues with low tissue specificity, consistent with developmental regulatory roles, with subcellular localization to nucleoplasm and Golgi apparatus—supporting dual functions in transcriptional regulation and potential secretory pathway involvement. Protein interaction networks (STRING) reveal medium-confidence partners including GLI1 (score 0.493, Hedgehog pathway transcription factor), HOXA7 (0.455, homeobox protein for anterior-posterior patterning), CBY1 (0.453, chibby homolog antagonizing beta-catenin), SMARCE1 (0.442, BAF chromatin remodeling complex subunit), EXD1 (0.438, exonuclease), ZFHX2 (0.423, zinc finger homeobox protein), and LGALS4 (0.416, galectin-4). These interactions place TSHZ2 within chromatin remodeling and developmental transcription factor networks. ClinVar documents 10 pathogenic or likely pathogenic variants including large copy number gains (20q13.13-13.33, 20p13-q13.33) and copy number losses (20q13.13-13.2), indicating strong dosage sensitivity and disease association, though specific disease phenotypes remain undefined. The mechanistic hypothesis proposes that TSHZ2 functions as a transcriptional repressor recruiting chromatin remodeling complexes (SMARCE1/BAF) to target gene promoters through homeodomain DNA recognition and zinc finger-mediated co-repressor recruitment, antagonizing activators like GLI1 and HOX proteins to refine developmental gene expression boundaries. Dosage alterations may contribute to developmental disorders, cancer (given 20q13 amplification in breast/ovarian cancers), or immune dysregulation (immune cell enhanced expression). This gene exemplifies successful resolution from "C20orf" dark gene annotation to characterized developmental transcription factor.
