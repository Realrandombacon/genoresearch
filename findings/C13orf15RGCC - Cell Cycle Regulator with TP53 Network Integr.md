## Description
C13orf15 (RGCC/Response Gene to Complement 32) encodes a 137-amino acid regulator of cell cycle localized to nucleoplasm and nucleoli. InterPro identifies three domain signatures: IPR029252 (Regulator of cell cycle RGCC family), PF15151 (Response gene to complement 32 protein family), and PTHR32193, all spanning residues 1-137, confirming this is a defined protein family. AlphaFold predicts a well-folded structure with global pLDDT 70.1 (confident range), indicating stable tertiary structure suitable for protein-protein interactions. HPA data shows tissue-enhanced expression with exceptionally high levels in lung (668.1 nTPM) and bone marrow (391.7 nTPM), plus immune cell enhancement, consistent with roles in proliferative tissues. Subcellular localization to nucleoplasm/nucleoli supports cell cycle regulatory function. STRING interactions reveal 10 medium-to-high confidence partners forming a coherent cell cycle network: TP53 (0.794 - tumor suppressor hub), CDK1 (0.645 - G2/M kinase), CCNB1 (0.634 - cyclin B1), PLK1 (0.632 - polo-like kinase), AKT1 (0.637 - survival signaling), HIF1A (0.632 - hypoxia response), CNN1 (0.658), TAGLN (0.652), CARS2 (0.623), SMAD3 (0.447). The TP53 interaction is particularly significant as RGCC is known to modulate p53-dependent cell cycle arrest. ClinVar contains 47 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly copy number gains and losses across 13q regions, indicating disease relevance though specific monogenic phenotypes remain undefined. Functional hypothesis: RGCC acts as a nucleolar scaffold protein that integrates DNA damage response (via TP53), cell cycle progression (via CDK1-CCNB1-PLK1 axis), and survival signaling (via AKT1) to coordinate proliferation decisions in response to cellular stress. High lung expression suggests potential role in pulmonary cell turnover or repair. This gene is NOT truly 'dark' - it has established cell cycle regulatory function with defined interaction network, though disease mechanisms remain incompletely characterized.
