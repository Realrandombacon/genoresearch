# C16orf8/RHBDF1: Inactive Rhomboid Protease Regulating EGFR Signaling Through ADAM17 Interaction

**Date:** 2026-03-18T02:14:22.663844

**Quality Score:** 8/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
C16orf8 (official symbol RHBDF1) encodes an 855 amino acid inactive rhomboid protease localized to vesicular membranes. Despite lacking catalytic serine residues typical of active rhomboid proteases, RHBDF1 retains the transmembrane rhomboid fold (PF01694, positions 651-787; IPR022764) and an N-terminal domain (PF12595, positions 91-285; IPR022241). AlphaFold predicts a medium-confidence structure (pLDDT 68.4) consistent with partially ordered transmembrane regions. HPA data shows vesicular localization with broad tissue distribution and low tissue specificity. STRING interactions reveal functional connections to EGFR pathway components: ADAM17 (0.775), TGFA (0.706), EGF (0.613), and related rhomboid family members RHBDL1 (0.641), RHBDD2 (0.503). RHBDF1 lacks catalytic activity but acts as a regulatory scaffold, modulating ADAM17-mediated shedding of EGFR ligands. No ClinVar pathogenic variants identified, suggesting either functional redundancy or that loss-of-function is tolerated. The inactive rhomboid architecture suggests RHBDF1 evolved from an ancestral protease to become a regulatory module controlling growth factor signaling through protein-protein interactions rather than catalysis.
