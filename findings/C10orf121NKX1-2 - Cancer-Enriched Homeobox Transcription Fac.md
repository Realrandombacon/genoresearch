# C10orf121/NKX1-2 - Cancer-Enriched Homeobox Transcription Factor with 56 Pathogenic Variants

## Description
C10orf121 (Gene ID: 414257, alias bB238F13.2) encodes NK1 Homeobox 2 (NKX1-2), a 310 amino acid transcription factor (UniProt Q9UD57) located on chromosome 10q26. Despite the 'orf' nomenclature, the protein contains a highly conserved NKTR homeodomain (InterPro IPR001356, Pfam PF00046, residues 161-225) belonging to the NK-like homeobox family (IPR050394, PTHR24340). The homeodomain confers DNA-binding specificity (GO:0003677) and transcriptional regulation activity (GO:0000981). AlphaFold structure prediction yields medium confidence (pLDDT 63.3), reflecting a structured homeodomain core flanked by disordered regions typical of transcription factors. Human Protein Atlas reveals a striking expression pattern: undetected in normal tissues but enriched in cancers, with subcellular localization in nucleoli fibrillar centers and cytosol. This cancer-specific upregulation suggests a potential oncogenic role or dedifferentiation marker. STRING database reports no interaction partners, indicating this factor operates in isolation or its interactome remains uncharacterized—a hallmark of dark genes. ClinVar documents 56 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly large copy number losses and gains spanning 10q26, implicating NKX1-2 dosage sensitivity in disease, though specific monogenic phenotypes are not yet defined. The combination of a canonical DNA-binding domain, cancer-restricted expression, and complete lack of protein interaction data positions NKX1-2 as a functionally opaque regulator whose target genes and cofactors await discovery.
