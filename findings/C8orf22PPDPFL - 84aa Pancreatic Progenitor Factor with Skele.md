## Description
C8orf22 (PPDPFL) encodes an 84-amino acid pancreatic progenitor cell differentiation and proliferation factor-like protein with UniProt accession Q8WWR9. InterPro analysis reveals conserved domain architecture across residues 1-81 (IPR026754, PF15060, PTHR14572), confirming membership in the pancreatic progenitor cell differentiation factor family with GO annotations for multicellular organism development and cell differentiation. AlphaFold structure (AF-Q8WWR9-F1) shows medium confidence (pLDDT 61.3), indicating partial disorder consistent with regulatory proteins that modulate protein-protein interactions. HPA expression demonstrates tissue-enriched pattern with highest expression in skeletal muscle (741.2 nTPM) and cancer-enriched specificity, suggesting dual roles in muscle physiology and oncogenesis. STRING interactions identify three medium-confidence partners: LYPD2 (0.484), NAT8L (0.462), and C9orf135 (0.412), placing PPDPFL in a network with other poorly characterized proteins. ClinVar contains 23 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly large copy number gains spanning 8p-8q regions, indicating clinical significance though specific disease associations remain undefined. The combination of developmental GO terms, skeletal muscle enrichment, cancer association, and structural disorder suggests PPDPFL functions as a regulatory scaffold protein modulating differentiation pathways in both normal development and malignant transformation. Its conservation across vertebrates (chicken, chimpanzee orthologs) and interaction with LYPD2 (Ly6/PLAUR domain protein) suggests potential membrane-associated signaling roles.
