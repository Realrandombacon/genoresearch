## Description
LTBP2 (formerly C14orf141, also known as MSTP031) encodes a 1821 amino acid latent transforming growth factor beta-binding protein that functions as an extracellular matrix scaffold for elastic fiber assembly and TGF-beta regulation. InterPro analysis reveals multiple conserved domains including 3 EGF-like calcium-binding domains (IPR001881, residues 622-662, 844-886, 887-929), 3 TGF-beta binding TB domains (IPR017878, residues 552-596, 672-724, 1411-1463), and growth factor receptor cysteine-rich domain superfamily (IPR009030). AlphaFold structure shows medium confidence (pLDDT 58.3), reflecting the elongated modular architecture with flexible linker regions typical of extracellular matrix proteins. HPA data shows tissue-enriched expression in blood vessels (337.5 nTPM), classified as secreted plasma protein with disease involvement in dwarfism and glaucoma. STRING interactions identify 10 protein partners including TGFB2 (0.941), FBN1 (0.887), FBLN5 (0.881), CYP1B1 (0.809), ADAMTS10 (0.792), LTBP1 (0.786), WNT10A (0.751), LOXL1 (0.716), MSTN (0.568), and TGFB1 (0.506), demonstrating functional connections to elastic fiber biogenesis, TGF-beta signaling, and extracellular matrix organization. ClinVar contains 139 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including frameshifts (p.Tyr1808fs, p.Asn181fs, p.Leu502fs), nonsense mutations (p.Glu1175Ter, p.Trp417Ter, p.Glu1362Ter), and splice variants, causing autosomal recessive Weill-Marchesani syndrome and primary congenital glaucoma. This gene represents a transition from 'dark' to well-characterized status - originally annotated as chromosome 14 ORF, now recognized as a critical extracellular matrix regulator with established disease mechanisms.
