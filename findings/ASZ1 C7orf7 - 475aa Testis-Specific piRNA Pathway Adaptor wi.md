## Description
ASZ1 (C7orf7) encodes a 475 amino acid ankyrin repeat and SAM domain-containing protein (UniProt Q8WWH4) at chromosomal locus 7q31.1. This represents a PARTIALLY CHARACTERIZED gene with strong testis-specific function but incomplete mechanistic understanding. ClinVar contains 26 pathogenic variants (primarily copy number variations and deletions) though no specific disease phenotypes are assigned, suggesting potential reproductive fitness effects. AlphaFold structure prediction shows medium confidence (pLDDT 67.2), consistent with structured ankyrin/SAM domains connected by flexible linkers. Functional hypothesis: ASZ1 serves as a scaffold adaptor protein bridging PIWIL2-piRNA complexes with Tudor domain proteins (TDRD family) during piRNA biogenesis in male germ cells. The ankyrin repeats likely mediate protein-protein interactions while the SAM domain may facilitate RNA-protein complex assembly. Despite strong pathway association, specific molecular mechanisms remain incompletely defined - whether ASZ1 directly binds piRNA precursors, recruits helicases (MOV10L1, DDX4), or stabilizes piRNA processing intermediates requires experimental validation. This gene exemplifies a 'gray zone' dark gene - pathway assignment is clear but molecular mechanism needs elucidation.
