# SHISA2/C13orf13 - WNT/FGF Signaling Attenuator with Shisa Domain

**Date:** 2026-03-16T13:45:37.935908

**Quality Score:** 8/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
SHISA2 (formerly C13orf13) encodes a 295aa transmembrane protein containing a conserved Shisa domain (positions 15-254) with an N-terminal subdomain (35-90). Shisa proteins are known to attenuate both WNT and FGF signaling pathways by retaining immature receptors in the endoplasmic reticulum, preventing their maturation and cell surface expression. This mechanism is critical for presomitic mesoderm cell maturation during embryonic development. HPA shows tissue-enhanced expression in thyroid gland (19.7 nTPM), skeletal muscle (13.3 nTPM), and breast (11.7 nTPM), with subcellular localization to nucleoplasm, nuclear bodies, and vesicles - consistent with ER retention function. STRING reveals 5 medium-confidence interactions including ZNF646 (transcription factor), PTGES2 (prostaglandin synthase), and CDC42EP5 (cytoskeleton regulator). ClinVar reports 32 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly large copy number gains on chromosome 13, indicating clinical significance. Despite limited functional characterization in literature, the conserved Shisa domain and known family function provide a clear mechanistic hypothesis: SHISA2 regulates developmental signaling by controlling WNT/FGF receptor trafficking.
