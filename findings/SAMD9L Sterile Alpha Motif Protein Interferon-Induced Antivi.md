# SAMD9L Sterile Alpha Motif Protein: Interferon-Induced Antiviral Restriction Factor Paralog of SAMD9

**Date:** 2026-03-18T06:12:54.742145

**Quality Score:** 8/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
C7orf6 (SAMD9L) encodes a 1584 amino acid sterile alpha motif domain-containing protein 9-like (UniProt Q8IVG5), a close paralog of SAMD9. InterPro reveals an N-terminal SAM domain (IPR001660/PF00536, aa 14-60) for protein-protein oligomerization, with additional annotations to the SAM/pointed domain superfamily (IPR013761, aa 8-85) and DED domain-containing protein family (PTHR16155, aa 1-1583). AlphaFold structure (pLDDT 83.8) shows high-confidence folding across the full-length protein, nearly identical to SAMD9 (pLDDT 83.6). HPA shows tissue-enhanced expression with highest levels in lymphoid tissue (34.0 nTPM), cytosolic localization, and disease associations with neurodegeneration and spinocerebellar ataxia. STRING interactions form an interferon-stimulated gene network: IFI44 (0.914), IFIT3 (0.913), STAT1 (0.887), DDX60 (0.872), PARP9 (0.872), IFI44L (0.866), IFIT2 (0.853), and EPSTI1 (0.795), plus paralog SAMD9. ClinVar contains 365 pathogenic variants including missense mutations (p.Ser1473Gly, p.Lys1110Thr, p.Ser116Leu, p.Val834Leu) causing cerebellar ataxia and myelodysplasia. SAMD9L functions as an interferon-induced restriction factor with overlapping yet distinct specificity from SAMD9—both contain SAM domains for oligomerization and P-loop NTPase motifs for GTPase activity. SAMD9L restricts viral replication and cell proliferation through similar mechanisms but shows stronger lymphoid expression, suggesting specialized roles in immune cell antiviral defense. Germline mutations cause neurodevelopmental phenotypes distinct from SAMD9-associated MIRAGE syndrome.
