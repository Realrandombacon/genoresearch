## Description
C19orf47 (Q8N9M1) is a 422 aa uncharacterized dark gene encoding a nucleoplasmic/nuclear speckle protein with striking muscle-enriched expression (skeletal muscle 335.4 nTPM, heart muscle 164.2 nTPM, tongue 128.4 nTPM). Contains two conserved domains: N-terminal SAM domain (aa 38-121, IPR013761/IPR040772, PF18017) for protein-protein interaction/oligomerization, and large C-terminal DUF5577 (aa 130-418, IPR041477, PF17740) of unknown function. Highly conserved in mouse (84.7%) and rat (84.9%), indicating strong mammalian conservation. STRING interactions reveal mRNA processing/export network: NCBP3 (0.611, nuclear cap-binding complex), THOC1 (0.520, TREX mRNA export complex), THOC5 (0.439, TREX complex), GPATCH1 (0.444, mRNA splicing), ZNF780A (0.449, zinc finger), plus dark gene partners C15orf39, C19orf54, FAM124A. HPA shows group-enriched tissue specificity, immune cell enhancement, nuclear speckle localization (sites of mRNA splicing/export). ClinVar contains 11 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including multiple 19q copy number gains, confirming disease relevance. True dark gene: conserved SAM-DUF5577 architecture, extreme muscle enrichment (335 nTPM highest observed), nuclear speckle localization, mRNA export complex interactions, clinically implicated, but molecular function completely unknown. Hypothesis: may function as muscle-specific mRNA export adaptor, using SAM domain for oligomerization and DUF5577 for RNA binding or recruitment of TREX/cap-binding complexes to muscle-specific transcripts, potentially coordinating nuclear export with muscle differentiation programs.
