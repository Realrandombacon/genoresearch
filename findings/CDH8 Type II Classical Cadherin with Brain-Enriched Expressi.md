# CDH8: Type II Classical Cadherin with Brain-Enriched Expression and Catenin-Mediated Adhesion Function

**Date:** 2026-03-18T02:32:30.319163

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
CDH8 encodes a 799 amino acid type II classical cadherin (UniProt P55286) localized to the plasma membrane. InterPro analysis reveals 5 extracellular cadherin tandem repeat domains (positions 81-387) containing calcium-binding motifs (IPR000233, IPR002126) and a C-terminal catenin-binding domain (IPR027397, residues 699-796) that mediates cytoplasmic scaffold assembly. AlphaFold predicts a well-folded structure (pLDDT 77.3). STRING interactions identify 10 high-confidence partners: CTNNB1 (β-catenin, score 0.955), CTNND1 (δ-catenin, 0.829), CDH4 (0.760), CTNNA2 (α-catenin-2, 0.749), and JUP (plakoglobin, 0.748), indicating canonical cadherin-catenin complex formation for adherens junction assembly. HPA shows brain-enriched expression (16.0 nTPM) with 3.4-fold higher levels than seminal vesicle (4.7 nTPM), suggesting neuronal adhesion roles. ClinVar documents 19 pathogenic variants including copy number gains/losses at 16q21, implicating CDH8 in neurodevelopmental disorders. Conservation across vertebrates (mouse/rat 799 aa orthologs) indicates functional constraint. Mechanistically, CDH8 likely mediates homophilic cell-cell adhesion in developing brain through calcium-dependent extracellular domain interactions, while intracellularly recruiting catenins to link with actin cytoskeleton for synaptic stability.
