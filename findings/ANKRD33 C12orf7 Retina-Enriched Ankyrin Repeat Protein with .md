# ANKRD33 (C12orf7): Retina-Enriched Ankyrin Repeat Protein with E3 Ligase Substrate Recognition Function

**Date:** 2026-03-18T00:35:38.038375

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
ANKRD33 (C12orf7) encodes a 452 amino acid photoreceptor ankyrin repeat protein (UniProt Q7Z3H0) functioning as a substrate recognition component of Cullin-2 RING E3 ubiquitin ligase complexes. InterPro analysis reveals multiple ankyrin repeat domains (IPR002110, PF12796) spanning residues 87-254, with three canonical repeats at positions 94-123, 130-160, and 164-193, characteristic of protein-protein interaction scaffolds. The PTHR24173 family assignment indicates homology to Fem-1 substrate recognition proteins, suggesting role in targeted protein degradation. STRING interaction network identifies medium-confidence partners including APRT (0.526), FIGNL2 (0.505), POLR2F (0.482), and nucleotide metabolism enzymes GART, TYMS, and GMPS, positioning ANKRD33 at the intersection of protein turnover and purine biosynthesis pathways. HPA expression profiling demonstrates retina-enriched expression (17.0 nTPM) with tissue-specific distribution, consistent with the photoreceptor designation and potential role in retinal protein homeostasis. ClinVar documents 8 pathogenic variants predominantly as chromosomal copy number variations (deletions and duplications spanning 12q13), indicating dosage sensitivity and disease relevance. AlphaFold structure prediction (pLDDT 60.5) suggests mixed ordered/disordered architecture, with structured ankyrin repeats embedded in flexible regions typical of ubiquitin ligase adaptors. The combination of retina-specific expression, ankyrin-mediated protein binding, and Cullin-2 ligase association supports a mechanistic model where ANKRD33 recruits photoreceptor-specific substrates for ubiquitin-dependent degradation, maintaining protein turnover critical for photoreceptor function and retinal health.
