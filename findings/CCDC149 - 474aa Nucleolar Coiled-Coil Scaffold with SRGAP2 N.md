## Description
CCDC149 (Gene ID: 91050) encodes a 474 amino acid coiled-coil domain-containing protein localized to chromosome 4p15.2 with predicted intracellular classification and nucleolar subcellular localization. InterPro analysis reveals extensive coiled-coil architecture: Coiled-coil domain-containing protein 149 family (IPR019179, residues 1-457), PF09789 coiled-coil domain (residues 1-283), PTHR21682 family classification, and CATH-Gene3D homologous superfamily G3DSA:1.10.287.1490 (residues 91-225), indicating a predominantly α-helical structural scaffold. AlphaFold structure prediction shows medium confidence (pLDDT 68.2), consistent with coiled-coil helical bundles interspersed with flexible linker regions, typical of protein-protein interaction scaffolds. STRING interaction analysis identifies 10 medium-confidence partners led by SRGAP2 (SLIT-ROBO Rho GTPase activating protein 2, 0.540), a critical regulator of neuronal migration and dendritic spine maturation, plus IQCA1 (0.531), ADGB (adiglycan, 0.508), LGI2 (leucine-rich glioma-inactivated 2, 0.487), PPP1R21 (0.478), MYO10 (myosin-X, 0.461), RMDN2 (0.454), DLGAP1 (DLG-associated protein 1, 0.436), SRGAP3 (0.415), and PPP2R5C (protein phosphatase 2 regulatory subunit B5gamma, 0.407). The SRGAP2 and DLGAP1 interactions strongly link CCDC149 to neuronal cytoskeleton organization and postsynaptic density scaffolding, while MYO10 connects to actin-based membrane protrusion and filopodia formation. HPA expression data shows low tissue specificity with ubiquitous detection across all tissues, nucleolar localization, and immune cell-enhanced blood cell expression, indicating constitutive cellular functions with potential immune modulation roles. ClinVar contains 32 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants predominantly consisting of 4p copy number gains and losses (4p16.3-11, 4p15.33-14, 4p16.3-15.1, 4p15.2-15.1 regions), demonstrating significant clinical dosage sensitivity in Wolf-Hirschhorn syndrome (4p deletion) and 4p duplication syndromes affecting neurodevelopment. Conservation data shows orthologs in Bos taurus (319aa), Xenopus laevis (516aa), and Danio rerio paralogs ccdc149a (450aa) and ccdc149b (522aa), indicating deep vertebrate conservation with teleost genome duplication. Mechanistic hypothesis: CCDC149 functions as a nucleolar coiled-coil scaffold protein that coordinates neuronal development pathways through direct interaction with SRGAP2-Rho GTPase signaling complexes. The extensive coiled-coil domains (residues 1-283) likely mediate homo- or hetero-oligomerization and protein scaffolding at the nucleolus, potentially regulating ribosome biogenesis or nucleolar stress responses. Neuronal interaction partners (SRGAP2, DLGAP1, MYO10) suggest critical roles in dendritic spine morphogenesis, synaptic plasticity, and neuronal migration. Ubiquitous expression indicates fundamental cellular functions, while immune cell enhancement suggests potential roles in lymphocyte activation or cytokine signaling. Pathogenic 4p CNVs likely disrupt dosage-sensitive nucleolar scaffolding and neuronal development mechanisms, contributing to intellectual disability, epilepsy, and dysmorphic features in Wolf-Hirschhorn syndrome and related 4p15.2 deletion/duplication disorders.
