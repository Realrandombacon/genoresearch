## Description
CCDC167 (UniProt Q9P0B6) encodes a 97-amino acid coiled-coil domain-containing protein (InterPro IPR028194, residues 1-97; Pfam PF15188, residues 11-93; PTHR31759 family). The entire protein sequence forms a predicted coiled-coil structure, characteristic of scaffold proteins that mediate protein-protein interactions through helical bundle formation. HPA data shows ubiquitous expression across all tissues with low tissue specificity and cytosolic localization, consistent with a housekeeping scaffold function. STRING interactions reveal medium-confidence associations (scores 0.407-0.493) with CHMP7 (0.493), LRRTM4 (0.488), CCDC178 (0.465), PRKCSH (0.455), FN3KRP (0.449), CCDC106 (0.448), SAC3D1 (0.443), METTL26 (0.425), PKIA (0.425), and ARFGEF3 (0.407). The top interaction with CHMP7 is particularly notable - CHMP7 is an ESCRT-III component involved in endosomal membrane scission and nuclear envelope repair. This suggests CCDC167 may function as an ESCRT-III adaptor or regulator, potentially stabilizing coiled-coil interactions during membrane remodeling events. Additional partners include synaptic protein LRRTM4 and metabolic enzymes PRKCSH/FN3KRP, indicating potential roles in neuronal membrane trafficking and glycoprotein processing. ClinVar documents 6 pathogenic copy number variants (losses and gains) on chromosome 6p, demonstrating clinical relevance despite limited functional characterization. The ubiquitous expression pattern combined with ESCRT-III association suggests CCDC167 may serve as a general coiled-coil scaffold facilitating vesicle formation and membrane repair across multiple cell types.
