# XKR7: Brain-Enriched XK-Related Phospholipid Scramblase with Membrane Topology and Clinical Variant Burden

**Date:** 2026-03-18T04:08:11.052806

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
XKR7 (C20orf159, XK-related protein 7) is a 579-amino acid integral membrane protein functioning as a phospholipid scramblase that catalyzes bidirectional translocation of phospholipids across the lipid bilayer. Structural analysis reveals a conserved XK-related protein domain (IPR018629, PF09815, residues 58-441) embedded within a larger XK-related phospholipid scramblase family signature (IPR050895, residues 20-539) spanning most of the protein sequence, characteristic of multi-pass transmembrane transporters. The AlphaFold predicted structure (Q5GH72) shows medium confidence (pLDDT 67.4), consistent with membrane proteins where transmembrane helices are well-predicted but extracellular/intracellular loops show flexibility. Expression profiling from the Human Protein Atlas demonstrates group-enriched expression with highest levels in brain (14.5 nTPM), followed by retina (4.4 nTPM) and pituitary gland (4.1 nTPM), with subcellular localization predicted to plasma membrane—supporting a neuronal/sensory function. Protein interaction networks (STRING) identify CCM2L (score 0.628, cerebral cavernous malformation 2-like protein) as the highest confidence partner, a regulator of endothelial cell junctions and Rho GTPase signaling, alongside PGBD5 (0.608, piggyBac transposable element-derived protein 5, a DNA transposase), CCDC137 (0.529, coiled-coil domain-containing protein 137), ARFGAP3 (0.453, ADP-ribosylation factor GTPase-activating protein 3), ZC3H3 (0.420, zinc finger CCCH-type-containing protein 3), and MRPL12 (0.406, mitochondrial ribosomal protein L12). These interactions place XKR7 within membrane trafficking and signaling networks. ClinVar documents 16 pathogenic or likely pathogenic variants including large copy number gains (20p11.21-q11.23, 20p13-q13.33), duplications, and missense SNVs (p.Arg492His), indicating dosage sensitivity and disease association. The mechanistic hypothesis proposes that XKR7 functions as an ATP-independent phospholipid scramblase disrupting membrane lipid asymmetry by facilitating rapid bidirectional movement of phosphatidylserine, phosphatidylethanolamine, and phosphatidylcholine between leaflets. In neurons, this activity may regulate synaptic vesicle fusion, neurotransmitter release, or apoptotic phosphatidylserine externalization. Brain-enriched expression and CCM2L interaction suggest roles in neurovascular integrity or synaptic plasticity. High variant burden warrants investigation for neurological or ophthalmologic phenotypes.
