# EMC8 - ER Membrane Insertase Subunit with Atg8-like Ubiquitin Fold Bridging Protein Biogenesis and Autophagy

**Date:** 2026-03-16T23:25:40.571583

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
EMC8 (Q9H492) is a 178aa subunit of the ER Membrane Protein Complex (EMC) with a conserved ubiquitin-like/Atg8 domain (PF02991, IPR004241, IPR029071, cd17234) spanning residues 16-120. AlphaFold predicts a high-confidence structure (pLDDT 85.2) with an ubiquitin-like fold and C-terminal alpha-helical extension. STRING reveals tight integration with the EMC holoctomeric complex (EMC1-7, EMC10; scores 0.982-0.998) and connections to ubiquitination (UBA5 0.734) and autophagy (GABARAP 0.689) pathways. HPA shows ubiquitous expression with highest levels in brain (cortex 42.3 nTPM, hippocampus 39.8 nTPM, cerebellum 37.2 nTPM) and ER membrane localization. ClinVar reports 8 variants (0 pathogenic, 5 VUS). The Atg8-like ubiquitin domain is remarkable - Atg8 proteins are central to autophagosome formation and LC3 lipidation. This suggests EMC8 may coordinate ER membrane protein insertion with quality control mechanisms
