## Description
RUSF1 (C16orf58) encodes a 468 aa protein (UniProt Q96GQ5) belonging to the Root UVB-sensitive (RUS) family (IPR006968, PF04884). Contains conserved RUS domain (residues 70-302) annotated as 'Vitamin B6 photo-protection and homoeostasis domain' plus C-terminal RUS domain (residues 304-459, IPR055412). Evolutionarily related to plant proteins involved in UVB light-sensing during root morphogenesis, suggesting ancient photoprotective function. Shows ubiquitous expression across all tissues with low tissue specificity. Multi-compartment localization: nucleoplasm, nucleoli, Golgi apparatus, vesicles, and cytosol - consistent with predicted membrane protein classification. High-confidence interaction with MVD (mevalonate diphosphate decarboxylase, 0.837) links to cholesterol/isoprenoid biosynthesis pathway. Additional interactions include CEBPZOS (transcription regulation), MTRES1 (mitochondrial tRNA modification), SLC5A2 (sodium/glucose cotransporter), IFNGR1 (interferon gamma receptor - immune signaling), and C6orf62 (another dark gene). The MVD interaction combined with Golgi/vesicle localization suggests potential role in lipid metabolism or membrane trafficking. Vitamin B6 domain annotation raises hypothesis about pyridoxine-dependent enzymatic activity or cofactor binding. Represents a functionally enigmatic dark gene with plant homologs but uncharacterized mammalian function.
