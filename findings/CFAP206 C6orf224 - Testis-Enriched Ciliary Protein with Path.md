## Description
CFAP206 (Cilia- and Flagella-Associated Protein 206) is a 296 amino acid protein encoded on chromosome 6q15. This dark gene shows strong testis-specific expression (55.2 nTPM in testis, 21.0 nTPM in fallopian tube per HPA) with group-enriched RNA tissue specificity, consistent with its role in sperm motility and respiratory cilia beating frequency regulation. InterPro analysis reveals a conserved CFAP206 domain (IPR021897, PTHR21442) spanning residues 109-200, indicating structural conservation across vertebrates. AlphaFold predicts a well-folded 3D structure (pLDDT 85.4, high confidence) for residues 1-622, suggesting stable tertiary structure suitable for protein-protein interactions. STRING interaction network identifies 10 high-confidence partners including CCDC113 (0.707), CFAP61 (0.508), CFAP58 (0.493), RSPH4A (0.495), and DRC1 (0.445) - all cilia/flagella-associated proteins forming the radial spoke complex essential for ciliary beating. ClinVar documents 20 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including frameshift mutations (p.Asn477fs) and copy number losses, confirming clinical relevance though specific disease associations remain uncharacterized. The combination of testis-enriched expression, ciliary protein interaction network, and pathogenic variants suggests CFAP206 functions as a structural scaffold protein within the ciliary axoneme radial spoke apparatus, where it stabilizes microtubule-dynein connections required for coordinated ciliary beating. Loss-of-function variants likely cause primary ciliary dyskinesia (PCD) phenotypes including male infertility (asthenozoospermia) and respiratory defects, though this remains to be clinically confirmed.
