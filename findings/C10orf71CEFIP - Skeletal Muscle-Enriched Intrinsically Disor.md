## Description
C10orf71 (Gene ID: 118461), also known as CEFIP (Cardiac-enriched FHL2-interacting protein) or CMD1QQ, encodes a 1435 amino acid intrinsically disordered scaffold protein (UniProt Q711Q0) on chromosome 10q11.23. Despite the 'orf' nomenclature, CEFIP is a recognized protein with defined interaction partners, though its molecular function remains incompletely characterized. The protein contains a C-terminal Domain of Unknown Function (DUF4585, IPR027838/PF15232, residues 1270-1340) embedded within the cardiac-enriched FHL2-interacting family (IPR052303). AlphaFold prediction reveals extremely low confidence (pLDDT 41.8), indicating CEFIP is predominantly intrinsically disordered - a hallmark of scaffold proteins that function through flexible protein-protein interactions rather than enzymatic catalysis. Human Protein Atlas shows striking skeletal muscle enrichment (262.5 nTPM) with secondary expression in tongue (76.5 nTPM), consistent with its cardiac-enriched naming and predicted role in muscle physiology. Subcellular localization includes nucleoplasm and mitochondria, suggesting dual nuclear-cytoplasmic functions. STRING interaction network identifies high-confidence partners including the dual-specificity tyrosine kinases DYRK1A (0.796) and DYRK1B (0.771), the DDB1-CUL4 adaptor DCAF7 (0.689), the mitochondrial AAA ATPase CLPB (0.680), and protein farnesyltransferase subunits FNTA/FNTB (0.631-0.644). These interactions position CEFIP at the intersection of kinase signaling, protein prenylation, and mitochondrial quality control. The gene summary predicts involvement in calcineurin-NFAT signaling, cardiac muscle contraction, and heart morphogenesis - pathways critical for muscle development and hypertrophy. ClinVar documents 69 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including nonsense mutations (p.Arg467Ter), frameshifts (p.Pro119fs, p.Glu638fs), and large 10q copy number variations, indicating dosage sensitivity though no specific monogenic disease is definitively assigned. Mechanistically, CEFIP likely functions as a disordered scaffold that recruits DYRK kinases and FHL2 (Four-and-a-half LIM domains protein 2) to regulate NFAT transcription factor activity in skeletal and cardiac muscle, potentially modulating muscle fiber specification, hypertrophic signaling, or sarcomeric organization at the Z-disc. The mitochondrial localization and CLPB interaction suggest additional roles in mitochondrial proteostasis during muscle metabolism. This gene is NOT truly dark - it has protein evidence and interaction data - but its precise molecular mechanism remains to be elucidated.
