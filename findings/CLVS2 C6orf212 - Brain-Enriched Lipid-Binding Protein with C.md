# CLVS2 (C6orf212) - Brain-Enriched Lipid-Binding Protein with Clathrin Adaptor Interactions

**Date:** 2026-03-16T19:06:20.354924

**Quality Score:** 9/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
CLVS2 (Clavesin-2, UniProt Q5SYC1) is a 327-amino acid intracellular protein characterized by a canonical CRAL-TRIO lipid-binding domain (InterPro IPR001251, Pfam PF00650, residues 105-251) and an N-terminal Sec14-like domain (IPR011074, PF03765, residues 48-72). These domains suggest a function in binding and transporting hydrophobic ligands such as tocopherols, retinaldehyde, or phosphoinositides. HPA data reveals brain-enriched expression (16.5 nTPM) with additional presence in the retina (7.6 nTPM), indicating a potential role in neuronal lipid homeostasis or phototransduction. STRING interaction analysis highlights strong associations with clathrin machinery components, specifically CLTC (clathrin heavy chain, 0.556) and multiple subunits of the AP-1 adaptor complex (AP1G1:0.535, AP1S1/2/3:0.521, AP1M1:0.506), suggesting CLVS2 operates at the trans-Golgi network or endosomes to coordinate lipid transfer with vesicular trafficking. The presence of 21 pathogenic/likely pathogenic ClinVar variants, predominantly copy number losses and gains, underscores its clinical relevance, potentially linking haploinsufficiency to neurodevelopmental or retinal disorders. Mechanistically, CLVS2 likely acts as a lipid chaperone that recruits specific lipids to AP-1/clathrin-coated vesicles, facilitating their sorting and delivery to neuronal membranes or synaptic compartments.
