## Description

This represents a critical finding: C6orf116 is NOT a dark gene but rather a fully annotated scaffold protein with established function in Wnt/beta-catenin and TGF-beta signaling pathways. Functional role: DACT2 encodes a cytoplasmic scaffold protein that antagonizes Wnt/beta-catenin signaling by binding Dishevelled (DVL) and promoting beta-catenin degradation, while also modulating TGF-beta signaling through ACVR1B/TGFBR1 interactions. Mechanistic proposal: DACT2 likely functions through multi-domain protein-protein interactions, recruiting ubiquitin ligases (e.g., SIAH1/2) to DVL complexes, promoting DVL ubiquitination and degradation, thereby preventing beta-catenin stabilization and nuclear translocation.
