## Description
C6orf182 encodes CEP57L1 (Centrosomal protein CEP57L1, UniProt Q8IYX8), a 460 aa intracellular protein required for microtubule attachment to centrosomes. InterPro identifies three structured domains: a centrosome microtubule-binding domain (IPR024957, PF06657, positions 312-384) mediating direct microtubule interactions, a centrosome localisation domain (IPR025913, PF14073, positions 54-231) with coiled-coil structure for centrosomal targeting and gamma-tubulin binding (GO:0043015), and a centrosomal microtubule-associated protein family signature (IPR051756, PTHR19336, positions 2-460). AlphaFold predicts a high-confidence structure (pLDDT 72.6), indicating a well-folded protein with stable tertiary architecture. HPA classifies CEP57L1 as a predicted intracellular protein with low tissue specificity, detected ubiquitously across tissues and cell types, consistent with a housekeeping role in centrosome function. STRING interactions reveal associations with centrosomal proteins: ZNF572 (0.593), CEP152 (0.504, centriole duplication), CEP63 (0.488, centrosome assembly), CEP295 (0.459, centriole biogenesis), POC5 (0.427, centriole stability), and ESCO2 (0.400, cohesin acetyltransferase). ClinVar documents 23 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including missense (p.Lys298Met), splice site (c.744+5G>A), and large copy number losses/gains spanning 6q15-22 regions, though no specific disease phenotypes are established. Mechanistic hypothesis: CEP57L1 likely functions as a structural adaptor at centrosomes, anchoring microtubules via its C-terminal microtubule-binding domain while recruiting gamma-tubulin complexes through its N-terminal coiled-coil localisation domain. The ubiquituous expression suggests essential roles in cell division, ciliogenesis, or microtubule organization across cell types. Disease-associated variants may disrupt centrosome integrity, leading to mitotic defects or ciliopathies. Despite structural characterization and interaction data, the precise molecular mechanism of microtubule anchoring and disease pathogenesis remain incompletely defined - CEP57L1 represents a 'dark gene' transitioning from annotation to functional understanding.
