# C6orf2/TRAF3IP2 - SEFIR Domain Adaptor Protein in IL-17/NF-kappaB Signaling with Immune Cell Enhancement

## Description
C6orf2 encodes TRAF3IP2 (TRAF3-interacting protein 2, also known as ACT1, UniProt O43734), a 574 aa E3 ubiquitin ligase adaptor protein that catalyzes Lys-63-linked polyubiquitination and activates NF-kappaB and AP-1 signaling pathways. InterPro identifies a SEFIR domain (IPR013568, PF08357, PS51534, positions 409-552) mediating homotypic interactions with SEFIR domains in IL-17 receptors (IL17RA, IL17RC) and adaptors, and a TRAF3IP2 family signature (IPR053047, PTHR34257, positions 1-574). AlphaFold predicts a medium-confidence structure (pLDDT 53.2), suggesting a partially folded protein with ordered SEFIR domain and intrinsically disordered regions typical of signaling adaptors. HPA classifies TRAF3IP2 as a transferase and enzyme involved in inflammatory response and Ubl conjugation pathway, localized to Golgi apparatus and vesicles, with immune cell-enhanced expression and detection across all tissues. STRING interactions reveal associations with NF-kappaB and IL-17 signaling components: TRAF3 (0.988, TNF receptor-associated factor 3), TRAF6 (0.968, E3 ubiquitin ligase), IKBKG (0.937, NF-kappaB essential modulator), IKBKB (0.885, inhibitor of NF-kappaB kinase beta), IL17RA (0.876, IL-17 receptor A), IL17A (0.849, IL-17 cytokine), HSP90AA1 (0.749, heat shock protein), IL17RC (0.545), TRAF5 (0.431), and IL17RB (0.418). This network places TRAF3IP2 as a central adaptor bridging IL-17 receptor activation to downstream NF-kappaB and MAPK signaling. ClinVar documents 41 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including nonsense (p.Ser132Ter, p.Arg283Ter, p.Glu325Ter), splice site (c.1201+1G>A, c.1202-2A>G), frameshift (p.Lys465fs), and copy number variants, though no specific disease phenotypes are established. Mechanistic hypothesis: TRAF3IP2 functions as a SEFIR domain-mediated adaptor that recruits TRAF E3 ubiquitin ligases (TRAF3, TRAF6) to activated IL-17 receptors, catalyzing Lys-63-linked polyubiquitination of signaling intermediates. This ubiquitination scaffolds the IKK complex (IKBKG, IKBKB) leading to NF-kappaB activation and proinflammatory gene expression. The Golgi/vesicle localization suggests potential roles in receptor trafficking or signalosome assembly at membrane compartments. Despite extensive pathway characterization, the specific substrate targets, regulatory post-translational modifications, and disease mechanisms of TRAF3IP2 variants remain incompletely defined - it represents a 'partially dark' gene transitioning from pathway annotation to molecular mechanism.
