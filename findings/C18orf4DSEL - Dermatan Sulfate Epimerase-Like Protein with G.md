## Description
C18orf4 (now designated DSEL, Gene ID: 170666) encodes a 1212 amino acid dermatan-sulfate epimerase-like protein (UniProt Q8IZU8) localized to nucleoplasm and plasma membrane. InterPro analysis reveals multiple functional domains: sulfotransferase domain (IPR000863, PF00685, residues 853-1161), chondroitin AC/alginate lyase homologous superfamily (IPR008929, residues 59-401), P-loop NTP hydrolase domains (IPR027417, residues 836-1210), and N-terminal DUF4962 repeat (PF16332, residues 62-392). The protein belongs to the dermatan-sulfate isomerase family (IPR052447, PTHR15532). AlphaFold structure prediction shows high confidence (pLDDT 85.9), indicating a well-folded stable structure. STRING interactions identify 10 protein partners involved in glycosaminoglycan biosynthesis: CHST15 (0.587), CHST3 (0.551), CHST13 (0.539), CSGALNACT2 (0.495), BGN (0.492), DCN (0.448), CHST14 (0.425), CSPG5, TMX3, and FAM240B - all suggesting role in dermatan/chondroitin sulfate modification. HPA shows cancer-enhanced expression with detection in many tissues. ClinVar contains 116 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including copy number gains/losses on chromosome 18q, indicating clinical significance. Functional hypothesis: DSEL catalyzes epimerization of glucuronic acid to iduronic acid in dermatan sulfate chains, modifying glycosaminoglycan structure and affecting extracellular matrix organization, cell signaling, and tissue development. Mechanistic proposal: The sulfotransferase domain transfers sulfate groups while the chondroitin lyase-like domain mediates substrate recognition; P-loop domains may provide nucleotide-binding for catalytic cycling. Disease link: Chromosome 18q aberrations affecting DSEL may disrupt glycosaminoglycan homeostasis, contributing to connective tissue disorders or cancer progression.
