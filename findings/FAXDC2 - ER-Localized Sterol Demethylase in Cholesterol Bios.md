# FAXDC2 - ER-Localized Sterol Demethylase in Cholesterol Biosynthesis

**Date:** 2026-03-17T08:59:52.467310

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
FAXDC2 encodes a 333aa fatty acid hydroxylase domain-containing protein with a C-4 methylsterol oxidase catalytic domain (IPR006694, PF04116, aa 176-299) belonging to the sterol desaturase-related family (IPR050307, PTHR11863, aa 58-304). The AlphaFold structure (pLDDT 92.2) shows very high confidence with well-ordered catalytic core, consistent with a stable enzymatic fold. HPA data reveals parathyroid gland-enriched expression (286.7 nTPM) with ubiquitous detection across tissues, suggesting housekeeping metabolic function. STRING interactions place FAXDC2 within the cholesterol biosynthesis pathway with high-confidence partners: CYP51A1 (0.972, lanosterol 14-alpha-demethylase), SQLE (0.949, squalene monooxygenase), FDFT1 (0.885, squalene synthase), LSS (0.876, lanosterol synthase), SC5D (0.869, sterol C5-desaturase), NSDHL (0.790, sterol-4-alpha-carboxylate 3-dehydrogenase), and ERG28 (0.710, ER membrane protein complex subunit). ClinVar contains 11 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly large copy number losses and gains on chromosome 5q33-q35, indicating dosage sensitivity. Conservation data shows orthologs across vertebrates. The mechanistic hypothesis: FAXDC2 functions as an ER-localized C-4 methylsterol oxidase catalyzing oxidative decarboxylation of 4-alpha-methyl sterol intermediates during cholesterol biosynthesis, working in concert with NSDHL and HSD17B7; parathyroid enrichment suggests specialized role in steroid hormone precursor metabolism.
