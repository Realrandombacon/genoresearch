## Description
C10orf73 (Gene ID: 399567), now standardized as FAM170B (Family with sequence similarity 170 member B), encodes a 283 amino acid protein (UniProt A6NMN3) located on chromosome 10q11.23. Originally annotated as a hypothetical open reading frame, FAM170B is now recognized as a member of the spermatogenesis-associated protein 46 (SPATA46) family, suggesting a conserved role in male germ cell development. InterPro analysis identifies a defining SPATA46-like domain (IPR040879, residues 1-282) and a specific Pfam family match (PF17734, residues 177-239), indicating a structured core despite its 'family with sequence similarity' name. AlphaFold predicts a medium-confidence structure (pLDDT 61.5), suggesting the protein possesses a defined fold but may contain flexible termini or linker regions typical of germline-specific factors. Human Protein Atlas reveals strict testis-enriched expression (6.1 nTPM), with no detection in somatic tissues or cancers, confirming its specialization for spermatogenesis. Subcellular localization is predicted to be intracellular, likely within the developing acrosome or flagellar apparatus given its interaction partners. STRING interaction network highlights associations with key spermatogenic factors: WIPF3 (0.591, Wiskott-Aldrich syndrome protein interacting protein family member 3, involved in actin remodeling), TSSK4 (0.529, testis-specific serine kinase 4, essential for sperm head shaping), PRSS55 (0.527, acrosin-like protease), and EQTN (0.496, equatorin, an acrosomal membrane protein). These partners place FAM170B within the molecular machinery governing acrosome biogenesis, sperm head morphogenesis, and cytoskeletal reorganization during spermiogenesis. ClinVar documents 64 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly large copy number losses and gains spanning the 10q11 region, implying that FAM170B dosage is critical for fertility, though specific monogenic infertility phenotypes are still being mapped. Mechanistically, FAM170B likely acts as a structural or regulatory component of the acrosomal matrix or the manchette (a transient microtubule structure), facilitating the transport of cargo proteins or the remodeling of the actin cytoskeleton required for sperm head elongation. Its interaction with WIPF3 suggests a direct link to actin nucleation, while kinase TSSK4 proximity implies potential phosphorylation-dependent regulation of its assembly function. Loss of FAM170B function could lead to globozoospermia (round-headed sperm) or asthenozoospermia (reduced motility) due to defective acrosome formation or flagellar defects.
