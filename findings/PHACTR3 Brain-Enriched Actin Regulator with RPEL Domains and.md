# PHACTR3: Brain-Enriched Actin Regulator with RPEL Domains and Disease-Associated Variants

**Date:** 2026-03-18T03:55:47.172277

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
PHACTR3 (C20orf101) is a 559-amino acid intracellular protein functioning as a phosphatase and actin regulator. Structural analysis reveals three conserved RPEL repeats (IPR004018, PF02755) at positions 93-118, 401-426, and 439-464, which are known actin-binding motifs that regulate cofilin activity and actin dynamics. The AlphaFold predicted structure (Q96KR7) shows medium confidence (pLDDT 62.7), suggesting a mix of ordered domains and flexible regions typical of cytoskeletal regulators. Expression data from the Human Protein Atlas indicates strong brain enrichment (94.1 nTPM) compared to the pituitary gland (26.0 nTPM), with nucleoplasmic localization. Protein interaction networks (STRING) identify PPP1CA (protein phosphatase 1 catalytic subunit alpha) as the highest confidence partner (score 0.888), supporting its role in phosphatase regulation, alongside TAFA4 and TMA16. ClinVar records 19 pathogenic or likely pathogenic variants, primarily large deletions and copy number gains on chromosome 20q13, implicating PHACTR3 dosage sensitivity in human disease, potentially neurodevelopmental disorders given its tissue specificity. Conservation within the PHACTR family (PTHR12751) suggests an ancient role in linking actin cytoskeleton remodeling to phosphatase signaling.
