## Description
C1orf141 encodes a 400-amino acid uncharacterized protein (UniProt Q5JVX7) containing a DUF4545 domain of unknown function (IPR027847/PF15078, residues 114-395). AlphaFold prediction shows low confidence (pLDDT 47.7), indicating an intrinsically disordered protein that likely functions through protein-protein interactions rather than enzymatic catalysis. HPA classifies C1orf141 as a predicted intracellular protein with intermediate filament subcellular localization, showing testis-enriched expression (14.3 nTPM) and moderate expression in fallopian tube (8.7 nTPM), with tissue-enhanced specificity. STRING interactions reveal medium-confidence associations with ITPRID1 (0.576), IL23R (0.505), SLC35D1 (0.476), LAPTM4B (0.463), and FIG4 (0.447). IL23R is the interleukin-23 receptor involved in Th17 cell differentiation and inflammatory responses. LAPTM4B is a lysosomal/autophagy protein, and FIG4 is a phosphoinositide phosphatase regulating lysosomal function and membrane trafficking. This interaction pattern suggests C1orf141 may function at the intersection of intermediate filament cytoskeleton and immune/lysosomal membrane dynamics. ClinVar documents 25 pathogenic/likely pathogenic copy number variants (deletions and duplications) spanning 1p31.3-22.3, indicating dosage sensitivity and disease relevance. Orthologs exist in Mus musculus (Q9D5Q8, 446 aa), Bos taurus (Q2KIL1, 430 aa), and Equus caballus, demonstrating evolutionary conservation across mammals. We hypothesize C1orf141 functions as a disordered scaffold protein that anchors IL23R signaling complexes or lysosomal trafficking machinery to intermediate filament networks in testis and reproductive tissues, potentially regulating cytokine receptor turnover or autophagic flux in germ cells.
