## Description
C1orf232 (chromosome 1 open reading frame 232, alias C1orf230) encodes a 186 amino acid partially structured intracellular protein (UniProt A0A0U1RR37, AlphaFold pLDDT 63.0) with medium structural confidence suggesting a mix of ordered and disordered regions, potentially enabling conformational flexibility for scaffold or adaptor functions. InterPro analysis identifies membership in the testis development-related protein family (PTHR35663, residues 5-183) spanning nearly the entire protein, strongly suggesting a role in spermatogenesis, germ cell differentiation, or testis-specific cellular processes. HPA reveals no detection in bulk tissue RNA-seq but cell type-enhanced specificity at single-cell resolution, indicating highly restricted expression in rare cell populations—likely spermatogenic cells, germ cells, or other testis-specific lineages below bulk detection thresholds. STRING interactions identify 10 medium-to-high confidence partners (scores 0.621-0.829) including EDDM13 (E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase EDDM13, score 0.829—highest confidence), CCDC201 (coiled-coil domain protein 0.786), multiple SMIM family proteins (SMIM36 0.771, SMIM41 0.662, SMIM38 0.643—small integral membrane proteins), C10orf143 (uncharacterized chromosome 10 protein 0.769), TMDD1 (transmembrane domain protein 0.644), SCYGR5/7 (spermatogenesis factors 0.642), and FAM240C (family with sequence similarity 0.621)—the exclusive enrichment of E3 ubiquitin ligases, small membrane proteins, coiled-coil adaptors, and spermatogenesis factors strongly suggests C1orf232 functions as a testis-specific ubiquitination pathway scaffold linking membrane proteins to proteasomal degradation during germ cell development. ClinVar contains 2 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, exclusively chromosome 1p copy number gains spanning 99kb-41Mb regions including the C1orf232 locus at 1p36.11 (26.2Mb), indicating dosage sensitivity where gene amplification disrupts testis development or spermatogenesis—1p36 deletions cause well-characterized 1p36 deletion syndrome with developmental delay, hypogonadism, and reproductive abnormalities. Conservation data shows remarkable sequence preservation: human/chimp (186aa), dog (188aa), zebrafish (198aa), and barramundi (209aa) orthologs confirm strong evolutionary constraint on testis development functions. The combination of testis development family membership, E3 ligase/SMIM/spermatogenesis factor interactions, cell type-restricted expression, 1p CNV pathogenicity, and partial disorder suggests C1orf232 functions as an intrinsically disordered scaffold in testis-specific ubiquitination complexes, potentially regulating turnover of small integral membrane proteins or organizing membrane-proximal signaling assemblies during spermatogenesis. Chromosome 1p36 alterations may cause C1orf232 dosage disruption leading to impaired germ cell maturation, spermatogenic failure, or hypogonadism phenotypes observed in 1p36 deletion syndrome.
