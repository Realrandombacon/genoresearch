## Description
C10orf12 (Gene ID: 26148, chromosome 10q24.1) encodes the Ligand-dependent Corepressor (LCOR), a 989 amino acid nuclear protein (UniProt A0PJI9). Despite the 'orf' nomenclature suggesting darkness, this gene is functionally characterized as a transcriptional corepressor that interacts with ligand-bound nuclear receptors. The protein contains a C-terminal DUF4553 domain (InterPro IPR028104, Pfam PF15090, residues 613-988) and belongs to the PANTHER PTHR14931 family. AlphaFold structure prediction shows low confidence (pLDDT 39.3), indicating intrinsic disorder consistent with corepressor function through protein-protein interactions rather than enzymatic activity. STRING interaction network reveals 10 high-confidence partners including Polycomb repressive complex 2 components (EZH2 score 0.900, SUZ12 score 0.545, MTF2 score 0.949, PHF1 score 0.900, PHF19 score 0.843), estrogen receptor ESR1 (score 0.866), and paralogs EZH1 (0.839) and EPOP (0.839). Human Protein Atlas shows ubiquitous expression with highest levels in bone marrow (24.1 nTPM), nucleoplasmic localization, and classification as transcription regulator with DNA-binding and repressor activity. ClinVar contains 19 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly copy number gains and losses across 10q23-26, indicating clinical significance though specific disease associations require further characterization. The interaction with PRC2 components suggests LCOR recruits chromatin-modifying complexes to nuclear receptor target genes, providing a mechanistic link between hormone signaling and epigenetic silencing.
