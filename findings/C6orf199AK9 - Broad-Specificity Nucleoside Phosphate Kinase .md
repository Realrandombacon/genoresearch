## Description
C6orf199 encodes AK9 (Adenylate kinase 9, UniProt Q5TCS8), a 1911 aa broad-specificity nucleoside phosphate kinase involved in cellular nucleotide homeostasis. InterPro identifies multiple catalytic domains: adenylate kinase/UMP-CMP kinase domains (IPR000850, PF00406, positions 993-1161 and 1370-1578) with ATP binding (GO:0005524) and kinase activity (GO:0019205), AAA+ ATPase domains (IPR003593, SM00382, positions 29-241, 990-1333, 1410-1577) for nucleotide-dependent conformational changes, and P-loop NTP hydrolase folds (G3DSA:3.40.50.300, IPR027417) across multiple regions. Expression is ubiquitous (detected in all tissues) with low tissue specificity and cell type enhancement. ClinVar documents 32 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including missense (p.Gly1494Val, p.Pro815Leu), frameshift (p.Cys669fs), splice site, and copy number variants, though no specific disease phenotypes are established. Mechanistic hypothesis: AK9 functions as a nucleoside phosphate kinase catalyzing reversible phosphate transfer between nucleoside mono-, di-, and triphosphates (e.g., ATP + NMP ↔ ADP + NDP). The multiple adenylate kinase domains suggest broad substrate specificity across purine and pyrimidine nucleotides. AAA+ domains may regulate catalytic activity through nucleotide-dependent conformational changes or oligomerization. Nuclear and calyx localization suggests roles in nuclear nucleotide pool maintenance, DNA/RNA precursor supply, or chromatin-associated nucleotide metabolism. Despite structural and biochemical characterization, the specific physiological substrates, regulatory mechanisms, and disease relevance of AK9 remain incompletely defined - it represents a 'partially dark' gene with enzymatic potential awaiting functional validation.
