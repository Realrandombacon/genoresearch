# WFIKKN1: Multi-Domain Secreted Protease Inhibitor Regulating TGF-β Superfamily Signaling

**Date:** 2026-03-18T01:42:26.870103

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
WFIKKN1 (C16orf12) encodes a 548 aa secreted multi-domain protease inhibitor with complex domain architecture. InterPro analysis reveals 6 distinct domain types: two Kunitz domains (IPR002223, positions 297-352 and 356-410) conferring serine protease inhibition, one Kazal domain (cd00104, 120-155), an immunoglobulin I-set domain (cd05765, 186-280), and a C-terminal NTR/netrin domain (IPR001134, 409-540) implicated in metalloprotease inhibition. AlphaFold predicts a well-folded structure (pLDDT 78.1). STRING interactions identify 10 high-confidence partners including NTSR1 (0.875), MSTN/myostatin (0.796), BMP2 (0.668), FST/follistatin (0.575), and TGFB1 (0.444), placing WFIKKN1 in TGF-β superfamily regulation. HPA shows brain-enriched expression (5.0 nTPM), tissue-enhanced pattern, and cancer-enhanced status, consistent with secreted protein classification. ClinVar documents 47 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants (primarily 16p13.3 CNVs), suggesting dosage sensitivity. The protein likely functions as a extracellular scaffold that simultaneously inhibits serine proteases via Kunitz/Kazal domains and sequesters TGF-β family ligands (MSTN, BMP2/4, GDF11) via immunoglobulin/NTR domains, analogous to follistatin but with broader specificity. This dual inhibitory mechanism positions WFIKKN1 as a negative regulator of muscle growth (myostatin pathway), neurodevelopment (BMP signaling), and extracellular matrix remodeling.
