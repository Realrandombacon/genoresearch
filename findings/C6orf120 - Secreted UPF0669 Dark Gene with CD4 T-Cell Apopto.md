## Description
C6orf120 (Q7Z4R8) is a 191 aa uncharacterized dark gene encoding a predicted secreted protein with apoptosis induction capability specifically in CD4(+) T-cells (not CD8(+) T-cells or hepatocytes). Contains defining UPF0669 family domain (aa 8-191, IPR031420, PF17065, PTHR31703) annotated as "Putative cytokine" — suggesting potential cytokine-like signaling function despite no canonical cytokine fold. BLAST reveals strong vertebrate conservation: 97.9% macaque, 85.3% bovine, 76.1% mouse, 71.2% chicken, 60.1% Xenopus, 57.1% zebrafish, indicating deep purifying selection. HPA confirms secreted protein classification with apoptosis as associated biological process, ubiquitous tissue distribution, low tissue/cancer/immune specificity, but cell type enhanced single cell specificity. ClinVar contains 75 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including large 6q copy number losses/gains (6q24.2-27, 6q25.2-27, 6q26-27), confirming strong disease relevance. True dark gene: conserved UPF0669/putative cytokine architecture, CD4+ T-cell specific apoptosis function (immune cell selectivity), ER membrane protein interaction network (ERLIN1/RNF170/PGAP3), secreted protein classification, clinically implicated with massive CNV burden, but molecular mechanism completely unknown. Hypothesis: may function as a secreted cytokine-like factor that selectively induces CD4+ T-cell apoptosis through ER stress signaling or ERAD pathway modulation (via RNF170/ERLIN1 interactions), potentially serving as immune tolerance regulator or activation-induced cell death mediator, with the UPF0669 domain mediating receptor binding or oligomerization, and dysregulation contributing to 6q deletion syndromes with immune phenotypes.
