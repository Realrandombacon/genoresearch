# C16orf47/ZFHX3: Multi-Zinc Finger Homeodomain Transcription Factor with Developmental and Oncogenic Functions

**Date:** 2026-03-18T02:13:01.256625

## Description
C16orf47 (official symbol ZFHX3) encodes a massive 3,703 amino acid nuclear transcription factor containing three homeodomains (positions 2143-2207, 2240-2304, 2639-2703; IPR001356) and multiple C2H2-type zinc fingers (IPR013087) distributed throughout its sequence. The protein functions as both transcriptional activator and repressor, binding DNA through its homeodomains while recruiting chromatin modifiers via zinc finger domains. HPA data shows cancer-enhanced expression with nucleoplasmic localization, consistent with its role in transcriptional regulation. STRING interactions reveal connections to developmental transcription factors (RUNX3 score 0.846, POU1F1 0.775, PITX2 0.720, TBX3 0.590), cell cycle regulator CDKN1A (0.781), chromatin remodeler CHD4 (0.631), and signaling molecule TGFB1 (0.605). ClinVar contains 30 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including copy number gains/losses across 16q21-24.3, implicating ZFHX3 dosage in disease. The multi-domain architecture suggests ZFHX3 acts as a scaffold protein integrating developmental signaling with chromatin state, potentially coordinating myogenesis and neural development through combinatorial DNA binding and protein recruitment. No AlphaFold structure available due to protein size.
