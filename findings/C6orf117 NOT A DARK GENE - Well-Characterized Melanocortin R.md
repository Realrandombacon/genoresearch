## Description
C6orf117 (Gene ID: 353265), historically named "chromosome 6 open reading frame 117", is actually a WELL-CHARACTERIZED G-protein coupled receptor (GPCR) accessory protein now officially named MRAP2 (Melanocortin 2 Receptor Accessory Protein 2). This represents a critical finding: C6orf117 is NOT a dark gene but rather a fully annotated membrane protein with established function in melanocortin receptor signaling and energy homeostasis. UniProt accession Q96G30 encodes a 205-amino acid single-pass transmembrane protein. InterPro domains confirm functional annotation: IPR028111 (Melanocortin-2 receptor accessory protein family, residues 1-205), PF15183 (MRAP family domain, 13-99), PTHR28675 (MRAP2 family, 1-205). The MRAP domain architecture is characteristic of GPCR chaperones that facilitate receptor trafficking and ligand binding. AlphaFold structure prediction shows global pLDDT score of 57.1 (medium confidence), indicating partially structured transmembrane protein with ordered transmembrane helix but flexible extracellular/interacellular regions, consistent with small membrane accessory protein architecture. STRING interactions reveal 10 high-confidence protein partners within melanocortin signaling: MC2R (0.941, melanocortin-2 receptor), MC4R (0.857, melanocortin-4 receptor), MC5R (0.835, melanocortin-5 receptor), PROKR1 (0.752, prokineticin receptor 1), MC1R (0.706, melanocortin-1 receptor), POMC (0.629, proopiomelanocortin), AGRP (0.599, agouti-related peptide), MRAP (0.589, MRAP paralog), MC3R (0.474, melanocortin-3 receptor), GHSR (0.444, growth hormone secretagogue receptor). These interactions place MRAP2 as a broad-spectrum melanocortin receptor modulator. Human Protein Atlas (ENSG00000135324) classifies MRAP2 as disease-related gene, predicted membrane protein, tissue enriched RNA specificity, detected in many tissues, top expression in blood vessel (97.2 nTPM), low cancer specificity, and obesity disease involvement. ClinVar contains 18 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including deletions (c.116del p.Lys39fs), missense variants (c.289C>T p.Pro97Ser, c.521A>G p.Gln174Arg), splice variants (c.228-1835C>T), and copy number alterations (6q14.1-16.1 deletions/duplications), indicating strong association with monogenic obesity and metabolic disorders. Functional role: MRAP2 encodes a single-pass transmembrane accessory protein that physically interacts with melanocortin receptors (MC2R, MC4R, MC5R), facilitating receptor maturation, cell surface expression, and ligand responsiveness. Mechanistic proposal: MRAP2 likely functions through homodimerization or heterodimerization with MRAP, forming antiparallel transmembrane helices that chaperone melanocortin receptors through ER/Golgi trafficking, stabilize receptor conformation, and modulate G-protein coupling efficiency. MRAP2 exhibits broader receptor specificity than MRAP1, interacting with MC4R (energy balance), MC2R (adrenal steroidogenesis), and MC5R (exocrine function). Loss-of-function MRAP2 variants cause monogenic obesity through impaired MC4R signaling in hypothalamic appetite regulation.
