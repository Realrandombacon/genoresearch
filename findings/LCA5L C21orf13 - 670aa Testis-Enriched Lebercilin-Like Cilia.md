## Description
LCA5L (Gene ID: 54095, alias: C21orf13) encodes a 670 amino acid lebercilin-like protein localized to chromosome 21q22.2 with predicted intracellular classification and dual nucleoplasmic/nuclear speckle/cytosolic/flagellar centriole/mid piece subcellular localization. InterPro analysis reveals conserved domain architecture: Lebercilin-like family (IPR026188, residues 3-665), Lebercilin domain (IPR028933, PF15619, residues 140-331), and PTHR16650 family classification (C21orf13-related), indicating a deeply conserved eukaryotic ciliary protein family with homology to LCA5 (lebercilin) mutations causing Leber congenital amaurosis type 5, a severe early-onset retinal dystrophy. AlphaFold structure prediction shows medium confidence (pLDDT 62.3), consistent with partially structured architecture containing both ordered coiled-coil domains and intrinsically disordered regions, typical of ciliary scaffold proteins involved in axonemal assembly and intraflagellar transport. STRING interaction analysis identifies 10 medium-confidence partners with striking ciliary/flagellar functional coherence: KRTAP21-2 (keratin associated protein 21-2, 0.585), a structural protein; WDR90 (WD repeat domain 90, 0.577), a conserved centriolar protein critical for ciliogenesis and microtubule organization; SH3BGRL (SH3 binding glutamate-rich protein like, 0.568), a signaling adapter; EFHB (EF-hand calcium binding domain protein, 0.541), a calcium sensor; XKR6 (xk-related protein 6, 0.539), a phospholipid scramblase; DNAH12 (dynein axonemal heavy chain 12, 0.537), a motor protein for ciliary/flagellar beating; B3GALT5 (beta-1,3-galactosyltransferase 5, 0.537), a glycosyltransferase; SH3BGR (SH3 binding glutamate-rich protein, 0.431), a cardiac development regulator; MTMR9 (myotubularin-related protein 9, 0.426), a phosphatase; and PSMG1 (proteasome assembly chaperone 1, 0.425), a proteasome assembly factor. The WDR90 interaction (0.577) strongly links LCA5L to centriolar satellite function and ciliogenesis - WDR90 is essential for distal appendage assembly and ciliary membrane protein recruitment. DNAH12 connection (0.537) places LCA5L within axonemal dynein complexes responsible for ciliary/flagellar motility. HPA expression data reveals tissue-enriched RNA pattern with exclusive testis enrichment (103.2 nTPM, highest expression), detected in many tissues pattern, low cancer specificity, not detected in immune cells, and group enriched cell type specificity, indicating highly specialized reproductive functions with critical roles in spermatogenesis, sperm flagellar assembly, or sperm motility. The flagellar centriole and mid piece localization confirms roles in sperm tail (flagellum) structure and function. ClinVar contains 71 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants consisting predominantly of 21q22 copy number losses and gains (21q11.2-22.3, 21q22.11-22.3, 21q22.13-22.3, 21q21.3-22.3 regions including chr21:13644166-44968483, chr21:32634806-43353470, chr21:39652840-45862615, chr21:34586759-48097372, chr21:15380398-48100790, chr21:15023401-48097372, chr21:34092685-48097372, chr21:35872675-48097372, chr21:26929299-48097372, chr21:30685776-48097372), demonstrating significant clinical dosage sensitivity in chromosomal rearrangement syndromes affecting the 21q22 locus (Down syndrome critical region). Trisomy 21 (Down syndrome) features male infertility and impaired spermatogenesis, potentially linked to LCA5L dosage effects. Conservation data shows orthologs across vertebrates (PTHR16650 family), indicating deep evolutionary conservation with ciliary/flagellar functions preserved. Mechanistic hypothesis: LCA5L functions as a testis-enriched ciliary/flagellar scaffold protein that coordinates axonemal assembly and sperm motility through lebercilin domain-mediated protein-protein interactions. The medium AlphaFold confidence (62.3 pLDDT) and PF15619 coiled-coil annotation suggest an extended helical architecture capable of multimerization and microtubule binding. WDR90 interaction places LCA5L within centriolar distal appendage complexes essential for ciliary vesicle docking and ciliogenesis initiation. DNAH12 interaction links LCA5L to axonemal dynein motor complexes, suggesting roles in regulating flagellar beating frequency or waveform. Testis enrichment (103.2 nTPM) indicates critical functions in spermatid differentiation, sperm flagellum assembly, or sperm motility acquisition. Lebercilin homology to LCA5 (mutated in Leber congenital amaurosis type 5) suggests shared molecular mechanisms between photoreceptor cilia and sperm flagella - both are specialized ciliary structures requiring intraflagellar transport and axonemal maintenance. LCA5L may function in cargo trafficking along the sperm flagellum or structural integrity of the axoneme. Pathogenic 21q22 CNVs likely disrupt dosage-sensitive ciliary networks, contributing to male infertility phenotypes in trisomy 21 (Down syndrome) and 21q deletion/duplication syndromes via impaired spermatogenesis, defective sperm motility (asthenozoospermia), or abnormal flagellar morphology. The nuclear speckle localization suggests potential additional roles in RNA splicing or transcriptional regulation in germ cells.
