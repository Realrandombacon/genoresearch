## Description
C10orf138 (Gene ID: 414234, chromosome 10q26.11) encodes EEF1A Lysine Methyltransferase 2 (EEF1AKMT2), a 236 amino acid epigenetic enzyme (UniProt Q5JPI9) that catalyzes lysine methylation on translation elongation factor 1-alpha. Despite the 'orf' nomenclature, this protein contains well-characterized catalytic machinery including an S-adenenosyl-L-methionine (SAM)-dependent methyltransferase domain (InterPro IPR025714/ IPR029063, residues 81-213, PF13847) belonging to the Efm4/METTL10 family (IPR026635, residues 24-236). The catalytic core adopts a classic Rossmann-like methyltransferase fold (G3DSA:3.40.50.150, SSF53335) that binds SAM cofactor for methyl group transfer. AlphaFold structure prediction yields very high confidence (pLDDT 89.8), indicating a stable, well-folded enzymatic structure consistent with catalytic function. Human Protein Atlas reveals ubiquitous expression across all tissues with low tissue specificity, localized to nucleoplasm and cytosol, suggesting housekeeping epigenetic regulation roles. STRING interaction network identifies 10 partners including the substrate EEF1A1 (0.887, high confidence) and paralogous lysine methyltransferases EEF2KMT (0.806), EEF1AKMT1 (0.786), VCPKMT (0.749), ETFBKMT (0.622), EEF1AKMT4 (0.561), METTL21C (0.465), EEF1AKNMT (0.446), METTL21A (0.432), and METTL25 (0.413), placing EEF1AKMT2 within the elongation factor methylation network that regulates translation fidelity and protein stability. ClinVar documents 57 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly large copy number alterations spanning 10q26, indicating dosage sensitivity though no specific monogenic disease phenotype is defined. The combination of canonical methyltransferase structure, EEF1A1 substrate interaction, and ubiquitous expression suggests EEF1AKMT2 functions as a constitutive epigenetic regulator of translation elongation, potentially modulating protein synthesis rates, co-translational folding, or stress response pathways through reversible lysine methylation marks.
