# CHCHD2 Mitochondrial CHCH Protein: Oxidative Phosphorylation Regulator Linked to Parkinson Disease

## Description
C7orf17 (CHCHD2) encodes a 151 amino acid mitochondrial protein (UniProt Q9Y6H1) containing a C-terminal coiled-coil-helix-coiled-coil-helix (CHCH) domain (IPR010625/PF06747, aa 114-146) with conserved cysteine motifs for mitochondrial import and protein stabilization. AlphaFold structure (pLDDT 62.9) shows medium confidence with structured CHCH domain and flexible N-terminus. HPA confirms mitochondrial localization with ubiquitous tissue expression. STRING interactions reveal functional network within mitochondrial machinery: CHCHD10 (0.888) and CHCHD4/MIAC59 (0.779) paralogues for mitochondrial protein import, COX4I1 (0.752) and COX6A1 (0.529) for cytochrome c oxidase complex assembly, ATP5PD (0.417) for ATP synthase, plus Parkinson disease-associated proteins PLA2G6 (0.743), VPS35 (0.729), and PARK7/DJ-1 (0.540). ClinVar contains 24 pathogenic variants including missense mutations (p.Ala147Gly, p.Met56Thr, p.Gln100His) associated with Parkinson disease and mitochondrial encephalomyopathy. CHCHD2 functions as a transcriptional coactivator binding the COX10 promoter via N-terminal region, upregulating cytochrome c oxidase subunit expression to maintain oxidative phosphorylation capacity. The CHCH domain mediates mitochondrial intermembrane space anchoring through disulfide bonds with Mia40/CHCHD4 import machinery. CHCHD2 dysfunction causes mitochondrial respiratory chain defects, increased ROS production, and dopaminergic neuron degeneration in Parkinson disease pathogenesis.
