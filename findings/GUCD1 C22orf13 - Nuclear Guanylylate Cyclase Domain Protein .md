# GUCD1 C22orf13 - Nuclear Guanylylate Cyclase Domain Protein with 22q11.23 Disease Association

## Description
GUCD1 (C22orf13) encodes a 240aa nuclear protein containing a guanylylate cyclase domain (PF09778, positions 22-236) within a cysteine proteinase fold (G3DSA:3.90.70.10, positions 16-230). Despite harboring a cyclase domain, GUCD1 lacks canonical catalytic residues found in active guanylyl cyclases (GUCY1A1, GUCY2C), suggesting it functions as a pseudokinase or regulatory scaffold rather than a catalytic enzyme. The protein shows high structural confidence (AlphaFold pLDDT 86.6) indicating a well-folded stable domain.\n\nExpression profile (HPA): GUCD1 is ubiquitously expressed across all tissues with low tissue specificity, localized to the nucleoplasm. This pan-expression pattern suggests a fundamental cellular role rather than tissue-specific function.\n\nProtein interactions (STRING): GUCD1 interacts with 10 partners at medium confidence (0.51-0.65), including LRRC75B (0.652), ZNF652 (0.606), ASCC2 (0.602), MTMR8 (0.593), and SLC25A39 (0.556). These interactions span chromatin regulation (ZNF652 zinc finger), mRNA processing (ASCC2), phosphatase activity (MTMR8 myotubularin), and mitochondrial metabolism (SLC25A39). The diversity suggests GUCD1 may serve as a nuclear scaffold coordinating multiple processes.\n\nClinical significance (ClinVar): 53 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants identified, predominantly copy number gains in 22q11.23 region. This genomic locus is associated with DiGeorge/velocardiofacial syndrome phenotypes, though GUCD1-specific disease mechanisms remain uncharacterized. The variant burden indicates functional importance despite limited mechanistic understanding.\n\nFunctional hypothesis: GUCD1 likely functions as a nuclear regulatory protein that binds cyclic nucleotides or mimics cyclase signaling without catalytic activity. The guanylylate cyclase domain may serve as a protein-protein interaction module recruiting partners to chromatin or transcriptional complexes. Given nuclear localization and interaction with chromatin regulators, GUCD1 may modulate gene expression through cGMP-independent scaffolding mechanisms.
