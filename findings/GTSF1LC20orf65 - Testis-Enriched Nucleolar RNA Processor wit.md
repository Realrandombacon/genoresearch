## Description
GTSF1L (Gametocyte-specific factor 1-like, UniProt Q9H1H1, 148 aa) is a UPF0224 family dark gene encoding a nucleolar RNA-processing protein with testis-specific expression. InterPro analysis reveals two CHHC-type zinc finger domains (PF05253, IPR022776) at positions 6-33 and 40-67, characteristic of U11-48K spliceosome components, plus UPF0224/FAM112 RNA processing family signatures (IPR051591, PTHR21402) spanning residues 2-92. The CHHC zinc finger motif (C-X2-C-X9-H-X2-C) coordinates zinc ions for RNA binding, suggesting direct nucleic acid interaction. Human Protein Atlas shows strong testis enrichment (210.9 nTPM) with nucleolar localization, consistent with gametocyte-related RNA processing during spermatogenesis. STRING interactions identify 10 partners including TMEM225 (0.649), TMEM270 (0.562), and C5orf58 (0.503) - notably many are uncharacterized FAM/TMEM proteins, indicating GTSF1L operates within a 'dark interactome' of poorly annotated factors. The combination of nucleolar localization, CHHC zinc fingers, testis specificity, and UPF0224 family membership strongly implicates GTSF1L in U11-type minor spliceosome assembly or rRNA/tRNA modification during germ cell development. No ClinVar variants suggest essential function with purifying selection or redundancy. Mechanistically, GTSF1L likely binds U11 snRNA or precursor tRNAs via its tandem zinc fingers, recruiting modification enzymes or stabilizing spliceosome intermediates in male germline cells.
