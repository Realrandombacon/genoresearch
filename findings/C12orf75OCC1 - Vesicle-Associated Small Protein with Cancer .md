## Description
C12orf75 (OCC1/Overexpressed in Colon Carcinoma 1) encodes a 63-amino acid intracellular protein localized to vesicles. Despite its small size, it contains a defined OCC1 protein family domain (IPR029133, PF15506) spanning residues 1-63, suggesting a structured functional unit. Expression profiling reveals tissue-enhanced RNA distribution with highest levels in blood vessels (129.2 nTPM) and seminal vesicle (102.9 nTPM), plus cancer-enhanced expression pattern and immune cell enrichment. Protein interaction network identifies 8 partners including BTRC (beta-transducin repeat-containing E3 ubiquitin protein ligase, score 0.557), IRS4 (insulin receptor substrate 4, score 0.555), and ELAVL1 (RNA-binding protein, score 0.468), suggesting roles in ubiquitination signaling, growth factor pathways, and post-transcriptional regulation. Clinical significance is established with 10 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants in ClinVar, predominantly copy number variations affecting 12q23-24 region. The combination of vesicular localization, cancer overexpression, vascular enrichment, and disease-associated variants suggests OCC1 functions as a small regulatory adaptor protein modulating vesicle trafficking or signal transduction in proliferating cells. Its interaction with BTRC (SCF ubiquitin ligase component) hints at potential role in targeted protein degradation pathways.
