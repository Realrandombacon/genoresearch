## Description
ABRACL (C6orf115) encodes an 81-amino acid Costars family protein (UniProt Q9P1F3) with a highly conserved Costars domain (IPR027817, PF14705) spanning residues 1-79. AlphaFold predicts a well-folded structure with exceptional confidence (pLDDT 94.8), indicating stable tertiary structure despite small size. The protein localizes to nucleoplasm and cytosol per HPA, with ubiquitous expression across all tissues (low tissue specificity). STRING analysis reveals 8 medium-confidence interaction partners, notably MEF2C (score 0.415) - a critical transcription factor for cardiac/neural development - plus SMIM18 (0.554), IRGQ (0.494), and TTC9B (0.468). ClinVar documents 13 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly copy number losses and gains on chromosome 6q23-24, suggesting dosage sensitivity. The Costars domain is known to regulate actin dynamics via ABRA-family proteins that bind G-actin and modulate SRF signaling. Given nuclear localization, MEF2C interaction, and disease-associated CNVs, ABRACL likely functions as a chromatin-associated cofactor that bridges actin cytoskeleton signaling to MEF2-dependent transcriptional programs. Dosage disruption may cause developmental phenotypes through impaired MEF2C target gene regulation.
