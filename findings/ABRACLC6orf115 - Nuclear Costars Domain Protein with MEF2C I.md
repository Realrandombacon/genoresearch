# ABRACL/C6orf115 - Nuclear Costars Domain Protein with MEF2C Interaction and Dosage-Sensitive CNVs

**Date:** 2026-03-16T18:35:00.151644

**Quality Score:** 9/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
ABRACL (ABRA C-terminal Like, C6orf115/HSPC280) encodes an 81aa highly structured nuclear protein (UniProt Q9P1F3, AlphaFold pLDDT 94.8) containing a conserved Costars domain (IPR027817, IPR038095, PF14705, aa 1-79) characteristic of the Costars family (IPR044302, PTHR46334). The Costars domain is known from ABRACL's paralog ABRACL/ABRA C-terminal regulator which binds actin and regulates SRF transcription. HPA reveals ubiquitous expression (detected in all tissues) with nucleoplasmic and cytosolic localization, classified as plasma protein. STRING identifies 8 interaction partners including MEF2C (myocyte enhancer factor 2C, score 0.415), a critical transcription factor for cardiac and neuronal development, plus SMIM18 (0.554), IRGQ (0.494), TTC9B (0.468), and LRFN3 (0.412). ClinVar documents 13 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly large copy number gains/losses spanning 6q23.2-24.2, indicating extreme dosage sensitivity. The Costars domain combined with MEF2C interaction and nuclear localization supports a hypothesis that ABRACL functions as a chromatin-associated transcriptional co-regulator that modulates MEF2C-dependent gene expression programs in cardiac and neuronal tissues. The structural homology to actin-binding Costars proteins suggests potential coupling between cytoskeletal dynamics and transcriptional regulation. Pathogenic CNVs likely disrupt dosage-dependent transcriptional control, contributing to neurodevelopmental and cardiac phenotypes. The ubiquitous expression pattern implies housekeeping regulatory function across multiple tissue types.
