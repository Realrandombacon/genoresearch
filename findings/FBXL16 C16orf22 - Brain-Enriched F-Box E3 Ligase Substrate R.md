## Description
FBXL16 encodes a 479aa F-box/LRR-repeat protein (UniProt Q8N461) that functions as a substrate receptor for SCF (SKP1-CUL1-F-box) E3 ubiquitin ligase complexes. The protein contains a canonical F-box domain (cd22127, residues 95-136) that mediates binding to SKP1, followed by multiple leucine-rich repeat (LRR) domains (IPR006553 LRR-cysteine subtype, residues 241-318; PF25372 LRR domain, residues 322-445) that typically recognize specific protein substrates through curved solenoid structures.\n\nAlphaFold predicts a high-confidence structure (pLDDT 83.2), consistent with well-folded LRR solenoids characteristic of substrate recognition modules. The LRR architecture (G3DSA:3.80.10.10 Ribonuclease Inhibitor superfamily) forms a horseshoe-shaped binding surface for peptide motifs.\n\nSTRING-DB reveals 10 interaction partners that define FBXL16's mechanistic role: CUL1 (0.801, high confidence; scaffold cullin), SKP1 (0.778, high confidence; adaptor linking F-box to CUL1), RBX1 (0.434; RING finger recruiting E2 ubiquitin-conjugating enzymes), NEDD8 (0.602; ubiquitin-like modifier activating cullin), CAND1 (0.595; cullin-associated deneddylated inhibitor), plus paralogs FBXO16 (0.598), FBXL18 (0.571), and FBXL6 (0.521). This interaction network confirms FBXL16 assembles into canonical SCF complexes.\n\nHPA shows striking brain-enriched expression (504.7 nTPM in brain, tissue enriched), with cancer-enhanced expression and cell type specificity. This neuronal enrichment suggests FBXL16 targets brain-specific substrates—potentially synaptic proteins, ion channels, or transcription factors regulating neurodevelopment.\n\nClinVar reports 48 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly copy number gains and losses at 16p13.3, indicating dosage sensitivity. While specific disease associations remain uncurated, the clinical significance suggests FBXL16 dysfunction contributes to neurodevelopmental or neuropsychiatric disorders.\n\nHYPOTHESIS: FBXL16 is a brain-specific SCF E3 ubiquitin ligase substrate receptor that uses its LRR domains to recognize neuronal proteins (synaptic scaffolds, ion channels, or transcription factors) for proteasomal degradation. Through CUL1-SKP1-RBX1 assembly, FBXL16 couples substrate recognition to ubiquitination, regulating protein turnover critical for neurodevelopment. The 48 ClinVar CNVs suggest FBXL16 dosage perturbation contributes to neurological disease phenotypes.
