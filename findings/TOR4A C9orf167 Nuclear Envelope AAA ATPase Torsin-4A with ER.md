## Description
TOR4A (alias C9orf167) encodes a 423 amino acid nuclear envelope and ER lumen-localized AAA+ ATPase (UniProt Q9NXH8) containing a canonical Torsin family domain (IPR010448, PF06309 residues 147-253, PTHR10760 residues 31-422) and AAA+ ATPase domain (IPR003593, SM00382 residues 186-319) characteristic of membrane-remodeling ATPases with ATP binding (GO:0005524) and hydrolysis activity (GO:0016887). AlphaFold predicts confident structure (pLDDT 79.2) indicating well-folded tertiary architecture with stable AAA+ core fold suitable for ATP-dependent conformational changes and substrate remodeling. HPA data reveals nucleoplasm localization with low tissue specificity (detected in many tissues), cell type enhanced single cell expression, and predicted intracellular protein classification, suggesting ubiquitous ER/nuclear envelope functions with cell-type-specific regulation. STRING interactions identify 10 partners including phosphatidylethanolamine cytidylyltransferase PCYT2 (0.538, medium confidence, phospholipid biosynthesis), uncharacterized protein C20orf27 (0.522), ER membrane protein TMEM59L (0.522, ER-Golgi trafficking), junctional cadherin-associated protein JCAD (0.490, endothelial junctions), endosomal sorting phosphatase PTPN23 (0.475, ESCRT machinery), sulfatase activating factor SUMF1 (0.451, ER quality control), torsin interacting protein TOR1AIP2 (0.444, nuclear envelope anchoring), calcium-dependent phospholipid binding protein CPNE1 (0.442, membrane trafficking), spatacsin SPART (0.436, ER membrane shaping, lysosome function), and PDZ-LIM domain protein PDLIM2 (0.415, cytoskeletal organization), implicating TOR4A in ER membrane dynamics, protein quality control, and nucleocytoplasmic transport. The AAA+ ATPase domain combined with nuclear envelope/ER lumen localization and interaction with TOR1AIP2 (torsin anchor), SPART (ER membrane shaping), and PTPN23 (endosomal sorting) positions TOR4A as an ER/nuclear envelope membrane remodeler that may coordinate protein quality control through ATP-dependent substrate extraction from membranes or dislocation of misfolded proteins for degradation. High-confidence interaction with PCYT2 (phospholipid synthesis enzyme) suggests functional coupling between membrane lipid composition and torsin ATPase activity, potentially regulating ER membrane curvature during vesicle budding or nuclear pore complex assembly. Interaction with SUMF1 (sulfatase activation factor) indicates roles in ER quality control and glycosylation enzyme maturation. ClinVar documents 99 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly chromosome 9q34.3 copy number gains and losses encompassing the TOR4A locus, indicating dosage sensitivity in disease etiology, potentially affecting nuclear envelope integrity and ER stress responses. Disease-associated CNVs suggest haploinsufficiency or triplosensitivity mechanisms disrupting nuclear envelope mechanics or ER-associated degradation (ERAD), potentially leading to neurodevelopmental defects through impaired nucleocytoplasmic transport or accumulation of misfolded proteins in the ER lumen. The torsin family includes TOR1A (DYT1 dystonia gene), suggesting TOR4A may contribute to movement disorder pathogenesis through shared mechanisms of nuclear envelope dysfunction in neurons.
