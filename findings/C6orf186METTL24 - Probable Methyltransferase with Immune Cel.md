## Description
C6orf186 encodes METTL24 (Probable methyltransferase-like protein 24, UniProt Q5JXM2), a 366 aa enzyme with predicted methyltransferase activity. InterPro identifies a methyltransferase domain (IPR025714, PF13383, positions 163-299) containing the catalytic core, and a METTL24 family signature (IPR026913, PTHR32026, positions 15-365) defining this protein subfamily. AlphaFold predicts a high-confidence structure (pLDDT 77.9), indicating a well-folded enzyme with stable tertiary architecture suitable for substrate binding and catalysis. HPA classifies METTL24 as a predicted secreted protein with low tissue specificity but immune cell enrichment in blood cells, detected across many tissues and enhanced in specific cell types. STRING interactions reveal associations with protein methyltransferases: EEF1AKNMT (0.649, elongation factor methyltransferase), EEF1AKMT2 (0.598), METTL9 (0.572, histone H4 methyltransferase), METTL25 (0.556), METTL2B (0.553), METTL16 (0.524, U6 snRNA methyltransferase), METTL21C (0.486), RNF151 (0.473), SHCBP1L (0.441), and DDO (0.433). This interaction network suggests METTL24 functions within the protein methylation machinery, potentially modifying histones, translation factors, or other nuclear/cytoplasmic substrates. ClinVar documents 18 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly large copy number losses and gains spanning 6q15-22 regions, though no specific disease phenotypes are established. Mechanistic hypothesis: METTL24 likely functions as a S-adenosylmethionine (SAM)-dependent methyltransferase targeting lysine or arginine residues on protein substrates. The immune cell enrichment suggests roles in epigenetic regulation of immune gene expression or post-translational modification of immune signaling proteins. The methyltransferase domain architecture and interaction with known methyltransferases (METTL9, METTL16, EEF1AKMT2) supports a catalytic role in chromatin modification or RNA processing. Despite structural characterization and network associations, the specific substrate(s), cellular localization, and biological function of METTL24 remain undefined - it represents a 'dark gene' with enzymatic potential awaiting experimental validation.
