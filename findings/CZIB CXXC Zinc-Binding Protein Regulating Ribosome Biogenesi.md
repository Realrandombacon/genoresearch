# CZIB: CXXC Zinc-Binding Protein Regulating Ribosome Biogenesis and Mitotic Spindle Assembly

**Date:** 2026-03-18T01:23:11.126850

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
CZIB encodes a 160 amino acid CXXC motif containing zinc binding protein (UniProt Q9NWV4) with a CXXC zinc-binding domain (IPR008584, PF05907, residues 5-157) belonging to the MAL13P1.257-like superfamily (SSF141678). AlphaFold predicts a well-folded structure with very high confidence (pLDDT 97.0). HPA data shows ubiquitous expression with vesicles, mitotic spindle, and cytosol localization, enhanced in specific cell types. STRING interactions reveal strong associations with GTPBP6 (0.728, mitochondrial ribosome assembly GTPase), CCS (0.667, copper chaperone for SOD1), and ribosomal proteins RPS13 (0.467), RPS15 (0.454), plus chaperone DNAJC17 (0.441) and kinase UBLCP1 (0.434). ClinVar reports 10 pathogenic variants including copy number gains/losses and inversions on chromosome 1p32.3. The gene is highly conserved across vertebrates: mouse (Q8BHG2, 160 aa, ~98% identity), rat (Q498R7, 160 aa, ~97% identity), cattle (Q32P66, 160 aa, ~95% identity), xenopus (Q3B8G0, 160 aa, ~85% identity).
