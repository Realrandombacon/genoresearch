## Description
TRMT9B (C8orf79) encodes a 454 amino acid probable tRNA methyltransferase 9B that modifies wobble uridines in specific arginine and glutamic acid tRNAs. InterPro analysis reveals multiple methyltransferase domain signatures: IPR013216 (Methyltransferase type 11, residues 50-139), IPR029063 (SAM-dependent methyltransferase superfamily, residues 7-191 and 357-453), IPR051422 (AlkB Family tRNA Methyltransferase/Dioxygenase, residues 5-449), PF08241 (Methyltransferase domain, residues 50-139), and PTHR13069 family classification. The catalytic domain adopts a Rossmann-like fold (G3DSA:3.40.50.150) characteristic of AdoMet_MTases (cd02440, residues 48-139). AlphaFold structure prediction (AF-Q9P272-F1) shows medium confidence (pLDDT 61.8) across the full 454 aa sequence, consistent with a partially structured methyltransferase containing both ordered catalytic domains and flexible regions. STRING-DB identifies 10 high-confidence protein interaction partners including TRMT112 (0.981 - obligate methyltransferase activator), CTU1 (0.650 - tRNA wobble modification), TRMT12 (0.581), ALKBH1 (0.543 - tRNA demethylase), TRMT13 (0.517), TRMT11 (0.516), NSUN2 (0.511 - tRNA methyltransferase), LIN9 (0.486 - tumor suppressor), and ELP3 (0.484 - elongator complex). ClinVar reports 92 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly chromosome 8p copy number losses and gains (8p23.3-21.2 regions), indicating strong disease association through genomic instability. The enzyme likely functions as a heterodimer with TRMT112, catalyzing S-adenosylmethionine-dependent methylation of tRNA wobble positions to ensure translational fidelity. Loss of TRMT9B function may cause tRNA hypomodification, ribosomal frameshifting, and proteotoxic stress contributing to disease phenotypes.
