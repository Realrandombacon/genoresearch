## Description
C1orf141 (Q5JVX7) is a 400 aa uncharacterized dark gene encoding a predicted intracellular protein localized to intermediate filaments. Contains a DUF4545 domain of unknown function (aa 114-395, IPR027847, PF15078) covering ~70% of the protein sequence, defining its core architecture. BLAST reveals vertebrate conservation: 50.8% bovine, 38.3% mouse, indicating purifying selection across mammals. STRING interactions reveal a mixed network including immune and metabolic partners: ITPRID1 (0.576, inositol trisphosphate receptor interacting), IL23R (0.505, interleukin-23 receptor, key Th17 pathway component), SLC35D1 (0.476, nucleotide sugar transporter), LAPTM4B (0.463, lysosomal protein linked to cancer), FIG4 (0.447, phosphoinositide phosphatase, Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease), plus dark gene partners SPATA45 and C8orf74. HPA confirms intermediate filament localization with testis-enhanced expression (14.3 nTPM) and fallopian tube expression (8.7 nTPM); not detected in immune cells despite IL23R interaction, suggesting potential regulatory role in non-immune contexts or developmental stages. ClinVar contains 25 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly large copy number losses/gains and deletions on 1p, confirming strong disease relevance although specific phenotypes are not yet delineated in the summary. True dark gene: conserved DUF4545 architecture, intermediate filament association (structural/cytoskeletal role), testis-specific enhancement, IL23R proximity (potential immune modulation), clinically implicated with massive CNV burden, but molecular mechanism completely unknown. Hypothesis: may function as a cytoskeletal adaptor or regulator of intermediate filament dynamics in germ cells (testis) and ciliated epithelia (fallopian tube), potentially modulating signal transduction via interaction with inositol phosphate pathways (ITPRID1) or cytokine receptors (IL23R), with loss contributing to genomic instability or developmental defects reflected in 1p CNV syndromes.
