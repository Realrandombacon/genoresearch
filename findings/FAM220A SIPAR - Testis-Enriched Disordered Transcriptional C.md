# FAM220A SIPAR - Testis-Enriched Disordered Transcriptional Corepressor with SIPAR Domain and STAT Binding

**Date:** 2026-03-17T11:15:57.773682

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
FAM220A (alias SIPAR, C7orf70) encodes a 259 amino acid intrinsically disordered protein (UniProt Q7Z4H9, AlphaFold pLDDT 47.5) functioning as a STAT family transcriptional corepressor. InterPro reveals a conserved SIPAR domain (IPR029155, residues 2-253) and FAM220 family signature (IPR040355/PF15487), suggesting this domain mediates protein-protein interactions rather than catalytic activity. HPA shows testis-enriched expression at 72.9 nTPM with skeletal muscle expression at 65.9 nTPM, and vesicle/nuclear subcellular localization consistent with its predicted role in intracellular signal transduction and negative regulation of RNA polymerase II transcription. STRING interactions identify 10 medium-confidence partners (scores 0.419-0.495) including SPAG7 (sperm antigen), CD34 (hematopoietic stem cell marker), PAWR (prostate apoptosis response), ZNF142 (zinc finger transcription factor), and several dark genes (C7orf26, FAM222A, ANKRD13D), suggesting coordinated regulatory networks in germ cells and hematopoietic lineages. ClinVar contains 36 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly chromosome 7p22 copy number gains, indicating haploinsufficiency or dosage sensitivity. The combination of disordered structure, SIPAR domain, STAT binding activity, testis expression, and disease-associated CNVs suggests FAM220A functions as a scaffold protein recruiting transcriptional corepressors to STAT-bound enhancers, potentially through phase separation mechanisms. The SIPAR domain may mediate interaction with STAT proteins while disordered regions facilitate multivalent binding to chromatin modifiers.
