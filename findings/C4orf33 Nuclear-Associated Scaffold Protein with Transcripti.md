# C4orf33: Nuclear-Associated Scaffold Protein with Transcriptional Regulatory Function

**Date:** 2026-03-17T23:50:28.129284

## Description
C4orf33 encodes a 199 amino acid UPF0462 family protein (UniProt Q8N1A6) with a well-defined 3D structure (AlphaFold pLDDT: 95.4). InterPro analysis reveals membership in the UPF0462 protein family (PTHR31475, residues 1-196) and a CATH-Gene3D immunoglobulin-like fold superfamily (G3DSA:2.60.40.1190, residues 40-177), suggesting a stable β-sandwich scaffold architecture.\n\nProtein interaction data (STRING-DB) identifies 9 partners with medium confidence scores (0.40-0.50), including TAF11 (0.496, TATA-box binding protein associated factor), SYNRG (0.495, synergin gamma), VAC14 (0.477, PI3K complex regulator), and KHDRBS2 (0.459, RNA-binding protein). This interaction profile strongly suggests C4orf33 functions as a nuclear scaffold protein that bridges transcriptional machinery (TAF11, BTAF1) with RNA processing factors (KHDRBS2, GPATCH11).\n\nHPA data shows ubiquitous expression across all tissues with nuclear bodies and Golgi apparatus localization, consistent with a constitutive regulatory role. The low tissue specificity indicates housekeeping function rather than specialized tissue roles.\n\nClinVar contains 26 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly copy number gains and losses spanning 4q24-q35 regions. While no single-gene disease is definitively established, the variant burden suggests haploinsufficiency sensitivity and potential contribution to chromosomal instability syndromes.\n\nMechanistic hypothesis: C4orf33 serves as a molecular scaffold that stabilizes pre-initiation complexes at gene promoters via TAF11 interaction, while simultaneously recruiting RNA processing factors through KHDRBS2/GPATCH11 interfaces. The immunoglobulin-like fold provides a rigid platform for multi-protein assembly, positioning C4orf33 as a coordinator of transcription-to-mRNA processing coupling.
