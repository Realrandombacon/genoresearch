## Description
C11orf96 (Q7Z7L8) is a 122 aa uncharacterized dark gene encoding a predicted intracellular protein localized to nucleoplasm, focal adhesion sites, and cytosol. Contains defining DUF4695 domain of unknown function (aa 1-122, IPR031521, PF15766) covering the entire protein sequence, establishing its core architecture. BLAST reveals strong mammalian conservation: 98.3% cat, 96.7% mouse, 96.7% rat, indicating purifying selection across mammals. STRING interactions reveal a lipid-binding/cell signaling network: SEC14L6 (0.512, SEC14-like lipid-binding protein, phosphatidylinositol transfer), L1TD1 (0.471, LINE-1 type transposase domain 1, stem cell pluripotency factor), PTP4A3 (0.435, protein tyrosine phosphatase type IVA 3, metastasis-associated phosphatase, cell migration signaling). HPA confirms striking blood vessel enrichment (1697.4 nTPM top tissue — exceptionally high), detected in all tissues with tissue-enhanced specificity, nucleoplasm/focal adhesion/cytosol localization, group-enriched single cell specificity — the extreme vascular enrichment combined with focal adhesion localization strongly suggests endothelial cell function and vascular integrity role. ClinVar contains 15 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants dominated by large 11p copy number losses and gains (11p13-11.2, 11p11.2-q12.2, 11p13-q25, 11p15.5-q25), confirming strong disease relevance in 11p deletion/duplication syndromes. True dark gene: conserved DUF4695 architecture spanning mammals, extreme blood vessel expression (1697.4 nTPM — vascular signature), focal adhesion localization (cell-matrix adhesion), lipid-binding/phosphatase interaction network (SEC14L6/PTP4A3), stem cell factor partnership (L1TD1), clinically implicated with 11p CNV burden, but molecular mechanism completely unknown. Hypothesis: may function as a vascular endothelial scaffolding protein that coordinates focal adhesion assembly (focal adhesion localization), phosphoinositide lipid metabolism (via SEC14L6 interaction), and tyrosine phosphorylation signaling (via PTP4A3), using DUF4695 domain for lipid binding or protein scaffolding, potentially serving as endothelial barrier integrity factor with L1TD1 interaction suggesting stem/progenitor cell role in vascular maintenance, with dysregulation contributing to 11p deletion syndromes with vascular/renal phenotypes.
