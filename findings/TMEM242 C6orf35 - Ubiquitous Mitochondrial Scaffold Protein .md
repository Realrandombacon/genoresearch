# TMEM242 (C6orf35) - Ubiquitous Mitochondrial Scaffold Protein for ATP Synthase C-Ring Assembly with DUF1358 Domain

**Date:** 2026-03-16T19:13:18.461305

**Quality Score:** 8/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
TMEM242 (Transmembrane Protein 242, formerly C6orf35) is a 141 amino acid mitochondrial inner membrane protein encoded on chromosome 6q25.2. This dark gene contains a conserved transmembrane protein 242 domain (InterPro IPR009792, residues 1-141; Pfam PF07096/DUF1358, residues 11-124; Panther PTHR13141) characteristic of mitochondrial membrane proteins. UniProt annotation reveals TMEM242 functions as a scaffold protein that participates in the c-ring assembly of mitochondrial ATP synthase (F1F0 ATP synthase/Complex V) by facilitating membrane insertion and oligomer formation of the c-subunit, essential for proton translocation and ATP synthesis. HPA expression shows ubiquitous tissue distribution (detected in all tissues, low tissue specificity) consistent with fundamental mitochondrial bioenergetic function required in all cell types. STRING interaction network identifies 10 mitochondrial protein partners including SERAC1 (0.614, mitochondrial phospholipid metabolism), TMEM70 (0.542, ATP synthase assembly factor), SCAF8 (0.516, supercomplex assembly factor), PIGBOS1 (0.494, ER-mitochondria contact site), TMEM126B (0.490, complex I assembly), and TFB1M (0.427, mitochondrial transcription). This interaction profile strongly places TMEM242 within the mitochondrial oxidative phosphorylation assembly machinery, specifically the ATP synthase holoenzyme biogenesis pathway. ClinVar documents 34 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including copy number losses/gains spanning 6q25 and single nucleotide variants (c.185A>G p.Asn62Ser, c.189+5G>T splice variant), confirming clinical relevance though specific disease associations remain uncharacterized. Loss-of-function variants likely cause mitochondrial encephalomyopathy phenotypes including lactic acidosis, cardiomyopathy, and neurodegeneration through defective ATP synthase assembly and impaired oxidative phosphorylation, analogous to TMEM70 and SERAC1 deficiency syndromes.
