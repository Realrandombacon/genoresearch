## Description
ZC3H15 (zinc finger CCCH-type containing 15, aliases DFRP1/HT010/LEREPO4/MSTP012) encodes a 426 amino acid well-folded cytosolic RNA-binding protein (UniProt Q8WU90, AlphaFold pLDDT 71.1) with high structural confidence indicating stable tertiary structure suitable for molecular recognition and protein-protein interactions. InterPro analysis reveals dual CCCH-type zinc finger domains (IPR000571, PF00642, residues 99-126 and 174-212) conferring RNA binding and metal ion coordination (GO:0046872), plus a ZC3H15/TMA46 family C-terminal domain (IPR032378, PF16543, residues 229-313) homologous to DRG (Developmentally-Regulated GTP-binding) family regulatory proteins, suggesting GTPase regulatory or ribosome-associated functions. HPA confirms ubiquitous expression across all tissues with low specificity but cell type-enhanced patterns at single-cell resolution, consistent with housekeeping roles in fundamental cellular processes like translation. STRING interactions identify 10 high-confidence partners (scores 0.418-0.917) dominated by ribosomal proteins (RPS15 0.876, RPL23A 0.875, RPS2 0.820, RPS12 0.728), translation factors (RACK1 0.829, DNAJC2 0.844), ubiquitin-related proteins (RWDD1 0.917-highest, FAU 0.884), and DRG family GTPases (DRG1 0.674, DRG2 0.418)—this exclusive enrichment of ribosomal subunits, translation machinery, and GTP-binding regulatory proteins strongly suggests ZC3H15 functions as a ribosome-associated adaptor linking RNA recognition via CCCH zinc fingers to GTPase-dependent translational control or ribosome quality control pathways. The DRG/TMA46 domain homology indicates potential regulation of developmentally-programmed translation during differentiation or stress responses. ClinVar contains 27 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, exclusively chromosome 2q copy number alterations (losses and gains) spanning 169kb-243Mb regions including the ZC3H15 locus at 2q32.1, indicating dosage sensitivity where gene copy number disruption causes disease—2q32 deletions cause well-characterized 2q32.1 microdeletion syndrome with developmental delay, intellectual disability, and growth abnormalities. Conservation spans vertebrates (mouse Q3TIV5 426aa, bovine Q1RMM1 426aa) confirming evolutionary constraint on ribosome-associated RNA-binding functions. The combination of dual CCCH RNA-binding zinc fingers, DRG/TMA46 GTPase regulatory domain, ribosomal protein interactions, ubiquitous expression, 2q CNV pathogenicity, and high structural confidence suggests ZC3H15 functions as a ribosome-associated RNA-binding adaptor that coordinates GTPase-dependent translational regulation, potentially monitoring mRNA quality, regulating translation elongation during development, or coupling ribosome stalling to quality control pathways. Chromosome 2q32.1 alterations may cause ZC3H15 haploinsufficiency or overexpression leading to disrupted translational fidelity, impaired developmental protein synthesis, or ribosome quality control defects underlying 2q32.1 microdeletion syndrome phenotypes.
