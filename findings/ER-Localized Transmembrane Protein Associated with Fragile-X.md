# ER-Localized Transmembrane Protein Associated with Fragile-X Syndrome Pathway

**Date:** 2026-03-18T07:00:14.928797

## Description
CXorf13 (TMEM185A) encodes a 350 amino acid type I transmembrane protein localized to the endoplasmic reticulum. The protein belongs to the Fragile-X-F transmembrane protein family (IPR019396, PF10269: aa30-253) with strong homology to FAM11A/B proteins (PTHR13568). AlphaFold predicts a well-folded structure (pLDDT 78.0) consistent with stable transmembrane topology. STRING interaction network reveals striking enrichment for Fragile-X syndrome pathway components: FMR1 (0.897, high confidence; Fragile-X mental retardation protein), FRA10AC1 (0.658; Fragile-X-related locus), AFF1/2/3 (0.456-0.528; AF4/FMR2 family transcriptional activators), and CSNK1G1 (0.612; casein kinase 1 gamma). Additional interactions include HSFX4 (0.543; heat shock transcription factor), TMEM185B (0.540; paralog), BGN (0.463; biglycan ECM protein), and CBL (0.431; E3 ubiquitin ligase). HPA confirms ER localization with ubiquitous expression across all tissues (low tissue specificity) and cell type-enhanced expression pattern. No ClinVar variants documented (0 pathogenic entries), suggesting either functional redundancy with TMEM185B paralog or absence from clinical screening. The interaction profile strongly suggests TMEM185A functions as an ER membrane scaffold protein modulating FMR1-dependent translational regulation or FMR1 protein stability. Given FMR1 role in synaptic plasticity and mRNA transport, TMEM185A may regulate ER-to-Golgi trafficking of FMR1 target mRNAs or serve as co-factor in FMR1-mediated translational repression complexes.
