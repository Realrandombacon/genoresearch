# EVA1C (C21orf64) - Dual SUEL Lectin Domain Protein with Clinical Significance

**Date:** 2026-03-16T17:44:03.339452

**Quality Score:** 9/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
EVA1C (Eva-1 homolog C), formerly C21orf64, encodes a 441 amino acid protein (UniProt P58658) containing two tandem SUEL-type lectin domains (IPR000922, PF02140) at positions 67-159 and 168-260 that bind D-galactoside/L-rhamnose carbohydrates (GO:0030246), plus a C-terminal EVA1 domain (IPR039500, PF14851, aa 301-441). AlphaFold predicts a well-folded structure (pLDDT 70.2) consistent with stable lectin function. HPA shows ubiquitous expression with cytosolic localization. STRING interactions reveal connections to URB1 (0.587), POFUT2 (0.587), DONSON (0.581), and N6AMT1 (0.575) - proteins involved in glycosylation, DNA replication, and tRNA modification. The dual lectin architecture suggests EVA1C functions as a carbohydrate-binding adaptor in glycoprotein quality control or cell surface receptor modulation. ClinVar reports 56 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants (primarily chromosome 21 CNVs), indicating clinical relevance though specific disease associations remain undefined. The SUEL lectin fold is evolutionarily conserved from C. elegans (EVA-1) to humans, suggesting fundamental biological role in glycan recognition. Medium-confidence interactions with glycosyltransferases (POFUT2) and replication factors (DONSON) suggest potential roles in coordinating glycosylation status with cell cycle progression.
