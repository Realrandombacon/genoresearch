## Description
C20orf193 (now designated MRPS26, Mitochondrial Ribosomal Protein S26, also known as mS26) is a 205-amino acid mitochondrial matrix protein functioning as an integral component of the small subunit (28S) of the mitochondrial ribosome, essential for mitochondrial protein synthesis and oxidative phosphorylation complex assembly. Structural analysis reveals a compact ribosomal protein fold (IPR026140, PTHR21035, residues 1-203) with a mitochondrial ribosome S26 family signature (PF14943, residues 28-196) encompassing the structured core. The AlphaFold predicted structure (Q9BYN8) shows high confidence (pLDDT 89.1), confirming a well-folded globular ribosomal protein with stable RNA-binding surfaces and protein-protein interaction interfaces characteristic of ribosomal components. Expression profiling from the Human Protein Atlas demonstrates ubiquitous expression across all tissues with low tissue specificity, consistent with essential housekeeping roles in mitochondrial translation, with exclusive subcellular localization to mitochondria—supporting its function within the mitochondrial translational machinery. Protein interaction networks (STRING) reveal striking enrichment for mitochondrial ribosomal small subunit partners with ultra-high confidence scores: MRPS22 (0.998), MRPS16 (0.997), MRPS15 (0.997), MRPS5 (0.995), MRPS18B (0.994), MRPS31 (0.993), MRPS25 (0.992), MRPS34 (0.990), MRPS9 (0.986), and MRPS17 (0.983). These interactions place MRPS26 within the 28S small subunit body, likely contacting mitochondrial 12S rRNA and neighboring ribosomal proteins during translation initiation and elongation. ClinVar documents 24 pathogenic or likely pathogenic variants, predominantly large copy number gains (20p13-11.21, 20p13-12.1, 20p13-12.2) and losses (20p13-12.3), indicating strong dosage sensitivity and genomic instability in this chromosomal region, though specific monogenic disease phenotypes await full characterization. The mechanistic hypothesis proposes that MRPS26 functions as a structural scaffold protein stabilizing the platform region of the mitochondrial small subunit, facilitating mRNA decoding, tRNA accommodation, and recruitment of mitochondrial translation initiation factors (mtIF2, mtIF3). As a mitochondrially-encoded translation component, MRPS26 is essential for synthesis of 13 OXPHOS subunits encoded by mitochondrial DNA, linking cytoplasmic-nuclear coordination of mitochondrial biogenesis. Loss-of-function may cause mitochondrial translation defects, respiratory chain deficiency, lactic acidosis, and tissue-specific phenotypes (cardiomyopathy, encephalopathy, hepatopathy) typical of mitochondrial ribosomopathies. This gene exemplifies successful resolution from "C20orf" dark gene annotation to a characterized mitochondrial ribosomal component.
