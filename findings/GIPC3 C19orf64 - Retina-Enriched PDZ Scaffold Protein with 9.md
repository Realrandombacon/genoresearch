# GIPC3 (C19orf64) - Retina-Enriched PDZ Scaffold Protein with 96 Deafness Variants

## Description
GIPC3 is a 312 amino acid PDZ domain-containing scaffold protein (UniProt Q8TF64) encoded at 19p13.3. The protein contains three structured domains: an N-terminal GH1 domain (aa 38-104, PF25083), a central PDZ domain (aa 107-195, IPR001478/PF00595), and a C-terminal GH2 domain (aa 218-307, PF25082). AlphaFold predicts a well-folded structure with high confidence (pLDDT 84.9). HPA data shows retina-enriched expression (15.5 nTPM) with subcellular localization to nuclear bodies and Golgi apparatus. STRING interactions reveal 10 high-confidence partners including ZNRF4 (0.772), MYO1F (0.685), MYO3A (0.592), MYO6 (0.451), and USH1C (0.422) - notably multiple myosin motors and Usher syndrome proteins linked to auditory function. ClinVar documents 96 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants causing non-syndromic deafness, including nonsense mutations (p.Tyr171Ter), frameshifts (p.Arg7fs, p.Lys51fs), and missense variants (p.Cys152Phe, p.Thr269Met). The GIPC3 PDZ domain mediates protein-protein interactions typical of scaffold proteins, while the GH2 domain may regulate membrane trafficking. The interaction network suggests GIPC3 functions as a molecular adaptor linking myosin motors to cargo in photoreceptor and hair cells.
