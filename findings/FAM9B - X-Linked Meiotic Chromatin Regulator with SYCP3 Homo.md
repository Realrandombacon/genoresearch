## Description
FAM9B (UniProt Q8IZU0) encodes a 186aa X-linked nuclear protein (Xp22.31) with strong testis-enriched expression (5.9 nTPM testis, 3.6 nTPM liver per HPA) and nucleoplasmic localization. InterPro analysis reveals membership in the XLR/SYCP3/FAM9 family (IPR006888, IPR051443, PTHR19368) with a conserved Cor1/Xlr/Xmr coiled-coil domain (PF04803, positions 62-186) showing homology to synaptonemal complex protein SYCP3, critical for meiotic chromosome pairing. AlphaFold structure prediction shows high confidence (pLDDT 79.1), indicating a well-folded stable structure suitable for protein-protein interactions. STRING interaction network identifies 10 medium-confidence partners including OGFOD1 (0.641, tRNA modification), PHYH (0.594, peroxisomal), ZNF41 (0.554, X-linked zinc finger), SPHAR (0.518, spermatogenesis), NUTM2F (0.456, chromatin regulator), and IZUMO3 (0.415, sperm-egg recognition), suggesting roles in meiosis and chromatin regulation. ClinVar contains 182 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly X chromosome copy-number variations (deletions, duplications, CNV gains/losses) spanning Xp22.33-q28, indicating strong disease relevance despite no specific Mendelian disease annotation. The combination of testis enrichment, SYCP3 homology, nuclear localization, meiosis-associated interactions, and X-linked position strongly supports a mechanistic role as a meiotic chromatin adaptor protein, potentially facilitating homologous chromosome synapsis or recombination complex assembly during spermatogenesis. X-linked location explains male-specific phenotypic consequences of CNVs.
