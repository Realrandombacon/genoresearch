# X-Linked Spermatid Nuclear Transition Protein Paralog with Higher Testis Expression than CXorf51A

**Date:** 2026-03-18T07:08:31.501854

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
CXorf51B encodes a 108 amino acid X-linked spermatid nuclear transition protein (IPR040433: aa1-106, PF27987: aa1-104, PTHR37876) critical for chromatin remodeling during spermiogenesis. AlphaFold predicts medium confidence structure (pLDDT 68.1) indicating partial folding consistent with DNA-binding chromatin factor. HPA reveals extreme testis-specific expression (78.7 nTPM) with tissue-enriched RNA pattern, detection in single tissue only, representing the highest testis-specific expression level among CXorf51 paralogs (CXorf51A: 67.3 nTPM). STRING interactions connect CXorf51B to armadillo repeat protein ARMCX1 (0.517), suggesting potential scaffold interactions in spermatogenic cells. ClinVar documents 189 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly Xq26.3-28 and Xq23-28 copy number gains/losses affecting the locus, identical to CXorf51A variant profile, demonstrating clear clinical significance for male reproductive health. Spermatid nuclear transition proteins replace somatic histones with protamines during late spermatogenesis, enabling extreme chromatin compaction in mature sperm. CXorf51B likely functions as DNA-binding chromatin factor recruited to haploid spermatid nuclei, facilitating histone-to-protamine transition. The 189 pathogenic CNVs disrupt dosage-sensitive expression required for normal chromatin remodeling, potentially causing male infertility through defective sperm chromatin compaction. Higher testis expression (78.7 vs 67.3 nTPM) suggests CXorf51B may be the dominant paralog during spermiogenesis. Functional redundancy between CXorf51A and CXorf51B paralogs may explain why single gene mutations are not documented while CNVs affecting both loci cause disease.
