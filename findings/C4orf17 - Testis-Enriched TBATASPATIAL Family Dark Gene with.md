## Description
C4orf17 (Q53FE4) is a 359 aa uncharacterized dark gene encoding a testis-enriched protein localized to the sperm midpiece (flagellar structure critical for motility). Contains conserved TBATA-like/SPATIAL domain (aa 2-330, IPR037394, PF15256, PTHR33772) — the TBATA family name literally derives from "Thymus-Brain-And-Testes-Associated", matching the observed testis-specific expression pattern (18.7 nTPM, tissue-enriched). BLAST shows 92.5% identity to macaque homolog, confirming strong primate conservation, with distant homology to characterized TBATA/SPATIAL stromal protein. STRING interactions reveal zinc finger/chromatin network: ZNF512 (0.578, C2H2 zinc finger transcription factor), ARRDC5 (0.507, arrestin domain-containing protein), LKAAEAR1 (0.506, uncharacterized long non-coding RNA-associated), DNAJC17 (0.418, DnaJ chaperone), NT5C1B-RDH14 readthrough fusion. HPA shows testis-enriched expression with midpiece subcellular localization — the midpiece contains mitochondria arranged helically around the axoneme, providing ATP for sperm motility. ClinVar contains 15 pathogenic/likely pathogenic copy number variants on 4q22-25, confirming strong disease relevance. True dark gene: conserved TBATA/SPATIAL architecture, testis-enriched expression, sperm midpiece localization (critical fertility structure), zinc finger transcription factor interactions, clinically implicated with extensive CNV burden, but molecular function completely unknown. Hypothesis: may function as testis-specific chromatin regulator or mitochondrial-axoneme linker in sperm midpiece, using TBATA domain for protein oligomerization and ZNF512 interaction for transcriptional regulation of spermatogenesis genes, potentially coordinating mitochondrial energy production with flagellar assembly.
