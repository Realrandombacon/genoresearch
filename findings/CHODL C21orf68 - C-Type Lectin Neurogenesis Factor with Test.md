# CHODL (C21orf68) - C-Type Lectin Neurogenesis Factor with Testis-Immune Expression

**Date:** 2026-03-16T17:45:45.176787

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
CHODL (Chondrolectin), formerly C21orf68, encodes a 273 amino acid type II transmembrane C-type lectin receptor (UniProt Q9H9P2) with a canonical carbohydrate-recognition domain (IPR001304, PF00059, aa 51-179) belonging to the C-type lectin-like superfamily (G3DSA:3.10.100.10). AlphaFold predicts a well-folded structure (pLDDT 75.9) consistent with stable lectin function. HPA reveals tissue-enhanced expression with testis enrichment (29.0 nTPM) and lymphoid tissue expression (13.3 nTPM), plus cancer-enhanced and immune cell-enriched patterns. Subcellular localization includes centrosome and cytosol. STRING interactions identify 10 partners including LAMP5 (0.558) - a lysosomal membrane protein in dendritic cells, TMEM41B (0.523) - an ER membrane scramblase, PCDH20 (0.472) - a protocadherin, and VIPR2 (0.471) - a vasoactive intestinal peptide receptor. This interaction network suggests CHODL functions at the intersection of immune surveillance and neurogenesis. The C-type lectin domain mediates calcium-dependent carbohydrate binding, potentially recognizing glycan patterns on pathogens or developing neurons. HPA annotates neurogenesis as the biological process, consistent with PCDH20/NCAM2 interactions (cell adhesion molecules). ClinVar reports 72 pathogenic variants, predominantly chromosome 21 CNVs, implicating CHODL dosage sensitivity in disease. As a centrosome-localized lectin, CHODL may coordinate glycan-dependent cell-cell recognition during neural progenitor division or immune synapse formation. The testis-lymphoid expression pattern mirrors SPATC1L, suggesting convergent roles in specialized differentiation programs.
