# C6orf120 - UPF0669 ER Membrane Protein with ERLIN1 Interaction and Apoptosis Role

**Date:** 2026-03-16T23:05:20.348465

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
C6orf120 encodes a 191-amino acid UPF0669 family protein (UniProt Q7Z4R8) containing a conserved UPF0669 domain (IPR031420/PF17065, residues 8-191) annotated as a putative cytokine domain. AlphaFold prediction shows high confidence folding (pLDDT 79.8), indicating a stable globular structure rather than intrinsic disorder. The protein interacts strongly with ERLIN1 (STRING score 0.757, high confidence), an ER lipid raft protein that mediates ER-associated degradation (ERAD) of IP3 receptors. Additional interaction partners include ER membrane proteins RNF170 (0.556), PGAP3 (0.519), TMCO1 (0.455), and ubiquitin ligases RNF185 (0.454), suggesting localization to ER membrane microdomains. HPA classifies C6orf120 as a predicted secreted protein detected in all tissues with low tissue specificity, and associates it with apoptosis biological processes - consistent with RefSeq annotation describing apoptosis induction in CD4+ T-cells and response to ER stress. ClinVar documents 75 pathogenic/likely pathogenic copy number variants (gains and losses) spanning 6q25-27, indicating strong dosage sensitivity and disease relevance. Orthologs exist in Mus musculus (Q9DAY5), Rattus norvegicus (Q6AY64), and Bos taurus (A2VDZ5), demonstrating evolutionary conservation across mammals. We hypothesize C6orf120 functions as an ER membrane scaffold protein that modulates ERAD complex assembly through ERLIN1 interaction, regulating unfolded protein response and apoptosis threshold in lymphocytes. The UPF0669 domain may mediate protein-protein interactions within ER lipid rafts, potentially competing with or stabilizing ERLIN1-ERLIN2 hetero complexes.
