## Description
RUSF1 (Gene ID: 64755, alias C16orf58, RUS) encodes a 468 amino acid RUS family protein localized to chromosome 16p11.2 with multiple subcellular localizations including nucleoplasm, nucleoli, Golgi apparatus, vesicles, and cytosol. InterPro analysis reveals extensive domain architecture: Root UVB sensitive family (IPR006968, residues 9-454), RUS domain (IPR054549/PF04884, residues 70-302) annotated as Vitamin B6 photo-protection and homeostasis domain, and C-terminal RUS domain (IPR055412/PF24160, residues 304-459), with family classification PTHR12770. The PF04884 domain links this protein to vitamin B6 metabolism and UVB stress response pathways, homologous to plant proteins involved in root UVB sensing during morphogenesis. AlphaFold structure prediction shows high confidence (pLDDT 85.5), indicating a well-folded stable tertiary structure suitable for enzymatic or scaffolding functions. STRING interaction analysis identifies 6 partners with one high-confidence interaction: MVD (mevalonate diphosphate decarboxylase, 0.837), a key enzyme in cholesterol/sterol biosynthesis pathway, plus CEBPZOS (0.608), MTRES1 (0.461), SLC5A2 (sodium/glucose cotransporter 2, 0.449), IFNGR1 (interferon gamma receptor 1, 0.446), and C6orf62 (0.418). The strong MVD interaction suggests RUSF1 functions in sterol metabolism or membrane biogenesis pathways. HPA expression data shows low tissue specificity with detection in all tissues and low cell type specificity, consistent with housekeeping metabolic functions. ClinVar contains 15 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including 16p copy number gains (16p13.3-q24.3, 16p13.3-11.2 regions) and intronic splice variants, demonstrating clinical significance in chromosomal rearrangement syndromes. Conservation extends across vertebrates (Mus musculus 466 aa, Rattus norvegicus 466 aa, Pongo abelii 468 aa) and even Dictyostelium discoideum (527 aa), demonstrating deep evolutionary constraint. Mechanistic hypothesis: RUSF1 functions as a metabolic scaffold protein that regulates vitamin B6 homeostasis and sterol biosynthesis through direct interaction with MVD in the mevalonate pathway. The Vitamin B6 domain (PF04884) may bind pyridoxal phosphate cofactors or mediate redox sensing, while the RUS domains facilitate protein-protein interactions within metabolic enzyme complexes. Golgi/vesicle localization suggests roles in membrane trafficking of sterol synthesis enzymes or vitamin B6 transporters. Pathogenic 16p CNVs likely disrupt dosage-sensitive metabolic regulation, contributing to sterol metabolism disorders and developmental phenotypes.
