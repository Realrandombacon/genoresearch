## Description
C1orf198 encodes a 327 aa uncharacterized protein (UniProt Q9H425) containing a DUF4706 domain (IPR031600/PF15797, residues 31-134) of unknown function. Despite its 'uncharacterized' annotation, comprehensive multi-omics data reveals strong mitochondrial localization signals through protein-protein interactions. STRING-DB analysis identifies 10 high-confidence interaction partners (all scores >0.96), exclusively composed of mitochondrial electron transport chain subunits: COX5A (0.995), COX7C (0.994), UQCRFS1 (0.987), UQCRQ (0.984), UQCRB (0.981), CYC1 (0.980), UQCR10 (0.974), MT-CO3 (0.973), MT-CO2 (0.972), and UQCRC2 (0.966). These partners represent Complex III (ubiquinol-cytochrome c reductase) and Complex IV (cytochrome c oxidase) components, strongly suggesting C1orf198 functions as an assembly factor or regulatory chaperone for oxidative phosphorylation complexes. HPA expression profiling shows brain-enriched transcript abundance (144.0 nTPM) with cytosolic protein localization, consistent with a role in neuronal mitochondrial metabolism. AlphaFold structural prediction yields medium confidence (pLDDT 60.1), indicating partial disorder that may facilitate dynamic protein-protein interactions typical of assembly chaperones. ClinVar documents 43 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly copy number variations (gains/losses at 1q42), implicating dosage sensitivity and potential neurological disease etiology. Conservation across vertebrates (mouse Q8C3W1, zebrafish Q4KMC9) confirms evolutionary constraint. Mechanistic hypothesis: C1orf198 acts as a DUF4706-domain scaffold protein that transiently associates with nascent Complex III/IV assemblies in the mitochondrial intermembrane space, facilitating proper subunit incorporation and preventing aggregation. Brain-enriched expression explains neurological phenotypes in CNV carriers. This gene should be reclassified from 'dark' to 'mitochondrial assembly factor' pending experimental validation.
