## Description
DSCR8 (Down syndrome critical region protein 8), formerly C21orf65, encodes a small 97 amino acid protein (UniProt Q96T75) located within the Down syndrome critical region on chromosome 21q22. InterPro analysis returned no detectable domains, classifying this as a true 'dark' protein without characterized functional motifs. AlphaFold prediction shows medium-low confidence (pLDDT 64.7), suggesting intrinsically disordered characteristics typical of regulatory peptides or linear motif-containing proteins. STRING database found no protein interaction partners, indicating either transient interactions below detection threshold or functional independence. HPA expression data is unavailable, suggesting low/ubiquitous expression or technical detection limitations. Critically, ClinVar reports 70 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants - all chromosome 21 copy number variations (CNVs) spanning the Down syndrome critical region. These CNVs (duplications and deletions) implicate DSCR8 dosage sensitivity in Down syndrome pathophysiology. The gene's location within the DSCR (chr21:34-35 Mb) alongside DSCR1/RCAN1 and DSCR2 suggests coordinated dysregulation in trisomy 21. As a small disordered peptide, DSCR8 may function as a regulatory module - potentially a linear motif mediator, post-translational modification substrate, or non-coding RNA host. The absence of structured domains combined with clinical CNV burden suggests DSCR8 contributes to Down syndrome phenotypes through dosage-sensitive regulatory mechanisms rather than enzymatic activity. Conservation and interaction data would clarify evolutionary constraints.
