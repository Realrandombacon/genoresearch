# MORN5 (C9orf18) - Membrane-Associated MORN Repeat Protein with Testis-Fallopian Tube Expression and Cilia-Linked Interactions

**Date:** 2026-03-16T20:10:23.026891

**Quality Score:** 9/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
MORN5 encodes a 161 aa uncharacterized protein containing three tandem MORN (Membrane Occupation and Recognition Nexus) repeats (IPR003409, PF02493; positions 6-73) that mediate plasma membrane binding, structurally homologous to junctophilins and PIP-5-kinases (SM00698). The N-terminal region (aa 5-112) shows structural homology to the SET7/9 histone H3 K4 methyltransferase domain (G3DSA:2.20.110.10), suggesting potential chromatin or protein interaction capability. HPA RNA-seq reveals tissue-enhanced expression with highest levels in fallopian tube (104.3 nTPM), testis (78.2 nTPM), and choroid plexus (76.5 nTPM), indicating roles in reproductive tissues and CNS barrier function. STRING interactome (10 partners, scores 0.42-0.70) connects MORN5 to MORN3/MORN4 paralogs, cilia/flagella proteins (TEKT1, CFAP161), and the dark gene C9orf135, suggesting involvement in cytoskeletal-membrane anchoring complexes. Cancer-enhanced expression pattern implies potential dysregulation in tumors. The combination of membrane-binding MORN domains, testis/fallopian tube enrichment, and ciliary protein interactions supports a mechanistic hypothesis: MORN5 functions as a membrane-cytoskeleton linker in ciliated epithelia, potentially stabilizing membrane domains at ciliary bases or facilitating vesicle trafficking in reproductive tissues.
