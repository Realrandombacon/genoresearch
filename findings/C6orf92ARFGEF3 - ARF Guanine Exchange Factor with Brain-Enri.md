# C6orf92/ARFGEF3 - ARF Guanine Exchange Factor with Brain-Enriched Vesicle Trafficking Function

**Date:** 2026-03-16T19:26:30.553966

## Description
C6orf92 (ARFGEF3) encodes a 2177-amino acid Brefeldin A-inhibited guanine nucleotide-exchange protein 3 (UniProt Q5TH69) functioning as an ARF GTPase activator regulating vesicular trafficking and membrane dynamics. InterPro analysis reveals extensive multi-domain architecture: catalytic Sec7 domain (IPR000904/cd00171, aa 586-798) mediating guanine nucleotide exchange on ARF GTPases, 6 Armadillo-type repeats (IPR016024/SSF48371, aa 91-1612) forming extended protein-protein interaction scaffold, HDS domain (IPR015403/PF09324, aa 1245-1307) for membrane association, dimerization/cyclophilin-binding domain (IPR032629/PF16213, aa 5-168) enabling oligomerization and immunophilin regulation, and C-terminal BIG2-like domain (IPR046455/PF20252, aa 1732-1843). STRING interaction network shows exceptionally high-confidence binding to prohibitin-2 PHB2 (0.962), linking ARFGEF3 to mitochondrial cristae organization and inner membrane scaffolding, plus interactions with TMEM253 (0.541), protein phosphatase PPP1CA (0.519), CCDC167 (0.507), and chromatin regulator EAPP (0.413). HPA data indicates brain-enriched expression (20.1 nTPM), salivary gland expression (17.0 nTPM), tissue-enhanced pattern, cancer-enhanced expression, classified as guanine-nucleotide releasing factor with predicted intracellular localization. ClinVar contains 17 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including copy number gains/losses at 6q23-24 and truncating mutations (p.Arg1644Ter), indicating disease relevance. AlphaFold structure (pLDDT 64.8) confirms partially structured architecture with ordered catalytic domains and flexible linker regions. ARFGEF3 likely functions as a master regulator coordinating ARF-dependent vesicle budding from Golgi and endosomal compartments with mitochondrial quality control via PHB2 interaction. The brain enrichment suggests specialized roles in neuronal synaptic vesicle trafficking and neurotransmitter release, while cancer enhancement indicates oncogenic potential through dysregulated membrane trafficking and growth factor receptor recycling. The armadillo repeat scaffold may recruit multiple effectors simultaneously, positioning ARFGEF3 as a signaling hub integrating vesicle transport with metabolic and proliferative cues.
