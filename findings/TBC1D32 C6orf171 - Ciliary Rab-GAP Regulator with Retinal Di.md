# TBC1D32 (C6orf171) - Ciliary Rab-GAP Regulator with Retinal Disease Link

**Date:** 2026-03-16T18:53:43.808205

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
TBC1D32 encodes a 1257 aa protein 'broad-minded' containing a C-terminal TBC/Rab-GAP domain (PF23440, IPR035969, positions 853-1252) that catalyzes GTP hydrolysis on Rab GTPases. The protein features extensive BROMI domains (PF14961, IPR032735, positions 173-844) of unknown function. AlphaFold predicts a well-folded structure (pLDDT 80.5) across the full length. HPA shows retina-enriched expression (6.9 nTPM) with vesicular/mitochondrial localization. STRING interactions reveal strong connections to ciliopathy genes: CFAP20 (0.883), FAM149B1 (0.826), WDPCP (0.653), OFD1 (0.529), TMEM67 (0.506), ANKS6 (0.480) - all critical for primary cilia assembly and function. ClinVar contains 65 pathogenic variants including frameshifts (p.Leu718fs, p.Leu592fs) and truncations (p.Arg1242*, p.Arg734*). The TBC domain position and cilia protein interaction network strongly suggest TBC1D32 functions as a Rab GTPase-activating protein regulating vesicular trafficking to the ciliary base. Retinal expression combined with pathogenic variants implies potential role in photoreceptor cilia maintenance - defects could cause retinal dystrophy. The BROMI middle region may scaffold effector recruitment.
