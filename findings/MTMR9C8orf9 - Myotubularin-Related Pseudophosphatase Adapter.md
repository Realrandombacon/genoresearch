# MTMR9/C8orf9 - Myotubularin-Related Pseudophosphatase Adapter with PH-GRAM Domain and 116 Pathogenic CNVs

## Description
MTMR9 (C8orf9) encodes a 549 amino acid myotubularin-related protein 9 that functions as a catalytically inactive pseudophosphatase adapter regulating myotubularin lipid phosphatase activity. InterPro analysis reveals two critical domain architectures: a PH-GRAM domain (PF21098/IPR048994, residues 23-100; cd13211, residues 1-97) for membrane phosphoinositide binding, and a myotubularin-like phosphatase domain (PF06602/IPR010569, residues 108-445; PS51339, residues 123-498) that lacks catalytic activity due to active site mutations. The protein belongs to the myotubularin family (IPR030564/PTHR10807) with a protein-tyrosine phosphatase-like fold (G3DSA:2.30.29.30, IPR029021). AlphaFold structure prediction (AF-Q96QG7-F1) shows very high confidence (pLDDT 95.1) across all 549 residues, indicating a well-folded stable structure suitable for protein-protein interactions. STRING-DB identifies 10 interaction partners including MTMR6 (0.998 - obligate heterodimer partner), MTMR8 (0.988), FAM167A (0.871), EMILIN1 (0.821), BLK (0.710), XKR6 (0.663), MTMR7 (0.647), KRT9 (0.612), CDA (0.597), and LCA5L (0.541). HPA expression data shows ubiquitous tissue distribution with low specificity, subcellular localization to actin filaments and centrosome, and classification as plasma proteins involved in endocytosis. ClinVar reports 116 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly chromosome 8p23 copy number losses and gains, indicating disease association through genomic instability. Mechanistically, MTMR9 heterodimerizes with catalytically active MTMR6 via their phosphatase domains, enhancing MTMR6's lipid phosphatase activity toward phosphatidylinositol 3,5-bisphosphate (PI(3,5)P2). The PH-GRAM domain anchors the complex to endosomal membranes. Loss of MTMR9 disrupts PI(3,5)P2 homeostasis, impairing endosomal trafficking, autophagy, and lysosomal function - processes linked to myopathy and neurodegeneration phenotypes observed in 8p23 CNV syndromes.
