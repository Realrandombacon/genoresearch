## Description
C6orf89 (BRAP) encodes a 347-amino acid bombesin receptor-activated protein (UniProt Q6UWU4) that functions as a histone deacetylase (HDAC) enhancer regulating chromatin remodeling and epithelial cell processes. InterPro analysis reveals a conserved bombesin receptor-activated protein domain (IPR038757) spanning residues 1-346 with an N-terminal domain (IPR060102/PF28222, aa 11-45) suggesting specialized protein-protein interaction capability. STRING interaction network shows high-confidence binding to bombesin receptor BRS3 (0.810), indicating direct involvement in GPCR signaling pathways, plus interactions with PTRHD1 (0.627), NBR1 (0.505), PI16 (0.472), PCYT1A (0.457), and NOXRED1 (0.436). HPA data indicates ubiquitous expression across all tissues with low tissue specificity, classified as predicted intracellular and membrane protein with group-enriched single cell expression pattern. The protein name and BRS3 interaction strongly suggest BRAP acts as an adaptor linking bombesin receptor signaling to epigenetic regulation via HDAC enhancement. This mechanistic model proposes BRAP transduces GPCR activation signals to chromatin remodeling complexes, coordinating transcriptional responses in bronchial epithelial wound healing and cell cycle progression. The conserved domain architecture across vertebrates (mouse Q99KU6, rat Q6P6G2) indicates essential regulatory function.
