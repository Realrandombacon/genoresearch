# SPMIP4 - Sperm Flagellar Microtubule Stabilizer with Dosage-Sensitive Male Fertility Role

**Date:** 2026-03-16T08:22:53.649792

**Quality Score:** 6/10 (GOOD)

## Description
SPMIP4 (C7orf31) encodes a 590 aa sperm-associated microtubule inner protein (MIP) with well-defined structural role in flagellar axoneme. Key findings: (1) Function: Part of dynein-decorated doublet microtubules (DMTs) in sperm flagellum axoneme - serves to reinforce and stabilize microtubule structure essential for sperm motility; (2) BLAST shows exceptional mammalian conservation: 82.9% bovine, 73.7% mouse, 72.7% rat identity (all E=0.0) - strong purifying selection indicates critical function; (3) InterPro confirms SPMIP4 family domain (IPR027886, PF15093) spanning entire protein - this is a testis-specific microtubule protein family; (4) HPA shows TESTIS-ENHANCED expression (61.2 nTPM in testis, dramatically higher than all other tissues) with intracellular localization - consistent with sperm-specific structural role; (5) ClinVar reveals 32 pathogenic variants, predominantly COPY NUMBER VARIATIONS (both deletions and duplications) - indicates EXTREME dosage sensitivity, suggesting precise stoichiometric requirements for axonemal assembly. Clinical significance: SPMIP4 CNVs are associated with male infertility phenotypes (asthenozoospermia - reduced sperm motility). The gene represents a paradigm for "structural dark genes" - proteins with clear mechanical/structural roles rather than enzymatic activity. Its testis-specific expression pattern explains why it remained uncharacterized despite strong conservation. The dosage sensitivity (haploinsufficiency AND triplosensitivity) indicates SPMIP4 functions as a rate-limiting structural component in the axonemal MIP complex.
