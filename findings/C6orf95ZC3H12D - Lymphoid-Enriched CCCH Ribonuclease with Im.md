## Description
C6orf95 (ZC3H12D) encodes a 527-amino acid probable ribonuclease zinc finger CCCH-type containing protein 12D (UniProt A2A288) functioning as an endoribonuclease regulating mRNA stability and immune responses. InterPro analysis reveals catalytic NYN ribonuclease domain (IPR021869/PF11977/cd18729, aa 89-243) with PIN-domain fold (G3DSA:3.40.50.11980, aa 88-250) meding RNA cleavage, N-terminal UBA-like domain (IPR040546/PF18039, aa 3-44) for ubiquitin-related protein interactions, and C-terminal CCCH-type zinc finger (IPR000571/PS50103, aa 248-279) for RNA binding and substrate recognition. The ZC3H12/N4BP1 ribonuclease family (IPR051101/PTHR12876, aa 1-515) indicates functional homology with inflammatory regulator ZC3H12A/MCPIP1. STRING interaction network shows exceptionally high-confidence binding to cannabinoid receptor CNR2 (0.910), linking ribonuclease activity to GPCR signaling and immune modulation, plus interleukin-12 receptor IL12RB1 (0.773) for Th1 differentiation, paralog ZC3H12A (0.407) suggesting heterodimerization, RC3H2/ROQUIN2 (0.510) and RC3H1/ROQUIN1 (0.437) for post-transcriptional regulation, SIDT1 (0.506) for RNA transport, LY9 (0.478) for lymphocyte activation, and BCAS4 (0.421). HPA data indicates lymphoid tissue-enriched expression (11.8 nTPM), intestine expression (4.4 nTPM), bone marrow (3.4 nTPM), group-enriched pattern, classified as disease-related gene with endonuclease/hydrolase/nuclease molecular function, nucleoplasmic and cytoplasmic body localization. ClinVar contains 21 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including copy number gains/losses at 6q24-27, indicating disease relevance. ZC3H12D likely functions as a post-transcriptional regulator degrading inflammatory mRNAs in lymphoid tissues through NYN catalytic activity, with the CCCH zinc finger recognizing AU-rich elements in 3′UTRs. The CNR2 interaction suggests coupling to cannabinoid signaling pathways modulating immune responses, while ZC3H12A partnership indicates functional redundancy or heteromeric complex formation analogous to MCPIP1-mediated mRNA decay. Lymphoid enrichment positions ZC3H12D as a critical checkpoint controlling cytokine production and lymphocyte activation, with clinical variants potentially disrupting ribonuclease function leading to immune dysregulation.
