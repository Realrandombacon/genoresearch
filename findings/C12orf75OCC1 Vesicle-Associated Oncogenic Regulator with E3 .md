# C12orf75/OCC1: Vesicle-Associated Oncogenic Regulator with E3 Ligase Links

**Date:** 2026-03-16T19:19:40.149211

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
C12orf75 (OCC1) encodes a 63-amino acid intrinsically disordered protein (AlphaFold pLDDT=68.4) containing the OCC1 family domain (PF15506, IPR029133). Despite its small size, it exhibits strong tissue-specific expression in blood vessels (129.2 nTPM) and seminal vesicles (102.9 nTPM), and is classified as "cancer enhanced" by the Human Protein Atlas. Subcellular localization is predicted to be vesicular. Critically, ClinVar reports 10 pathogenic/likely pathogenic copy number variants (gains/losses) affecting this locus, suggesting a dosage-sensitive role in human disease. STRING interaction analysis reveals high-confidence associations (score >0.55) with BTRC (Beta-TrCP, an F-box E3 ubiquitin ligase substrate receptor) and IRS4 (Insulin Receptor Substrate 4), alongside links to LUZP6 and ELAVL1. The physical interaction with BTRC suggests OCC1 may function as a regulatory substrate or modulator of the SCF(BTRC) E3 ubiquitin ligase complex, potentially influencing NF-kappaB signaling or cell cycle progression. Its overexpression in colon carcinoma and vascular tissue, combined with structural disorder and E3 ligase binding, supports a hypothesis that OCC1 acts as a disordered hub protein regulating ubiquitination-dependent pathways in vascular integrity and oncogenesis.
