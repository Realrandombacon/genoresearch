# PTCHD4 (C6orf138) - Sterol-Sensing Patched Family Protein with Hedgehog Pathway Association

**Date:** 2026-03-16T18:44:28.038311

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
PTCHD4 (UniProt Q6ZW05) encodes an 846-amino acid multi-pass transmembrane protein containing canonical sterol-sensing and Patched domains. InterPro analysis reveals multiple conserved domains: IPR000731 sterol-sensing domain (residues 233-392), IPR003392 Patched SSD domain (residues 241-391; PF02460), and IPR051697 Patched domain-containing protein family (residues 143-818; PTHR10796). Structural homology to multidrug efflux transporter AcrB transmembrane domain (G3DSA:1.20.1640.10, residues 192-398 and 613-826) suggests a 12-transmembrane helix topology characteristic of sterol transporters. HPA data shows no detectable expression in normal tissues but cancer group-enriched expression with plasma membrane and cytosolic localization, indicating potential oncogenic function. STRING interactions reveal medium-confidence associations with DISP3 (0.619), TMEM178A (0.495), and HIVEP1 (0.456). The DISP3 interaction is mechanistically significant - DISP proteins are essential for Hedgehog ligand secretion, and PTCHD4's Patched/sterol-sensing domains suggest it may function as a Hedgehog pathway regulator or cholesterol transporter. The Patched family (PTCH1/PTCH2) are well-known Hedgehog receptors that regulate Smoothened activity through sterol binding. PTCHD4 may represent a divergent family member with similar cholesterol-sensing capabilities. ClinVar documents 8 pathogenic copy number variants (losses and gains) on chromosome 6p, demonstrating clinical relevance. Cancer-enriched expression combined with Hedgehog pathway association suggests PTCHD4 may contribute to oncogenic Hedgehog signaling or cholesterol metabolism dysregulation in tumors.
