## Description

The protein contains a conserved C-terminal domain (IPR057896/PF25818, residues 133-235) belonging to the PTHR13633 family, essential for its ribosome-binding function. These interactions place MTRES1 at the heart of mitochondrial translation quality control, specifically in rescuing stalled ribosomes on mitochondrial mRNAs.\n\nHPA shows ubiquitous expression across all tissues with low specificity (detected everywhere), consistent with essential housekeeping function in oxidative phosphorylation. ClinVar documents 19 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly copy number losses and gains spanning 6q15-22, indicating dosage sensitivity and disease association when disrupted. The combination of mitoribosomal binding, tRNA-binding activity, and transcription rescue function positions MTRES1 as a critical factor maintaining mitochondrial proteome integrity during translational stress.
