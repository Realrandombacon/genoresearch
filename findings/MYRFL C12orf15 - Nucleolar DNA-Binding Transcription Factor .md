## Description
MYRFL (Myelin Regulatory Factor-Like, UniProt Q96LU7) is a 910 aa nuclear protein with compelling evidence for DNA-binding transcription factor activity. InterPro analysis reveals multiple DNA-binding domains: NDT80 DNA-binding domain (IPR024061, residues 331-408, PF05224), p53-like transcription factor DNA-binding superfamily (IPR008967, residues 218-404), and a C-terminal myelin regulatory domain (IPR025719, residues 769-909). The protein also contains an intramolecular chaperone auto-processing domain (IPR030392, residues 451-559) suggesting potential autoproteolytic activity. AlphaFold structure prediction (pLDDT 67.2) indicates a partially folded protein with structured DNA-binding regions and potentially disordered regulatory segments. HPA data shows intestine-enriched expression (21.6 nTPM) with nucleolar subcellular localization, consistent with ribosomal biogenesis or rRNA processing roles. STRING interactions identify 5 medium-confidence partners: SPO11 (0.497, meiotic DNA double-strand break initiator), MAK (0.417, male germ cell-associated kinase), ICK (0.417, intestinal cell kinase), MOK (0.417, kinase), and DNMBP (0.410, dynamin-binding protein). ClinVar contains 13 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly copy number losses and gains spanning 12q15, indicating disease relevance though specific phenotypes remain uncharacterized. The combination of DNA-binding domains, nucleolar localization, intestine-specific expression, and kinase/meiosis protein interactions suggests MYRFL functions as a transcriptional regulator coordinating intestinal epithelial cell proliferation and differentiation, potentially through rRNA synthesis control or cell cycle gene regulation.
