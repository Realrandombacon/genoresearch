# C21orf101 (MRPS6): Well-Characterized Mitochondrial Ribosomal Protein S6 - Not a True Dark Gene

**Date:** 2026-03-16T17:03:24.041649

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
C21orf101 is the historical symbol for MRPS6 (Mitochondrial Ribosomal Protein S6, Gene ID 118422), a well-characterized 125 amino acid structural component of the small subunit of the mitochondrial ribosome (UniProt P82932). InterPro analysis reveals a defining Mitochondrial Ribosomal Protein S6 domain (cd15465, residues 3-96) and the conserved Ribosomal protein S6 family signature (IPR000529, PF01250, residues 3-95), with GO annotations for structural constituent of ribosome, rRNA binding, and translation. STRING interactions demonstrate extremely high-confidence binding to other mitochondrial ribosomal proteins including MRPL17 (0.999), MRPS9 (0.998), MRPL32 (0.993), MRPL27 (0.993), MRPS10 (0.982), MRPS5 (0.982), and MRPS7 (0.972), as well as cytosolic ribosomal proteins RPS3 (0.999) and RPL35 (0.984), reflecting its integration into the mitochondrial translational machinery. HPA localizes MRPS6 to mitochondria, classifies it as a ribosomal protein, and shows ubiquitous expression with enrichment in choroid plexus (320.1 nTPM) and kidney (308.2 nTPM). ClinVar contains 63 pathogenic variants including copy number losses and gains on chromosome 21, confirming clinical significance likely related to mitochondrial dysfunction. The presence of defined ribosomal domain architecture, established interaction network with mitochondrial ribosome partners, specific mitochondrial localization, tissue-enriched expression, and pathogenic variant burden indicates C21orf101/MRPS6 is NOT a dark gene but a functionally annotated essential component of the mitochondrial translation apparatus.
