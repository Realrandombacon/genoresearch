## Description
C14orf93 (Q9H972) is a 538 aa uncharacterized dark gene encoding a predicted intracellular/secreted protein with dual subcellular localization (nucleoplasm + plasma membrane). Contains single defining feature: DUF4616 domain (IPR028101, PF15394) covering entire protein (aa 1-538, ~100%) — this domain of unknown function represents the core functional module with no homology to characterized proteins. BLAST shows strong mouse conservation (87.2% identity, E-value 0.0), indicating functional constraint across mammals. STRING interactions reveal interaction network with mixed characterized and dark gene partners: HAUS4 (0.569, HAUS augmin-like complex subunit 4 — centrosome/mitotic spindle assembly), C1orf189 (0.543, dark gene on chromosome 1), RETN (0.492, resistin — adipokine hormone), RETNLB (0.444, resistin-like beta), SAPCD1 (0.447, suppressor of APCD1), MRFAP1 (0.407, MORF4L1-binding protein), TMCO6 (0.401, transmembrane coiled-coil protein 6). The HAUS4 interaction is particularly notable — HAUS4 is part of the augmin complex critical for centrosome-dependent microtubule nucleation during mitosis, suggesting C14orf93 may function in cell division/spindle assembly. HPA shows ubiquitous expression pattern (detected in all tissues, low tissue specificity) — unlike testis-enriched dark genes, this suggests housekeeping or fundamental cellular function. ClinVar contains 21 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly 14q copy number gains and losses (14q11.2-23.1, 14q11.2-21.3, 14q11.2-32.33), confirming strong disease relevance in 14q deletion/duplication syndromes. True dark gene: DUF4616 single-domain architecture (unknown function), ubiquitous expression (housekeeping signature), HAUS4 centrosome interaction (mitotic spindle potential), dark gene partnership (C1orf189), clinically implicated with 14q CNV burden, but molecular mechanism completely unknown. Hypothesis: may function as a centrosome-associated factor that regulates mitotic spindle assembly or microtubule dynamics during cell division (HAUS4 augmin complex interaction), with DUF4616 domain mediating protein-protein interactions at the centrosome or spindle pole, ubiquitous expression indicating fundamental role in all dividing cells, and dysregulation contributing to 14q deletion syndromes with developmental/growth phenotypes through mitotic checkpoint disruption.
