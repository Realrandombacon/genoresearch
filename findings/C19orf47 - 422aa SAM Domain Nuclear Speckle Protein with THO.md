## Description
C19orf47 (Gene ID: 126526, alias: chromosome 19 open reading frame 47) encodes a 422 amino acid uncharacterized protein localized to chromosome 19q13.2 with nucleoplasm and nuclear speckles subcellular localization. InterPro analysis reveals rich conserved domain architecture: SAM domain of CS047 subfamily (cd09531, residues 45-109), Sterile alpha motif/Pointed domain superfamily (IPR013761, residues 45-115), SAM domain (PF18017, residues 38-121), C19orf47 SAM domain (IPR040772, residues 45-109), Domain of unknown function DUF5577 (IPR041477, residues 130-418), DUF5577 family (PF17740, residues 130-418), uncharacterized protein C19orf47-like family (IPR039161, residues 40-420), and DUF5577 domain-containing protein family (PTHR21359, residues 40-420). The presence of a SAM (Sterile Alpha Motif) domain is particularly significant - SAM domains are protein interaction modules that mediate homo- and hetero-oligomerization, enabling polymerization into supramolecular assemblies. AlphaFold structure prediction shows medium confidence (pLDDT 60.6), consistent with a partially structured protein containing both ordered domains (SAM fold) and flexible linkers - this architecture is typical of scaffold proteins that combine structured interaction domains with disordered regions for conformational adaptability. Mechanistic hypothesis: C19orf47 functions as a SAM domain-mediated oligomerization scaffold that recruits THO/TREX mRNA export complexes to nuclear speckles during transcriptional elongation in muscle tissues. The 422aa size with N-terminal SAM domain (residues 38-121) and C-terminal DUF5577 module (residues 130-418) suggests bipartite architecture - the SAM domain mediates polymerization into higher-order assemblies while DUF5577 provides interaction surfaces for mRNA export factors. SAM domains typically form end-to-end polymers or sheet-like assemblies - C19orf47 may polymerize into nuclear speckle scaffolds that concentrate THO/TREX components (THOC1, THOC5, NCBP3) for efficient mRNA export. Nuclear speckles localization places C19orf47 within splicing factor compartments - C19orf47 may couple splicing to export by recruiting THO complex to speckle-associated nascent transcripts. The exceptionally high skeletal muscle expression (335.4 nTPM) suggests specialized roles in muscle gene expression - muscle tissues express large gene sets encoding sarcomeric proteins requiring efficient mRNA export; C19orf47 may facilitate bulk mRNA export in highly transcriptionally active myonuclei. THO complex interactions suggest R-loop prevention functions - C19orf47 may prevent transcription-replication conflicts in muscle genes prone to genomic instability. The medium AlphaFold confidence (60.6 pLDDT) suggests flexible linkers between SAM and DUF5577 domains, enabling conformational switching for regulated assembly. DUF5577 represents an uncharacterized structural motif - C19orf47 may bind RNA, nascent transcripts, or export adaptors through this conserved fold. Pathogenic 19q13.2 CNVs likely disrupt dosage-sensitive mRNA export networks, contributing to developmental and neuromuscular phenotypes in 19q deletion/duplication syndromes via impaired transcription-export coupling, R-loop accumulation, or defective nuclear speckle organization in developing tissues.
