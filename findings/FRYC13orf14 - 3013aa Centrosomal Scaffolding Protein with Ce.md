## Description
FRY (formerly C13orf14) encodes a massive 3013aa cytoskeletal scaffolding protein containing multiple conserved cell morphogenesis domains: N-terminal (165-697), central regions (1209-1369, 1637-1698, 1781-1898), and C-terminal domains (2042-2294, 2319-3013). The protein belongs to the Furry/Tao3/Mor2 family (IPR039867) conserved from yeast to humans. FRY plays a crucial role in mitotic centrosome structural integrity and spindle bipolarity maintenance by promoting PLK1 kinase activity at spindle poles during early mitosis. STRING analysis reveals interactions with STK38L and STK38 (NDR family kinases, scores 0.697 and 0.687), FRYL (paralog, 0.487), suggesting functions in Hippo signaling pathway and cell division regulation. HPA shows low tissue specificity with expression detected in many tissues, consistent with essential cell cycle function. ClinVar reports 50 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including copy number losses and intronic variants on chromosome 13, indicating clinical significance. Despite the "C13orf14" dark gene label, FRY has established molecular function in centrosome biology and mitotic spindle assembly.
