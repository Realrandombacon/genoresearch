## Description
C6orf47-AS1 (Gene ID: 106480731) is a long non-coding RNA (lncRNA) transcribed antisense to the C6orf47 locus on chromosome 6p21.33, within the major histocompatibility complex (MHC) class I/III region. As an antisense transcript, C6orf47-AS1 lacks protein-coding potential and therefore has no UniProt accession, no InterPro domains, and no STRING protein-protein interactions. ClinVar search returned zero pathogenic variants, indicating no established disease associations to date. Human Protein Atlas has no expression data, suggesting either low/conditional expression, tissue-specificity, or lack of validated probes. The genomic position at 6p21.33 places C6orf47-AS1 within a gene-dense immune regulatory region containing HLA class I genes, complement factors, and cytokine loci. Antisense lncRNAs typically function through cis-regulatory mechanisms: transcriptional interference with sense strand, RNA duplex formation triggering RNAi pathways, recruitment of chromatin modifiers (PRC2, LSD1), or modulation of mRNA stability. Given its MHC localization, C6orf47-AS1 may regulate C6orf47 expression or neighboring immune genes during inflammatory responses. The absence of clinical variants suggests either functional redundancy, subtle phenotypic effects, or that this transcript remains truly uncharacterized. Future work should determine expression patterns across immune cell types, identify RNA-binding protein partners via CLIP-seq, and assess knockout phenotypes in immune challenge models.
