# NOP16: Nucleolar Ribosome Biogenesis Factor with High-Confidence Structure and Extensive Pre-rRNA Processing Interactome

**Date:** 2026-03-18T04:09:10.181251

**Quality Score:** 9/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
NOP16 (nucleolar protein 16, also known as HSPC111) is a 178-amino acid nucleolar protein functioning as an essential factor in ribosome biogenesis and pre-ribosomal RNA (pre-rRNA) processing. Structural analysis reveals a conserved Nop16 domain (IPR019002, PF09420, residues 70-155) spanning the core of the protein, characteristic of eukaryotic ribosome assembly factors. The AlphaFold predicted structure (Q9Y3C1) shows high confidence (pLDDT 85.5), indicating a well-folded globular domain typical of stable protein-protein interaction scaffolds. Expression profiling from the Human Protein Atlas demonstrates ubiquitous expression across all tissues with low tissue specificity, consistent with an essential housekeeping role in fundamental translation machinery biogenesis, with exclusive subcellular localization to nucleoli—the site of ribosomal RNA transcription and ribosome subunit assembly. Protein interaction networks (STRING) reveal striking enrichment for pre-rRNA processing and ribosome biogenesis complex components: NIP7 (score 0.823, nucleolar pre-rRNA processing factor), MRTO4 (0.800, Mrt4 homolog required for 25S/28S rRNA processing), NIFK (0.798, nucleolar protein involved in cytokinesis and ribosome biogenesis), NHP2 (0.758, H/ACA box snoRNP component for rRNA pseudouridylation), IMP4 (0.756, U3 snoRNP component for 18S rRNA processing), WDR12 (0.711, PeBow complex member for 60S subunit maturation), EBNA1BP2 (0.691, nucleolar transcriptional regulator), RRP15 (0.660, pre-rRNA processing factor), LLPH (0.533, nucleolar protein), and FBL (0.449, fibrillarin, catalytic snoRNP methyltransferase). These interactions place NOP16 within the small subunit (SSU) processome and pre-60S ribosome assembly pathways. ClinVar documents 46 pathogenic or likely pathogenic variants including large copy number losses (5q35.2-35.3), copy number gains, deletions, and missense SNVs (p.Tyr130Cys), indicating strong dosage sensitivity and disease association. The mechanistic hypothesis proposes that NOP16 functions as a scaffold protein within the SSU processome, stabilizing transient RNA-protein interactions during 18S rRNA folding and cleavage at sites A0, A1, and A2. Its nucleolar confinement and interaction with U3 snoRNP components (IMP4) and H/ACA snoRNPs (NHP2) suggest coordination between rRNA modification and endonucleolytic processing. Dysregulation may contribute to nucleolar stress, impaired translation capacity, and ribosomopathy phenotypes. High variant burden and essential protein classification warrant investigation for developmental disorders, cancer susceptibility (given Myc induction and breast cancer prognosis marker status), or bone marrow failure syndromes.
