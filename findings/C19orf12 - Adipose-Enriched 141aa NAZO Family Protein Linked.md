# C19orf12 - Adipose-Enriched 141aa NAZO Family Protein Linked to Mitochondrial Membrane Protein-Associated Neurodegeneration (MPAN)

**Date:** 2026-03-16T03:07:39.260920

**Quality Score:** 8/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
C19orf12 encodes a small 141 aa protein (UniProt Q9NSK7) belonging to the NAZO family (IPR033369, PF20721, PTHR31493). Originally annotated as chromosome 19 open reading frame 12, now recognized as disease-causing gene. Shows adipose tissue-enriched expression (110.6 nTPM) with secondary high expression in tongue (98.1 nTPM) - tissue-enhanced specificity pattern. Ubiquitous detection across all tissues suggests fundamental cellular role. Localizes to cytosol as predicted intracellular protein. Clinically significant: mutations cause Mitochondrial Membrane Protein-Associated Neurodegeneration (MPAN), a form of neurodegeneration with brain iron accumulation (NBIA). Also associated with hereditary spastic paraplegia. Disease variant classification confirms pathogenic mutations. The small size (141 aa) and conserved NAZO domain architecture across metazoans suggests essential function in mitochondrial membrane integrity or lipid metabolism. Adipose tissue enrichment raises hypothesis about lipid droplet or mitochondrial function in fat cells. Represents a successfully characterized former 'dark gene' now recognized as critical for neuronal survival and mitochondrial homeostasis. Therapeutic target for NBIA/MPAN neurodegenerative disorders.
