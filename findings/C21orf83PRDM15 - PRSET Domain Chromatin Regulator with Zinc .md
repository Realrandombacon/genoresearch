## Description
C21orf83 (now annotated as PRDM15, UniProt P57071) encodes a 1141 amino acid nuclear protein with a canonical PR/SET methyltransferase domain (residues 41-166, IPR044409, PF21549) and an extensive array of C2H2-type zinc fingers (IPR013087, PF00096) at positions 197-219, 372-394, 399-427, 460-482, 538-558, and 563-585. The protein localizes to nucleoplasm and nuclear bodies per HPA, with ubiquitous expression across all tissues (low tissue specificity) and functions as a transcription regulator with DNA-binding and methyltransferase activity. AlphaFold prediction (pLDDT 56.0) indicates a partially disordered structure consistent with chromatin-associated proteins that require conformational flexibility. STRING interactions reveal high-confidence partnerships with CDAN1 (0.781), SETD4 (0.672), SMYD4 (0.647), histone H1-1 (0.571), SMYD5 (0.541), and other PRDM family members (PRDM8, 0.517), suggesting participation in multiprotein chromatin modification complexes. ClinVar contains 80 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly copy number gains and losses across 21q22, indicating dosage sensitivity and developmental disease associations. The combination of SET catalytic domain, multiple DNA-binding zinc fingers, nuclear localization, and interaction with histone modifiers supports a mechanism where PRDM15 recruits histone methyltransferase activity to specific genomic loci via zinc finger-mediated DNA recognition, regulating developmental gene expression programs. Disease-associated CNVs suggest haploinsufficiency or triplosensitivity disrupts this epigenetic regulation.
