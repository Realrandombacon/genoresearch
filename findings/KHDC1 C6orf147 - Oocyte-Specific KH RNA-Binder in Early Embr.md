# KHDC1 (C6orf147) - Oocyte-Specific KH RNA-Binder in Early Embryo Development

**Date:** 2026-03-16T07:57:35.064010

**Quality Score:** 9/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
KHDC1 (C6orf147) encodes a 237 aa protein with a conserved KH-like RNA-binding domain (aa 67-181). Highly conserved in mammals (55% mouse identity). Interacts strongly with oocyte-specific factors KHDC3L, DPPA5, OOEP, and TRIM43 (scores >0.97), placing it in the subcortical maternal complex (SCMC) essential for early embryogenesis. Expressed in brain (14.1 nTPM) and enhanced in cancers; localized to vesicles/cytosol. 13 pathogenic ClinVar variants (CNVs) suggest dosage sensitivity. Function hypothesis: RNA-binding regulator of maternal mRNA stability/translation during oocyte maturation and early cleavage stages.
