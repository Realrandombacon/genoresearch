## Description
SRMS (C20orf148) is a 488-amino acid cytoplasmic non-receptor tyrosine kinase functioning as an intracellular signaling enzyme. Structural analysis reveals a canonical Src-homology domain architecture: N-terminal SH3 domain (IPR001452, residues 51-112) for proline-rich motif binding, central SH2 domain (IPR000980, residues 118-212) for phosphotyrosine recognition, and C-terminal catalytic kinase domain (IPR000719, residues 230-488) with ATP-binding and substrate phosphorylation activity. Unlike canonical Src family kinases, SRMS lacks the C-terminal regulatory tyrosine phosphorylation site and N-terminal myristoylation motif, suggesting distinct regulatory mechanisms and membrane association properties. The AlphaFold predicted structure (Q9H3Y6) shows high confidence (pLDDT 83.3), indicating a well-folded catalytic core typical of tyrosine kinases. Expression profiling from the Human Protein Atlas demonstrates tissue-enhanced expression with highest levels in lymphoid tissue (2.1 nTPM), stomach (2.1 nTPM), and epididymis (2.0 nTPM), with subcellular localization to cytosol, actin filaments, and intermediate filaments. Protein interaction networks (STRING) identify PTK6 (score 0.777, protein tyrosine kinase 6/Brk) as the highest confidence partner, another atypical Src-family kinase, alongside DOK1 (0.415, docking protein 1), a Ras GTPase-activating protein adaptor involved in immune receptor signaling. These interactions place SRMS within tyrosine kinase signaling networks regulating cell proliferation, differentiation, and immune responses. ClinVar documents 57 pathogenic or likely pathogenic variants including splice site mutations (c.1128+1G>A), copy number losses on chromosome 20q13.33, and deletions, indicating strong dosage sensitivity and disease association. The mechanistic hypothesis proposes that SRMS phosphorylates substrate proteins on tyrosine residues to modulate signaling cascades downstream of growth factor receptors or immune receptors, potentially acting as a positive regulator of cell proliferation or differentiation. Functional similarity to PTK6 suggests overlapping substrate specificity, though distinct subcellular localization may confer pathway specificity. High variant burden warrants investigation for roles in cancer susceptibility or developmental disorders.
