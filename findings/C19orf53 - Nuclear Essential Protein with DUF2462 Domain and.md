## Description
C19orf53 (Q9UNZ5) encodes a 99 aa nuclear protein containing the uncharacterized DUF2462 (PF09495) and UPF0390 (IPR019034) domains. AlphaFold predicts a well-folded structure (pLDDT 72.9), suggesting stable tertiary structure despite unknown function. HPA localizes it to nucleoplasm and nucleoli with ubiquitous expression across all tissues, classified as an essential protein. STRING interactions reveal connections to proteasome subunit PSMB3 (score 0.626), RNA polymerase II subunit POLR2J (0.584), COP9 signalosome component COPS9 (0.538), and mitochondrial complex I subunits NDUFA11/NDUFB7. ClinVar documents 13 pathogenic copy number variants on chromosome 19, indicating clinical significance though no specific disease phenotype is yet defined. The nuclear localization combined with proteasome and transcription machinery interactions suggests C19orf53 may function as a chromatin-associated regulator of protein turnover or transcriptional elongation. The DUF2462 family is conserved across vertebrates but functionally unannotated, representing a genuine dark gene target.
