# LIX1 (C5orf11) - CNS-Retina Enriched Autophagy-Linked Protein with EPG5 Homology and Cytokinetic Bridge Localization

**Date:** 2026-03-16T18:20:50.273172

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
LIX1 encodes a 282aa limb and CNS expressed protein (UniProt Q8N485) with strong structural homology to autophagy regulator EPG5. InterPro analysis identifies two key domains: a Limb expression 1 family domain (IPR029270/PF14954, aa 11-258) spanning most of the protein, and an Autophagy-related EPG5 domain (IPR051436, aa 27-110) at the N-terminus, suggesting involvement in autophagosomal maturation or vesicle trafficking. AlphaFold predicts a well-folded structure with high confidence (pLDDT 85.5), indicating stable tertiary structure suitable for protein-protein interactions. STRING interactions reveal connections to LNPEP/leucyl aminopeptidase (0.868), SLCO1A2/organic anion transporter (0.805), CWC25/spliceosome factor (0.788), and RUBCNL/rubicon-like autophagy regulator (0.772), placing LIX1 at the intersection of vesicle trafficking, autophagy, and RNA processing. HPA data shows CNS/retina enrichment (retina 39.5 nTPM, brain 25.9 nTPM) with subcellular localization to vesicles, cytokinetic bridge, and cytosol - consistent with roles in membrane trafficking during cell division. Cancer-enhanced expression and immune cell enhancement suggest proliferative functions. ClinVar contains 22 pathogenic variants including large 5q copy number losses/gains, indicating dosage sensitivity. The EPG5 homology domain suggests LIX1 may function as an autophagy adaptor protein, potentially linking autophagosomes to lysosomes in neuronal tissues where autophagy is critical for proteostasis.
