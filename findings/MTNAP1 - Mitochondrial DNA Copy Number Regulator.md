# MTNAP1 - Mitochondrial DNA Copy Number Regulator

**Date:** 2026-03-16T08:38:29.384083

**Quality Score:** 8/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
MTNAP1 (C17orf80) is a 609 aa mitochondrial nucleoid-associated protein that critically regulates mtDNA abundance. Contains N-terminal zinc finger domain (IPR060516, aa 9-31) for DNA binding. Highly conserved across vertebrates (95% orangutan, 47% mouse identity). Localizes to mitochondria and cytosol, expressed ubiquitously across all tissues. Binds dsDNA without sequence specificity to control mtDNA copy number. ClinVar shows 13 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants (mostly chr17 CNVs), indicating disease relevance despite limited functional characterization. Represents a functionally characterized "dark gene" - known molecular role but understudied in disease contexts.
