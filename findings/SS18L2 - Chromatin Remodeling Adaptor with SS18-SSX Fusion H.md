# SS18L2 - Chromatin Remodeling Adaptor with SS18-SSX Fusion Homology

**Date:** 2026-03-17T08:33:07.335339

**Quality Score:** 9/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
SS18L2 (UniProt Q9UHA2) encodes a 77aa nuclear protein on chromosome 3p22.1 with homology to SS18, the synovial sarcoma translocation breakpoint protein. InterPro analysis confirms membership in the SS18 N-terminal domain family (IPR007726, PF05030, positions 13-72), which mediates interaction with SSX family proteins in the oncogenic SS18-SSX fusion complex. AlphaFold structure prediction shows confident folding (pLDDT 74.8), consistent with a stable protein-protein interaction domain. STRING interaction network reveals high-confidence associations with SSX family chromatin regulators (SSX4B 0.750, SSX2B 0.750, SSX4 0.720, SSX1 0.668, SSX2 0.638) and SWI/SNF chromatin remodeling complex components (SMARCA4 0.632, DPF2 0.642, YY1AP1 0.625, GON4L 0.618), plus CRAMP1 (0.701). HPA subcellular localization confirms nucleoplasmic, nucleolar, and mitochondrial distribution with ubiquitous low-level expression across tissues. ClinVar contains 7 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly chromosome 3p copy-number alterations (gains and losses spanning 3p22.1-q29), indicating disease relevance though no specific Mendelian phenotype is established. The combination of SS18 homology, SSX interaction, chromatin regulator binding, and nuclear localization strongly supports a mechanistic role as a chromatin remodeling adaptor protein, potentially competing with or modulating SS18-SSX fusion oncoprotein activity in synovial sarcoma pathogenesis or serving as a physiological regulator of Polycomb/Trithor-group chromatin states during development.
