# RBM33: Large Intrinsically Disordered Nuclear RNA-Binding Protein with C-Terminal RRM Domain Regulating mRNA Export and Stability

**Date:** 2026-03-18T04:15:38.627216

**Quality Score:** 9/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
RBM33 (RNA binding motif protein 33, also known as PRR8) is a 1170-amino acid nuclear protein functioning as an N6-methyladenosine (m6A)-containing RNA reader and lncRNA-binding factor involved in mRNA export from nucleus and regulation of mRNA stability. Structural analysis reveals a modular domain architecture with a C-terminal RNA recognition motif (RRM) domain (IPR000504, SM00360, residues 1099-1166) embedded within a larger RBM33 family signature (IPR039878, PTHR22014, residues 591-1170) spanning the C-terminal half of the protein. The AlphaFold predicted structure (Q96EV2) shows low confidence (pLDDT 46.6), indicating extensive intrinsic disorder characteristic of large RNA-binding proteins that function through flexible RNA-protein interactions and multivalent protein-protein contacts rather than rigid enzymatic scaffolds. Expression profiling from the Human Protein Atlas demonstrates ubiquitous expression across all tissues with low tissue specificity, consistent with a housekeeping role in fundamental RNA metabolism, with exclusive subcellular localization to nucleoplasm—supporting nuclear RNA processing functions. Protein interaction networks (STRING) identify moderate-confidence partners including RNF32 (score 0.664, RING-type E3 ubiquitin ligase), NCBP3 (0.609, nuclear cap-binding complex subunit 3 for mRNA export), EME1 (0.583, DNA repair endonuclease), NOM1 (0.571, nucleolar protein), LMBR1 (0.554, limb membrane region protein), CNPY1 (0.541, canopy homolog 1), FGGY (0.510, carbohydrate kinase), THOC7 (0.507, THO complex subunit 7 for mRNA export), FAM83C (0.506, family with sequence similarity 83 member C), and USP24 (0.412, deubiquitinating enzyme). These interactions place RBM33 within nuclear RNA processing and export machineries, particularly the THO/TREX complex for mRNA export. ClinVar documents 92 pathogenic or likely pathogenic variants including large copy number gains (7q36.2-36.3), copy number losses (7q33-36.3, 7q34-36.3), deletions, and missense SNVs (p.Arg240Ser), indicating strong dosage sensitivity and disease association. The mechanistic hypothesis proposes that RBM33 functions as an m6A reader protein recognizing N6-methyladenosine modifications on nascent transcripts, recruiting export factors (NCBP3, THOC7) and stability regulators to coordinate nuclear export with cytoplasmic fate determination. The intrinsically disordered N-terminal region (residues 1-590) may serve as a flexible scaffold for multivalent RNA and protein interactions, enabling dynamic assembly of ribonucleoprotein complexes. Dysregulation may contribute to mRNA export defects, aberrant gene expression programs, and neurological or developmental disorders given the high variant burden.
