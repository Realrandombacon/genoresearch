The FAM167 domain combined with cytoplasmic localization and interaction with BANK1 (B-cell scaffold), BLK (BCR kinase), and PXK (BCR endocytosis) positions FAM167A as a B-cell receptor signaling scaffold that may coordinate proximal BCR signal transduction through transient interactions with kinase complexes and phosphatase regulators. High-confidence interaction with XKR6 (0.744, apoptosis regulator) suggests functional coupling between BCR signaling and apoptotic cell death, potentially modulating B-cell survival thresholds during immune activation. Interaction with MTMR7/MTMR9 (myotubularin phosphatases) indicates roles in phosphatidylinositol-3-phosphate metabolism and endosomal trafficking, consistent with BCR internalization and signaling termination. ClinVar documents 117 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly chromosome 8p copy number gains and losses encompassing the FAM167A locus, indicating strong dosage sensitivity in disease etiology, potentially affecting B-cell development and immune function. Disease-associated CNVs suggest haploinsufficiency or triplosensitivity mechanisms disrupting BCR signaling complex assembly or phosphoinositide homeostasis, potentially leading to immunodeficiency or autoimmunity through dysregulated B-cell activation thresholds. The 8p23.1 chromosomal location overlaps with GATA4 and other cardiac development genes, suggesting potential pleiotropic effects on cardiac and immune development in large CNV syndromes.
