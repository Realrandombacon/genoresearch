# C12orf76 - Brain-Enriched DUF5541 Protein with SVIP Interaction and Clinical CNVs

**Date:** 2026-03-16T23:04:21.690111

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
C12orf76 encodes a 135-amino acid uncharacterized protein containing a DUF5541 domain (IPR041240/PF17695, residues 20-135). The protein is brain-enriched with expression of 39.2 nTPM in brain tissue per HPA, classified as tissue-enhanced and detected in many tissues. AlphaFold prediction (Q8N812) shows low confidence (pLDDT 41.4), indicating an intrinsically disordered protein that likely functions through protein-protein interactions rather than enzymatic catalysis. STRING interactions reveal medium-confidence associations with SVIP (0.435), C12orf65 (0.409), and FRMD3 (0.405). SVIP (Small VCP/p97 Interacting Protein) is an ER-associated degradation cofactor, suggesting C12orf76 may participate in ER quality control or protein homeostasis pathways. ClinVar documents 9 pathogenic/likely pathogenic copy number variants (gains and losses) spanning the 12q23-24 region, implicating dosage sensitivity and potential disease relevance. The DUF5541 family is conserved across mammals (orthologs in Pongo abelii, Felis catus), indicating functional constraint. Sequence composition shows 23.7% charged residues and 37.0% hydrophobic residues, consistent with a disordered scaffold protein. We hypothesize C12orf76 functions as a membrane-associated disordered scaffold that recruits SVIP and potentially other cofactors to regulate ER-associated degradation or membrane protein quality control in neural tissues.
