# C2orf66 - DUF4720 Secreted Protein with Endocrine Expression and Clinical Variants

**Date:** 2026-03-16T18:05:04.190749

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
C2orf66 encodes a 117aa uncharacterized protein (UniProt Q6UXQ4) containing a DUF4720 domain (IPR031699/PF15846, residues 14-117) of unknown function. HPA data shows tissue-enhanced expression in endocrine tissues: adrenal gland (9.8 nTPM), pituitary gland (7.3 nTPM), and brain (4.7 nTPM), with cancer-enhanced expression pattern. Subcellular localization predicted in nucleoplasm and cytosol despite signal peptide prediction for secretion. STRING interactions reveal high-confidence association with TMED10 (score 0.850), a transmembrane emp24 domain transporter involved in ER-Golgi protein trafficking, plus medium-confidence interactions with CCDC190 (0.515), TMEM217 (0.479), and ELOA3 (0.447). ClinVar contains 37 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly copy number losses and gains spanning 2q32-33, suggesting dosage sensitivity. The DUF4720 domain spans nearly the entire protein (residues 14-117), indicating this is a single-domain protein. Conservation across vertebrates (human, chimp, dog, chicken, cat) suggests functional constraint. The combination of endocrine tissue expression, ER-Golgi trafficking partner (TMED10), and clinical CNV associations suggests C2orf66 may function as a secreted regulatory peptide in neuroendocrine pathways, potentially modulating hormone secretion or stress response. Pathogenic CNVs disrupting C2orf66 dosage could contribute to endocrine disorders or neurodevelopmental phenotypes.
