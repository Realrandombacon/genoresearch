# C10orf50/KXD1 - BLOC-1 Complex Adaptor with KxDL Domain and Centrosomal Localization

## Description
C10orf50 (Gene ID: 414150) is an outdated alias for KXD1 (KxDL motif containing 1, Gene ID: 79036), a 176 amino acid protein (UniProt Q9BQD3) encoded on chromosome 19p13.12, not chromosome 10 as the 'orf' name suggests. KXD1 contains a defining KxDL domain (InterPro IPR019371, residues 14-99) and belongs to the KxDL motif-containing protein 1 family (IPR039843, residues 3-174, PTHR13511) with a conserved PF10241 domain (14-99). AlphaFold structure prediction yields high confidence (pLDDT 75.7), indicating a stable folded structure. Human Protein Atlas shows ubiquitous expression across all tissues with low tissue specificity, localizing to nucleoli fibrillar center, centrosome, and cytosol — a distribution consistent with vesicular trafficking roles. STRING interaction network reveals 10 high-confidence partners dominated by BLOC-1 (Biogenesis of Lysosome-related Organelles Complex 1) and BORCS (BLOC-one-related complex) members: BORCS6 (0.999), BLOC1S1 (0.999), BORCS5 (0.998), BORCS7 (0.998), BLOC1S2 (0.998), BORCS8 (0.998), SNAPIN (0.997), plus ribosomal proteins RPS27A (0.956) and UBA52 (0.902), and actin-binding TPM1 (0.871). This interaction profile places KXD1 within the endolysosomal trafficking machinery, specifically the BLOC-1 complex that sorts cargo to lysosome-related organelles (melanosomes, platelet dense granules, lamellar bodies). ClinVar documents 8 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly large copy number gains spanning 19p13, suggesting dosage sensitivity though no specific monogenic disease is defined. The centrosomal localization combined with BLOC-1 interactions suggests KXD1 functions as an adaptor protein coordinating vesicle trafficking at the centrosome, potentially regulating ciliary membrane protein sorting, melanosome biogenesis, or cytokinetic abscission. The KxDL domain likely mediates protein-protein interactions within the BLOC-1 holoctameric complex, positioning KXD1 as a critical scaffold for lysosome-related organelle biogenesis.
