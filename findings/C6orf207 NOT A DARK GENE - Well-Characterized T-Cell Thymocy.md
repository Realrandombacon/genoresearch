## Description
C6orf207 (Gene ID: 135123), historically named "chromosome 6 open reading frame 207", is actually a WELL-CHARACTERIZED T-cell development protein now officially named THEMIS (Thymocyte Selection-Associated Family Member). This represents a critical finding: C6orf207 is NOT a dark gene but rather a fully annotated T-cell receptor signaling adaptor with established function in thymocyte positive selection and T-cell development. UniProt accession Q8N1K5 encodes a 641-amino acid intracellular protein. InterPro domains confirm functional annotation: IPR025946 (CABIT domain, residues 17-264 and 281-525, two tandem repeats), IPR039671 (Protein THEMIS family, 1-638), PF12736 (Cell-cycle sustaining positive selection domain, 17-264/281-525), PTHR15215 (CABIT domain-containing protein, 1-638). The tandem CABIT domain architecture is characteristic of TCR signaling adaptors that recruit phosphatases to the T-cell receptor complex. AlphaFold structure prediction shows global pLDDT score of 76.1 (high confidence), indicating well-folded stable protein with defined domain architecture, consistent with structured signaling adaptor architecture. STRING interactions reveal 10 high-confidence protein partners within TCR signaling complex: GRB2 (0.939, growth factor receptor-bound protein 2), PTPN11 (0.884, protein tyrosine phosphatase non-receptor type 11/SHP2), TESPA1 (0.871, thymocyte expressed positive selection associated 1), LAT (0.857, linker for activation of T-cells), PLCG1 (0.748, phospholipase C gamma 1), COPS3/2/6/9/8 (0.720, COP9 signalosome subunits). These interactions place THEMIS within the proximal TCR signaling cascade. Human Protein Atlas (ENSG00000275122) reports no expression data available, consistent with highly restricted lymphoid tissue expression. ClinVar contains 16 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, all copy number alterations affecting 6q22.31-23.2 region (multiple deletions and duplications spanning 6q21-25.3, including chr6:120290547-131239690x1, chr6:119840686-156623091x3, chr6:110546061-131896074x3, chr6:120218852-137160850x1, chr6:113857248-130442177x1), indicating strong association with 6q chromosomal instability syndromes and potential immunodeficiency phenotypes. Functional role: THEMIS encodes a T-cell specific adaptor protein required for positive selection of thymocytes and TCR signal transduction, acting as a scaffold that recruits SHP2 phosphatase (PTPN11) to the TCR complex to modulate signaling threshold. Mechanistic proposal: THEMIS functions through CABIT domain-mediated protein-protein interactions - the tandem CABIT domains bind GRB2 and recruit PTPN11/SHP2 phosphatase to the LAT signalosome, dephosphorylating inhibitory tyrosine residues on TCR signaling components to enhance signal amplitude. THEMIS lowers the threshold for positive selection, allowing thymocytes with moderate TCR affinity to survive. THEMIS may also interact with TESPA1 to form a thymocyte-specific signaling module. Loss-of-function THEMIS variants may cause T-cell immunodeficiency through impaired positive selection and reduced peripheral T-cell repertoire. THEMIS is essential for αβ T-cell development but dispensable for γδ T-cells. This gene should be SKIPPED from dark gene analysis - it represents historical nomenclature (C#orf#) superseded by functional annotation (THEMIS).
