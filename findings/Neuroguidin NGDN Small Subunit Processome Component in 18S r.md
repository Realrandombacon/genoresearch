# Neuroguidin (NGDN): Small Subunit Processome Component in 18S rRNA Biogenesis

## Description
C14orf120 encodes Neuroguidin (NGDN), a 315 amino acid protein (UniProt Q8NEJ9) containing a conserved Sas10/Utp3/C1D domain (IPR007146, PF04000, residues 18-98) characteristic of ribosome biogenesis factors. AlphaFold prediction shows high structural confidence (pLDDT 75.8), indicating a well-folded stable protein. HPA data reveals ubiquitous expression across all tissues with dual nucleolar and mitochondrial localization, consistent with roles in both ribosome assembly and mitochondrial translation. STRING interaction analysis reveals exceptionally high-confidence associations (all scores 0.998-0.999) with core small subunit (SSU) processome components: KRR1, WDR46, NOL10, WDR36, UTP18, UTP3, UTP6, UTP4, NOC4L, and UTP11. This interaction network places NGDN within the 5''-external transcribed spacer (5''-ETS) ribonucleoprotein complex responsible for 18S rRNA processing and small ribosomal subunit assembly. The Sas10/Utp3/C1D domain mediates RNA-protein interactions essential for pre-rRNA cleavage at sites A0, A1, and A2. No ClinVar variants identified (0 pathogenic), suggesting either functional redundancy with paralogs or that complete loss is incompatible with viability. Mechanistically, NGDN likely functions as a scaffold protein recruiting endonucleases and helicases to specific pre-rRNA cleavage sites, with its nucleolar localization signal directing it to sites of active ribosome transcription. The mitochondrial localization suggests a parallel role in mitoribosome assembly, potentially explaining the essential nature of this protein.
