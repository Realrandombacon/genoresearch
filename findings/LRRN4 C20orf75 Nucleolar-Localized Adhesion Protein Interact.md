# LRRN4 (C20orf75): Nucleolar-Localized Adhesion Protein Interacting with Latrophilins

**Date:** 2026-03-16T16:53:03.411418

**Quality Score:** 7/10 (GOOD)

## Description
LRRN4 (Leucine-rich repeat neuronal protein 4, C20orf75) is a 740 aa putative membrane protein (UniProt Q8WUT4) containing leucine-rich repeats. Despite its 'neuronal' name, HPA data shows highest expression in Lung (9.8 nTPM) and Adipose tissue (7.8 nTPM), with subcellular localization to the Nucleoli rim and Mitotic chromosomes. STRING analysis reveals specific interactions with Adhesion GPCRs ADGRL1, ADGRL2, and ADGRL3 (Latrophilins; scores ~0.51) and membrane proteins GPM6A and MSLN. This suggests LRRN4 functions as a co-receptor or ligand for Latrophilins in non-neuronal tissues. The unexpected nucleolar localization implies a potential mechanism where the intracellular domain is cleaved and translocates to the nucleus to regulate ribosome biogenesis or cell cycle progression, paralleling signaling mechanisms seen in other adhesion receptors.
