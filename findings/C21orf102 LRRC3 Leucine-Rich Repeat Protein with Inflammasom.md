# C21orf102 (LRRC3): Leucine-Rich Repeat Protein with Inflammasome Interaction Network and Liver Expression

**Date:** 2026-03-16T17:04:05.637695

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
C21orf102 encodes LRRC3 (Leucine-Rich Repeat-Containing Protein 3, Gene ID 118423), a 257 amino acid protein (UniProt Q9BY71) characterized by multiple leucine-rich repeat (LRR) domains that typically mediate protein-protein interactions. InterPro analysis reveals an N-terminal LRR domain (IPR000372, PF01462, residues 32-63), canonical LRR repeats (IPR001611, PF00560, residues 69-136), and the LRR domain superfamily (IPR032675, residues 32-200), with structural homology to ribonuclease inhibitor fold (G3DSA:3.80.10.10). STRING interactions demonstrate high-confidence associations with inflammasome components: CASP1 (caspase-1, 0.858), PYCARD (ASC adaptor, 0.728), and NLRP3 (NOD-like receptor, 0.690), suggesting LRRC3 may function as a regulatory scaffold in innate immune signaling complexes. Additional partners include adhesion GPCRs ADGRL1/2/3 (0.551-0.581) and membrane proteins TMEM9B (0.473), consistent with HPA classification as a predicted membrane protein. HPA shows liver-enriched expression (15.1 nTPM), tissue-enhanced specificity, and plasma protein detection. ClinVar contains 89 pathogenic variants including chromosome 21 copy number alterations. The LRR domain architecture combined with inflammasome protein interactions suggests LRRC3 functions as a protein-protein interaction module that may regulate caspase-1 activation or NLRP3 inflammasome assembly, potentially linking innate immunity to metabolic processes in liver tissue.
