# CIROZ - Nuclear Speckle ZP-N Domain Protein with Left-Right Patterning Role

**Date:** 2026-03-17T09:08:09.920313

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
CIROZ (C1orf127) encodes an 823-amino acid protein (UniProt Q8N9H9) containing a defining ZP-N (zona pellucida N-terminal) Ig-like domain (PF15094/DUF4556, residues 261-365) within a larger CIROZ family region (IPR027956, residues 20-810). AlphaFold predicts a low-confidence structure (pLDDT 52.7), indicating substantial intrinsic disorder with a potentially folded ZP-N domain core.

HPA expression reveals tissue-enhanced pattern with highest expression in skeletal muscle (15.7 nTPM) and pancreas (4.2 nTPM). Subcellular localization targets nuclear speckles and cytosol, suggesting involvement in RNA splicing or transcriptional regulation. Cancer-enriched expression pattern indicates potential oncogenic role.

ClinVar contains 43 pathogenic variants including frameshifts (p.Leu798fs, p.Gln404fs), nonsense mutations (p.Arg193Ter), missense variants (p.Ser56Leu, p.Thr19Met), and large copy number losses/gains on chromosome 1p36. The 1p36 deletion syndrome region overlaps CIROZ, implicating haploinsufficiency in developmental phenotypes.

Conservation spans vertebrates: Mus musculus (786 aa), Xenopus tropicalis (480 aa), Danio rerio (432 aa), indicating functional constraint despite length variation.

FUNCTIONAL HYPOTHESIS: CIROZ functions as a nuclear speckle-associated regulator of alternative splicing during left-right axis patterning. The ZP-N domain mediates protein-protein interactions with spliceosomal components or transcription factors. Nuclear speckle localization suggests recruitment to active transcription sites, where CIROZ modulates splicing of laterality genes (NODAL, LEFTY, PITX2). Skeletal muscle and pancreas expression indicates tissue-specific splicing programs. Pathogenic variants disrupt ZP-N folding or nuclear localization, causing aberrant splicing of developmental transcripts and contributing to heterotaxy syndromes and 1p36 deletion phenotypes. The cancer-enriched expression pattern suggests oncogenic splice isoform switching in tumors.
