## Description
C20orf182 (now designated RSPO4, R-spondin-4) is a 234-amino acid secreted glycoprotein functioning as a potent Wnt signaling agonist that amplifies canonical beta-catenin pathway activity through dual mechanisms: LGR receptor engagement and membrane E3 ubiquitin ligase inhibition. Structural analysis reveals a compact domain architecture characteristic of R-spondin family proteins: two N-terminal furin-like repeats (IPR006212, SM00261, PF15913, IPR043601, residues 32-79 and 85-136) forming the LGR4/5/6 binding interface, a C-terminal thrombospondin type-1 repeat (IPR000884, PS50092, SM00209, residues 138-197) mediating heparin binding and extracellular matrix interactions, and an N-terminal signal peptide for secretion. The furin-like cysteine-rich domain (IPR051514, PTHR46987, residues 15-217) encompasses the functional core. The AlphaFold predicted structure (Q2I0M5) shows high confidence (pLDDT 78.6), confirming a well-folded secreted ligand with stable disulfide-bonded architecture typical of growth factor-like proteins. Expression profiling from the Human Protein Atlas demonstrates tissue-enhanced expression with top expression in lung (11.6 nTPM) and brain (8.2 nTPM), with RNA tissue distribution detected in some tissues, consistent with context-dependent Wnt pathway modulation, with cancer-enhanced expression suggesting oncogenic potential. Protein interaction networks (STRING) reveal striking enrichment for Wnt pathway components: LGR4 (score 0.995), LGR6 (0.982), LGR5 (0.970)—G-protein coupled receptors serving as high-affinity RSPO4 co-receptors—ZNRF3 (0.976) and RNF43 (0.973)—membrane E3 ubiquitin ligases targeting Frizzled receptors for degradation—FURIN (0.803)—proprotein convertase for RSPO4 maturation—CTNNB1 (0.680)—beta-catenin, the pathway effector—RSPO1 (0.603) and RSPO3 (0.586)—family paralogs—and WNT3 (0.561)—canonical Wnt ligand. These interactions place RSPO4 within the Wnt/beta-catenin signaling amplification module. ClinVar documents 62 pathogenic or likely pathogenic variants including nonsense mutations (p.Arg22Ter, p.Gln26fs, p.Tyr112fs), missense SNVs (p.His175Leu, p.Arg214His), 3′ UTR variants (c.*168C>A), and large copy number losses (20p13) and gains (20p13-12.1, 20p13-11.21), indicating strong dosage sensitivity and disease association, particularly with nail dysplasia and limb development defects. The mechanistic hypothesis proposes that RSPO4 functions as a bivalent ligand binding simultaneously to LGR4/5/6 receptors and ZNRF3/RNF43 E3 ligases, triggering ZNRF3/RNF43 membrane clearance and preventing Frizzled receptor ubiquitination and degradation, thereby increasing cell surface Wnt receptor density and potentiating Wnt ligand responsiveness. This amplification mechanism enables graded Wnt pathway responses critical for stem cell maintenance, tissue regeneration, and developmental patterning. Dysregulated RSPO4 expression contributes to cancer progression (colon, ovarian, breast) through constitutive Wnt pathway activation and beta-catenin stabilization. This gene exemplifies successful resolution from "C20orf" dark gene annotation to characterized Wnt pathway regulator.
