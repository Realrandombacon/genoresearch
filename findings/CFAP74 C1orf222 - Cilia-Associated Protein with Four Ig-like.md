## Description
CFAP74 (C1orf222) encodes a 1584 aa cilia- and flagella-associated protein 74 (UniProt Q9C0B2) localized to the ciliary apparatus. The protein contains four tandem immunoglobulin-like domains: first Ig-like (IPR059255/PF24770, aa 515-611), second Ig-like (IPR056306/PF24770, aa 618-800), third Ig-like (IPR056307/PF24778, aa 802-914), and fourth Ig-like (IPR056310/PF24798, aa 920-1016), suggesting a scaffold function for protein-protein interactions within the ciliary complex. AlphaFold prediction (pLDDT 69.3) indicates medium confidence across the 1584 aa length. STRING interactions reveal high-confidence partners including GABRD (0.824, GABA receptor delta subunit), TSNAXIP1 (0.615), PRKCZ (0.580), CCDC189 (0.572), LRGUK (0.564), and cilia-related proteins SPAG17 (0.514), CFAP221 (0.440), CFAP47 (0.432), placing CFAP74 within the ciliary axoneme or basal body complex. HPA shows tissue-enhanced expression with highest levels in fallopian tube (6.5 nTPM), testis (4.8 nTPM), and choroid plexus (3.6 nTPM) - all ciliated tissues. Disease involvement includes ciliopathy and primary ciliary dyskinesia (PCD). ClinVar contains 147 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including missense (p.Gly594Val, p.Arg280Ter, p.Arg818Trp), frameshift (p.Leu572fs, p.Leu975fs), and copy number variants, indicating strong dosage sensitivity. The Ig-like domain architecture suggests CFAP74 functions as a structural scaffold protein that mediates protein-protein interactions within the ciliary apparatus, potentially anchoring dynein arms or radial spoke complexes. The GABRD interaction is intriguing and may link ciliary function to GABAergic signaling in choroid plexus or neuronal cilia.
