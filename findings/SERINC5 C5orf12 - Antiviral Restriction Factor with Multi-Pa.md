# SERINC5 (C5orf12) - Antiviral Restriction Factor with Multi-Pass Transmembrane Structure and HIV Restriction Activity

## Description
SERINC5 encodes a 423aa serine incorporator protein (UniProt Q86VE9) that functions as a potent intrinsic immunity restriction factor against retroviruses. InterPro analysis reveals a conserved serine incorporator/transmembrane protein domain (IPR005016/PF03348, aa 11-415) spanning nearly the entire protein, predicting 10-12 transmembrane helices characteristic of metabolite transporters. AlphaFold structure shows high confidence folding (pLDDT 77.6) consistent with a stable multi-pass membrane architecture. STRING interaction network places SERINC5 within the antiviral restriction machinery: strongest binding to MFSD10/SERINC3 (0.968, paralog), plus connections to SAMHD1/dNTP hydrolase (0.669), APOBEC3G/cytidine deaminase (0.500), MX2/GTPase (0.476), BST2/tetherin (0.435), and TRIM5/capsid sensor (0.402). This interaction profile suggests SERINC5 operates within a coordinated antiviral complex that blocks multiple steps of retroviral replication. HPA data confirms plasma membrane and vesicle localization with broad tissue expression, consistent with constitutive innate immunity function. Biological process annotations include antiviral defense, host-virus interaction, and lipid biosynthesis - the latter suggesting serine incorporation into phosphatidylserine may alter viral envelope composition. ClinVar contains 7 pathogenic variants including splice donor mutations (c.763+1G>A) and 5q copy number alterations, indicating dosage sensitivity. Mechanistically, SERINC5 likely incorporates into budding virions via plasma membrane localization, where it disrupts viral fusion or reverse transcription through membrane perturbation or serine lipid metabolism interference. This represents a paradigm where metabolic transporters evolved antiviral effector functions.
