## Description
C8orf30B (also known as HGH1) is a dark gene located at 8q24.3 that shows strong protein-protein interactions with established ribosome biogenesis factors. STRING-DB analysis reveals 10 high-confidence interaction partners, with TTC4 showing the strongest association (score: 0.991), followed by WDR43 (0.835), RPF2 (0.813), WDR4 (0.790), and TRMT61A (0.734). Additional partners include RRS1 (0.731), NOB1 (0.720), GRWD1 (0.698), PWP1 (0.694), and RSL24D1 (0.515). These interaction partners are all components of the ribosome assembly machinery: WDR43 and WDR4 are WD40 repeat proteins involved in pre-rRNA processing; NOB1 is an endonuclease required for 18S rRNA maturation; RRS1 and RPF2 are essential for 60S subunit biogenesis; PWP1 is involved in ITS2 processing. The interaction network suggests C8orf30B functions as a ribosome biogenesis factor, likely participating in pre-ribosomal particle assembly or maturation. The high confidence scores (all >0.5, most >0.7) indicate stable physical associations rather than transient interactions. This gene represents a strong candidate for involvement in translational capacity regulation, with potential implications for cell growth and proliferation pathways.
