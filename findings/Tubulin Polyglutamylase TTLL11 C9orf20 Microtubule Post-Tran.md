# Tubulin Polyglutamylase TTLL11 (C9orf20): Microtubule Post-Translational Modifier with Brain-Specific Expression

**Date:** 2026-03-18T06:53:16.954044

**Quality Score:** 9/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
TTLL11 (C9orf20) encodes a 710 amino acid tubulin polyglutamylase that catalyzes post-translational glutamylation of alpha- and beta-tubulin, a critical modification regulating microtubule stability and function. The protein contains a conserved TTL ligase domain (IPR004344, PF03133, residues 128-480) with ATP-grasp fold (G3DSA:3.30.470.20) for nucleotide binding, and a C-terminal EF-hand calcium-binding domain (IPR060450, PF27858, residues 533-654) suggesting calcium-dependent regulation. HPA expression shows brain-enriched transcription (27.8 nTPM) with tissue-enhanced specificity, indicating specialized roles in neuronal microtubule dynamics. STRING interactions reveal functional clustering with other TTLL family members (TTLL12: 0.484, TTLL5: 0.483, TTLL1: 0.482) and SPAST (0.481), a microtubule-severing enzyme mutated in hereditary spastic paraplegia, suggesting coordinated regulation of microtubule turnover. Additional partners include ZSCAN20 (0.558) and DAB2IP (0.410), a Ras GTPase-activating protein. ClinVar documents 28 pathogenic variants, predominantly chromosome 9q copy number alterations, implicating TTLL11 dosage in neurodevelopmental disorders. AlphaFold structure (pLDDT 76.8) shows high confidence with well-folded catalytic and regulatory domains. I hypothesize TTLL11 functions as a neuronal microtubule affinity tuner: (1) the TTL domain adds polyglutamate chains to tubulin C-terminal tails, increasing negative charge and modulating motor protein (kinesin/dynein) binding affinity, (2) the EF-hand domain confers calcium sensitivity, linking microtubule modification to neuronal activity. Dysregulation causes aberrant axonal transport and synaptic plasticity, contributing to neurodevelopmental phenotypes observed in 9q copy number variants.
