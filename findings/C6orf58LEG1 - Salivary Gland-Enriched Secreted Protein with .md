## Description
C6orf58 (LEG1, Q6P5S2) is a 330 aa secreted developmental protein with extraordinary salivary gland enrichment (2318.7 nTPM - among highest expression levels observed). Contains conserved Leg1 family domain (IPR008499, residues 1-314) present across vertebrates (58.6% mouse identity) and even Dictyostelium (33.2%), indicating ancient evolutionary origin. STRING reveals high-confidence interaction with BPIFB1 (0.734) and medium-confidence interactions with BPIFA2, BPIFB2, CA6, PRR4 - all salivary/secretory proteins involved in innate immunity and antimicrobial defense. The BPI (bactericidal permeability increasing) family interaction network strongly suggests LEG1 functions in salivary innate immunity, potentially as an antimicrobial peptide or modulator of oral microbiome. UniProt notes "may be involved in early liver development" but HPA data indicates primary salivary function. This represents a partially characterized dark gene - domain and expression pattern reveal likely function, but precise molecular mechanism remains unknown.
