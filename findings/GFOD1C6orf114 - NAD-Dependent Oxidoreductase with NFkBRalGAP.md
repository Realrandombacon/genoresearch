## Description
GFOD1 (Glucose-Fructose Oxidoreductase Domain-containing protein 1, C6orf114/ADG-90) encodes a 390aa well-folded enzyme (UniProt Q9NXC2, AlphaFold pLDDT 92.7) belonging to the Gfo/Idh/MocA oxidoreductase family (IPR000683, IPR050463, PF01408/PF22725). The protein contains canonical NAD(P)-binding Rossmann fold (aa 4-148, IPR036291) and C-terminal catalytic domain (aa 128-258), characteristic of sugar oxidoreductases that catalyze NAD-dependent oxidation-reduction reactions. HPA reveals ubiquitous expression with blood cell enrichment and dual intracellular/secreted topology (nucleoplasm, plasma membrane, cytosol). STRING identifies 10 high-confidence interaction partners including NKIRAS2 (NFkB inhibitor-associated protein, score 0.784), RALGAPA2/RALGAPA1 (Ral GTPase activating proteins, 0.727/0.646), TGDS (TDP-glucose 4-epimerase, 0.724), and GFOD2 paralog (0.625). ClinVar documents 25 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly large copy number gains/losses spanning 6p25.3-22.3, indicating extreme dosage sensitivity. The NKIRAS2 interaction suggests coupling between cellular redox metabolism and NFkB inflammatory signaling, while RALGAPA links implicate small GTPase regulation. The blood cell enrichment combined with oxidoreductase function supports a hypothesis that GFOD1 functions as a metabolic sensor that modulates immune cell activation through NAD+/NADH ratio-dependent regulation of NFkB and Ral signaling pathways. Pathogenic CNVs likely disrupt redox homeostasis and immune signaling balance, contributing to hematologic and inflammatory disease phenotypes. The paralog GFOD2 interaction suggests functional redundancy or heterodimerization.
