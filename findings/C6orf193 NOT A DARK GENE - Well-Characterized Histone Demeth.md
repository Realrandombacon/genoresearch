## Description
C6orf193 (Gene ID: 254751), historically named "chromosome 6 open reading frame 193", is actually a WELL-CHARACTERIZED histone demethylase now officially named KDM1B (Lysine Demethylase 1B), also known as LSD2 (Lysine-Specific Demethylase 2). This represents a critical finding: C6orf193 is NOT a dark gene but rather a fully annotated epigenetic enzyme with established function in histone modification and transcriptional regulation. UniProt accession Q8NB78 encodes an 822-amino acid nuclear protein. InterPro domains confirm extensive functional annotation: IPR007526 (SWIRM domain, residues 275-373, protein binding GO:0005515, chromatin binding), IPR002937 (Amine oxidase, 393-819, oxidoreductase activity GO:0016491, FAD-dependent demethylation), IPR011124 (Zinc finger CW-type, 133-193, zinc ion binding GO:0008270, chromatin reader), IPR009057 (Homeodomain-like superfamily, 272-378), IPR036188 (FAD/NAD(P)-binding domain superfamily, 382-821), G3DSA:1.10.10.10 (Winged helix DNA-binding domain, 276-373). The SWIRM+amine oxidase+C2H2 zinc finger architecture is characteristic of flavin-dependent histone demethylases that remove methyl groups from lysine residues. AlphaFold structure prediction shows global pLDDT score of 91.2 (very high confidence), indicating well-folded stable protein with defined catalytic and regulatory domains, consistent with structured chromatin enzyme architecture. STRING interactions reveal 10 high-confidence protein partners within chromatin regulation complex: BAP1 (0.960, BRCA1-associated protein 1), ASXL2 (0.953, additional sex combs-like 2), GLYR1 (0.947, glyoxylate reductase 1), FOXK1 (0.911, forkhead box K1), ASXL1 (0.909, additional sex combs-like 1), NSD3 (0.894, nuclear receptor binding SET domain 3), OGT (0.885, O-GlcNAc transferase), KDM4A (0.878, lysine demethylase 4A), HCFC1 (0.877, host cell factor C1), RCOR1 (0.623, REST corepressor 1). These interactions place KDM1B within Polycomb/Trithorax chromatin remodeling complexes. Human Protein Atlas (ENSG00000165097) classifies KDM1B as enzyme/chromatin regulator/developmental protein/oxidoreductase/repressor, predicted intracellular protein with nucleoplasm localization, low tissue specificity (detected in all tissues), low cancer specificity, cell type enhanced single-cell specificity. ClinVar contains 14 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, all copy number alterations affecting 6p22.3-25.3 region (multiple deletions and duplications spanning 6p22.3-25.3, including chr6:12020008-22846454x1, chr6:820000-21700000x3, chr6:12005630-22849647x1, chr6:13910125-22000204x1), indicating strong association with 6p chromosomal instability syndromes and potential developmental/epigenetic phenotypes. Functional role: KDM1B encodes a FAD-dependent amine oxidase that catalyzes demethylation of mono- and di-methylated lysine residues on histone H3 (particularly H3K4me1/2 and H3K9me1/2), regulating chromatin accessibility and gene expression patterns during development and differentiation. Mechanistic proposal: KDM1B functions through flavin adenine dinucleotide (FAD)-dependent oxidative demethylation - the amine oxidase domain uses FAD cofactor to oxidize methylated lysine residues, generating formaldehyde and demethylated lysine. The SWIRM domain mediates protein-protein interactions with nucleosome remodeling complexes, while the CW-type zinc finger recognizes specific histone marks (H3K4me3) to recruit KDM1B to target chromatin loci. KDM1B differs from KDM1A/LSD1 in substrate specificity and complex association (KDM1B associates with de novo DNA methyltransferases rather than REST/CoREST). Loss-of-function KDM1B variants may cause developmental defects through disrupted histone methylation patterns and aberrant gene silencing/activation. This gene should be SKIPPED from dark gene analysis - it represents historical nomenclature (C#orf#) superseded by functional annotation (KDM1B/LSD2).
