## Description
SLC39A11 encodes a 342 amino acid zinc transporter ZIP11 (UniProt Q8N1S5) belonging to the SLC39/ZIP family of metal ion permeases. InterPro analysis identifies a conserved ZIP zinc transporter domain (PF02535, IPR003689, residues 26-337) with characteristic metal ion transmembrane transporter activity (GO:0046873). AlphaFold predicts a confident folded structure (pLDDT 76.5) spanning all 342 residues. STRING interactions reveal 10 high-confidence partners from the same transporter families: SLC39A12 (0.781), SLC39A10 (0.773), SLC39A1 (0.763), SLC30A7 (0.686), and SLC30A9 (0.612), indicating functional clustering within zinc homeostasis networks. HPA shows ubiquitous expression across all tissues with nucleoplasmic subcellular localization, suggesting intracellular zinc regulation rather than plasma membrane transport. ClinVar documents 16 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including copy number gains/losses at 17q24-25, implicating SLC39A11 in genomic instability disorders. Conservation across vertebrates (ZIP family orthologs in mouse/zebrafish) indicates functional constraint. Mechanistically, SLC39A11 likely functions as an intracellular zinc mobilizer, transporting zinc from nucleoplasmic stores to zinc-requiring nuclear enzymes (transcription factors, DNA repair proteins, chromatin modifiers). The nucleoplasmic localization distinguishes it from plasma membrane ZIP transporters, suggesting a specialized role in nuclear zinc homeostasis affecting epigenetic regulation and genome stability.
