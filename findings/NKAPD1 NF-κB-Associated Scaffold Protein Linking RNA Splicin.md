## Description
NKAPD1 (292 aa, UniProt Q6ZUT1) is a cytosolic scaffold protein with an N-terminal NKAP domain (IPR043407, PF15692, residues 88-163) homologous to NF-kappa-B-activating proteins. AlphaFold predicts a medium-confidence structure (pLDDT 61.7) suggesting intrinsically disordered regions flanking the structured NKAP domain, consistent with scaffold protein architecture. HPA shows ubiquitous expression across all tissues with low specificity (cytosol localization). STRING interactions reveal connections to splicing machinery (RP9 score 0.527, SYF2 0.482, DHX8 0.509), casein kinase 2 subunits (CSNK2A1 0.615, CSNK2A2 0.505), mitochondrial import chaperone TIMM8B (0.589), and RNA-binding proteins PNN (0.564), SREK1IP1 (0.502). ClinVar contains 18 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including deletions and duplications, though no specific disease phenotype is yet established. The interaction network suggests NKAPD1 functions as a molecular bridge between NF-κB signaling pathways and pre-mRNA splicing complexes, potentially modulating alternative splicing in response to inflammatory signals. The disordered regions may enable conformational flexibility for binding multiple partners. Conservation data needed for mouse/zebrafish orthologs.
