## Description
C11orf21 (Q9P2W6) is a 132 aa uncharacterized dark gene encoding a predicted intracellular protein localized to nucleoplasm and cytosol. Contains defining DUF4620 domain of unknown function (aa 20-132, IPR027894, PF15399) covering ~85% of the protein sequence, establishing its core architecture. BLAST shows human-specific sequence with no significant cross-species hits, suggesting potential lineage-specific function or rapid evolution. STRING interactions reveal an olfactory receptor/zinc finger network: OR2L13 (0.583, olfactory receptor family GPCR), ZNF888 (0.543, C2H2 zinc finger transcription factor), ACOXL (0.447, acyl-CoA oxidase-like protein, peroxisomal fatty acid oxidation), TSPAN32 (0.402, tetraspanin membrane organizer). HPA confirms nucleoplasm/cytosol localization with striking bone marrow enrichment (47.6 nTPM top tissue), heart muscle (19.4 nTPM), and lymphoid tissue (15.0 nTPM) — group enriched expression pattern with cell type enhanced single cell specificity, indicating hematopoietic and cardiac functional relevance. ClinVar contains 32 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants dominated by large 11p15.5 copy number gains (11p15.5-15.4, 11p15.5-14.2) and duplications, confirming strong disease relevance in 11p15.5 imprinting disorders (Beckwith-Wiedemann syndrome, Silver-Russell syndrome region). True dark gene: conserved DUF4620 architecture, bone marrow-enriched expression (hematopoietic signature), olfactory receptor/zinc finger interaction network (OR2L13/ZNF888), cardiac/lymphoid co-expression, clinically implicated with massive 11p15.5 CNV burden in imprinting disorder locus, but molecular mechanism completely unknown. Hypothesis: may function as a nucleoplasmic chromatin regulator or transcriptional co-factor that coordinates hematopoietic differentiation (bone marrow enrichment), cardiac development (heart muscle expression), and epigenetic regulation in the 11p15.5 imprinting control region, using DUF4620 domain for DNA/RNA binding or protein scaffolding, with olfactory receptor interaction (OR2L13) suggesting potential GPCR signaling crosstalk and zinc finger partnership (ZNF888) indicating transcriptional regulation role, potentially serving as imprinting control factor with dysregulation contributing to Beckwith-Wiedemann/Silver-Russell spectrum disorders.
