# NUBPL (C14orf127) - Mitochondrial Iron-Sulfur Cluster Assembly Factor for Complex I Biogenesis

## Description
NUBPL (formerly C14orf127, also known as IND1) encodes a 319 amino acid mitochondrial iron-sulfur cluster transfer protein essential for respiratory chain Complex I assembly. InterPro analysis reveals multiple conserved domains including Mrp/NBP35 ATP-binding protein domain (IPR019591, PF10609, residues 61-311), P-loop NTPase fold (G3DSA:3.40.50.300, residues 62-316), and iron-sulfur cluster carrier protein signature (IPR000808, residues 174-190). AlphaFold structure shows high confidence (pLDDT 84.8), indicating a well-folded enzyme with nucleotide-binding capability. HPA data shows ubiquitous expression across all tissues with low tissue specificity, localized to mitochondria, classified as disease-related with primary mitochondrial disease involvement. STRING interactions identify 10 high-confidence partners all related to Complex I biogenesis: NDUFS2 (0.902), NDUFS8 (0.898), NDUFS3 (0.885), NDUFAF2 (0.883), NDUFV1 (0.868), NDUFS1 (0.866), NDUFV2 (0.864), FOXRED1 (0.859), NDUFS7 (0.857), and NDUFAF4 (0.851), demonstrating exclusive functional connection to NADH dehydrogenase assembly. ClinVar contains 75 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including nonsense mutations (p.Gln237Ter), frameshifts (p.Ala89fs, p.Glu150fs), missense (p.Pro231Leu, p.Arg16Pro), and copy number variations, causing mitochondrial complex I deficiency (MC1DN21). This gene represents a transition from 'dark' to well-characterized status - originally annotated as chromosome 14 ORF, now recognized as a critical mitochondrial chaperone with established disease mechanism.
