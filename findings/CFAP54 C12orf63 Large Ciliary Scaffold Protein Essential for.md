## Description
CFAP54 (C12orf63) encodes a 3096 amino acid cilia- and flagella-associated protein (UniProt Q96N23) that functions as a large molecular scaffold in ciliary assembly. InterPro analysis identifies the PF14858/CFAP54 domain (IPR027912) spanning residues 99-638, characteristic of the ciliary protein family. STRING interaction network reveals high-confidence partnerships with TMEM212 (0.980), WDPCP (0.775), CFAP221 (0.711), and SPEF2 (0.657), placing CFAP54 within the ciliopathy interactome alongside basal body and microtubule organizing center components. HPA expression profiling shows tissue-enhanced RNA expression with highest levels in choroid plexus (6.5 nTPM) and retina (5.6 nTPM), consistent with cilia-rich tissues. Subcellular localization includes microtubules, cytokinetic bridge, primary cilium, and basal body. ClinVar documents 19 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including frameshift mutations (p.Leu2882fs, p.Phe1571fs) and missense changes, indicating disease relevance though specific phenotypes remain under investigation. The protein''s massive size (3096 aa) and interaction profile suggest a scaffolding role in intraflagellar transport or ciliary membrane protein organization, potentially coordinating assembly of axonemal microtubule doublets during ciliogenesis.
