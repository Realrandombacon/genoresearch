# WDR90: Centriolar WD40 Scaffold Maintaining Microtubule Wall Integrity via EFHB/PACRG Interactions

**Date:** 2026-03-18T01:43:42.556432

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
WDR90 (C16orf15) encodes a massive 1748 aa intracellular protein essential for centriole core architecture and microtubule stability. InterPro analysis reveals a complex domain organization: an N-terminal CFA20 domain (IPR007714, residues 2-185) followed by four distinct WD40/YVTN beta-propeller repeats (IPR001680, IPR015943) spanning residues 367-870, and a large C-terminal region with quinoprotein alcohol dehydrogenase-like fold (IPR011047). Specific InterPro entries identify the 2nd (IPR055441, 707-997) and 4th (IPR055440, 1437-1745) beta-propeller domains unique to WDR90/POC16 family. AlphaFold predicts a confident global structure (pLDDT 77.0), consistent with a rigid scaffold. STRING interactions highlight a tight functional module with EFHB (0.944), EFHC1 (0.909), PACRG (0.902), PACRGL (0.900), and POC5 (0.870), all known centriolar or ciliary proteins, suggesting WDR90 acts as a central hub in the "inner core" complex. HPA data shows ubiquitous expression with strong testis enrichment (59.4 nTPM), aligning with roles in spermatogenesis and ciliogenesis, and localizes to centrosomes/basal bodies. ClinVar lists 51 pathogenic variants (mostly 16p13.3 CNVs and splice sites), indicating dosage sensitivity likely linked to ciliopathy or microcephaly phenotypes. Mechanistically, WDR90 likely serves as a structural strut within the centriole lumen, using its multiple beta-propellers to recruit EFHB/PACRG complexes that polymerize or stabilize the inner microtubule wall, preventing centriole disintegration during cell division.
