# MCM9 DNA Helicase: Homologous Recombination Repair Factor with MCM8 Partnership

**Date:** 2026-03-18T06:03:45.268935

**Quality Score:** 8/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
C6orf61 (MCM9) encodes a 1143 amino acid DNA helicase (UniProt Q9NXL9) belonging to the minichromosome maintenance (MCM) protein family. InterPro analysis reveals canonical MCM domains including IPR001208 (MCM domain, aa 302-508), IPR003593 (AAA+ ATPase, aa 344-493), PF00493 (MCM P-loop domain), and IPR033762 (MCM OB domain). These domains confer ATP-dependent DNA helicase activity essential for DNA unwinding during replication and repair. STRING interaction data shows MCM9 forms a tight complex with MCM8 (score 0.999) and associates with the replicative MCM2-7 helicase complex (MCM3-7 scores 0.958-0.998), plus replication licensing factors CDC6 (0.895) and CDT1 (0.874). HPA expression shows ubiquitous tissue distribution with nucleoplasmic localization, consistent with DNA repair function. ClinVar contains 41 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including nonsense mutations (p.Gln252Ter, p.Trp519Ter) and frameshifts (p.Leu47fs), indicating disease relevance. AlphaFold structure (pLDDT 61.9) shows medium confidence with structured catalytic core and potentially disordered regulatory regions. MCM9 heterodimerizes with MCM8 to promote homologous recombination repair of DNA double-strand breaks, recruiting RAD51 recombinase to damage sites. Biallelic MCM9 mutations cause primordial ovarian failure and genomic instability syndromes.
