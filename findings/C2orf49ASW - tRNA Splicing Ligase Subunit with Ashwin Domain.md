## Description
C2orf49 encodes ASW (Ashwin), a 232 amino acid protein (UniProt Q9BVC5) that functions as a subunit of the tRNA-splicing ligase complex. The protein contains a conserved Ashwin domain (IPR024887, residues 1-232) and PF15323 developmental protein family domain (residues 13-232). AlphaFold structure prediction shows medium confidence (pLDDT 65.3), suggesting a partially folded structure with possible intrinsically disordered regions. STRING interaction analysis reveals high-confidence protein partners involved in tRNA processing: FAM98B (0.998), RTRAF (0.997), DDX1 (0.993), ZBTB8OS (0.948), and RTCB (0.590). These interactions place ASW within the tRNA splicing machinery, where it likely facilitates endonucleolytic cleavage and ligation of intron-containing tRNAs. HPA data shows ubiquitous expression across all tissues with nucleoplasmic localization, consistent with its role in nuclear tRNA processing. ClinVar contains 24 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, though no specific disease associations are yet established. The conservation of the Ashwin domain across vertebrates (Xenopus laevis ortholog Q9I8G4, 226 aa, 97% identity) suggests essential function. Mechanistic hypothesis: ASW acts as a scaffold protein that recruits tRNA substrates to the ligase complex, potentially coordinating the sequential cleavage and ligation steps through protein-protein interactions with FAM98B and RTRAF. Disease variants may disrupt tRNA splicing fidelity, leading to translational defects.
