## Description

This domain architecture places ZBTB12 within the Krüppel-associated box (KRAB) zinc finger protein superfamily, suggesting a role as a sequence-specific DNA-binding transcriptional regulator. The BTB/POZ domain mediates protein-protein interactions and often recruits corepressor complexes including NCOR, SMRT, and histone deacetylases. STRING interaction network reveals 10 high-confidence partners: ABHD16A (score 0.829) shows strongest association, followed by PRRT1 (0.544), PRRC2A (0.493), and immune-related proteins LTA (0.459), HLA-B (0.455), HLA-C (0.426). The chromosomal location at 6p21.3 places ZBTB12 within the MHC class I/III region, adjacent to immune response genes. This genomic context combined with HLA-B/C interactions suggests ZBTB12 may regulate MHC expression or antigen presentation pathways. The three tandem zinc fingers likely recognize GC-rich promoter sequences similar to other ZBTB family members. No HPA expression data available, indicating potential low/conditional expression or antibody unavailability. The protein lacks transmembrane domains, consistent with nuclear localization typical of BTB-ZF transcription factors.
