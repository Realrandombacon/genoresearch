# C10orf67 - Mitochondrial Dark Gene with DUF4709/DUF4724 Dual Domain Architecture, Cilia/Centrosome Interaction Network, and Testis-Enriched Expression

**Quality Score:** 9/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
C10orf67 (Q8IYJ2) is a 551 aa uncharacterized dark gene encoding a predicted mitochondrial protein with dual domain of unknown function architecture. Contains three defining features: DUF4709 domain (aa 70-179, IPR031651, PF15821) as N-terminal functional module, DUF4724 domain (aa 458-546, IPR031711, PF15852) as C-terminal functional module, and C10orf67-like family (aa 34-545, IPR040119, PTHR22382) covering ~93% of the protein. BLAST shows human-specific sequence with no significant cross-species hits in local database, suggesting potential rapid evolution or lineage-specific function. STRING interactions reveal a cilia/centrosome network: CFAP100 (0.610, cilia and flagella-associated protein), C2orf81 (0.602, uncharacterized dark gene), CEP72 (0.594, centrosome protein required for microtubule nucleation), CCDC38 (0.475, coiled-coil centrosome protein), ARMC3 (0.420, armadillo repeat protein), ANXA11 (0.400, annexin family membrane trafficking protein). HPA confirms vesicles/microtubules/mitochondria localization with testis-enriched expression (11.6 nTPM top tissue), choroid plexus (5.4 nTPM), fallopian tube (4.6 nTPM), immune cell enhanced blood cell specificity, and cell type enhanced single cell specificity — the testis enrichment combined with cilia/centrosome interactors strongly suggests role in spermatogenesis and ciliogenesis. ClinVar contains 11 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including large 10p copy number gains (10p15.3-q26.3, 10p15.1-11.21) and losses (10p13-q26.3), confirming disease relevance in 10p deletion/duplication syndromes. True dark gene: conserved DUF4709/DUF4724 dual domain architecture, mitochondrial/vesicle/microtubule localization, cilia/centrosome interaction network (CFAP100/CEP72/CCDC38), testis-enriched expression pattern, clinically implicated with 10p CNV burden, but molecular mechanism completely unknown. Hypothesis: may function as a mitochondrial outer membrane or vesicle-associated scaffolding protein that coordinates cilia assembly (via CFAP100), centrosome-dependent microtubule nucleation (via CEP72/CCDC38), and membrane trafficking (via ANXA11), using DUF4709/DUF4724 domains for protein-protein interaction or lipid binding, potentially serving as mitochondria-cilia metabolic coupling factor critical for spermatogenesis with dysregulation contributing to 10p deletion syndromes with ciliary/renal phenotypes.
