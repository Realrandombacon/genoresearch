# TTLL9: Tubulin Polyglutamylase with ATP-Grasp Fold Enriched in Choroid Plexus and Ciliary Structures

**Date:** 2026-03-18T03:57:17.520233

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
TTLL9 (C20orf125) is a 439-amino acid intracellular enzyme functioning as a probable tubulin polyglutamylase that catalyzes post-translational glutamylation of tubulin. Structural analysis reveals a conserved tubulin-tyrosine ligase domain (IPR004344, PF03133, residues 40-380) with an ATP-grasp fold (G3DSA:3.30.470.20, residues 190-381) characteristic of ligase enzymes that use ATP to form peptide bonds. The AlphaFold predicted structure (Q3SXZ7) shows high confidence (pLDDT 78.6), indicating a well-folded catalytic core. Expression profiling from the Human Protein Atlas demonstrates tissue-enriched expression in choroid plexus (14.6 nTPM) with subcellular localization to nucleoplasm, microtubules, mitotic spindle, primary cilium, and basal body—structures critical for cell division and ciliary function. Protein interaction networks (STRING) identify TTLL12 (score 0.677) as the top partner, another tubulin polyglutamylase family member, alongside ciliary and centrosomal proteins including RSPH1 (0.630), CCDC175 (0.596), CEP72 (0.594), and DNAH1 (0.564), suggesting TTLL9 functions in multi-protein complexes regulating microtubule stability and ciliary assembly. ClinVar documents 16 pathogenic or likely pathogenic variants, predominantly large copy number gains on chromosome 20q11, indicating dosage sensitivity and potential disease association. The mechanistic hypothesis proposes that TTLL9 adds polyglutamate chains to tubulin C-terminal tails, modulating microtubule-associated protein binding affinity and regulating ciliary dynamics, mitotic spindle positioning, and intracellular transport—processes essential for neurodevelopment given choroid plexus enrichment.
