## Description
TCP10L (T-complex 10 like), formerly C21orf77, encodes a 215 amino acid protein (UniProt Q8TDR4) belonging to the TCP10/CENPJ family (IPR026581, PTHR10331, aa 27-209) - homologs of the centrosomal protein CENPJ/CPAP. AlphaFold predicts medium-confidence structure (pLDDT 66.2) consistent with partially ordered functional domains. HPA reveals striking testis-enriched expression at 142.6 nTPM with cancer-enriched patterns, annotated as a transcriptional repressor and tumor suppressor. Subcellular localization is predicted intracellular. STRING analysis identifies 10 protein interactions enriched for centriole/centrosome machinery: STIL (0.631) - a centriole assembly factor mutated in microcephaly, SASS6 (0.478) - a cartwheel protein essential for centriole symmetry, CEP295 (0.482) - a centriole distal appendage protein, C12orf4/MBD3 (0.625) - chromatin remodeling, TBCK (0.593) - mTOR pathway regulator, and DAPK3 (0.552) - death-associated kinase. This interaction network strongly implicates TCP10L in centriole biogenesis and centrosome regulation. The TCP10L-CENPJ homology suggests functional parallelism with CPAP, which controls centriole elongation and microtubule anchoring. Testis enrichment (142.6 nTPM) aligns with spermatogenesis requirements for flagellar axoneme formation. The tumor suppressor annotation combined with STIL/DAPK3 interactions suggests TCP10L may coordinate cell cycle progression with centrosome duplication - dysregulation could drive aneuploidy in cancer. ClinVar reports 56 pathogenic chromosome 21 CNVs (duplications/deletions), implicating TCP10L dosage sensitivity in chromosome 21 aneuploidy phenotypes. As a centrosome-associated transcriptional repressor, TCP10L may couple centrosome status to cell cycle gene expression, functioning as a checkpoint regulator that prevents centrosome amplification and chromosomal instability.
