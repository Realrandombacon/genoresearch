# VMA12 (C17orf32/TMEM199): ER-Localized V-ATPase Assembly Chaperone with Congenital Glycosylation Disorder Association

**Date:** 2026-03-18T02:58:30.039044

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
VMA12 (formerly C17orf32, also known as TMEM199) encodes a 208 aa vacuolar ATPase assembly protein (UniProt Q8N511) belonging to the VMA12/Ecm30 family (IPR021013, PF11712). The protein contains an ER-based V-ATPase assembly domain (residues 78-202) with GO annotation for vacuolar proton-transporting V-type ATPase complex assembly (GO:0070072). AlphaFold predicts a well-folded structure (pLDDT 80.4) across the full-length protein (1-208 aa). HPA classifies VMA12 as an essential protein with ubiquitous tissue expression, associated with congenital disorder of glycosylation. STRING reveals extremely high-confidence interactions with V-ATPase subunits: ATP6V0D1 (0.987), ATP6V0D2 (0.971), ATP6V1F (0.962), ATP6AP2 (0.961), ATP6V1E1 (0.960), ATP6V0A2 (0.960), ATP6V0A1 (0.955), ATP6V0C (0.953), ATP6V1A (0.952), and assembly factor CCDC115 (0.853). ClinVar contains 16 pathogenic variants including frameshift mutations (p.Glu69fs, p.Leu5fs) and missense variants (p.Ala7Val, p.Ala37Val, p.Ala7Thr). Orthologs exist in rainbow trout (199 aa), indicating vertebrate conservation. FUNCTIONAL HYPOTHESIS: VMA12 functions as an ER membrane chaperone facilitating V-ATPase holoenzyme assembly by coordinating proteolipid ring and catalytic sector integration. MECHANISTIC PROPOSAL: VMA12 localizes to ER membrane via C-terminal transmembrane region, recruiting V-ATPase subunits (ATP6V0D1/D2, ATP6V1A/E1/F) for coordinated assembly. Interaction with CCDC115 suggests parallel assembly pathway coordination. Pathogenic variants disrupt V-ATPase assembly, impairing organelle acidification and protein glycosylation, causing congenital disorder of glycosylation. NOTE: This gene is NOT truly "dark" - it is well-studied as essential V-ATPase assembly factor.
