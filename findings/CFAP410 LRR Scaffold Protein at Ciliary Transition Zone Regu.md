# CFAP410: LRR Scaffold Protein at Ciliary Transition Zone Regulating NEK1-Mediated Ciliogenesis and DNA Damage Response

**Date:** 2026-03-18T01:25:46.743894

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
CFAP410 (alias C21orf2, LRRC76) encodes a 256 amino acid cilia- and flagella-associated protein 410 (UniProt O43822) containing leucine-rich repeats (IPR001611, residues 41-62, 63-84), ribonuclease inhibitor superfamily fold (G3DSA:3.80.10.10, residues 8-136), U2A/phosphoprotein 32 C-terminal domain (IPR003603, residues 104-122), and belongs to the tilB family (PTHR18849, residues 2-254). AlphaFold predicts a well-folded structure with confident score (pLDDT 77.6). HPA data shows localization to Golgi apparatus, primary cilium transition zone, basal body, and cytosol with ubiquitous expression across tissues. STRING interactions reveal strong association with NEK1 kinase (0.982), antiviral protein MX1 (0.773), mitochondrial proteins CHCHD10 (0.631), ALS2 (0.614), frataxin FXN (0.597), spermatogenesis protein SPATA7 (0.581), nuclear matrix protein MATR3 (0.570), vitronectin VTN (0.568), and glycolytic enzyme PFKL (0.456). ClinVar reports 158 pathogenic variants including copy number gains/losses, frameshift deletions (c.171_178del p.Ser59fs, c.621del p.Ser208fs), nonsense mutations (c.278G>A p.Trp93Ter, c.77G>A p.Trp26Ter), and splice site variants (c.97-1G>A, c.78-1G>T). The gene is conserved across vertebrates: mouse (Q8C6G1, 249 aa, ~90% identity).
