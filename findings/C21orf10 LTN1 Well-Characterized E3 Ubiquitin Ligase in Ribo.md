# C21orf10 (LTN1): Well-Characterized E3 Ubiquitin Ligase in Ribosome Quality Control - Not a True Dark Gene

**Date:** 2026-03-16T17:02:31.912605

**Quality Score:** 8/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
C21orf10 is the historical symbol for LTN1 (Listerin E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase 1, Gene ID 89753), a well-characterized 1766 amino acid RING-type E3 ubiquitin ligase (UniProt O94822) with essential function in ribosome quality control (RQC). InterPro analysis reveals a C-terminal RING-CH zinc finger domain (IPR001841, IPR011016, residues 1713-1762) conferring E3 ligase activity, plus N-terminal armadillo-like helical folds (IPR011989, IPR016024, residues 64-452) for ribosome binding, and the defining LTN1 family signature (IPR039795, residues 2-1765) with GO annotations for ubiquitin protein ligase activity and ribosome-associated ubiquitin-dependent protein catabolic process. STRING interactions demonstrate high-confidence binding to large ribosomal subunit proteins RPL11 (0.916), RPL23A (0.863), RPL6 (0.861), RPL31 (0.848), the RQC complex E3 ligase ZNF598 (0.858), and RQC factor NEMF (0.806), placing LTN1 directly in the ribosome quality control pathway that ubiquitinates nascent chains on stalled ribosomes for proteasomal degradation. HPA localizes LTN1 to cytosol, classifies it as an enzyme in the Ubl conjugation pathway, and shows ubiquitous expression across all tissues. ClinVar contains 57 pathogenic variants including multiple copy number gains on chromosome 21, confirming clinical significance. The presence of defined catalytic RING domain, established RQC interaction network with ribosomal proteins and NEMF, ubiquitous expression, and pathogenic variant burden indicates C21orf10/LTN1 is NOT a dark gene but a functionally annotated E3 ubiquitin ligase with known mechanism in co-translational protein quality control.
