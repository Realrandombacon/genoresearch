## Description
C6orf131 (Gene ID: 387091), historically named "chromosome 6 open reading frame 131", is actually a WELL-CHARACTERIZED retinal calcium-binding protein now officially named GUCA1A (Guanylate Cyclase Activator 1A), also known as Guanylyl Cyclase-Activating Protein 1 (GCAP1). This represents a critical finding: C6orf131 is NOT a dark gene but rather a fully annotated phototransduction regulator with established function in visual signal recovery. UniProt accession P43080 encodes a 201-amino acid myristoylated calcium sensor protein. InterPro domains confirm functional annotation: IPR002048 (EF-hand domain, residues 51-166, calcium ion binding GO:0005509), IPR011992 (EF-hand domain pair, 12-171), IPR018247 (EF-Hand 1 calcium-binding site, 64-76/100-112/144-156), IPR028846 (Recoverin family, 13-168, calcium ion binding), PF00036 (EF hand domain, 56-81), PF13499 (EF-hand domain pair, 89-160), PTHR23055 (Calcium binding proteins, 13-168). The triple EF-hand architecture is characteristic of neuronal calcium sensor (NCS) family proteins that undergo calcium-dependent conformational changes. AlphaFold structure prediction shows global pLDDT score of 70.4 (high confidence), indicating well-folded stable protein with defined EF-hand calcium-binding folds, consistent with structured calcium sensor architecture. STRING interactions reveal 10 high-confidence protein partners within phototransduction cascade: GUCY2D (0.999, retinal guanylyl cyclase 1), GUCY2F (0.982, retinal guanylyl cyclase 2), GUCA1B (0.944, GCAP2 paralog), GUCA1C (0.909, GCAP3 paralog), RD3 (0.836, retinal degeneration 3 protein), GUF1 (0.833, G U-factor 1), CABP1 (0.793, calcium binding protein 1), AIPL1 (0.773, aryl hydrocarbon receptor interacting protein-like 1), CNGA1 (0.758, cyclic nucleotide gated channel alpha 1), CNGB1 (0.709, cyclic nucleotide gated channel beta 1). These interactions place GUCA1A within the photoreceptor outer segment cGMP synthesis/regulation module. Human Protein Atlas (ENSG00000048545) classifies GUCA1A as disease-related gene, predicted intracellular protein with plasma membrane localization, tissue enriched RNA specificity (retina 703.2 nTPM - extremely high retina-specific expression), detected in some tissues, cancer enriched expression, and cone-rod dystrophy disease involvement. ClinVar contains 57 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including missense mutations (c.300T>G p.Asp100Glu, c.332A>G p.Glu111Gly, c.305A>G p.Asp102Gly, c.527T>C p.Leu176Pro, c.431A>C p.Asp144Ala, c.430G>A p.Asp144Asn, c.442G>C p.Asp148His), indels (c.428delinsCACA p.Ile143delinsThrHis), frameshifts (c.554del p.Asn185fs), and large deletions (6p12.1-12.3), indicating strong association with autosomal dominant cone-rod dystrophy and retinal degeneration. Functional role: GUCA1A encodes a photoreceptor-specific calcium sensor that regulates retinal guanylyl cyclase (GUCY2D/GUCY2F) activity in response to intracellular calcium concentration changes during light adaptation. Mechanistic proposal: GUCA1A functions through calcium-dependent conformational switching - at low calcium (dark state), myristoylated GUCA1A activates guanylyl cyclase to synthesize cGMP, maintaining cyclic nucleotide-gated (CNG) channels open; at high calcium (light state), calcium binding to EF-hands induces conformational change that inhibits guanylyl cyclase, reducing cGMP and closing CNG channels. This calcium feedback loop enables photoreceptor light adaptation and recovery. GUCA1A works redundantly with GUCA1B/GCAP2 and GUCA1C/GCAP3. Loss-of-function or dominant-negative GUCA1A variants cause cone-rod dystrophy through disrupted cGMP homeostasis and photoreceptor degeneration. This gene should be SKIPPED from dark gene analysis - it represents historical nomenclature (C#orf#) superseded by functional annotation (GUCA1A).
