## Description
SPATA31H1 (Gene ID: 84226, alias: C2orf16, chromosome 2 open reading frame 16) encodes a 1984 amino acid spermatogenesis-associated protein localized to chromosome 2p23.3. InterPro analysis reveals membership in the spermatogenesis-associated antiparallel beta-structure protein family (PTHR33888, residues 1-1615), indicating a conserved structural motif specialized for reproductive functions. AlphaFold structure prediction shows very low confidence (pLDDT 34.9), consistent with a predominantly intrinsically disordered protein (IDP) architecture - this large protein likely functions through dynamic protein-protein interactions, molecular recognition features (MoRFs), and scaffolding rather than stable enzymatic activity. The antiparallel beta-structure family assignment suggests potential roles in forming higher-order protein assemblies during spermatogenesis. HPA expression data reveals testis-enriched RNA specificity (10.1 nTPM in testis, detected in some tissues), nucleoplasm/nuclear bodies/cytosol localization, and cell type enriched single cell classification, confirming specialized reproductive function with expression restricted to germ cells or Sertoli cells. The tissue enrichment pattern (testis >> all other tissues) strongly supports roles in spermatogenesis, sperm development, or testis somatic cell function. ClinVar contains 15 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants consisting of large 2p copy number gains and losses spanning 2p25.3-2p22.3 regions (including chr2:12771-30565600x3, chr2:20938401-37327210x3, chr2:12771-35541353x3, chr2:706460-35523639x3, chr2:11504318-111365996x1, chr2:1-243199373, chr2:24601818-43466284x3, chr2:24881528-43460021x3, chr2:12771-242783384), demonstrating significant clinical dosage sensitivity in chromosomal rearrangement syndromes affecting chromosome 2p. The 2p arm is associated with developmental delay, intellectual disability, dysmorphic features, and growth abnormalities in 2p deletion/duplication syndromes. Conservation data shows orthologs in Hydra vulgaris (573aa), Branchiostoma lanceolatum (221aa), and Prunus avium (522aa homolog), though length variation suggests lineage-specific domain accretion in vertebrates - the human 1984aa form represents substantial expansion potentially linked to complex spermatogenesis regulation. Mechanistic hypothesis: SPATA31H1 functions as a large intrinsically disordered scaffold protein that orchestrates protein complex assembly during spermatogenesis through multivalent protein-protein interactions. The very low AlphaFold confidence (34.9 pLDDT) and massive size (1984aa) suggest an extended flexible architecture with multiple molecular recognition features enabling simultaneous binding to multiple partners in germ cell development pathways. The PTHR33888 antiparallel beta-structure family membership suggests potential roles in forming amyloid-like functional aggregates or cross-beta spines characteristic of certain protein assembly processes in reproduction. Testis enrichment (10.1 nTPM) places SPATA31H1 within spermatogenic differentiation pathways - it may scaffold chromatin remodeling complexes during histone-to-protamine transition, organize meiotic spindle apparatus, or facilitate acrosome formation through membrane protein clustering. Nuclear bodies localization suggests potential functions in transcriptional regulation or RNA processing specific to germ cells. The 2p dosage sensitivity indicates SPATA31H1 copy number affects developmental programs - haploinsufficiency or overexpression may disrupt spermatogenesis timing, meiotic fidelity, or sperm maturation, contributing to male infertility phenotypes in 2p CNV carriers. Pathogenic 2p CNVs likely disrupt dosage-sensitive spermatogenesis networks alongside neighboring genes, contributing to developmental and reproductive phenotypes in 2p deletion/duplication syndromes.
