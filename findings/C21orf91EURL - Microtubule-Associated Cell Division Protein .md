# C21orf91/EURL - Microtubule-Associated Cell Division Protein with Nuclear Pore Interactions and High Clinical Variant Burden

**Date:** 2026-03-16T17:34:50.547318

**Quality Score:** 9/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
C21orf91 (EURL - Enhanced Expression in Undifferentiated Lymphoblasts/Protein EURL Homolog) encodes a 297-amino acid protein belonging to the conserved EURL family (IPR009704, PF06937, PTHR15961), indicating ancient evolutionary origins across metazoans. HPA data reveals ubiquitous expression across all tissues with specific subcellular localization to microtubules and the cytokinetic bridge—the structure that forms between daughter cells during late cytokinesis—strongly implicating a role in cell division completion. The protein's association with neurogenesis and differentiation processes suggests additional functions in neural development. STRING interaction network identifies 9 medium-confidence partners, most notably NUP54 (score 0.533), a nuclear pore complex component, suggesting potential nucleocytoplasmic trafficking roles; NEURL1 (score 0.441), an E3 ubiquitin ligase critical for neural development and Notch signaling; and RLF (score 0.496), a chromatin-binding protein involved in transcriptional regulation. Additional partners include SCOC (short coiled-coil protein), FBXL17 (F-box ubiquitin ligase), and BTG3 (anti-proliferative factor), collectively suggesting involvement in protein turnover, cell cycle control, and transcriptional regulation. AlphaFold structural prediction yields medium confidence (pLDDT 64.9), consistent with a partially structured protein containing both ordered domains and flexible regions—typical of scaffold proteins that mediate multiple protein-protein interactions. ClinVar reports 70 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly large copy number gains and losses on chromosome 21, demonstrating significant dosage sensitivity. The convergence of microtubule/cytokinetic bridge localization, nuclear pore interactions, neural development associations, and high clinical variant burden supports a mechanistic hypothesis: EURL functions as a scaffold protein coordinating cytokinetic abscission with nuclear pore assembly and chromatin regulation, potentially linking cell division completion to transcriptional reprogramming in daughter cells. Disruption of EURL dosage may cause cytokinetic defects, aneuploidy, or aberrant neural development, explaining its clinical significance in developmental disorders.
