## Description
C8orf34 (also known as VEST1) is a dark gene located at 8q13.2 encoding a 538 amino acid uncharacterized protein (UniProt: Q49A92). InterPro domain analysis reveals a dimerization/docking domain (residues 92-136) with strong homology to the cAMP-dependent protein kinase regulatory subunit dimerization-anchoring domain (G3DSA:1.20.890.10, SSF47391), suggesting this protein functions as an A-kinase anchoring protein (AKAP) that tethers PKA signaling complexes to specific subcellular locations. The protein also contains DUF5586 (PF17824, residues 87-455 and 465-538), a domain of unknown function conserved across vertebrates. STRING-DB identifies 8 protein interaction partners with medium confidence scores: C1orf194 (0.607), TEX55 (0.510), ASB7 (0.508), C19orf18 (0.447), ANKUB1 (0.443), EFCAB10 (0.441), OR1E2 (0.418), and PREX2 (0.417). Notably, ASB7 is an ankyrin/SOCS box E3 ubiquitin ligase and PREX2 is a Rac guanine nucleotide exchange factor, suggesting C8orf34 may scaffold signaling complexes involved in cytoskeletal regulation and cell migration. Clinically, naturally occurring mutations in C8orf34 are associated with increased risk for severe chemotherapy toxicities including diarrhea and neutropenia, indicating potential roles in rapidly dividing tissues. The PKA regulatory domain architecture combined with interaction partners suggests C8orf34 anchors cAMP-dependent signaling to regulate cell proliferation and stress responses.
