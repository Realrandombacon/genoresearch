# ANO2 (C12ORF3): Retina-Enriched Calcium-Activated Chloride Channel with Dimerization Interface

**Date:** 2026-03-18T00:28:40.849412

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
ANO2 (C12ORF3) encodes a 998 amino acid transmembrane protein (UniProt Q9NQ90) functioning as a calcium-activated chloride channel. InterPro analysis identifies the conserved anoctamin family domain (IPR007632, residues 89-961) with two functional subdomains: an N-terminal dimerization domain (IPR032394/PF16178, 92-348) mediating protein-protein interactions, and a C-terminal transmembrane pore domain (IPR049452/PF04547, 351-937) forming the ion conduction pathway. AlphaFold predicts a high-confidence structure (pLDDT 73.6) consistent with a well-folded membrane protein. HPA data shows extreme retina enrichment (190.9 nTPM) with plasma membrane localization, indicating specialized photoreceptor function. STRING interactions reveal strong associations with chloride channel regulators CLCA4 (0.984) and CLCA2 (0.973), cyclic nucleotide-gated channel subunits CNGA4 (0.806) and CNGA2 (0.414), and the anoctamin paralog ANO1 (0.637), suggesting participation in phototransduction cascades. ClinVar contains 54 pathogenic variants including copy number gains and duplications, though specific disease associations remain uncharacterized. The dimerization domain likely mediates hetero-oligomerization with ANO1 or other anoctamins, while the transmembrane domain forms a calcium-gated chloride pore critical for retinal signal termination.
