## Description
C16orf21/WDR24 encodes a 790 amino acid protein that functions as a catalytic component of the GATOR2 complex, a multiprotein activator of the amino acid-sensing branch of mTORC1 signaling. The protein contains multiple WD40 repeats (IPR001680, positions 68-103, 109-192, 196-245) that form beta-propeller scaffolds for protein-protein interactions, plus a C-terminal modified RING finger domain (cd16693, residues 742-787) of H2 subclass (C3H3C2-type). AlphaFold prediction shows high confidence structure (pLDDT 73.4) across the full 790 aa length, indicating stable folding suitable for complex assembly. STRING interaction analysis reveals WDR24 forms tight associations with GATOR2 subunits SEH1L, NPRL3, KPTN, DEPDC5, MIOS (all scores 0.999), plus GATOR1 components NPRL2 (0.983), WDR59 (0.848), and Rag GTPases RRAGC/RRAGB (0.713-0.999), demonstrating its central role in the amino acid sensing machinery. HPA expression shows ubiquitous tissue distribution with low specificity, localized to vesicles and cytosol, consistent with mTORC1 pathway function. The protein is annotated with autophagy and Ubl conjugation pathway functions. ClinVar contains 48 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly copy number variations affecting 16p13.3, suggesting dosage sensitivity. The WD40 repeat architecture suggests WDR24 serves as a scaffold platform that brings together GATOR2 subunits and Rag GTPases, enabling amino acid-dependent mTORC1 activation at the lysosomal surface.
