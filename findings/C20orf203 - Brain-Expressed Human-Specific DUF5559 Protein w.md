# C20orf203 - Brain-Expressed Human-Specific DUF5559 Protein with Nicotine Addiction Association and 16 Pathogenic Variants

**Date:** 2026-03-16T01:50:36.889320

**Quality Score:** 7/10 (GOOD)

## Description
C20orf203 encodes a 194 aa uncharacterized human-specific protein containing a DUF5559 domain (IPR040965/PF17714, residues 1-194) covering the entire protein sequence. This gene is thought to be human-specific as orthologous regions in other organisms contain sequence differences that would not support protein production. Expression is tissue-enhanced with highest levels in brain (1.7 nTPM), also detected in immune cells (immune cell enriched). Predicted intracellular localization. Shows medium-confidence interactions with cannabinoid receptor interacting protein CNRIP1 (0.600) and POTE ankyrin domain protein POTEI (0.452), suggesting potential roles in cannabinoid signaling or ankyrin-related cellular processes. Contains 16 pathogenic/likely pathogenic ClinVar variants (all large CNVs on chromosome 20q), indicating disease relevance. A SNP in the 3' UTR (rs17123507) has been associated with nicotine addiction in GWAS studies, providing a specific phenotypic link. Potential roles in brain-specific neuronal function, cannabinoid receptor modulation, or nicotine addiction susceptibility.
