## Description
SHISA8 (C22orf17) encodes a 397 amino acid transmembrane protein belonging to the Shisa family (IPR026910, PF13908). The protein contains a conserved N-terminal Shisa domain (IPR053891, residues 57-108) characteristic of this family of ER retention regulators. AlphaFold prediction yields a medium-confidence structure (pLDDT 56.9), suggesting a mix of ordered transmembrane regions and intrinsically disordered cytoplasmic domains typical of membrane trafficking regulators. HPA data shows strong brain-specific expression (57.1 nTPM), classifying it as tissue-enriched with predicted membrane localization. STRING interaction network reveals 10 high-confidence partners, prominently including glutamate receptor subunits GRIN1 (0.612), GRIN2C (0.578), GRIA2 (0.566), and auxiliary subunits CACNG3 (0.565), CNIH2 (0.540), plus paralog SHISA9 (0.728) and SHISA7 (0.549). This interaction profile strongly suggests SHISA8 functions in regulating AMPA/NMDA receptor trafficking at excitatory synapses. ClinVar documents 19 pathogenic variants (primarily chromosome 22q copy number gains), indicating clinical relevance though specific disease phenotypes remain uncharacterized. The Shisa family is known to retain GPCRs and growth factor receptors in the ER by blocking maturation; SHISA8 likely employs this mechanism for glutamate receptors.
