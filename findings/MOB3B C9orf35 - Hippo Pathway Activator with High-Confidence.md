## Description
MOB3B (chromosome 9 open reading frame 35) is a 216-amino acid protein functioning as a critical activator in the Hippo signaling pathway, specifically modulating LATS1 expression to control organ size and suppress tumors. The protein contains a highly conserved Mob1/phocein domain (PF03637, IPR005301) spanning residues 35-207, which facilitates its role as a kinase activator. AlphaFold prediction reveals a stable, well-folded structure with a high global pLDDT score of 89.8, indicating structural reliability. Expression analysis via HPA shows tissue-enhanced RNA levels, peaking in the brain (30.9 nTPM), with subcellular localization to the cytosol and intermediate filaments. STRING interaction data confirms functional partnerships with core Hippo components LATS1 (score 0.524) and LATS2 (0.488), as well as C9orf72 (0.546) and MAPK14 (0.475). ClinVar records indicate significant clinical relevance, listing 73 pathogenic or likely pathogenic variants, including copy number gains and truncating mutations (e.g., p.Ser91Ter), suggesting that dysregulation of MOB3B contributes to developmental disorders or cancer susceptibility through Hippo pathway disruption.
