# TMEM217 (C6orf128) - Testis-Expressed DUF4534 Transmembrane Protein with Clinical Significance

**Date:** 2026-03-16T18:41:17.701621

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
TMEM217 (UniProt Q8N7C4) encodes a 229-amino acid transmembrane protein containing the DUF4534 domain of unknown function (InterPro IPR027862, residues 1-199; Pfam PF15049, residues 11-171). This conserved domain family (PTHR34928) spans nearly the entire protein sequence, suggesting the DUF4534 fold constitutes the core functional unit. HPA data shows testis-enriched expression (8.4 nTPM) with nucleolar and cytosolic localization, indicating potential roles in spermatogenesis or ribosome biogenesis. STRING interactions reveal medium-confidence associations (scores 0.405-0.479) with C2orf66, C1QTNF2, C2CD2L, CDK2AP2, and SLC25A30 - partners implicated in cell cycle regulation, metabolism, and mitochondrial transport. Critically, ClinVar documents 6 pathogenic/likely pathogenic copy number variants (losses and gains) spanning the 6p21.31 region, demonstrating clinical significance despite limited functional characterization. The combination of testis expression, nucleolar localization, and DUF4534 conservation suggests TMEM217 may function as a scaffold protein in nucleolar organizing regions during germ cell development. Copy number alterations disrupting this gene could impair ribosomal RNA processing or chromatin organization in spermatogenic cells, potentially contributing to male infertility or developmental disorders. The transmembrane classification combined with nucleolar localization indicates possible dual membrane-nucleus trafficking or membrane-associated nucleolar functions.
