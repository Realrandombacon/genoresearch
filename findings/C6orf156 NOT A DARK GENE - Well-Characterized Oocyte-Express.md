## Description

This represents a critical finding: C6orf156 is NOT a dark gene but rather a fully annotated maternal effect protein with established function in early embryonic development and subcortical maternal complex (SCMC) formation. UniProt accession A6NGQ2 encodes a 149-amino acid intracellular protein. Functional role: OOEP encodes an oocyte-specific KH domain protein that functions as a component of the subcortical maternal complex (SCMC), a multi-protein assembly required for early embryonic development, zygotic genome activation, and maternal mRNA regulation. Mechanistic proposal: OOEP likely functions through RNA binding via its KH domain, recognizing specific cis-elements in maternal transcripts to regulate their stability, localization, or translation during oocyte maturation and early cleavage stages. OOEP may scaffold with NLRP5/MATER, KHDC3L, and PADI6 to form the SCMC platform that anchors maternal factors at the oocyte cortex. The KH domain may bind AU-rich elements or specific stem-loop structures in maternal mRNAs, recruiting decay/translation machinery. OOEP paralogs (OOEP-like proteins) may provide functional redundancy. Loss-of-function OOEP variants may cause embryonic arrest through disrupted maternal mRNA regulation, though human phenotypes remain to be characterized. This gene should be SKIPPED from dark gene analysis - it represents historical nomenclature (C#orf#) superseded by functional annotation (OOEP).
