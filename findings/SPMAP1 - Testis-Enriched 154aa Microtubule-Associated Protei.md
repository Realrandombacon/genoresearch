## Description
SPMAP1 (C17orf98) encodes a 154-amino acid sperm microtubule-associated protein (UniProt A8MV24) belonging to the SPMAP1 family (IPR028027/PF15075, residues 6-154/12-145). AlphaFold prediction shows high confidence folding (pLDDT 76.4), indicating a stable globular structure. HPA classifies SPMAP1 as a predicted intracellular protein with testis-enriched expression (18.8 nTPM), tissue-enriched specificity, and detected in a single tissue type - consistent with its annotated role in spermatogenesis. No STRING interactions were identified, suggesting either transient interactions, testis-specific partners not captured in STRING, or limitations for small tissue-specific proteins. ClinVar documents 7 pathogenic/likely pathogenic copy number variants (primarily gains) spanning 17q12-25, indicating dosage sensitivity and potential disease relevance. Notably, orthologs in Lynx canadensis and Ailuropoda melanoleuca are annotated as "large ribosomal subunit protein uL14" rather than microtubule-associated protein, suggesting either annotation inconsistency, evolutionary neofunctionalization in primates, or dual functionality. The ribosomal uL14 protein typically binds 28S rRNA and participates in translation elongation, while microtubule-associated proteins regulate spindle dynamics during meiosis. We hypothesize SPMAP1 may represent a testis-specific paralog that evolved from a ribosomal ancestor to function in meiotic spindle assembly during spermatogenesis, potentially retaining RNA-binding capacity while acquiring microtubule-binding activity. The stable folded structure (pLDDT 76.4) supports a structured RNA/microtubule-binding domain rather than intrinsically disordered interaction motif.
