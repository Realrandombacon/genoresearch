## Description
C6orf177 encodes SYCP2L (Synaptonemal complex protein 2-like, UniProt Q5T4T6), an 812 aa intracellular protein with homology to meiotic synaptonemal complex components. InterPro identifies three structured domains: an armadillo-repeat-like domain (IPR041322, PF18581, positions 27-200) characteristic of protein-protein interaction scaffolds, an Spt16M-like domain (IPR040560, PF18584, positions 292-403) homologous to the histone chaperone SPT16 subunit of the FACT complex, and a SYCP2-like family region (IPR024835, PTHR15607, positions 20-441). AlphaFold predicts a medium-confidence structure (pLDDT 65.9), consistent with a multi-domain protein containing both structured regions and flexible linkers. HPA shows tissue-enriched expression with highest levels in parathyroid gland (64.6 nTPM), nucleoplasmic localization, and disease involvement in premature ovarian failure (POF). STRING interactions reveal associations with TMEM150B (0.610), MCM8 (0.599, DNA helicase in meiotic recombination), SCP2 (0.598, likely SYCP2 synaptonemal component), ELOVL2 (0.554, fatty acid elongase), and UIMC1/RAP80 (0.479, DNA damage response). ClinVar documents 35 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including splice site mutations, frameshifts (p.Ser52fs), nonsense (p.Gln510Ter), and missense changes (p.Ile333Met). The Spt16M-like domain suggests evolutionary relationship to histone chaperones involved in nucleosome assembly/disassembly. Mechanistic hypothesis: SYCP2L likely functions as a chromatin regulator in oocytes and parathyroid cells, potentially modulating nucleosome dynamics through its Spt16M histone chaperone domain. The armadillo-repeat domain may mediate protein complex assembly at centromeres or meiotic chromosomes. The premature ovarian failure association suggests critical roles in oocyte maintenance, meiotic progression, or DNA repair. The parathyroid enrichment is unexpected for a meiotic protein and may indicate tissue-specific isoforms or neofunctionalization. SYCP2L represents a 'dark gene' transitioning to characterization - domain architecture and disease links are established, but molecular mechanisms in chromatin regulation remain to be defined.
