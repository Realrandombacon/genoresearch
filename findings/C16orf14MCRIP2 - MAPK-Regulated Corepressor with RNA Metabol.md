## Description
C16orf14 (MCRIP2, MAPK Regulated Corepressor Interacting Protein 2) is a 160-amino acid nuclear protein encoded on chromosome 16p13.3. InterPro analysis identifies a conserved FAM195 domain (IPR029428, PF14799) spanning residues 62-157, characteristic of MAPK-regulated corepressor-interacting proteins. AlphaFold predicts a medium-confidence structure (pLDDT 64.3), indicating partial intrinsic disorder typical of transcriptional coregulators. HPA data shows tissue-enhanced expression with highest levels in liver (57.1 nTPM) and heart muscle (47.3 nTPM), with predicted intracellular localization. STRING interaction network reveals 10 partners with high-confidence connections to RNA helicase DDX6 (0.778), LSM12 (0.745) involved in P-body formation, and cell cycle regulator CCNDBP1 (0.622). Additional interactions with ATXN2L (0.617), CYC1 (0.583), and mitochondrial complex I subunits (NDUFV1 0.538, NDUFS8 0.516) suggest dual nuclear-cytoplasmic functions. ClinVar reports 47 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly copy number gains and losses on 16p13.3, indicating strong dosage sensitivity. The FAM195 domain architecture and DDX6/LSM12 interactions suggest MCRIP2 functions as a MAPK-responsive transcriptional corepressor that couples signaling pathways to RNA metabolism and mRNA decay machinery. The high variant burden without specific disease associations suggests MCRIP2 haploinsufficiency may contribute to developmental disorders through dysregulated gene expression programs.
