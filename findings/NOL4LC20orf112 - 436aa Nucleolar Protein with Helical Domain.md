## Description
NOL4L (Gene ID: 140688, alias C20orf112, C20orf113) encodes a 436 amino acid nucleolar protein 4-like protein localized to chromosome 20q11.21 with nucleoplasm subcellular localization. InterPro analysis reveals extensive domain architecture: Nucleolar protein 4 family (IPR039788, residues 15-374), Nucleolar protein 4 helical domain (IPR056549/PF23079, residues 185-279), and death domain-containing protein family classification (PTHR12449, residues 15-374), indicating conserved structural features related to protein-protein interaction domains. AlphaFold structure prediction shows medium confidence (pLDDT 61.2), consistent with mixed structured/disordered regions typical of nucleolar scaffold or regulatory proteins with intrinsically disordered segments. STRING interaction analysis identifies 10 partners with two high-confidence interactions: CTBP1 (C-terminal binding protein 1, 0.821) and TEX9 (testis expressed 9, 0.738), plus medium-confidence partners SKA3 (0.597), PAX5 (0.577), FAM227B (0.498), CTBP2 (0.483), KIF3B (0.476), RUNX1 (0.468), COMMD7 (0.462), and NOL4 (0.406). The strong CTBP1 interaction links NOL4L to transcriptional corepression complexes, as CTBP1 is a well-characterized NADH-dependent transcriptional corepressor that recruits histone deacetylases and chromatin modifiers. HPA expression data shows tissue-enhanced RNA pattern with highest expression in retina (45.9 nTPM), detected in all tissues, and cell type-enhanced specificity, indicating housekeeping nucleolar functions with retinal enrichment. ClinVar contains 20 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including 20q copy number losses and gains (20q11.21-13.12, 20q11.21-11.23, 20p11.21-q11.22 regions) and intronic/3-UTR splice variants, demonstrating clinical significance in chromosomal rearrangement syndromes affecting the 20q11.21 locus. Conservation extends to Mus musculus (494 aa), demonstrating mammalian evolutionary constraint. Mechanistic hypothesis: NOL4L functions as a nucleolar transcriptional coregulator that recruits CTBP1/CTBP2 corepressor complexes to target gene promoters through death domain-mediated protein-protein interactions. The helical domain (PF23079) may mediate oligomerization or nucleic acid binding within nucleolar organizing regions. Retina enrichment (45.9 nTPM) suggests specialized roles in photoreceptor gene regulation or retinal development. The NOL4 interaction indicates potential heterodimerization with the canonical NOL4 protein. Pathogenic 20q CNVs likely disrupt dosage-sensitive transcriptional repression and nucleolar organization, contributing to neurodevelopmental and retinal phenotypes in 20q11.21 deletion/duplication syndromes.
