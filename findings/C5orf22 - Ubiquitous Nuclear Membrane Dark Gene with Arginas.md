## Description
C5orf22 (Q49AR2) is a 442 aa uncharacterized dark gene encoding a nuclear membrane protein with ubiquitous expression across all tissues and immune cell enhancement. Contains a highly conserved Peptide arginase FlmR/OhkR-like domain (aa 9-436, IPR024131, PTHR13225) spanning nearly the entire protein, plus a specific UPF0489 domain (aa 19-257, PF12640). The FlmR/OhkR family is named after bacterial regulators (FlmR in Listeria, OhkR in Streptomyces) involved in stress response and virulence, suggesting an ancient regulatory role. BLAST shows extreme conservation: 98.6% identity to orangutan, 78.3% to mouse, 66.8% to Xenopus, 57.1% to zebrafish, indicating strong vertebrate purifying selection. STRING interactions reveal a pre-mRNA splicing and transcription elongation network: WBP11 (0.708, WW domain-binding splicing factor), ELOF1 (0.599, transcription elongation factor 1 homolog), TRMT13 (0.579, tRNA methyltransferase), SAYSD1 (0.577, Say domain-containing), PQBP1 (0.568, polyglutamine-binding protein linked to splicing), plus dark gene partner C5orf51. HPA confirms nuclear membrane localization, consistent with roles in mRNA processing/export or transcriptional regulation at the nuclear periphery. ClinVar contains 31 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including multiple 5p copy number gains/losses and splice site mutations, confirming strong disease relevance. True dark gene: conserved FlmR/OhkR arginase-like architecture (potential peptide/amino acid sensing or hydrolysis), nuclear membrane localization, splicing factor interactions, clinically implicated, but molecular function completely unknown. Hypothesis: may function as a nuclear membrane-associated metabolic sensor or peptide hydrolase (via FlmR domain) that regulates pre-mRNA splicing or transcription elongation in response to cellular stress or amino acid availability, bridging metabolism with gene expression control.
