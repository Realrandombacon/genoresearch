# SAPCD1 (C6orf26) - APC Domain Tumor Suppressor with Wnt Pathway Interactions and Pathogenic Variants

## Description
SAPCD1 (Suppressor APC Domain-Containing Protein 1, formerly C6orf26) is a 148 amino acid protein encoded on chromosome 6p21.33. This dark gene contains a conserved APC tumor suppressor domain (InterPro IPR026828, residues 25-123; Pfam PF11414, residues 36-114; Panther PTHR14907) homologous to the adenomatous polyposis coli protein, suggesting involvement in Wnt/β-catenin signaling pathway regulation. AlphaFold predicts a confident 3D structure (pLDDT 74.6) across all 148 residues, indicating stable folding suitable for protein-protein interactions. STRING interaction network reveals 10 medium-confidence partners including CTNNB1/β-catenin (0.698), CTNNA2/α-catenin-2 (0.620), ARHGAP23 (0.611), CDH2/N-cadherin (0.586), and VWA7 (0.570) - all components of adherens junctions and Wnt signaling complexes. This interaction profile strongly suggests SAPCD1 functions as a negative regulator of β-catenin stability, potentially competing with or modulating APC protein activity in the β-catenin destruction complex. ClinVar documents 8 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including copy number losses and gains spanning 6p21.33, plus a single nucleotide variant (c.31764104C>T), confirming clinical relevance though specific disease associations remain uncharacterized. The APC domain architecture combined with β-catenin interaction suggests SAPCD1 may function in epithelial cell adhesion, cell cycle control, and tumor suppression. Loss-of-function variants could predispose to colorectal cancer or other epithelial malignancies through dysregulated Wnt signaling, analogous to APC mutations in familial adenomatous polyposis. HPA expression data unavailable, but interaction partners suggest ubiquitous epithelial expression.
