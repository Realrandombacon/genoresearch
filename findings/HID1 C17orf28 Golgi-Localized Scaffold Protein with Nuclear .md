# HID1 (C17orf28): Golgi-Localized Scaffold Protein with Nuclear Pore Interactions and Neurological Disease Association

**Date:** 2026-03-18T02:56:51.156606

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
HID1 (formerly C17orf28) encodes a 788 aa conserved intracellular protein (UniProt Q8IV36) belonging to the HID1/Ecm30 family (IPR026705, PF12722). AlphaFold predicts a well-folded structure (pLDDT 85.3) across the full-length protein (1-788 aa). HPA shows brain-enriched expression (146.5 nTPM) with secondary expression in pancreas (104.3 nTPM), and subcellular localization to Golgi apparatus and cytosol. STRING reveals interactions with deubiquitinases USP7 (0.649) and USP40 (0.620), nucleoporins NUP155 (0.575), NUP54 (0.526), NUP214 (0.494), mRNA export factor GLE1 (0.609), and dynein modifier DYM (0.603), suggesting roles in nuclear-cytoplasmic transport and protein stability. ClinVar contains 28 pathogenic variants including missense (p.Gly306Ser, p.Thr159Met, p.Gly187Asp) and frameshift mutations, associated with epilepsy and intellectual disability. Orthologs exist in Xenopus (792 aa), Hydra (757 aa), and snake (796 aa), indicating strong metazoan conservation. FUNCTIONAL HYPOTHESIS: HID1 functions as a Golgi-associated scaffold protein coordinating nuclear pore complex assembly and mRNA export, with deubiquitinase interactions regulating protein turnover. MECHANISTIC PROPOSAL: HID1 localizes to Golgi membranes via N-terminal hydrophobic regions, recruiting nucleoporins (NUP155/54/214) for nuclear pore biogenesis. Interaction with USP7/USP40 suggests deubiquitination of nuclear transport factors. GLE1 binding links to mRNA export regulation. Brain-specific expression and pathogenic variants disrupt nucleocytoplasmic transport in neurons, causing epilepsy and intellectual disability.
