## Description
ACTMAP (actin maturation protease, chromosome 19 open reading frame 54) encodes a 351 amino acid well-folded cytoplasmic enzyme (UniProt Q5BKX5) containing an Actin maturation protease domain (IPR040043, residues 52-350), Actin maturation protease-like C-terminal domain (PF21646, residues 123-242), and UPF0692 PROTEIN C19ORF54 family (PTHR28631, residues 52-350) characteristic of cysteine-type aminopeptidases with N-terminal methionine excision capabilities from actin and other cytoskeletal substrates. AlphaFold predicts high-confidence structure (pLDDT 83.2) indicating a stable folded catalytic domain, consistent with proteolytic enzymes that function through precise active site geometry and substrate specificity for N-terminal residue recognition. HPA data reveals ubiquitous RNA expression (detected in all tissues), low tissue specificity, intermediate filament and centriolar satellite subcellular localization, cell type-enhanced single cell specificity, and aminopeptidase/hydrolase/protease molecular function classification, suggesting roles in actin cytoskeleton maturation, protein N-terminal processing, and centriolar satellite granule assembly with potential cell cycle and ciliogenesis association. STRING interactions identify 10 partners including LSM2 (0.944, high confidence, U6 snRNA binding, spliceosomal snRNP assembly), LSM5 (0.926, high confidence, U6 snRNA binding, mRNA decapping), SNRPD2 (0.921, high confidence, spliceosomal snRNP core protein, pre-mRNA splicing), LSM6 (0.916, high confidence, U6 snRNA binding, cytoplasmic mRNA decay), LSM1 (0.907, high confidence, mRNA decapping complex, 5'-3' decay), LSM7 (0.904, high confidence, U6 snRNA binding, spliceosome assembly), LSM8 (0.897, high confidence, U6 snRNA binding, nuclear snRNP), LSM4 (0.897, high confidence, U6 snRNA binding, Lsm ring complex), SNRPN (0.897, high confidence, Prader-Willi syndrome locus, alternative splicing), and XRN1 (0.607, medium confidence, 5'-3' exoribonuclease, mRNA turnover), implicating ACTMAP in RNA metabolism, spliceosome assembly, mRNA decapping, and exonucleolytic decay. The actin maturation protease domain combined with ubiquitous expression, centriolar satellite localization, well-folded structure (pLDDT 83.2), and high-confidence interaction with the complete LSM1-8 ring complex (all scores 0.897-0.944) positions ACTMAP as a bifunctional enzyme linking actin N-terminal processing with RNA metabolism through centriolar satellite granule co-localization. High-confidence interactions with all eight LSM proteins (LSM1-8, U6 snRNA binding, spliceosomal snRNP and mRNA decapping complexes) suggest functional coupling between ACTMAP and RNA processing machinery, potentially coordinating cytoskeletal maturation with spliceosome assembly or mRNA decay at centriolar satellite domains where both processes converge during cell cycle progression. Interaction with SNRPD2 (spliceosomal snRNP core protein, pre-mRNA splicing) and SNRPN (Prader-Willi syndrome locus, alternative splicing regulator) indicates link between ACTMAP and splicing regulation, consistent with centriolar satellite localization where splicing factors concentrate during mitosis. Interaction with XRN1 (5'-3' exoribonuclease, mRNA turnover) suggests coordination between ACTMAP and cytoplasmic mRNA decay pathways, potentially linking actin cytoskeleton remodeling with transcriptome reprogramming during cell division or stress responses. The well-folded structure (pLDDT 83.2) combined with cysteine aminopeptidase activity suggests ACTMAP functions as a processive N-terminal methionine excision enzyme that removes initiator methionine from nascent actin polypeptides, enabling proper actin filament polymerization and cytoskeletal dynamics. ClinVar documents 10 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly chromosome 19q copy number gains encompassing the ACTMAP locus (19q11-13.2 CNVs), indicating strong dosage sensitivity in disease etiology, potentially affecting actin maturation and RNA processing. Disease-associated CNVs suggest triplosensitivity mechanisms disrupting protease stoichiometry or centriolar satellite granule composition, potentially leading to cytoskeletal defects through impaired actin N-terminal processing or RNA metabolism defects through dysregulated LSM complex recruitment. The 19q13.2 chromosomal location, high pathogenic variant burden (10 variants), ubiquitous expression, centriolar satellite localization, well-folded catalytic structure (pLDDT 83.2), and LSM ring complex interaction network position ACTMAP as a candidate gene for cytoskeletal disorders, ciliopathies, and RNA processing syndromes through dysregulated actin maturation and spliceosome assembly coordination. Conservation across vertebrates suggests evolutionary constraint consistent with essential protein processing and RNA metabolism functions.
