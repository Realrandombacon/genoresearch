# PAQR8 (C6orf33) - Brain-Enriched Membrane Progesterone Receptor with AdipoR Domain and G-Protein Signaling

**Date:** 2026-03-16T19:12:30.481377

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
PAQR8 (Progestin and AdipoQ Receptor Family Member 8, formerly C6orf33) is a 354 amino acid G-protein coupled membrane receptor encoded on chromosome 6p12.3. This dark gene contains a conserved AdipoR/Haemolysin-III transmembrane domain (InterPro IPR004254, residues 37-307; Pfam PF03006, residues 70-296; Panther PTHR20855) characteristic of progestin and adiponectin receptors, indicating 7-transmembrane helix topology for ligand binding and G-protein coupling. HPA expression reveals brain-enriched transcription (138.7 nTPM in brain, tissue-enhanced specificity) with dual Golgi apparatus and plasma membrane localization, consistent with receptor trafficking and membrane signaling functions. STRING interaction network identifies 10 partners including EFHC1 (0.942, high confidence), PGRMC2 (0.842), PGRMC1 (0.636), SERBP1 (0.538), and canonical progesterone receptor PGR (0.534), plus paralogs PAQR5 (0.529) and PAQR7 (0.514). This interaction profile strongly suggests PAQR8 functions in progesterone signaling pathways, potentially mediating rapid non-genomic progestin effects through G(i) protein coupling as demonstrated for membrane progestin receptors. ClinVar documents 11 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including multiple copy number losses and gains spanning 6p12, confirming clinical relevance though specific disease associations remain uncharacterized. The combination of brain-enriched expression, progesterone receptor interaction network, and AdipoR domain architecture suggests PAQR8 functions in neurosteroid signaling, oocyte maturation, and reproductive hormone regulation. Loss-of-function variants could cause reproductive disorders, infertility, or neuroendocrine dysfunction through disrupted progesterone membrane signaling.
