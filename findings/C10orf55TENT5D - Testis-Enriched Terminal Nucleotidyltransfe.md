## Description
C10orf55 (Gene ID: 414236) is a misleading historical alias for TENT5D (Terminal nucleotidyltransferase 5D, Gene ID: 169966), a 389 amino acid enzyme (UniProt Q8NEK8) actually encoded on the X chromosome (Xq22.2), not chromosome 10 as the 'orf' name suggests. This represents an important annotation correction for dark gene research. TENT5D contains a defining terminal nucleotidyltransferase domain (InterPro IPR012937, residues 4-381) with a conserved nucleotidyltransferase catalytic core (PF07984, residues 9-328) and DUF1693 domain (SM01153, 9-328), indicating poly(A) RNA polymerase activity (GO:1990817). AlphaFold structure prediction yields high confidence (pLDDT 87.4), confirming a stable folded enzymatic structure. Human Protein Atlas shows testis-enriched expression (3.2 nTPM) with tissue-enriched RNA specificity and intracellular localization, consistent with a role in post-transcriptional RNA regulation during spermatogenesis. STRING interaction network reveals 10 medium-confidence partners including SPANXN4 (0.544), FAM133A (0.541), CYLC1 (0.525), SLC25A14 (0.514), and GPRASP1 (0.510), suggesting connections to X-linked cancer/testis antigens and metabolic processes. ClinVar documents 133 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly large X-chromosome copy number variations (gains and losses spanning Xq13.1-q28), indicating dosage sensitivity though no specific monogenic disease is defined. The testis-enriched expression combined with nucleotidyltransferase activity suggests TENT5D functions in mRNA polyadenylation or uridylation during germ cell development, potentially regulating transcript stability or translation efficiency in spermatogenesis. The X-chromosomal location and high variant burden warrant investigation for X-linked male infertility phenotypes. This gene is NOT dark - it is well-characterized with clear enzymatic function, making C10orf55 an important case of misleading nomenclature in genome annotation.
