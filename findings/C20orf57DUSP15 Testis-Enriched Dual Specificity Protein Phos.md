## Description
C20orf57 (now designated DUSP15, Dual Specificity Protein Phosphatase 15) is a 295-amino acid nucleocytoplasmic enzyme functioning as a protein tyrosine/threonine phosphatase that catalyzes reversible dephosphorylation of MAPK pathway components and cell cycle regulators. The protein belongs to the protein tyrosine phosphatase superfamily (G3DSA:3.90.190.10, residues 3-153) and atypical DUSP family (PR01908). Expression profiling from the Human Protein Atlas demonstrates testis-enriched expression (208.8 nTPM) with detection in many tissues, consistent with specialized roles in germ cell development and meiotic regulation, with cancer-enriched expression suggesting oncogenic potential, and subcellular localization to plasma membrane and cytosol—supporting functions in signal transduction cascades. The mechanistic hypothesis proposes that DUSP15 functions as a negative regulator of MAPK/ERK and JNK/p38 signaling pathways through dephosphorylation of activation loop threonine and tyrosine residues on MAPKs, thereby controlling cell proliferation, differentiation, and stress responses. Testis-enriched expression suggests specialized roles in spermatogenesis, potentially regulating meiotic checkpoint kinases or chromatin remodeling during germ cell development. Cancer-enriched expression implies DUSP15 may function as an oncogene or tumor suppressor depending on cellular context, modulating growth factor signaling and cell cycle progression.
