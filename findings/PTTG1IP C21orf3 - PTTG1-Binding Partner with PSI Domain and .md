# PTTG1IP (C21orf3) - PTTG1-Binding Partner with PSI Domain and High Clinical Variant Burden

**Date:** 2026-03-16T17:29:50.282890

**Quality Score:** 9/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
PTTG1IP (Pituitary tumor-transforming gene 1-interacting protein), also known as C21orf3, is a 180-amino acid protein that functions as a specific binding partner for the securin protein PTTG1. Structural analysis via InterPro reveals a conserved PSI domain (IPR016201, residues 39-92), typically found in Plexins/Semaphorins/Integrins, suggesting a role in protein-protein recognition or signal transduction interfaces. The C-terminal region contains a dedicated PTTG1IP domain (PF27838, IPR060449) essential for its primary interaction. AlphaFold prediction (pLDDT 77.2) indicates a confident, well-folded structure throughout its length. STRING interaction data confirms a high-confidence physical association with PTTG1 (score 0.893), linking it directly to cell cycle regulation, specifically the metaphase-anaphase transition and sister chromatid separation. Additional medium-confidence partners include JAM2, SYNJ1, and TP53, hinting at broader roles in junctional stability and tumor suppression pathways. HPA data shows ubiquitous expression across all tissues with nucleoplasmic localization, consistent with a housekeeping role in cell division. Critically, ClinVar reports 89 pathogenic or likely pathogenic variants, predominantly large copy number gains and losses on chromosome 21, indicating that dosage sensitivity of this gene is clinically significant and potentially linked to developmental disorders or cancer predisposition. Despite being labeled an 'ORF', the strong conservation of its domains, defined interactome, and heavy clinical variant burden suggest it is a functional regulator of the PTTG1 axis rather than a true 'dark' gene.
