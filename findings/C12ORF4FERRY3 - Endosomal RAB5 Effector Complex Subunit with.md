## Description
C12ORF4 (FERRY3) encodes a 552 aa protein that functions as subunit 3 of the FERRY (Five subunits Endosomal Rab5 and Ypt1 Recruiter) complex, a recently identified molecular machinery involved in endosomal trafficking and mRNA transport. The protein contains a conserved Ferry domain (IPR019311, PF10154, residues 42-545) spanning most of its sequence, indicating specialized structural role within the complex. AlphaFold prediction shows high-confidence folded structure (pLDDT 83.9), suggesting stable tertiary conformation suitable for protein-protein interactions. HPA data reveals ubiquitous expression across all tissues with nuclear body localization, consistent with roles in RNA metabolism and nucleocytoplasmic transport. High-confidence STRING interactions include TBCK (0.892), a mTOR pathway regulator linked to neurodevelopmental disorders, and PPP1R21 (0.818), a protein phosphatase regulator. Additional partners include EXOC8 (0.620, exocyst complex), IL6R (0.673, cytokine signaling), and TMEM184C (0.630). ClinVar contains 8 pathogenic variants including copy number losses, nonsense mutation p.Arg360Ter, and frameshift p.Lys401fs, implicating haploinsufficiency in disease. Disease associations include intellectual disability, suggesting critical neurodevelopmental function. The FERRY complex bridges early endosomes (RAB5-positive) with mRNA transport machinery, potentially coordinating spatial regulation of local translation in neurons. C12ORF4's nuclear body localization combined with endosomal complex membership suggests dual nucleocytoplasmic shuttling function, possibly linking transcriptional regulation with endosomal mRNA sorting. Conservation across vertebrates (mouse Q91YN0, rat D4A770, marmoset U3EQU4) indicates essential biological role maintained over evolution.
