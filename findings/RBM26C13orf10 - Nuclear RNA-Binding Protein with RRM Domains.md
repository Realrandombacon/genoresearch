# RBM26/C13orf10 - Nuclear RNA-Binding Protein with RRM Domains

**Date:** 2026-03-16T13:44:28.794920

**Quality Score:** 7/10 (GOOD)

## Description
RBM26 (formerly C13orf10) encodes a 1007aa nuclear RNA-binding protein with two canonical RNA recognition motif (RRM) domains (positions 531-606 and 886-957), a PWI domain (11-76), and a CCCH-type zinc finger (288-316). The protein localizes to nuclear speckles - subnuclear compartments involved in pre-mRNA splicing and RNA storage. STRING analysis reveals interactions with RNA metabolism factors including ZFC3H1 (nuclear exosome targeting factor, high confidence 0.747), MTREX (nuclear exoribonuclease), PABPN1 (poly(A) binding protein), and RBM7 (exosome complex). This interaction network strongly suggests RBM26 functions in nuclear poly(A)+ RNA turnover/degradation. ClinVar reports 82 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including large copy number losses and gains on chromosome 13, indicating clinical significance. Despite limited functional characterization in literature, the domain architecture and interaction partners provide a clear mechanistic hypothesis: RBM26 is an RNA-binding adaptor protein recruiting substrates to the nuclear exosome for degradation.
