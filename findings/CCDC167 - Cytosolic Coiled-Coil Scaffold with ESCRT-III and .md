## Description
CCDC167 (UniProt Q9P0B6) encodes a compact 97aa cytosolic protein on chromosome 6p21.2 dominated by a single extensive coiled-coil domain (PF15188, residues 11-93; IPR028194) with high structural confidence (AlphaFold pLDDT 87.8), indicating a stable rod-like architecture typical of protein-protein interaction scaffolds. HPA data confirms ubiquitous low-level expression across all tissues with cytosolic localization. STRING interaction network reveals 10 medium-confidence partners linking CCDC167 to the ESCRT-III membrane scission machinery (CHMP7, 0.493), synaptic leucine-rich repeat transmembrane proteins (LRRTM4, 0.488), and other coiled-coil nucleolar factors (CCDC178, 0.465; CCDC106, 0.448), alongside metabolic enzymes (PRKCSH, 0.455; FN3KRP, 0.449) and vesicle trafficking regulators (SAC3D1, 0.443; ARFGEF3, 0.407). This interactome suggests a dual role in coordinating cytosolic scaffold assembly for both endosomal sorting complexes and synaptic adhesion platforms. ClinVar contains 6 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, exclusively large chromosome 6 copy-number alterations (deletions/duplications spanning 6p21.31-q27), implicating the locus in genomic instability disorders though no specific monogenic disease is yet defined. The combination of a rigid coiled-coil structure, interaction with CHMP7 (ESCRT-III) and LRRTM4 (synapse), and ubiquitous expression supports a mechanistic hypothesis where CCDC167 acts as a modular adaptor bridging membrane remodeling complexes with cytoskeletal or synaptic organizing centers, potentially regulating vesicle abscission or postsynaptic density stability.
