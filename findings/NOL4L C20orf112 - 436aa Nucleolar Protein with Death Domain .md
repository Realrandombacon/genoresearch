## Description
NOL4L (Gene ID: 140688, alias: C20orf112, C20orf113) encodes a 436 amino acid nucleolar protein 4-like localized to chromosome 20q11.21 with predicted intracellular classification and nucleoplasmic subcellular localization. InterPro analysis reveals conserved domain architecture: Nucleolar protein 4 family (IPR039788, residues 15-374), Nucleolar protein 4 helical domain (IPR056549, PF23079, residues 185-279), and PTHR12449 family classification (death domain-containing protein, residues 15-374), indicating a deeply conserved eukaryotic protein family with death domain fold potentially involved in protein-protein interactions and apoptosis regulation. AlphaFold structure prediction shows medium confidence (pLDDT 61.2), consistent with partially structured architecture containing both ordered helical domains and intrinsically disordered regions, typical of scaffold proteins involved in transcriptional regulation and chromatin organization. Mechanistic hypothesis: NOL4L functions as a nucleoplasmic transcriptional coregulator that recruits CTBP1/CTBP2 corepressor complexes to target gene promoters through death domain-mediated protein-protein interactions. The helical domain (residues 185-279) likely mediates dimerization or nucleic acid binding, while the death domain fold (residues 15-374) serves as a protein interaction scaffold. CTBP1 recruitment suggests NOL4L modulates NADH-sensitive metabolic-transcriptional coupling, potentially linking cellular metabolic state to gene expression programs. RUNX1 interaction indicates roles in hematopoietic differentiation and potential involvement in AML1-ETO leukemogenic complexes. Retina enrichment (45.9 nTPM) indicates specialized roles in photoreceptor gene regulation or retinal development. Pathogenic 20q11 CNVs likely disrupt dosage-sensitive transcriptional networks, contributing to developmental delay, retinal dystrophy, or hematologic abnormalities in 20q11 deletion/duplication syndromes.
