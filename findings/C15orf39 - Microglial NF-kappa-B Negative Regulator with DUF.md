## Description
C15orf39 (Q6ZRI6) is a 1047 aa dark gene encoding a cytosolic protein containing DUF5525 domain (aa 1-1047, IPR037656). Well conserved in mouse (71.9% identity). Recent literature (PubMed:38892217) reveals function: negatively regulates microglial inflammatory responses by inhibiting NF-kappa-B signaling through PRMT2 interaction, reducing pro-inflammatory cytokine production. STRING interactions include FAM219B (0.569), UBL7 (0.480), CTBP1 (0.466, transcriptional corepressor), C19orf47 (0.451, another dark gene), TINF2 (0.413, telomere shelterin complex), PDCD1 (0.409, immune checkpoint), and SCAMP5 (0.404, secretory carrier membrane protein). HPA shows testis-enriched expression (69.4 nTPM), bone marrow enhancement (58.4 nTPM), and cytosolic localization. ClinVar contains 38 pathogenic/likely pathogenic copy number variants on 15q24.1-26.3, confirming strong disease relevance. True dark gene: conserved DUF, immune regulatory function newly discovered, testis/bone marrow enriched, clinically implicated with extensive CNV burden, but structural/biochemical mechanisms remain uncharacterized. Hypothesis: may act as scaffold protein recruiting PRMT2 to chromatin or signaling complexes to dampen NF-kappa-B activation in microglia, with potential role in neuroinflammation and telomere maintenance via TINF2 interaction.
