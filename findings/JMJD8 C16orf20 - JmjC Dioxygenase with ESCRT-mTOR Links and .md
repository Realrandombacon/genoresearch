## Description
JMJD8 encodes a 264aa Fe(II)/α-ketoglutarate-dependent dioxygenase (UniProt Q96S16) with a canonical JmjC catalytic domain (IPR003347, PF13621 Cupin-like, residues 131-264) embedded in a Cupin superfamily fold (G3DSA:2.60.120.650). AlphaFold predicts a very high-confidence structure (pLDDT 90.2), indicating a well-folded enzymatic core typical of 2-oxoglutarate-dependent oxygenases.\n\nThe JmjC domain defines a large enzyme family including histone demethylases (KDMs) and protein hydroxylases. JMJD8 belongs to the JMJD6 subfamily (IPR050910, PTHR12480), whose members catalyze arginine demethylation and lysyl-hydroxylation on histone and non-histone substrates. This catalytic capability positions JMJD8 as a post-translational modifier regulating protein function through epigenetic or proteomic marks.\n\nSTRING-DB reveals 10 interaction partners linking JMJD8 to membrane trafficking and nutrient signaling: WDR24 (0.720, high confidence; GATOR1 complex subunit regulating mTORC1), SNF8 (0.654; ESCRT-III complex component for endosomal sorting), RIOX1/RIOX2 (0.574/0.629; ribosomal oxygenases), JMJD4/JMJD7 (0.492/0.535; JmjC family paralogs), TYW5 (0.468; tRNA modification enzyme), OGFOD3 (0.457; oxygenase), GFER (0.451; mitochondrial intermembrane space oxidoreductase), and KDM7A (0.420; histone H3K9 demethylase).\n\nThe WDR24-SNF8 axis is particularly revealing: WDR24 anchors GATOR1 to lysosomes where it inhibits mTORC1 under amino acid starvation, while SNF8 mediates ESCRT-dependent lysosomal membrane repair. JMJD8's high-confidence interaction with both suggests it may hydroxylate or demethylate components of the mTORC1 regulatory machinery or ESCRT complex, coupling nutrient sensing to membrane trafficking.\n\nHPA shows ubiquitous expression (detected in all tissues, low tissue specificity) with cell type enhancement, consistent with a housekeeping enzymatic function. ClinVar reports 103 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, indicating strong dosage sensitivity and disease relevance—though specific disease associations remain uncurated.\n\nHYPOTHESIS: JMJD8 is a 2-oxoglutarate-dependent protein hydroxylase/demethylase that modifies GATOR1-ESCRT complex components (WDR24, SNF8) at lysosomal membranes, coupling amino acid sensing to mTORC1 signaling and endosomal sorting. Its 103 ClinVar variants suggest perturbation of this regulatory node contributes to metabolic or trafficking disorders.
