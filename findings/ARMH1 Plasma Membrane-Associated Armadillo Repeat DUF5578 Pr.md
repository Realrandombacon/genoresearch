## Description
ARMH1 encodes a 440 amino acid plasma membrane and cytosolic protein (UniProt Q6PIY5) containing a domain of unknown function DUF5578 (IPR041090, PF17741 residues 1-268) and armadillo-like helical domain (IPR011989 residues 72-381, IPR016024 residues 83-364) characteristic of proteins that mediate protein-protein interactions through curved superhelical scaffolds. AlphaFold predicts high-confidence structure (pLDDT 87.9) indicating well-folded tertiary architecture with stable armadillo repeat folds suitable for specific binding partners. HPA data reveals plasma membrane and cytosolic localization with tissue-enhanced expression pattern, highest in testis (10.9 nTPM) and fallopian tube (7.7 nTPM), and immune cell enhancement, suggesting roles in membrane signaling and reproductive tissue function. STRING interactions identify 8 partners including armadillo helical domain protein ARMH2 (0.875, high confidence paralog), small GTPase RAB19 (0.489, vesicle trafficking), apoptotic scramblase XKR9 (0.480, phospholipid asymmetry), armadillo protein ARMH4 (0.479, membrane scaffolding), transmembrane protein TMEM53 (0.479, membrane topology), collagen COL6A5 (0.470, extracellular matrix), glutamate receptor interacting protein GRIP2 (0.457, synaptic scaffolding), and pro-apoptotic protein FAM162B (0.400, cell death), implicating ARMH1 in membrane dynamics, vesicle trafficking, and cell survival pathways. ClinVar documents 6 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly chromosome 1p copy number gains encompassing the ARMH1 locus, indicating dosage sensitivity in disease etiology. The DUF5578 domain combined with armadillo repeat structure and plasma membrane localization positions ARMH1 as a membrane-associated scaffolding protein that may coordinate vesicle trafficking or receptor signaling through transient interactions with small GTPases and apoptotic regulators. High-confidence interaction with ARMH2 (0.875) suggests functional redundancy or heterodimerization between armadillo family paralogs. Testis enrichment (10.9 nTPM) indicates potential roles in spermatid membrane remodeling or acrosome formation. Immune cell enhancement suggests additional functions in lymphocyte or macrophage membrane receptor clustering during immune activation. Disease-associated CNVs indicate haploinsufficiency or triplosensitivity mechanisms affecting neurodevelopment and immune function, potentially through dysregulation of membrane scaffolding or vesicle trafficking in proliferating cells.
