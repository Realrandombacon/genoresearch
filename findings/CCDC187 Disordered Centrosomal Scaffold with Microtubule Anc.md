# CCDC187: Disordered Centrosomal Scaffold with Microtubule Anchoring Function and 44 Pathogenic CNVs

**Date:** 2026-03-18T06:38:43.555472

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
CCDC187 encodes a 1063 amino acid coiled-coil domain-containing protein localized to the centrosome. InterPro analysis identifies the centrosome-associated protein 350/CCDC187 family (IPR028750, aa 2-1015) with GO annotations for microtubule binding (GO:0008017), microtubule anchoring (GO:0034453), and centrosome localization (GO:0005813). STRING interactions reveal medium-confidence associations with cytoskeletal and centrosomal proteins: RADIL (0.591), RHOC (0.570), C20orf173 (0.544), CXorf65 (0.507), TEX33 (0.506), TMCO2 (0.505), and CCDC54 (0.484). HPA shows testis-enriched expression (28.3 nTPM) with lower levels in fallopian tube (7.1 nTPM) and pituitary gland (6.7 nTPM), classified as cancer-enhanced. ClinVar contains 44 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly chromosome 9q34.3 copy number losses affecting the locus. AlphaFold structure (pLDDT 44.3) indicates low confidence with predominantly disordered regions, consistent with an intrinsically disordered scaffold protein that functions through protein-protein interactions rather than enzymatic catalysis. Conservation extends to Mus musculus (958 aa, Q8C5V8) and Microcebus murinus (1102 aa), indicating conserved centrosomal function across mammals.
