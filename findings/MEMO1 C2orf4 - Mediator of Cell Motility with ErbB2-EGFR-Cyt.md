## Description
MEMO1 (Mediator of Cell Motility 1, formerly C2orf4) is a 297aa intracellular protein with a conserved MEMO1 family domain (IPR002737, PF01875, residues 9-294) and LigB-like fold (G3DSA:3.40.830.10) suggesting metal-binding capability. The protein localizes to vesicles and shows ubiquitous expression across all human tissues (HPA: low tissue specificity, detected in all). STRING interaction network reveals 10 high-confidence partners: RPL32 (0.900), UBE3D (0.624), RHOA (0.603), HMBOX1 (0.591), EGFR (0.557), CAMSAP2 (0.533), ERBB2 (0.532), KMO (0.526), DIAPH1 (0.522), NRG1 (0.513). This interaction profile places MEMO1 at the intersection of receptor tyrosine kinase signaling (EGFR/ERBB2/NRG1) and cytoskeletal dynamics (RHOA/DIAPH1/CAMSAP2). ClinVar contains 27 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including splice site mutations (c.658-2A>G/T) and copy number variations, indicating clinical relevance though specific disease associations remain undefined. The name 'mediator of ErbB2-driven cell motility' reflects its co-precipitation with ErbB2 C-terminus. UniProt accession Q9Y316. Functional hypothesis: MEMO1 serves as a signaling adaptor that physically links activated receptor tyrosine kinases (ErbB2/EGFR) to RHOA-mediated cytoskeletal reorganization, enabling directed cell migration. The vesicular localization suggests it may traffic signaling complexes or coordinate membrane-cytoskeleton coupling during motility. The LigB-like fold and metal-binding potential could enable redox sensing or metallochaperone activity, potentially regulating migration through oxidative state.
