## Description
C6orf98 (SYNE1) encodes nesprin-1, a massive 8797 amino acid nuclear envelope protein (UniProt Q8NF91). InterPro reveals extensive spectrin repeats (IPR002017, >30 repeats spanning aa 3175-8700) providing elastic rod-like structure, N-terminal actin-binding calponin homology domains (IPR001715, aa 27-283) for cytoskeletal anchoring, and a C-terminal KASH domain (IPR012315, aa 8738-8797) for outer nuclear membrane insertion. AlphaFold structure (pLDDT 76.6, residues 1-977) shows confident CH domain folding. HPA shows ubiquitous expression with nuclear membrane/nucleoplasmic localization. STRING interactions demonstrate LINC complex formation: SUN1/SUN2 (0.999) for KASH-SUN binding across perinuclear space, emerin/EMD (0.997) and lamin-A/LMNA (0.996) for inner nuclear membrane tethering, plus SYNE2/nesprin-2 (0.966) paralogue interaction. ClinVar contains 979 pathogenic variants including truncations (p.Gln6135Ter, p.Gln7188Ter) and frameshifts causing autosomal recessive cerebellar ataxia (ARCA1) and Emery-Dreifuss muscular dystrophy. Nesprin-1 forms mechanotransduction bridges: N-terminus binds F-actin via CH domains, C-terminal KASH domain docks to SUN proteins in perinuclear space, transmitting cytoskeletal forces to nuclear lamina for nuclear positioning, mechanosignaling, and chromatin organization.
