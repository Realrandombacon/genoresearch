## Description
C10orf132 (Gene ID: 414266, chromosome 10q24.1) encodes Golgin Subfamily A Member 7B (GOLGA7B), a 167 amino acid Golgi-associated protein (UniProt Q2TAP0). Despite the 'orf' nomenclature, this protein has well-characterized domains belonging to the Golgin/ERF4 family (InterPro IPR019383, Pfam PF10256, residues 21-133) and the Ras-specific palmitoyltransferase complex (IPR051371, PTHR13254, residues 4-156). This complex catalyzes protein S-palmitoylation, a reversible lipid modification critical for membrane anchoring and signal transduction. AlphaFold structure prediction yields high confidence (pLDDT 79.8), indicating a well-folded stable structure consistent with enzymatic function. Human Protein Atlas reveals brain-enriched expression (55.3 nTPM) with secondary expression in epididymis (22.1 nTPM), suggesting roles in neuronal protein trafficking and reproductive function. STRING interaction network identifies 10 partners including the catalytic palmitoyltransferase ZDHHC9 (0.718), sphingolipid biosynthesis enzymes (SPTSSA 0.651, SPTSSB 0.656, SPTLC1/2/3 0.580-0.586), and sphingolipid regulators (ORMDL1/2/3 0.558-0.608), placing GOLGA7B within the sphingolipid metabolism and protein palmitoylation pathway. The interaction with ZDHHC5 (0.487), another palmitoyltransferase, suggests functional redundancy or substrate specificity. ClinVar documents 17 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, exclusively large copy number alterations spanning 10q23-26, indicating dosage sensitivity though no monogenic disease phenotype is defined. The brain-enriched expression combined with palmitoyltransferase complex function suggests GOLGA7B may regulate neuronal receptor trafficking, synaptic protein localization, or myelin maintenance, with disruption potentially contributing to neurological disorders awaiting phenotypic correlation.
