## Description

This represents a critical finding: C6orf125 is NOT a dark gene but rather a fully annotated mitochondrial protein with established function in Complex III (cytochrome bc1 complex) biogenesis. UniProt accession Q9BRT2 encodes a 126-amino acid mitochondrial protein. These interactions place UQCC2 within the Complex III assembly module alongside UQCC1 and UQCC3. Human Protein Atlas (ENSG00000137288) classifies UQCC2 as disease-related gene, predicted intracellular protein with mitochondrial and nuclear body localization, low tissue specificity (detected in all tissues), low cancer specificity, cell type enhanced single-cell specificity, and primary mitochondrial disease involvement. ClinVar contains 20 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including in-frame deletions (c.337_339del p.Glu113del), missense variants (c.7G>C p.Ala3Pro, c.261G>C p.Glu87Asp, c.61A>C p.Thr21Pro, c.101G>A p.Arg34Gln), splice variants (c.214-9G>A), 5UTR variants (c.-2A>C), and copy number deletions (6q21 deletions), indicating strong association with mitochondrial disease and respiratory chain dysfunction. Functional role: UQCC2 encodes a mitochondrial matrix protein that functions as a dedicated assembly chaperone for Complex III (ubiquinol-cytochrome c reductase), facilitating proper folding, heme incorporation, and subunit assembly of the cytochrome bc1 complex. Mechanistic proposal: UQCC2 likely functions as part of a heterotrimeric complex with UQCC1 and UQCC3, forming a scaffold that stabilizes nascent Complex III subunits (particularly MT-CYB/cytochrome b), recruits assembly factors (LYRM7, COX20, TTC19), and coordinates heme b incorporation into the catalytic core. UQCC2 may act as a holdase chaperone preventing aggregation of hydrophobic transmembrane segments during membrane insertion. Loss-of-function UQCC2 variants cause primary mitochondrial disease through Complex III deficiency, impairing electron transport chain function and ATP production.
