# KIAA0408 - SOGA Family Coiled-Coil Dark Gene with Muscle/Brain Expression and Immune-Chromatin Interactome

**Date:** 2026-03-16T08:58:04.957434

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
KIAA0408 (Q6ZU52) is a 694 aa uncharacterized dark gene encoding a nucleoplasmic/cytosolic protein containing N-terminal SOGA1/2-like coiled-coil domain (aa 1-54, IPR027882, PF14818). BLAST reveals distant homology to microtubule cross-linking factor 1 (MTCL1, 49-82% identity on short regions), suggesting cytoskeletal association. STRING interactions reveal dual network: (1) SOGA3 (0.715, same coiled-coil family), ABI3 (0.717, immune adaptor protein in myeloid cells); (2) chromatin/transcription: POLR3C (0.591, RNA polymerase III subunit), PHF1 (0.591, Polycomb chromatin factor), ZNF628/ZNF491 (zinc finger transcription factors). HPA shows tissue-enhanced expression: skeletal muscle (26.4 nTPM), brain (21.6 nTPM), cancer-enhanced pattern, not detected in immune cells despite ABI3 interaction. ClinVar contains 16 pathogenic/likely pathogenic copy number variants on 6q21-25.3, confirming strong disease relevance. True dark gene: conserved SOGA coiled-coil domain, muscle/brain enriched, cancer-enhanced, clinically implicated with extensive CNV burden, interacts with both cytoskeletal (MTCL1 homology) and chromatin factors, but molecular function completely unknown. Hypothesis: may function as tissue-specific chromatin-cytoskeleton linker, potentially recruiting RNA polymerase III or Polycomb complexes to muscle/brain-specific loci via coiled-coil oligomerization, with ABI3 interaction suggesting latent immune regulatory role unmasked in disease states.
