## Description
DTD2 (formerly C14orf126) encodes a 168 amino acid D-aminoacyl-tRNA deacylase that functions as a translational proofreading enzyme. InterPro analysis reveals a conserved D-Tyr-tRNA(Tyr) deacylase domain (IPR003732, PF02580, residues 11-167) belonging to the DTD-like superfamily (G3DSA:3.50.80.10), with catalytic activity for deacylating mischarged D-aminoacyl-tRNAs and glycyl-tRNA(Ala). AlphaFold structure shows exceptional confidence (pLDDT 96.1), indicating a well-folded compact enzyme. HPA data shows ubiquitous expression across all tissues with low tissue specificity, localized to vesicles. STRING interactions identify 10 protein partners including DTD1-2 (0.793), TMEM19 (0.660), YARS1 (0.655), HEATR5A (0.630), and NUBPL (0.578), suggesting functional connections to tRNA metabolism and mitochondrial iron-sulfur cluster assembly. ClinVar contains 25 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly copy number variations affecting chromosome 14q12-21.2 region, though specific disease phenotypes remain to be fully characterized. This gene represents a transition from 'dark' to characterized status - originally annotated as chromosome 14 ORF, now recognized as a conserved tRNA proofreading enzyme with clinical significance.
