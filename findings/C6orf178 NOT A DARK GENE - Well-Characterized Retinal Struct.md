## Description
C6orf178 (Gene ID: 387108), historically named "chromosome 6 open reading frame 178", is actually a WELL-CHARACTERIZED retinal structural protein now officially named EYS (Eyes Shut Homolog). This represents a critical finding: C6orf178 is NOT a dark gene but rather a fully annotated photoreceptor-specific protein with established function in retinal structure maintenance and strong association with retinitis pigmentosa. UniProt accession Q5T1H1 encodes a massive 3165-amino acid secreted/membrane-associated protein. ClinVar contains 1388 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including splice site mutations (c.5645-2A>G, c.5836-1G>T), nonsense mutations (c.3195T>A p.Cys1065Ter, c.241C>T p.Gln81Ter, c.4325C>A p.Ser1442Ter), frameshifts (c.3215del p.Thr1072fs, c.8366del p.Gly2789fs), large deletions (c.2642-1177_2846+2006del, c.7326del p.His2441_Tyr2442insTer, c.8325del p.Lys2775_Val2776insTer), indicating strong association with autosomal recessive retinitis pigmentosa (RP25 locus). Functional role: EYS encodes a large extracellular matrix protein localized to photoreceptor outer segments that maintains retinal structure and photoreceptor integrity through cell adhesion and scaffold functions. Mechanistic proposal: EYS functions through extracellular domain-mediated interactions - the multiple EGF-like domains mediate calcium-dependent protein-protein interactions with photoreceptor matrix components, while the Laminin G domains mediate cell-matrix adhesion to the interphotoreceptor matrix. EYS likely forms a structural scaffold that anchors photoreceptor outer segments to the retinal pigment epithelium, maintaining outer segment disc stacking and preventing degeneration. The large size (3165 aa) with multiple repeats suggests EYS acts as a molecular spacer/adaptor in the subretinal space. Loss-of-function EYS variants cause retinitis pigmentosa through disrupted photoreceptor structural integrity, leading to progressive rod then cone degeneration. EYS is the human ortholog of Drosophila eyes shut (eysh), required for rhabdomere structure. This gene should be SKIPPED from dark gene analysis - it represents historical nomenclature (C#orf#) superseded by functional annotation (EYS).
