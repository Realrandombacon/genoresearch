## Description
C6orf86 (PSMG4) encodes a 123-amino acid proteasome assembly chaperone 4 (UniProt Q5JS54) that functions in proteasome biogenesis. InterPro analysis reveals a conserved proteasome assembly chaperone 4 domain (IPR032157, PF16093) spanning residues 26-97, indicating specialized chaperone activity. STRING interaction network shows high-confidence binding to core proteasome subunits: PSMA1 (0.822), PSMB9 (0.770), POMP (0.705), PSMA5 (0.696), and other assembly factors PSMG2 (0.601), PSMG1 (0.576), PSMG3 (0.422). HPA data indicates ubiquitous expression across all tissues with low tissue specificity, nucleoplasmic and mitochondrial localization, and cell type-enriched single cell expression pattern. The protein is classified as essential with chaperone molecular function. This suggests PSMG4 acts as a dedicated assembly factor facilitating proper folding and oligomerization of 20S proteasome core particles, potentially coordinating nuclear proteasome biogenesis with mitochondrial protein quality control. The conserved domain architecture and strong interactions with multiple proteasome subunits indicate a scaffold role in early proteasome assembly steps.
