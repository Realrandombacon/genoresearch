We hypothesize C7orf13 functions as an intrinsically disordered regulatory module in neuronal contexts—potentially modulating synaptic protein assemblies, transcriptional co-regulation, or stress granule dynamics through short linear motifs (SLiMs) that evade domain detection. The lack of clinical variants may reflect functional redundancy, tissue-specific essentiality, or simply insufficient clinical sequencing coverage. Future work should prioritize interaction proteomics in neural cell lines and phosphoproteomic mapping to identify condition-dependent binding partners.
