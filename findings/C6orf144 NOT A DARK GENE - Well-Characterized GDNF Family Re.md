## Description
C6orf144 (Gene ID: 387096), historically named "chromosome 6 open reading frame 144", is actually a WELL-CHARACTERIZED GDNF family receptor now officially named GFRAL (GDNF Family Receptor Alpha Like). This represents a critical finding: C6orf144 is NOT a dark gene but rather a fully annotated GPI-anchored co-receptor with established function in GDNF/RET signaling and energy balance regulation. UniProt accession Q6UXV0 encodes a 394-amino acid secreted/membrane-associated protein. InterPro domains confirm functional annotation: IPR003438 (Glial cell line-derived neurotrophic factor receptor, residues 9-343, signaling receptor activity GO:0038023), IPR016017 (GDNF/GAS1 domain, 24-101/131-210/220-316), IPR037193 (GDNF receptor alpha homologous superfamily, 130-214/216-320), PF02351 (GDNF/GAS1 domain, 131-210/220-316), PTHR10269 (GDNF receptor alpha family, 9-343), SM00907 (GDNF/GAS1 domain, 24-101/131-210/220-316). The triple GDNF/GAS1 domain architecture is characteristic of ligand-binding co-receptors that present neurotrophic factors to tyrosine kinase receptors. AlphaFold structure prediction shows global pLDDT score of 76.8 (high confidence), indicating well-folded stable protein with defined ligand-binding domains, consistent with structured receptor architecture. STRING interactions reveal 7 high-confidence protein partners within GDNF/RET signaling: RET (0.998, proto-oncogene tyrosine kinase receptor), GDNF (0.920, glial cell line-derived neurotrophic factor), NRTN (0.643, neurturin), ARTN (0.620, artemin), GDF15 (0.570, growth differentiation factor 15), PSPN (0.467, persephin), TMED1 (0.461, transmembrane p24 trafficking protein 1). These interactions place GFRAL within the GDNF family ligand-receptor complex, particularly as a GDF15 co-receptor regulating energy balance. Human Protein Atlas (ENSG00000187871) classifies GFRAL as predicted membrane protein with nucleoplasm/plasma membrane/actin filament/focal adhesion localization, not detected in bulk tissues (highly restricted expression), cell type enhanced single-cell specificity, and hormone/receptor molecular function. ClinVar contains 9 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, all copy number alterations affecting 6p12.3-12.1 region (multiple deletions and duplications spanning 6p12.1-q12, including chr6:50181657-55538355x1, chr6:43636308-64947206x3, chr6:48626041-55575545x1, chr6:50971182-57432788x1, chr6:54263893-56826637x1), indicating strong association with 6p chromosomal instability syndromes. Functional role: GFRAL encodes a GPI-anchored co-receptor that binds GDF15 (growth differentiation factor 15, a stress-induced cytokine) and presents it to RET tyrosine kinase receptor, activating downstream signaling in hindbrain appetite centers to suppress food intake and regulate energy homeostasis. Mechanistic proposal: GFRAL functions through ligand-induced heterodimerization with RET - GDF15 binding to GFRAL induces conformational change that recruits RET, triggering RET autophosphorylation and downstream MAPK/PI3K signaling in area postrema and nucleus tractus solitarius neurons. This GFRAL-RET-GDF15 axis mediates anorectic responses to metabolic stress, inflammation, and cachexia. GFRAL shares structural homology with GFRα1-4 co-receptors but exhibits selective GDF15 specificity. Loss-of-function GFRAL variants may cause metabolic dysregulation through impaired GDF15 signaling. This gene should be SKIPPED from dark gene analysis - it represents historical nomenclature (C#orf#) superseded by functional annotation (GFRAL).
