# C1orf39 (FNBP1L) - F-BAR Membrane Curvature Sensor with CDC42-WASP Actin Regulation

**Date:** 2026-03-17T02:09:46.135620

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
C1orf39 encodes FNBP1L (Formin-binding protein 1-like/TAB2), a 605aa multi-domain scaffold protein with high-confidence AlphaFold structure (pLDDT 79.2). The protein contains three functional domains: an N-terminal F-BAR domain (IPR031160, aa 1-263) for membrane curvature sensing and tubulation, a central HR1 Rho-binding domain (IPR011072, aa 397-474) for CDC42 interaction, and a C-terminal SH3 domain (IPR001452, aa 538-599) for proline-rich motif binding. STRING analysis reveals FNBP1L forms a high-confidence interactome with actin cytoskeleton regulators: CDC42 (0.997), WAS (0.960), WIPF1 (0.958), FNBP1 (0.945), ATG3 (0.917), SQSTM1 (0.909), WASF1 (0.868), and WASL (0.701), positioning it as a node in CDC42-WASP-mediated actin polymerization. HPA shows ubiquitous expression with immune cell enrichment, localized to vesicles and cytosol, consistent with endocytic function. ClinVar documents 10 pathogenic variants including copy number losses/gains at 1p22.1-21.3. The mechanistic hypothesis: FNBP1L senses membrane curvature via F-BAR dimerization, recruits CDC42 through HR1 domain, and activates WASP/WAVE complex via SH3-mediated proline-rich binding to drive localized actin polymerization during endocytosis, phagocytosis, and immune synapse formation. Loss-of-function disrupts membrane remodeling and vesicle trafficking.
