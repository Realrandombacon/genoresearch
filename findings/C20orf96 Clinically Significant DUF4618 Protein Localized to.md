# C20orf96: Clinically Significant DUF4618 Protein Localized to Centriolar Satellites with DNA Repair Interactions

## Description
C20orf96 (Gene ID 140680) encodes a 363 amino acid uncharacterized protein (UniProt Q9NUD7) containing a Domain of Unknown Function DUF4618 (IPR029236, PF15397, residues 105-362). Despite its 'uncharacterized' status, this gene shows remarkable clinical and functional signatures: (1) ClinVar contains 54 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including splice site mutations (c.20+5G>A, c.565+1G>A) and missense changes (p.Ile305Phe), plus large copy number alterations on chromosome 20p13 - indicating strong disease association; (2) STRING interactions reveal connections to the SHIELDIN DNA repair complex: SHLD2 (0.691), SHLD3 (0.648), SHLD1 (0.443), plus MAD2L2 (0.526) involved in DNA damage checkpoint control; (3) HPA localizes the protein to Centriolar Satellites - pericentriolar structures that traffic proteins to centrosomes during cell division. This triad of evidence suggests C20orf96 functions at the intersection of DNA damage response and centrosome biology, potentially coordinating repair factor recruitment during mitosis. The high pathogenic variant burden indicates loss-of-function causes human disease, making this a priority for functional characterization.
