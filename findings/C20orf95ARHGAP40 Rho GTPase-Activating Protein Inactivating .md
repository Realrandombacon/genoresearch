## Description
C20orf95 (now designated ARHGAP40, Rho GTPase-Activating Protein 40) is a 675-amino acid cytosolic enzyme functioning as a GTPase-activating protein (GAP) that accelerates intrinsic GTP hydrolysis of RHOA and CDC42 small GTPases, converting them from active GTP-bound to inactive GDP-bound states. Structural analysis reveals a canonical RhoGAP architecture: central RhoGAP catalytic domain (IPR000198, IPR008936, PF00620, PS50238, SM00324, residues 323-522) containing the catalytic arginine finger essential for GTPase stimulation, N-terminal Rho GTPase activation protein superfamily region (G3DSA:1.10.555.10, residues 294-536), and C-terminal ubiquitin-like domain (IPR057323, PF25442, residues 574-672) unique to the RHG40/28/18 subfamily potentially mediating protein-protein interactions or subcellular targeting. The protein belongs to the Rho GTPase-activating family (PTHR14963, residues 109-674). The AlphaFold predicted structure (Q5TG30) shows high confidence (pLDDT 71.9), confirming a well-folded GAP domain embedded within structured regulatory regions. Expression profiling from the Human Protein Atlas demonstrates group-enriched expression with highest levels in skin (42.4 nTPM), epididymis (33.6 nTPM), breast (28.5 nTPM), and esophagus (25.1 nTPM), with cancer-enhanced expression suggesting oncogenic potential, and subcellular localization to nucleoplasm, plasma membrane, centrosome, and cytosol—supporting functions in cytoskeletal dynamics, cell division, and signal transduction. Protein interaction networks (STRING) reveal medium-confidence enrichment for Rho GTPase pathway components: RHOA (score 0.631)—primary GTPase substrate regulating actin stress fibers and focal adhesions—CDC42 (0.626)—GTPase controlling filopodia formation and cell polarity—C11orf98 (0.507), SLC32A1 (0.476), VSTM2L (0.467), KIAA1755 (0.441), TENM2 (0.413), CD2AP (0.407), and SH3KBP1 (0.405). These interactions place ARHGAP40 within Rho GTPase regulatory networks controlling cell morphology, migration, and cytokinesis. ClinVar documents 9 pathogenic or likely pathogenic variants including large chromosomal copy number losses (20q11.21-13.12, 20q11.22-13.12) and gains (20p13-q13.33), plus missense SNVs (p.Gln615Glu, p.His516Asn), indicating dosage sensitivity and potential involvement in genomic instability disorders, though specific monogenic disease phenotypes await characterization. The mechanistic hypothesis proposes that ARHGAP40 functions as a negative regulator of Rho GTPase signaling by binding switch regions of GTP-loaded RHOA/CDC42 through the GAP domain, inserting an arginine finger into the GTPase active site to stabilize the transition state and accelerate GTP hydrolysis by >1000-fold. The C-terminal ubiquitin-like domain may mediate interactions with proteasomal machinery or ubiquitin-binding partners, linking GTPase regulation to protein turnover. Centrosomal localization suggests roles in mitotic spindle positioning and cytokinetic abscission. Cancer-enhanced expression implies ARHGAP40 may function as context-dependent oncogene or tumor suppressor, modulating Rho-driven cell invasion and metastasis. This gene exemplifies successful resolution from "C20orf" dark gene annotation to a characterized Rho GTPase regulatory enzyme.
