## Description
C6orf175 (Gene ID: 387027), historically named "chromosome 6 open reading frame 175", is actually a WELL-CHARACTERIZED general transcription factor now officially named GTF2H5 (General Transcription Factor IIH Subunit 5), also known as TTDA (Trichothiodystrophy A). This represents a critical finding: C6orf175 is NOT a dark gene but rather a fully annotated TFIIH complex subunit with established function in nucleotide excision repair (NER) and RNA polymerase II transcription initiation. UniProt accession Q6ZYL4 encodes a 71-amino acid nuclear protein. InterPro domains confirm functional annotation: IPR009400 (TFIIH subunit TTDA/Tfb5, residues 1-69, nucleotide-excision repair GO:0006289, transcription initiation at RNA polymerase II promoter GO:0006367, transcription factor TFIIH core complex GO:0000439), PF06331 (Transcription factor TFIIH complex subunit Tfb5, 1-67), PTHR28580 (General transcription factor IIH subunit 5, 1-69), SM01395 (TFIIH complex subunit Tfb5, 1-68), G3DSA:3.30.70.1220 (TFB5-like superfamily, 1-71). The TFB5 fold is characteristic of small zinc-finger-like TFIIH subunits that stabilize the complex. STRING interactions reveal 10 very high-confidence protein partners within TFIIH complex: GTF2H4 (0.999, general transcription factor IIH subunit 4), GTF2H1 (0.999, general transcription factor IIH subunit 1), ERCC3 (0.999, ERCC excision repair 3/XD/XPB helicase), ERCC2 (0.999, ERCC excision repair 2/XPD helicase), GTF2H3 (0.999, general transcription factor IIH subunit 3), GTF2H2 (0.998, general transcription factor IIH subunit 2), CCNH (0.997, cyclin H), CDK7 (0.994, cyclin-dependent kinase 7), GTF2E1 (0.990, general transcription factor IIE subunit 1), TBP (0.955, TATA-box binding protein). These interactions place GTF2H5/TTDA within the 10-subunit TFIIH holoenzyme complex. Human Protein Atlas (ENSG00000272047) classifies GTF2H5 as disease-related gene, predicted intracellular protein with low tissue specificity (detected in all tissues), low cancer specificity, cell type enhanced single-cell specificity, and ichthyosis disease involvement. ClinVar contains 40 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including missense mutations (c.1A>G p.Met1Val, c.20G>A p.Gly7Glu), frameshift (c.122del p.Thr41fs), and large copy number alterations (6q25.2-27 duplications/deletions), indicating strong association with trichothiodystrophy and DNA repair disorders. Functional role: GTF2H5 encodes a small zinc-finger-like subunit of the TFIIH complex that stabilizes the interaction between the core TFIIH (XPB, XPD, p62, p52, p44, p34, TTDA) and the kinase module (CDK7, CCNH, MAT1), enabling dual functions in global genome nucleotide excision repair (GG-NER) and transcription initiation. Mechanistic proposal: GTF2H5/TTDA functions through protein-protein interactions that bridge the p52 and p62 subunits, stabilizing the open conformation of TFIIH required for DNA unwinding at damaged sites. TTDA deficiency destabilizes the entire TFIIH complex, impairing both NER (causing UV sensitivity and DNA damage accumulation) and transcription (causing developmental defects). The small size (71 aa) suggests TTDA acts as a molecular staple rather than catalytic subunit. Loss-of-function GTF2H5 variants cause trichothiodystrophy (TTD) through combined DNA repair deficiency and transcriptional dysregulation, particularly affecting highly expressed genes in skin and hair follicles (explaining ichthyosis phenotype). This gene should be SKIPPED from dark gene analysis - it represents historical nomenclature (C#orf#) superseded by functional annotation (GTF2H5/TTDA).
