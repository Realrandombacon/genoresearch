# C16orf1/ZDHHC1 - Immune-Enhanced 485aa DHHC Palmitoyltransferase Modifying NCDN and NLRP3 in Innate Immune Regulation

**Date:** 2026-03-16T03:14:03.064608

**Quality Score:** 5/10 (GOOD)

## Description
C16orf1 (ZDHHC1) encodes a 485 aa palmitoyltransferase (UniProt Q8WTX9) named Zinc finger DHHC-type containing 1. Contains catalytic DHHC palmitoyltransferase domain (IPR001594, PF01529, residues 132-284) with conserved DHHC motif essential for palmitate transfer activity. Member of ZDHHC family of protein S-acyltransferases that catalyze addition of palmitate onto cysteine residues of substrate proteins. Recent research (PubMed:39173637) demonstrates palmitoyltransferase activity toward NCDN (neurocdin) and NLRP3 (NOD-like receptor family pyrin domain containing 3), regulating NLRP3 inflammasome activation - critical for innate immune response. Shows immune cell enhanced expression pattern with low tissue specificity - detected in many tissues. Localizes to cytosol as predicted membrane protein. Protein class: enzymes, metabolic proteins, acyltransferase, transferase. High-confidence interactions with other ZDHHC family members (ZDHHC13 0.634, ZDHHC22 0.621, ZDHHC24 0.467) indicate functional redundancy and shared substrate specificity. Interactions with ubiquitin ligases (RNF5 0.529, RNF26 0.492, TRIM56 0.462) suggest coordination between palmitoylation and ubiquitination pathways. Immune-related partners (NLRC3 0.498) consistent with inflammasome regulation role. Lipid metabolism enzymes (PPT1 0.472, LYPLA2 0.438) represent depalmitoylating enzymes - dynamic palmitate cycling. ER membrane protein AMFR (0.479) suggests ER localization for substrate modification. Represents a former 'dark gene' now recognized as critical post-translational modifier regulating inflammasome activation and innate immunity through protein palmitoylation.
