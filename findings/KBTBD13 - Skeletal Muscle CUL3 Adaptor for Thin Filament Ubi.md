# KBTBD13 - Skeletal Muscle CUL3 Adaptor for Thin Filament Ubiquitination (Nemaline Myopathy 6)

**Date:** 2026-03-17T08:42:04.113761

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
KBTBD13 (UniProt C9JR72) encodes a 458aa BTB-Kelch family protein on chromosome 15q22.31 with established function as a substrate-specific adapter of BCR (BTB-CUL3-RBX1) E3 ubiquitin ligase complexes. InterPro analysis reveals canonical BTB-Kelch architecture: N-terminal BTB/POZ domain (IPR000210, PF00651, residues 7-102) for CUL3 binding and dimerization, central BACK domain (residues 101-187) linking BTB to Kelch, and C-terminal Kelch-type beta-propeller (IPR015915, IPR006652, PF01344, residues 166-430) with multiple Kelch repeats forming a 6-bladed beta-propeller for substrate recognition. AlphaFold structure prediction shows very high confidence (pLDDT 90.2), indicating a stable, well-folded scaffold typical of functional E3 ligase adaptors. HPA data demonstrates striking skeletal muscle-enriched expression (28.8 nTPM) with tissue-enriched specificity and intracellular localization, consistent with sarcomeric regulatory roles. STRING interaction network reveals 10 partners with exceptionally high-confidence binding to thin filament structural proteins: TNNT1 (0.932, troponin T), TPM2 (0.897, tropomyosin-2), TPM3 (0.884, tropomyosin-3), NEB (0.864, nebulin), LMOD3 (0.816, leiomodin-3), plus CUL3 (0.827, cullin-3 scaffold), NAE1 (0.692, ubiquitin activator), CFL2 (0.615, cofilin-2), ACTA1 (0.601, alpha-actin), and paralogous KLHL40 (0.583, muscle-specific E3 adaptor). This interactome strongly supports mechanistic function as a muscle-specific CUL3 adaptor targeting thin filament components for ubiquitin-mediated turnover and sarcomere maintenance. ClinVar contains 56 pathogenic/likely pathogenic missense variants (e.g., p.His28Asp, p.Glu135Ala, p.Thr402Ser, p.Ala289Thr, p.Leu65Pro) causing Nemaline myopathy type 6 (NEM6), a congenital myopathy characterized by rod bodies in muscle fibers and hypotonia. The combination of muscle-enriched expression, canonical BTB-Kelch E3 ligase architecture, CUL3 interaction, thin filament protein binding, high structural confidence, and monogenic disease causation establishes KBTBD13 as a well-characterized disease gene rather than a true dark gene. Mechanistic hypothesis: KBTBD13 functions as a sarcomere quality control E3 adaptor that ubiquitinates thin filament proteins (troponin, tropomyosin, actin) for proteasomal degradation during sarcomere assembly/turnover, with pathogenic variants disrupting substrate recognition or CUL3 binding causing protein aggregation and nemaline rod formation.
