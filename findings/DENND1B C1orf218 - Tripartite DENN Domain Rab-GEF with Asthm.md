# DENND1B (C1orf218) - Tripartite DENN Domain Rab-GEF with Asthma Association and Vesicle Trafficking Role

**Date:** 2026-03-17T02:04:31.024664

**Quality Score:** 9/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
DENND1B (C1orf218) encodes a 775 aa DENN domain-containing protein 1B (UniProt Q6P3S1) functioning as a guanine-nucleotide exchange factor (GEF) for Rab GTPases. The protein contains a canonical tripartite DENN domain architecture: uDENN (IPR005113, aa 9-89), cDENN (IPR001194/PF02141, aa 90-291), and dDENN (IPR005112/PF03455, aa 322-405), characteristic of Rab-specific GEFs that activate membrane trafficking GTPases. AlphaFold prediction (pLDDT 66.7) indicates medium confidence with structured DENN domains and potentially disordered C-terminal regions. STRING interactions reveal high-confidence partners including DENND1A (0.792), ORMDL3 (0.680), MADD (0.677), CLTC (0.669), and RAB35 (0.535), placing DENND1B in vesicle trafficking networks. HPA shows low tissue specificity with expression detected in many tissues, subcellular localization to nuclear speckles and cytosol, and disease association with asthma. ClinVar contains 23 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants (primarily copy number gains/losses at 1q31.3), suggesting dosage sensitivity. The ORMDL3 interaction is particularly notable as ORMDL3 is a well-established asthma susceptibility gene, suggesting DENND1B may modulate sphingolipid metabolism or ER stress pathways implicated in asthma pathogenesis.
