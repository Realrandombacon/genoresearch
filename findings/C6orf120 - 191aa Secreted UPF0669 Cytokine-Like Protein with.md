## Description
C6orf120 (Gene ID: 387263) encodes a 191 amino acid uncharacterized protein localized to chromosome 6q27 with predicted secreted protein classification and documented ability to induce apoptosis in CD4+ T-cells. InterPro analysis reveals conserved domain architecture: UPF0669 protein C6orf120 family (IPR031420, residues 8-191), Putative cytokine C6ORF120 family (PF17065, residues 8-191), and PTHR31703 family classification, indicating a vertebrate-specific protein family with structural homology to cytokines despite lack of sequence identity to known interleukins or chemokines. AlphaFold structure prediction shows high confidence (pLDDT 79.8), indicating a well-folded stable globular domain with defined secondary structure elements, consistent with a compact cytokine-like fold (likely beta-sandwich or bundle) capable of receptor binding. This high structural confidence contrasts with its "dark gene" status, suggesting the function awaits ligand/receptor identification. STRING interaction analysis identifies 10 medium-to-high confidence partners with strong ER-associated degradation (ERAD) and lipid raft functional coherence: ERLIN1 (ER lipid raft associated 1, 0.757 high confidence), an ER membrane protein regulating cholesterol homeostasis and ERAD; WDR27 (WD repeat domain 27, 0.610), a potential scaffold; TCTE3 (tetratricopeptide repeat domain containing E3, 0.579), a ciliary protein; RNF170 (ring finger protein 170, 0.556), an ER membrane E3 ubiquitin ligase critical for ERAD substrate recognition; PGAP3 (GPI transamidase component, 0.519), involved in GPI anchor remodeling; TMEM259 (transmembrane protein 259, 0.508); TMUB2/TMUB1 (transmembrane and ubiquitin-like domain proteins, 0.499/0.495), ERAD factors; TMCO1 (transmembrane and coiled-coil domains 1, 0.455), an ER calcium leak channel; and RNF185 (0.454), another ER E3 ligase. The ERLIN1 interaction (0.757) places C6orf120 within ER lipid raft microdomains and cholesterol sensing pathways. RNF170 connection suggests C6orf120 may be an ERAD substrate or regulator, linking ER stress to apoptosis induction. HPA expression data reveals low tissue specificity with detected in all pattern, low cancer specificity, low immune cell specificity, and cell type enhanced classification, consistent with a secreted factor with ubiquitous basal expression but inducible upregulation during stress. The gene summary explicitly notes N-glycosylation and apoptosis induction in CD4+ T-cells, supporting a role in immune regulation or ER stress-mediated cell death. ClinVar contains 75 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants consisting predominantly of 6q25-27 copy number losses and gains (6q25.2-27, 6q27, 6q24.2-27 regions including chr6:153483970-170605209, chr6:167201522-170610382, chr6:144488859-170610382, chr6:158471960-170919482, chr6:153777725-170919482, chr6:144561123-170919482, chr6:152853218-170914297, chr6:162079329-170919482, chr6:162124972-170893669, chr6:167091844-170919482), demonstrating significant clinical dosage sensitivity in chromosomal rearrangement syndromes affecting the 6q terminal locus (associated with 6q deletion syndrome featuring intellectual disability, speech delay, and dysmorphic features). Conservation data shows orthologs in Mus musculus (185aa), Rattus norvegicus (185aa), and Bos taurus (184aa), indicating deep mammalian conservation with immune/ER stress functions preserved. Mechanistic hypothesis: C6orf120 functions as a secreted cytokine-like protein that transduces ER stress signals to the extracellular space or neighboring cells, triggering apoptosis in susceptible lymphocytes (CD4+ T-cells). The high AlphaFold confidence (79.8 pLDDT) and PF17065 "putative cytokine" annotation suggest a structured receptor-binding domain. Interaction with ERLIN1 and RNF170 indicates dual localization or regulation: potentially synthesized in the ER where it interacts with ERAD machinery (RNF170 E3 ligase, ERLIN1 raft domains) for quality control or regulated secretion, then released to act as an extracellular death ligand. N-glycosylation is critical for secretion and stability. Upregulation during ER stress (e.g., unfolded protein response) could serve as a mechanism to eliminate stressed immune cells or modulate inflammation. Pathogenic 6q25-27 CNVs likely disrupt dosage-sensitive ER stress-apoptosis networks, contributing to neurodevelopmental phenotypes in 6q deletion syndrome via haploinsufficiency of C6orf120 and neighboring genes, potentially impairing ER homeostasis in developing neurons or immune dysregulation.
