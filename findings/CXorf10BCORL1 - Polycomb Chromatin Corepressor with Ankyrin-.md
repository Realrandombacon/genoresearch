## Description
BCORL1 (CXorf10) encodes a 1785 aa nuclear protein functioning as a BCL-6 corepressor homolog in Polycomb group (PcG) chromatin regulation. InterPro reveals C-terminal ankyrin repeats (IPR002110, PF12796, positions 1500-1591) and a BCORL-PCGF1-binding domain (IPR032365, PF16553, positions 1667-1782) that mediates interaction with Polycomb group RING finger proteins. AlphaFold predicts low global confidence (pLDDT 38.5), indicating an intrinsically disordered protein (IDP) that functions through dynamic protein-protein interactions rather than enzymatic catalysis. STRING interactions confirm BCORL1 operates within PRC1 complex machinery: PCGF1 (0.981), SKP1 (0.927), RYBP (0.920), YAF2 (0.908), RING1/RNF2 (0.830/0.800), PCGF2/3 (0.806/0.817), KDM2B (0.760), and CTBP1 (0.471). These partners form Polycomb repressive complexes that silence target genes through H2AK119 ubiquitination and chromatin compaction. The ankyrin-PCGF1 domain architecture positions BCORL1 as a scaffold protein recruiting PRC1 to specific genomic loci, likely competing or cooperating with canonical BCOR. No ClinVar variants suggest no monogenic disease associations, though BCORL1 may contribute to cancer epigenetics. X-chromosome location (Xq26) indicates potential sex-biased expression patterns.
