# C10orf64/WDFY4 - Lymphoid-Enriched BEACH Domain Scaffold Protein in Autophagy and Immune Signaling

## Description
C10orf64 (Gene ID: 159491) is a historical alias for WDFY4 (WD repeat and FYVE domain containing 4, Gene ID: 159491), a massive 3184 amino acid scaffolding protein (UniProt Q6ZS81) encoded on chromosome 10q11.22. Despite the 'orf' name, WDFY4 is a well-characterized member of the BEACH domain protein family, crucial for vesicular trafficking and autophagy. The protein architecture is defined by multiple functional domains: an N-terminal Armadillo-like helical region (IPR011989, residues 452-936), a central BEACH domain (IPR000409, residues 2527-2821) critical for membrane dynamics, a Pleckstrin Homology (PH) domain (IPR011993, residues 2398-2508) for phosphoinositide binding, and a C-terminal cluster of WD40 repeats (IPR001680, residues 2921-3109) mediating protein-protein interactions. AlphaFold predicts a confident structure (pLDDT 78.5) for the modeled region (1-1042), supporting the stability of these domains. Human Protein Atlas reveals strong lymphoid tissue enrichment (26.6 nTPM) and bone marrow expression (11.0 nTPM), with group-enriched expression in blood cells, localizing to the nucleoplasm and cytosol. This expression pattern aligns with its role in immune cell function. STRING interaction analysis identifies high-confidence partners including LRRC18 (0.727), LAPTM5 (0.664), WDFY2 (0.587), and BANK1 (0.578), placing WDFY4 within a network of B-cell signaling and endolysosomal trafficking regulators. Notably, interactions with TLR4 (0.557) and transcription factors SPI1/IRF8 suggest a bridge between innate immune sensing and autophagic machinery. ClinVar documents 69 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, including splice site mutations and large copy number losses/gains on 10q11, indicating dosage sensitivity, though no single monogenic disease is yet definitively assigned. Mechanistically, WDFY4 likely functions as a selective autophagy adaptor (similar to its paralog WDFY3/ALFY), recruiting ubiquitinated cargo or signaling complexes (like TLR4) to the autophagosomal membrane via its BEACH and PH domains, thereby regulating inflammation and B-cell homeostasis in lymphoid tissues.
