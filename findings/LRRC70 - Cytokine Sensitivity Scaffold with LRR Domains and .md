# LRRC70 - Cytokine Sensitivity Scaffold with LRR Domains and Importin Interaction

**Date:** 2026-03-17T08:39:29.364461

**Quality Score:** 9/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
LRRC70 (UniProt Q7Z2Q7) encodes a 622aa leucine-rich repeat (LRR) protein on chromosome 5q12.1 with established role in rendering cells highly sensitive to cytokine and lipopolysaccharide (LPS) activation. InterPro analysis reveals extensive LRR architecture including multiple typical LRR repeats (IPR001611, positions 65-118, 158-226, 229-250), BspA-type LRR regions (IPR026906, PF13306, positions 217-338), and membership in the Multifunctional Developmental and Immune Receptor family (IPR050328, PTHR24373), consistent with pattern recognition or scaffolding functions in immune signaling. AlphaFold structure prediction shows high confidence (pLDDT 73.8), indicating a well-folded solenoid structure typical of LRR proteins that mediate protein-protein interactions. STRING interaction network identifies 10 partners with high-confidence binding to IPO11 (0.783, importin-11 nuclear transport receptor), medium-confidence links to ribosomal proteins RPL12 (0.621) and RPL23A (0.498), adhesion GPCRs ADGRL1/2/3 (0.551, latrophilins), ubiquitin conjugating enzyme UBE2E3 (0.516), and additional importin IPO5 (0.499), suggesting roles in nucleocytoplasmic trafficking, translational regulation, and GPCR signaling modulation. HPA data confirms low tissue specificity with detection across many tissues, cancer-enhanced expression pattern, and predicted membrane/intracellular localization. ClinVar contains 9 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, exclusively chromosome 5 copy-number alterations (deletions and duplications spanning 5q11.2-q13.2), indicating disease relevance through genomic instability though no specific Mendelian phenotype is defined. The combination of LRR scaffold architecture, importin interaction (nuclear transport), adhesion GPCR binding (synaptic/immune signaling), cancer-enhanced expression, and cytokine hypersensitivity function strongly supports a mechanistic hypothesis where LRRC70 acts as a cytoplasmic-nuclear shuttle adaptor that modulates inflammatory transcription factor trafficking or cell surface receptor turnover, potentially linking innate immune sensing to nuclear gene expression programs in stress-responsive contexts.
