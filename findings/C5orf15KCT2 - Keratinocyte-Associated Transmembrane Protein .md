## Description
C5orf15 (also known as KCT2 or HTGN29) encodes a 265-amino acid keratinocyte-associated transmembrane protein (UniProt Q8NC54) localized to the Golgi apparatus. The protein contains a conserved keratinocyte-associated transmembrane domain (IPR037645, PF17818, residues 66-265) characteristic of the PTHR16502 family. AlphaFold prediction shows medium confidence structure (pLDDT 57.4), suggesting a partially ordered protein with intrinsically disordered regions, consistent with regulatory or scaffolding functions. STRING analysis reveals 8 protein interaction partners including FXYD5 (score 0.503), TXNDC12 (0.443), EFCAB8 (0.434), and TMEM123 (0.423), indicating potential roles in membrane protein complexes and cellular signaling. HPA data shows ubiquitous expression across all tissues with Golgi apparatus localization, supporting a housekeeping function in protein trafficking or modification. The divergent transcript C5orf15-DT harbors 5 pathogenic copy number variants (gains and losses at 5q31.1), implicating this locus in genomic instability disorders. Conservation is evident across vertebrates (Gallus gallus homologs 264-389 aa). The combination of transmembrane topology, Golgi localization, and interaction with FXYD5 (a sodium-potassium ATPase regulator) suggests C5orf15/KCT2 functions as a Golgi membrane scaffold protein involved in ion transporter trafficking or glycosylation complex assembly. Medium pLDDT and disordered regions may enable conformational flexibility for dynamic protein-protein interactions.
