## Description
C7orf8 (CTTNBP2) encodes a 1663 amino acid cortactin-binding protein 2 (UniProt Q8WZ74). InterPro reveals domain architecture: N-terminal CTTNBP2 domain (IPR019131/PF09727, aa 36-138) for cortactin binding, central ankyrin repeats (IPR002110/PF00023, aa 708-876, three repeats) for protein-protein interactions, and C-terminal AAA+ ATPase lid domain (IPR057568/PF25408, aa 1305-1374). AlphaFold structure (pLDDT 58.0) shows low-medium confidence consistent with a large scaffold protein containing structured domains interspersed with flexible linkers. HPA shows midbody ring and cytosolic localization, cancer-enhanced expression, and immune cell enrichment. STRING interactions define membership in the STRIPAK complex (Striatin-interacting phosphatase and kinase): STRIP1 (0.998), STRN3/striatin-3 (0.998), STRIP2 (0.997), MOB4 (0.997), STRN4/striatin-4 (0.996), STK26/MST4 (0.996), PPP2CB/PP2A catalytic subunit (0.995), PPP2R1A (0.995), STK25 (0.995). ClinVar contains 25 pathogenic variants including frameshift (p.Ile1549fs) and chromosomal 7q copy number alterations. CTTNBP2 functions as a multivalent scaffold: N-terminus anchors to cortactin at actin-rich structures, ankyrin repeats recruit STRIPAK kinase/phosphatase modules, midbody localization enables cytokinetic regulation. The complex balances phosphorylation states of cytoskeletal regulators, coordinating cell migration, polarity, and abscission. Dysregulation contributes to cancer invasion and metastasis.
