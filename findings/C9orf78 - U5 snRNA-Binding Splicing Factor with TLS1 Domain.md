# C9orf78 - U5 snRNA-Binding Splicing Factor with TLS1 Domain

**Date:** 2026-03-16T08:15:40.665332

**Quality Score:** 7/10 (GOOD)

## Description
C9orf78 encodes a 289 aa splicing factor containing a Telomere Length and Silencing Protein 1 (TLS1) domain spanning nearly the entire sequence (residues 5-287) and an HCA59 (hepatocellular carcinoma-associated antigen) domain (101-197). Key functional insights: (1) UniProt annotation confirms role in pre-mRNA splicing - specifically promotes usage of upstream 3′-splice sites at alternative NAGNAG splice sites, enabling fine-tuned alternative splicing regulation; (2) STRING reveals 10 high-confidence spliceosome interactions: SNRNP200 (U5 snRNP helicase, 0.959), PRPF8 (scaffold protein, 0.951), SLU7 (3′-splice site recognition, 0.879), CDC40, EFTUD2, PPIL1 - placing C9orf78 at the catalytic core of the U5 snRNP; (3) HPA confirms nucleoplasmic localization with ubiquitous expression and Gene Ontology terms for mRNA splicing/chromosome partition; (4) 35 pathogenic ClinVar variants (CNVs and SNVs) demonstrate extreme dosage sensitivity - among the highest seen in dark genes; (5) BLAST shows remarkable conservation: 99.7% orangutan, 95.8% mouse, and significant homology to Arabidopsis COP1 SUPPRESSOR 2 (30.1%, E=2.28e-17) - indicating ancient eukaryotic origin. Function hypothesis: C9orf78 is a U5 snRNP-associated splicing regulator that fine-tunes alternative splice site selection at NAGNAG motifs. The TLS1 domain (typically associated with telomere silencing in yeast) may represent an evolutionary exaptation - in humans it likely mediates protein-protein interactions within the spliceosome rather than telomere functions. Its interaction with NKAPD1 (another dark gene with NF-kappa-B domain) suggests coordination between splicing regulation and inflammatory signaling. Extreme dosage sensitivity indicates it operates at stoichiometric thresholds in spliceosome assembly.
