## Description
C11orf68 (Q9H3H3) is a 292 aa uncharacterized dark gene (UPF0696 family) encoding a nucleolar protein with remarkable structural homology to eukaryotic translation initiation factor eIF4E. Contains eIF4E-like fold (aa 74-292, IPR023398, G3DSA:3.30.760.10) and Bles03 domain (aa 155-253, PF08939). Originally identified as basophilic leukemia-expressed protein (Bles03). Highly conserved across vertebrates: bovine 95.5%, rat 95.6%, zebrafish 56.9%, indicating ancient evolutionary origin. HPA shows ubiquitous expression across all tissues, nucleoplasmic/nucleolar/nucleolar rim localization, consistent with RNA processing function. ClinVar contains 11 pathogenic/likely pathogenic copy number variants on 11q12.1-13.3, confirming disease relevance. True dark gene: eIF4E-like structural fold suggests mRNA cap binding potential, nucleolar localization, RNA-binding protein interactome, leukemia association (Bles03 name), clinically implicated, but molecular function uncharacterized. Hypothesis: may function as non-canonical translation regulator or nucleolar RNA chaperone, potentially competing with eIF4E for cap binding or modulating ribosome biogenesis, with oncogenic potential in leukemia when dysregulated.
