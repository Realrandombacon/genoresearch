# C10orf90/FATS - p53 Activating E3 Ubiquitin Ligase with ALMS1 Homology

**Date:** 2026-03-16T08:16:43.520886

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
C10orf90 (FATS) encodes a 699 aa tumor suppressor with E2-independent E3 ubiquitin-conjugating enzyme activity. Critical functional insights: (1) UniProt confirms it activates p53/TP53 by inhibiting MDM2 binding and stimulating non-proteolytic polyubiquitination of p53, sustaining G2/M checkpoint after DNA damage; (2) InterPro reveals two domains: N-terminal centrosomal C10orf90 domain (52-563) and C-terminal ALMS motif (569-697) - homologous to Alström syndrome protein 1, suggesting centrosomal function; (3) STRING interactions confirm TP53 connection (0.568) plus CEP295 (centrosomal protein, 0.442) and DOCK1 (0.838); (4) HPA shows brain-enriched expression (18.7 nTPM) with cancer-enriched pattern, nucleoplasmic/cytosolic localization; (5) 70 pathogenic ClinVar variants (predominantly CNVs) demonstrate extreme dosage sensitivity - among the highest seen, confirming essential tumor suppressor function; (6) BLAST shows 66.3% identity with mouse, ALMS1 homology (E=9.86e-19). Function hypothesis: FATS is a centrosome-associated E3 ubiquitin ligase that couples DNA damage response to p53 activation. The ALMS1 homology suggests it localizes to centrosomes where it may coordinate cell cycle checkpoint signaling with centrosome integrity. Its E2-independent ubiquitin ligase activity represents an unusual mechanism - directly ubiquitinating p53 without E2 conjugating enzyme requirement. Extreme dosage sensitivity indicates it operates at precise stoichiometric thresholds in the DNA damage response pathway.
