## Description
TMEM151B (UniProt Q8IW70) encodes a 566-amino acid multi-pass transmembrane protein containing the conserved TMEM151 family domain (InterPro IPR026767, residues 36-566; Pfam PF14857, residues 46-492; PTHR31893 family). The domain spans nearly the entire protein sequence, indicating the TMEM151 fold constitutes the core functional unit. HPA data shows brain-enriched expression (135.4 nTPM) with cancer-enhanced expression pattern and predicted membrane localization, suggesting specialized neuronal functions. STRING interactions reveal medium-confidence associations (scores 0.404-0.620) with multiple transmembrane proteins (TMEM253, TMEM235, TMEM116, TMEM109) and neuronal partners including SNCB (synuclein-beta, 0.421), RIMS3 (regulating synaptic membrane exocytosis, 0.409), KCNJ9 (GIRK3 potassium channel, 0.404), and OPALIN (oligodendrocyte protein, 0.417). This interaction network strongly implicates TMEM151B in synaptic vesicle trafficking and neuronal membrane organization. The association with RIMS3 (active zone scaffold) and KCNJ9 (inward rectifier potassium channel) suggests TMEM151B may function as a synaptic membrane scaffold regulating neurotransmitter release or ion channel clustering. ClinVar documents 9 pathogenic copy number variants (losses and gains) on chromosome 6p, demonstrating clinical relevance. Disruption of TMEM151B could impair synaptic transmission or oligodendrocyte function, potentially contributing to neurological disorders. The brain-specific expression combined with synaptic protein interactions positions TMEM151B as a candidate regulator of neuronal excitability and synaptic plasticity.
