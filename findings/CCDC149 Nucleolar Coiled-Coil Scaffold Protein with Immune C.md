## Description

The coiled-coil architecture combined with nucleolar localization and immune enhancement positions CCDC149 as a nucleolar scaffold that may recruit ribosome biogenesis factors or nucleolar stress sensors in proliferating immune cells. Interaction with SRGAP family (RhoGAP regulators) and PPP phosphatase regulators suggests CCDC149 functions in coordinating cytoskeletal reorganization with cell cycle progression during immune activation. Disease-associated CNVs indicate haploinsufficiency or triplosensitivity mechanisms affecting neurodevelopment and immune function, potentially through dysregulation of nucleolar organization or ribosome production in developing neural and hematopoietic lineages.
