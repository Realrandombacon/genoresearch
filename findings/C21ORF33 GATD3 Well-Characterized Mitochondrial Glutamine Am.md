# C21ORF33 (GATD3): Well-Characterized Mitochondrial Glutamine Amidotransferase - Not a True Dark Gene

## Description
C21ORF33 is the historical symbol for GATD3 (Glutamine amidotransferase-like class 1 domain-containing protein 3, mitochondrial, Gene ID 54080), a well-characterized 268 amino acid enzyme (UniProt P0DPI2) with defined catalytic function. InterPro analysis reveals a canonical Type 1 glutamine amidotransferase domain (IPR029062, residues 41-268) belonging to the N-terminal nucleophile (Ntn) hydrolase superfamily (SSF52317), indicating enzymatic activity in glutamine metabolism. STRING interactions show high-confidence binding to N-acetyltransferases NAT1 (0.813) and NAT2 (0.767), plus autophagy receptor CALCOCO2 (0.712) and Parkinson's disease protein PARK7 (0.644), suggesting roles in xenobiotic metabolism and mitochondrial quality control. HPA dual-localizes GATD3 to mitochondria and centrosomes with highest expression in tongue (357.4 nTPM). ClinVar contains 93 pathogenic variants including copy number alterations on chromosome 21q22. The presence of catalytic amidotransferase domain, established metabolic interaction network, and clinical variant burden indicates C21ORF33/GATD3 is NOT a dark gene but a functionally annotated mitochondrial enzyme with roles in glutamine metabolism and potentially circadian regulation (per PANTHER family annotation).
