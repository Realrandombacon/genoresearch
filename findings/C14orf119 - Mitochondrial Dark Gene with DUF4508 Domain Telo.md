## Description
C14orf119 (Q9NWQ9) is a 140 aa uncharacterized dark gene encoding a predicted intracellular protein with dual mitochondrial and cytosolic localization. Contains single defining feature: DUF4508 domain (IPR028019, PF14969) covering nearly entire protein (aa 8-138, ~93%) — this domain of unknown function represents the core functional module with no homology to characterized proteins. BLAST shows strong mouse conservation (83.1% identity, E-value 3.42e-79), indicating functional constraint across mammals. STRING interactions reveal interaction network with mixed characterized and dark gene partners: STN1 (0.606, shelterin complex subunit — telomere capping/protection), SEL1L2 (0.540, SEL1-like repeat protein), PLAC9 (0.525, placenta-specific protein 9, dark gene), C5orf47 (0.515, dark gene on chromosome 5), LEO1 (0.506, RNA polymerase-associated protein — transcription elongation complex PAF1), HEATR3 (0.496, heat repeat-containing protein 3), JAKMIP3 (0.480, JAK-interacting protein 3). The STN1 interaction is particularly notable — STN1 is part of the CST complex critical for telomere capping and replication, suggesting C14orf119 may function in telomere maintenance or genome stability. LEO1 interaction suggests potential role in transcription elongation. HPA shows ubiquitous expression pattern (detected in all tissues, low tissue specificity) with cell type enhancement — unlike testis-enriched dark genes, this suggests housekeeping or fundamental cellular function. Mitochondrial localization is distinctive and may indicate role in energy metabolism or mitochondrial-nuclear communication. ClinVar contains 22 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly 14q copy number gains and losses (14q11.2-23.1, 14q11.2-21.3, 14q11.2-12), confirming strong disease relevance in 14q deletion/duplication syndromes. True dark gene: DUF4508 single-domain architecture (unknown function), ubiquitous expression (housekeeping signature), mitochondrial localization (energy metabolism potential), STN1 telomere interaction (genome stability), LEO1 transcription interaction (gene regulation), dark gene partnerships (PLAC9, C5orf47), clinically implicated with 14q CNV burden, but molecular mechanism completely unknown. Hypothesis: may function as a mitochondrial-nuclear shuttle factor that coordinates telomere maintenance (STN1 interaction) with transcriptional regulation (LEO1 interaction) during cell division, with DUF4508 domain mediating protein-protein interactions at telomeres or transcription complexes, ubiquitous expression indicating fundamental role in genome stability across all cell types, and dysregulation contributing to 14q deletion syndromes through telomere dysfunction or transcriptional dysregulation.
