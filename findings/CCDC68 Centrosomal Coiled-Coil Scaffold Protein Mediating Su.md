## Description
CCDC68 encodes a 335 amino acid coiled-coil domain-containing protein (UniProt Q9H2F9) localized to the centriole and subdistal appendage. InterPro analysis identifies membership in the Tuftelin/GRINL1A/MYZAP/CCD68 family (IPR051375, PTHR23171, residues 1-307) with a CATH-Gene3D homologous superfamily domain (G3DSA:1.10.287.1490, residues 154-321) suggesting structural similarity to centrosomal scaffolds. AlphaFold predicts a confident folded structure (pLDDT 80.5) across all 335 residues. STRING interactions reveal 10 high-confidence centrosomal partners: CCDC120 (0.976), CEP128 (0.962), MIA2 (0.960), CTAGE1 (0.955), CEP170 (0.896), ODF2 (0.853), NIN (0.837), CNTRL (0.779), DCTN1 (0.767), and KIF2A (0.763), indicating integration into the centriolar subdistal appendage complex for microtubule anchoring. HPA shows tissue-enhanced expression with highest levels in placenta (30.5 nTPM) and intestine (23.3 nTPM), with Golgi apparatus and cytosolic localization. ClinVar documents 60 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including large copy number gains/losses at 18q11-23, implicating CCDC68 in chromosomal instability syndromes. Conservation across vertebrates (mouse ortholog 333 aa) indicates functional constraint. Mechanistically, CCDC68 likely functions as a structural scaffold at the centriolar subdistal appendage, recruiting microtubule plus-end binding proteins and anchoring factors (CEP128, ODF2, ninein) to stabilize microtubule organizing center (MTOC) activity. The coiled-coil domains mediate oligomerization and protein-protein interactions, while the complex assembly enables proper spindle orientation during cell division and ciliogenesis. Disruption causes mitotic defects and ciliary dysfunction.
