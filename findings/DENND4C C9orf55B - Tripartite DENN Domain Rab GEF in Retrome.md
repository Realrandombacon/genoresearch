## Description
DENND4C (C9orf55B, UniProt Q5VZ89) is a 1909 aa multidomain protein featuring a canonical tripartite DENN domain architecture (uDENN: IPR005113/PF03456 aa 211-275; cDENN: IPR001194/PF02141 aa 309-492; dDENN: IPR005112/PF03455 aa 599-648) characteristic of Rab guanine nucleotide exchange factors (GEFs). Additional domains include an N-terminal MABP domain (IPR023341, aa 40-200) and C-terminal tetratricopeptide repeat (TPR) domain (IPR011990, aa 773-861) suggesting protein-protein interaction scaffolding. STRING interactions reveal functional partnerships with RAB10 (0.610), RAB8A (0.463), TBC1D5 (0.517), and retromer components VPS35 (0.495), VPS26A (0.437), VPS26B (0.473), plus GDI1 (0.462) and DENND5A (0.435). HPA localizes DENND4C to Golgi apparatus and vesicles with ubiquitous expression, annotated as a guanine-nucleotide releasing factor involved in protein transport. The interaction profile strongly suggests DENND4C activates RAB10/RAB8A GTPases to regulate retromer-mediated cargo sorting at endosomal-Golgi interfaces, potentially coordinating recycling of transmembrane receptors. The TPR domain may recruit specific cargo adaptors while the DENN catalytic core executes nucleotide exchange on Rab substrates.
