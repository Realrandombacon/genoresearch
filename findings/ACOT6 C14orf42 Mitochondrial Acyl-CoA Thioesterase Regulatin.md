# ACOT6 (C14orf42): Mitochondrial Acyl-CoA Thioesterase Regulating Fatty Acid Metabolism

**Date:** 2026-03-18T01:08:39.651901

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
ACOT6 (formerly C14orf42) encodes a 421 amino acid acyl-coenzyme A thioesterase that catalyzes hydrolysis of acyl-CoAs into free fatty acids and CoASH, regulating intracellular acyl-CoA levels. The protein contains characteristic acyl-CoA thioesterase domains (PF04775, PF08840, IPR006862, IPR014940) with an alpha/beta hydrolase fold (G3DSA:3.40.50.1820, IPR029058). AlphaFold predicts a well-folded structure with very high confidence (pLDDT: 94.9), consistent with stable enzymatic function. Tissue expression is enriched in metabolically active tissues: skeletal muscle (3.7 nTPM), kidney (3.4 nTPM), and liver (3.0 nTPM), supporting a role in fatty acid metabolism. STRING interactions reveal functional clustering with other acyl-CoA thioesterases (ACOT9: 0.623, ACOT8: 0.606, ACOT13: 0.565, ACOT12: 0.563, ACOT7: 0.503) and lipid metabolism enzyme MGLL (0.435), suggesting participation in coordinated acyl-CoA hydrolysis pathways. ClinVar documents 12 pathogenic/likely pathogenic copy number variants, indicating clinical relevance though specific disease associations remain unclear. The enzyme likely localizes to mitochondria or peroxisomes based on homology with related ACOT family members, where it modulates fatty acid beta-oxidation flux by controlling acyl-CoA substrate availability.
