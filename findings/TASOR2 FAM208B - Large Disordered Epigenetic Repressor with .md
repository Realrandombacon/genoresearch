## Description
TASOR2 (FAM208B) encodes a 2430 amino acid large intrinsically disordered protein (UniProt Q5VWN6, AlphaFold pLDDT 39.9) functioning as an epigenetic transcriptional repressor. InterPro reveals multiple functional domains: two DUF3715 repeats (IPR022188/PF12509, residues 553-701 and 857-1003), an N-terminal Rab2B-binding domain (IPR022168/PF12480, 94-166), and a C-terminal PIN domain (IPR056242/PF24630, 2281-2420) suggesting potential nucleic acid binding or catalytic activity. HPA shows ubiquitous expression with nucleoplasm and cytosol localization, consistent with epigenetic regulatory function. STRING interactions identify 10 medium-to-high confidence partners including ERH (0.777, enhancer of rudimentary homolog involved in transcription/splicing), ZNF200 (0.698, zinc finger transcription factor), TH (0.680, tyrosine hydroxylase), and several zinc finger proteins (ZNF597, ZZZ3), suggesting roles in transcriptional complexes. ClinVar contains 31 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including intronic SNVs and chromosome 10p15.3-12.31 copy number gains. The combination of large disordered structure, multiple DUF3715 repeats, PIN domain, and interaction with transcriptional machinery suggests TASOR2 functions as a scaffold protein recruiting epigenetic modifiers to chromatin, potentially through phase separation mechanisms. The PIN domain may provide nucleic acid binding specificity while DUF3715 repeats mediate protein-protein interactions.
