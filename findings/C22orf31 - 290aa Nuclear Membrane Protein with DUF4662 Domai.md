## Description
C22orf31 (Gene ID: 25770, aliases HS747E2A, bK747E2.1) encodes a 290 amino acid uncharacterized protein localized to chromosome 22q12.1 with predicted intracellular classification and dual nuclear membrane/cytosol subcellular localization. InterPro analysis reveals exclusive domain architecture: Protein of unknown function DUF4662 (IPR028970, residues 1-290), PF15578 domain (residues 2-263), and family classification PTHR15578, indicating a single-domain protein conserved across vertebrates including Pan troglodytes (290 aa) and Equus caballus (298-408 aa isoforms). AlphaFold structure prediction shows medium-low confidence (pLDDT 56.9), consistent with partially structured regions interspersed with intrinsically disordered segments, typical of regulatory scaffold proteins or chromatin adaptors. STRING interaction analysis identifies 9 medium-confidence partners led by HDAC2 (histone deacetylase 2, 0.601), a core catalytic subunit of Sin3 and NuRD chromatin repressor complexes, plus TEDC1 (0.541), ESYT3 (0.497), TMED10 (0.477), TP53I13 (0.476), LACRT (0.468), KRT33B (0.450), LONRF3 (0.447), and KLHL20 (0.408). The strong HDAC2 interaction strongly links C22orf31 to transcriptional repression and chromatin remodeling pathways. HPA expression data shows group-enriched RNA pattern with highest expression in testis (8.4 nTPM), followed by parathyroid gland (2.9 nTPM) and brain (2.6 nTPM), detected in some tissues, and cell type-enhanced specificity, indicating specialized functions in reproductive and endocrine tissues. ClinVar contains 28 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants predominantly consisting of 22q copy number losses and gains (22q12.1-12.2, 22q11.1-13.33 regions) and large deletions, demonstrating significant clinical dosage sensitivity in chromosomal rearrangement syndromes affecting the 22q12.1 locus. Mechanistic hypothesis: C22orf31 functions as a nuclear membrane-associated chromatin adaptor that recruits HDAC2-containing repressor complexes to specific genomic loci through direct protein-protein interactions. The DUF4662 domain may mediate membrane anchoring or oligomerization at the nuclear envelope, while disordered regions facilitate dynamic binding to chromatin modifiers. Testis enrichment (8.4 nTPM) suggests critical roles in spermatogenesis-specific gene silencing or meiotic chromosome organization. Brain and parathyroid expression indicates potential functions in neuroendocrine development. Pathogenic 22q CNVs likely disrupt dosage-sensitive chromatin repression mechanisms, contributing to neurodevelopmental, reproductive, and endocrine phenotypes in 22q12 deletion/duplication syndromes.
