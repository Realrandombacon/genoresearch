# CFAP96/C4orf47: Well-Folded Ciliary Microtubule-Binding Scaffold Protein in 9+0 Non-Motile Cilium and Centrosome Network with Choroid Plexus-Retina-Testis Expression and 4q35.1 Dosage Sensitivity

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
CFAP96 (cilia and flagella associated protein 96), also known as C4orf47 (chromosome 4 open reading frame 47), encodes a 309 amino acid well-folded ciliary structural protein (UniProt A7E2U8) containing Cilia-and flagella-associated protein 96 domain (IPR029358, residues 1-300, PF15239, residues 8-293, PTHR31144/UPF0602, residues 1-300) characteristic of uncharacterized protein family 0602 (UPF0602) with potential microtubule-binding or ciliary axoneme organization functions. AlphaFold predicts high-confidence structure (pLDDT 73.9) indicating well-folded conformation with stable tertiary structure, consistent with structured ciliary scaffold proteins that function through defined protein-protein interfaces in ciliary microtubule inner junction complexes or 9+0 non-motile cilium assemblies. HPA data reveals group-enriched RNA expression (detected in some tissues), choroid plexus-enriched expression (29.9 nTPM), retina expression (12.5 nTPM), fallopian tube expression (11.0 nTPM), testis expression (10.9 nTPM), principal piece and end piece subcellular localization (sperm flagella regions), cancer-enhanced specificity, immune cell-enhanced blood cell specificity, and group-enriched single cell specificity, suggesting roles in ciliary microtubule organization, spermatogenesis, choroid plexus cerebrospinal fluid production, retinal photoreceptor ciliary function, and fallopian tube ciliary transport. Subcellular localization in 9+0 non-motile cilium, centrosome, and cytoplasmic microtubule positions CFAP96 as a structural component of both motile and non-motile cilia, potentially coordinating microtubule doublet assembly or ciliary gate complex formation. The well-folded conformation (pLDDT 73.9) combined with UPF0602 domain architecture and ciliary/centrosome localization suggests CFAP96 functions as a structured ciliary scaffold that organizes microtubule-binding complexes, ciliary transition zone assemblies, or centrosome-associated microtubule nucleation through defined protein-protein interfaces. ClinVar documents 122 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly chromosome 4q35.1 copy number gains and losses encompassing the CFAP96 locus (4q32.1-35.2 CNVs, 4q34.3-35.2 CNVs, 4q35.1-35.2 CNVs), indicating strong dosage sensitivity in disease etiology, potentially affecting ciliary microtubule organization and centrosome function. Disease-associated CNVs suggest haploinsufficiency or triplosensitivity mechanisms disrupting scaffold stoichiometry or ciliary complex assembly, potentially leading to ciliopathy syndromes through impaired 9+0 non-motile cilium formation, retinal degeneration through disrupted photoreceptor connecting cilium organization, male infertility through defective sperm flagella principal piece/end piece assembly, or choroid plexus dysfunction through impaired ependymal ciliary beating. The 4q35.1 chromosomal location, very high pathogenic variant burden (122 variants), choroid plexus-retina-testis-fallopian tube expression pattern (29.9/12.5/11.0/10.9 nTPM), principal piece/end piece localization, cancer-enhanced and immune cell-enhanced specificity, well-folded conformation (309 aa, pLDDT 73.9), UPF0602 domain architecture, and ciliary/centrosome/microtubule localization position CFAP96 as a candidate gene for ciliopathy disorders, retinal dystrophy syndromes, male infertility, hydrocephalus through choroid plexus dysfunction, and cancer predisposition through dysregulated ciliary signaling in 9+0 non-motile cilia, centrosome-associated microtubule nucleation, and sperm flagella structural organization. Conservation across vertebrates (PTHR31144 panther family, orthologs in mouse, rat, zebrafish, fruit bat, guinea pig) suggests strong evolutionary constraint consistent with essential ciliary and centrosome functions.
