# C6orf187/CALHM6 - Lymphoid-Enriched ATP-Permeable Ion Channel with Immune Defense Network Associations

**Date:** 2026-03-17T03:50:20.343509

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
C6orf187 encodes CALHM6 (Calcium homeostasis modulator protein 6, UniProt Q5R3K3), a 315 aa pore-forming subunit of an ATP-permeable ion channel. InterPro identifies a calcium homeostasis modulator family domain (IPR029569, PF14798, PTHR32261, positions 1-287) defining this protein as a member of the CALHM channel family. AlphaFold predicts a high-confidence structure (pLDDT 84.6), indicating a well-folded transmembrane protein with stable tertiary architecture suitable for ion conduction. HPA classifies CALHM6 as a predicted intracellular and membrane protein with transporter and ion channel functions, showing tissue-enhanced expression with highest levels in lymphoid tissue (130.4 nTPM), immune cell enhancement in blood cells, and cell type enrichment. RNA is detected across all tissues. STRING interactions reveal associations with immune defense proteins: GBP5 (0.607, guanylate-binding protein), C1orf109 (0.535), GBP4 (0.486), CD4 (0.468, T-cell co-receptor), CXCL9 (0.468, chemokine), BATF2 (0.463, transcription factor), GBP1 (0.459), LCP2 (0.459, lymphocyte cytosolic protein), CD180 (0.447, B-cell receptor), and CXCL10 (0.434, interferon-induced chemokine). This interaction network suggests CALHM6 functions within immune cell signaling and antimicrobial defense pathways. ClinVar documents 28 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly large copy number losses and gains spanning 6q21-22 regions, though no specific disease phenotypes are established. Mechanistic hypothesis: CALHM6 likely functions as a voltage-gated or ligand-gated ion channel permeable to ATP and/or calcium, modulating extracellular ATP release in immune cells. The lymphoid tissue enrichment and immune protein interactions suggest roles in inflammasome activation, purinergic signaling, or antimicrobial defense. CALHM family members form hemichannels that release ATP as a danger signal during infection or inflammation. The immune cell localization and association with guanylate-binding proteins (GBPs), chemokines (CXCL9/10), and T/B-cell markers (CD4, CD180) supports a function in innate immune responses, potentially linking ion flux to cytokine production or pathogen restriction. Despite structural characterization and expression data, the specific gating mechanism, physiological substrates, and disease relevance of CALHM6 remain undefined - it represents a 'dark gene' with ion channel potential awaiting functional validation.
