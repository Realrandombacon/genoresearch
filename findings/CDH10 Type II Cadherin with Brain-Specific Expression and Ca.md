# CDH10: Type II Cadherin with Brain-Specific Expression and Catenin-Mediated Adhesion Function

**Date:** 2026-03-18T02:53:52.568375

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
CDH10 encodes a 788 aa type II classical cadherin (UniProt Q9Y6N8) with multiple extracellular cadherin repeat domains (IPR002126, IPR015919) positioned at residues 69-384, a catenin binding domain (IPR027397) at residues 689-785, and calcium-binding motifs (IPR000233). AlphaFold predicts a well-folded structure (pLDDT 78.4) consistent with canonical cadherin architecture. HPA shows brain-enriched expression (18.5 nTPM) with subcellular localization to nucleoplasm and cell junctions. STRING reveals high-confidence interactions with CTNNB1 (0.866), CTNNA1 (0.769), and other type II cadherins (CDH2: 0.843, CDH15: 0.834, CDH7: 0.827), indicating homophilic adhesion and adherens junction assembly. ClinVar contains 50 pathogenic variants (primarily chromosome 5p CNVs), suggesting dosage sensitivity. Orthologs exist in mouse, rat, chicken (788-789 aa), indicating strong conservation. FUNCTIONAL HYPOTHESIS: CDH10 mediates calcium-dependent homophilic cell-cell adhesion in neural tissues, recruiting catenins (CTNNB1/CTNNA1) to stabilize adherens junctions. MECHANISTIC PROPOSAL: Extracellular cadherin repeats bind calcium and mediate trans-interaction with opposing cells; cytoplasmic tail binds p120-catenin and β-catenin, linking to actin cytoskeleton. Brain-specific expression suggests role in neural development, synapse formation, or blood-brain barrier maintenance. CNV pathogenicity indicates gene dosage critical for neurodevelopment.
