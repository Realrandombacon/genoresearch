## Description
RNF207 is a 634 amino acid RING-type E3 ubiquitin ligase (UniProt Q6ZRF8) encoded at 1p36.31. The protein contains multiple structured domains: an N-terminal C3HC4 RING finger domain (aa 25-64, IPR001841/PF00096) conferring E3 ubiquitin ligase catalytic activity, a B-box type 1 zinc finger (aa 93-145, IPR000315) characteristic of TRIM family proteins, a PML-like coiled-coil region (aa 213-323, IPR021978) mediating oligomerization, and a C-terminal Actin-interacting protein 3-like domain (aa 414-547, G3DSA:1.20.58.1540). AlphaFold predicts a confident folded structure (pLDDT 75.2) consistent with stable tertiary architecture. HPA data reveals heart muscle-enriched expression (33.1 nTPM) with cytosolic localization and tissue-enhanced RNA distribution. STRING interactions identify 10 partners including DNA replication factor GINS3 (0.593), inflammation regulator LITAF (0.571), neuronal protein NDRG4 (0.507), cardiac ion channels KCNQ1 (0.463) and KCNH2 (0.444), and the E3 ligase RFFL (0.404). ClinVar documents 56 pathogenic/likely pathogenic copy number variants (deletions and duplications) spanning 1p36 regions, though clinical significance remains uncertain. The RING-B-box architecture places RNF207 within the TRIM/RBCC protein superfamily, suggesting it functions as a scaffold E3 ligase that ubiquitinates substrate proteins for proteasomal degradation. Heart muscle enrichment combined with ion channel interactions (KCNQ1, KCNH2) suggests potential roles in cardiac electrophysiology regulation through targeted protein turnover. The coiled-coil domain may enable higher-order assembly into nuclear bodies or cytoplasmic aggregates similar to PML nuclear bodies. Pathogenic CNVs disrupting RNF207 dosage could perturb cardiac protein homeostasis and contribute to arrhythmia or cardiomyopathy phenotypes.
