## Description
C2orf74 encodes a 187 aa uncharacterized protein containing DUF4642 domain (IPR027813, PF15484) spanning residues 28-187. Key findings: (1) Structural: Entire protein is a domain of unknown function - represents a compact single-domain protein; (2) Evolutionary: Moderate conservation with mouse ortholog (56.7% identity, E=2.46e-63) - suggests functional constraint but less than highly conserved structural proteins; (3) Localization: HPA assigns CENTROSOME localization - this is a critical clue suggesting potential role in cell division, microtubule organization, or ciliogenesis; (4) Expression: Low tissue specificity, detected in all tissues - consistent with housekeeping/structural role rather than tissue-specific function; (5) Clinical: 21 pathogenic ClinVar variants, predominantly COPY NUMBER VARIATIONS (both gains and losses) - indicates dosage sensitivity similar to SPMIP4 pattern; (6) Interactions: STRING shows medium-confidence interactions with KIAA1841 (nuclear pore complex), NPIPB8 (nucleoporin), SIRPD (immune receptor), FAM162B - suggests potential nuclear envelope or cell cycle connections. Hypothesis: C2orf74 may function at the centrosome-nuclear envelope interface, potentially involved in mitotic spindle organization or nuclear positioning. The centrosomal localization combined with CNV dosage sensitivity suggests it could be a stoichiometric component of centrosomal scaffolding complexes. The DUF4642 family represents an unexplored structural protein class worthy of functional characterization.
