# SPRING1 (C12orf49) - Golgi-Localized SREBP Pathway Regulator with Pathogenic Variants

**Date:** 2026-03-16T19:05:37.150149

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
SPRING1 (SREBF Pathway Regulator in Golgi 1, UniProt Q9H741) is a 205-amino acid Golgi membrane protein that positively regulates the SREBP signaling pathway, critical for lipid homeostasis and cholesterol biosynthesis. The protein contains a conserved SREBP-regulating domain (InterPro IPR019352, Pfam PF10218, residues 72-195) with high structural confidence (AlphaFold pLDDT 81.3). HPA data shows thyroid-enriched expression (35.0 nTPM) with ubiquitous tissue distribution. STRING interactions reveal functional connections to ER/Golgi proteases MBTPS1 (0.799) and MBTPS2 (0.415) - the site-1 and site-2 proteases that cleave SREBP precursors - plus TMEM41A (0.550), an ER membrane protein involved in lipid metabolism. ClinVar documents 12 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including copy number gains/losses and missense mutations (e.g., p.Arg150Gln), indicating clinical relevance though specific diseases remain uncharacterized. The mechanistic hypothesis: SPRING1 acts as a Golgi scaffold/regulator that modulates SREBP cleavage efficiency by recruiting or stabilizing the MBTPS1/MBTPS2 protease complex, thereby controlling the release of mature SREBP transcription factors that activate lipogenic genes. Disruption of SPRING1 function could impair cholesterol sensing and contribute to metabolic disorders.
