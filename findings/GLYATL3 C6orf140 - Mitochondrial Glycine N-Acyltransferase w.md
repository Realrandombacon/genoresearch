# GLYATL3 (C6orf140) - Mitochondrial Glycine N-Acyltransferase with Lipid Conjugation Function

**Date:** 2026-03-16T18:45:22.098141

**Quality Score:** 10/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
GLYATL3 (UniProt Q5SZD4) encodes a 288-amino acid glycine N-acyltransferase-like protein containing canonical acyltransferase domains. InterPro analysis reveals multiple conserved motifs: IPR010313 glycine N-acyltransferase family (residues 1-285), IPR013652 C-terminal domain (residues 192-279), IPR015938 N-terminal domain (residues 1-189), PF06021 aralkyl acyl-CoA:amino acid N-acyltransferase (residues 1-189), and PF08444 C-terminal region (residues 192-279). The protein belongs to the acyl-CoA N-acyltransferase superfamily (SSF55729, residues 43-263; PTHR15298), characteristic of enzymes that conjugate acyl-CoA thioesters with amino acids. HPA data shows liver-enriched expression (1.1 nTPM) with tissue-enhanced and cancer-enhanced patterns, and mitochondrial localization consistent with glycine conjugation pathway function. STRING interactions reveal medium-confidence associations with SPINT3 (0.577), FAM174A (0.426), SMIM3 (0.419), multiple TP53TG3 family members (0.416-0.418), TBC1D21 (0.414), HEATR5B (0.412), and VSIG10 (0.410). The SPINT3 interaction suggests potential roles in protease inhibition or epithelial integrity. GLYATL3 likely functions as a mitochondrial acyltransferase catalyzing glycine conjugation of xenobiotic carboxylic acids or endogenous lipid metabolites, analogous to canonical GLYAT which detoxifies benzoic acid and bile acids. Cancer-enhanced expression suggests upregulation in tumor metabolism, potentially supporting lipid remodeling or detoxification pathways. ClinVar documents 9 pathogenic variants including copy number alterations and a missense variant (p.Thr217Ser), indicating clinical relevance. Disruption could impair mitochondrial glycine conjugation, affecting lipid homeostasis or xenobiotic detoxification.
