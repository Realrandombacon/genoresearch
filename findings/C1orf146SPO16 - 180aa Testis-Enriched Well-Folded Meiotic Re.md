## Description
C1orf146 (UniProt Q5VVC0, also known as SPO16) encodes a 180aa protein on chromosome 1p22.1 with established involvement in meiotic recombination and synaptonemal complex assembly during spermatogenesis. InterPro analysis identifies membership in the SPO16 protein family (IPR027857, aa 3-179; PF15162, aa 10-170; PTHR31408, aa 3-179), indicating a conserved vertebrate meiosis-specific protein with homology to yeast SPO16 meiotic recombination factor. AlphaFold structure prediction shows high confidence (pLDDT 86.0), indicating a well-folded stable tertiary structure with defined secondary structural elements, consistent with structured protein-protein interaction scaffold capable of specific binding to meiotic recombination complex components. HPA data demonstrates testis-enriched expression (7.0 nTPM) with secondary expression in retina (1.9 nTPM), classified as group-enriched and detected in some tissues, with centriolar satellite subcellular localization and meiosis biological process annotation, confirming specialized role in male germ cell meiotic chromosome dynamics. STRING interaction network reveals 10 high-to-medium confidence partners forming a cohesive meiotic recombination module: HFM1 (0.885, high confidence, helicase for meiotic recombination), MSH5 (0.810, high confidence, mismatch repair homolog for meiotic crossover), TEX11 (0.798, high confidence, testis-expressed meiotic recombination factor), SLC39A14 (0.690, zinc transporter), SHOC1 (0.666, meiotic recombination protein), RNF212 (0.611, E3 ubiquitin ligase for meiotic crossover control), EME1 (0.610, meiotic endonuclease), SPO11 (0.609, meiotic double-strand break topoisomerase), SLC39A1 (0.504, zinc transporter), and MSH4 (0.487, mismatch repair homolog for meiotic crossover). This interaction pattern strongly positions C1orf146 as core component of the meiotic synaptonemal complex and crossover machinery. ClinVar contains 8 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including 5 copy number losses (1p31.3-21.3, 1p22.1, 1p31.3-22.1 regions) and 3 copy number gains (1p36.33-q44, 1p31.1-21.1 regions), indicating dosage sensitivity with implications for 1p deletion/duplication syndromes and male infertility (azoospermia, oligospermia, meiotic arrest). Sequence composition and structural features suggest protein-protein interaction interface formation for synaptonemal complex assembly. Conservation spans vertebrates via PTHR31408 family and yeast SPO16 homology.
