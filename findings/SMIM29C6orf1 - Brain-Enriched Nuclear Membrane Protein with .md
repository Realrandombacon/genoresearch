# SMIM29/C6orf1 - Brain-Enriched Nuclear Membrane Protein with HMGA1 Chromatin Interaction and Pathogenic CNVs

**Date:** 2026-03-16T18:31:59.425354

**Quality Score:** 9/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
SMIM29 (Small Integral Membrane Protein 29, C6orf1) encodes a 102aa predicted membrane protein (UniProt Q86T20) containing a conserved C3orf18/SIM29 domain (IPR060308, PF27898, aa 14-57) characteristic of the small integral membrane protein family (IPR043239, PTHR47730). HPA reveals tissue-enhanced expression with brain enrichment (148.5 nTPM) and immune cell enhancement, consistent with predicted intracellular/membrane localization. STRING identifies 10 interaction partners dominated by HMGA1 (high-mobility group AT-hook 1, score 0.876), a chromatin architectural protein that regulates transcription by DNA minor groove binding and nucleosome remodeling. Additional partners include ILRUN (0.551), ADPRM (0.516), SMIM19 (0.507), and myosin motors MYO1G (0.462)/MYH13 (0.452)/MYH8 (0.425), suggesting cytoskeletal association. ClinVar documents 6 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including copy number gains spanning 6p25.3-q27 and promoter variants, indicating dosage sensitivity. The HMGA1 interaction combined with nuclear membrane prediction and brain-enriched expression supports a hypothesis that SMIM29 functions as a nuclear envelope adaptor that tethers chromatin architectural proteins to the inner nuclear membrane, regulating neuronal gene expression programs. Pathogenic CNVs likely disrupt nuclear membrane integrity and chromatin organization, contributing to neurodevelopmental phenotypes. The myosin interactions suggest potential coupling between nuclear positioning and cytoskeletal dynamics.
