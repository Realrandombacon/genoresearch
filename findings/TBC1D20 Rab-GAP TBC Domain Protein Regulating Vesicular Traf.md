# TBC1D20: Rab-GAP TBC Domain Protein Regulating Vesicular Trafficking with High Clinical Variant Burden

**Date:** 2026-03-18T03:59:03.360251

**Quality Score:** 9/10 (EXCELLENT)

## Description
TBC1D20 (C20orf140) is a 403-amino acid cytoplasmic protein functioning as a GTPase-activating protein (GAP) for Rab family small GTPases. Structural analysis reveals a conserved Rab-GAP-TBC domain (IPR000195, PF00566, residues 57-269) with the characteristic catalytic arginine finger motif required for accelerating GTP hydrolysis on Rab substrates. The AlphaFold predicted structure (Q96BZ9) shows high confidence (pLDDT 81.6), indicating a well-folded catalytic domain typical of Rab-GAP enzymes. Expression profiling from the Human Protein Atlas demonstrates ubiquitous expression across all tissues with low tissue specificity, consistent with a housekeeping role in fundamental vesicular trafficking processes. Protein interaction networks (STRING) identify USP6 (score 0.896) as the highest confidence partner, a deubiquitinating enzyme, alongside multiple Rab GTPases including RAB1A (0.879), RAB18 (0.792), and RAB1B (0.519), as well as Rab-GAP regulatory complex components CDC16 (0.876) and RAB3GAP1/2 (0.789-0.820). These interactions place TBC1D20 within the Rab regulatory network controlling ER-to-Golgi transport, autophagy, and lipid droplet formation. ClinVar documents 72 pathogenic or likely pathogenic variants including missense mutations (p.Asp297Gly, p.Asn77Ser, p.Ala27Ser) and copy number losses/gains on chromosome 20p13, indicating strong dosage sensitivity and disease association. The mechanistic hypothesis proposes that TBC1D20 functions as a negative regulator of Rab1 family GTPases by catalyzing GTP hydrolysis, thereby controlling vesicle budding, fusion, and cargo delivery at ER-Golgi intermediate compartments. Dysregulation may contribute to trafficking defects in neurodevelopmental disorders and cancer given the high variant burden.
