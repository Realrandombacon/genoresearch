# CZIB (C1orf123): High-Confidence Zinc-Binding Regulator of Ribosome Biogenesis and Genomic Stability

## Description
CZIB encodes a compact 160-amino acid protein (UniProt Q9NWV4) characterized by a defining eukaryotic CXXC zinc-binding motif (IPR008584/PF05907). AlphaFold predicts a highly stable, well-folded structure with an exceptional global pLDDT score of 97.0, suggesting a rigid functional core essential for zinc coordination. While HPA data indicates ubiquitous expression across tissues with low specificity, subcellular localization is dynamic, associating with vesicles, the cytosol, and critically, the mitotic spindle. This mitotic presence, combined with STRING interactions linking CZIB to ribosomal proteins (RPS13, RPS15; scores ~0.46) and mitochondrial/chaperone factors (CCS, DNAJC17), hypothesizes a dual role: facilitating ribosome assembly or quality control during interphase and ensuring genomic stability or spindle checkpoint fidelity during division. Clinical relevance is underscored by 10 pathogenic/likely pathogenic copy number variants (gains/losses/inversions) in ClinVar, implicating dosage sensitivity in developmental disorders. Conservation across vertebrates (mouse/rat Xenopus homologs identified) further supports essential biological function. We propose CZIB acts as a zinc-dependent molecular bridge coordinating translational machinery integrity with cell cycle progression, where disruption leads to mitotic errors or proteotoxic stress.
