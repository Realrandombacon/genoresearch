## Description
EPCIP (Exosomal polycystin-1-interacting protein, C21orf120) is a 219 amino acid protein encoded on chromosome 21q22.11 (UniProt Q9NYP8). InterPro analysis reveals a conserved exosomal polycystin-1-interacting protein domain (IPR029250, PF15137) spanning residues 3-219, indicating this is a defined protein family rather than an intrinsically disordered region. AlphaFold prediction shows high structural confidence (pLDDT 76.9), suggesting a well-folded stable tertiary structure.\n\nHPA expression profiling demonstrates tissue-enhanced RNA expression with highest levels in choroid plexus (22.0 nTPM), followed by ovary (8.4 nTPM) and epididymis (7.9 nTPM). The protein is classified as both intracellular and secreted, consistent with its exosomal localization hypothesis. Expression is group-enriched in cancers and absent from immune cells.\n\nSTRING-DB identifies 10 medium-confidence interaction partners (scores 0.47-0.58) including C3orf22, RNASE12, C12orf56, SMIM17, and other chromosome ORF proteins, suggesting potential involvement in multi-protein complexes or shared regulatory pathways with other dark gene products.\n\nClinVar documents 56 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly copy number gains and losses spanning chromosome 21q21.3-22.3 regions. This high CNV burden indicates genomic instability at this locus contributes to disease phenotypes, though specific EPCIP-related Mendelian disorders remain uncharacterized.\n\nFUNCTIONAL HYPOTHESIS: EPCIP likely functions as an exosomal cargo protein that modulates polycystin-1 (PKD1) signaling pathways. Its choroid plexus enrichment suggests a role in cerebrospinal fluid exosome biogenesis or blood-CSF barrier maintenance. The polycystin-1 interaction domain implies potential involvement in ciliary function or mechanosensation pathways. Chromosome 21 CNVs affecting EPCIP may contribute to dosage-sensitive phenotypes in chromosomal aneuploidy syndromes.
