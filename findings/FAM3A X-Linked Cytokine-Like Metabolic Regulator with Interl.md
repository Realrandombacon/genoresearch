# FAM3A: X-Linked Cytokine-Like Metabolic Regulator with Interleukin Fold and Nuclear Localization

## Description
FAM3A (230 aa, UniProt P98173, chrXq28) is a cytokine-like protein with a well-defined interleukin-like EMT inducer fold (IPR039477, PF15711, residues 103-190) and FAM3 family signature (IPR039220, residues 1-227). AlphaFold predicts a high-confidence structure (pLDDT 88.5) indicating a stable globular fold despite being classified as "uncharacterized." HPA reveals ubiquitous expression across all tissues with nucleoplasm, nucleolar rim, and mitotic chromosome localization, suggesting cell cycle-regulated nuclear functions. The protein is predicted as both intracellular and secreted, consistent with cytokine-like signaling capability. STRING interactions identify 9 partners including SNX15 (0.779, sorting nexin involved in endosomal trafficking), WDR13 (0.543, X-linked WD-repeat protein), UNC45A/B (0.478/0.415, myosin folding chaperones), COQ4 (0.442, coenzyme Q biosynthesis), and ABHD1 (0.482, lipid metabolism enzyme). This interaction network suggests FAM3A functions at the intersection of vesicular trafficking, chromatin regulation, and metabolic control. ClinVar contains 225 pathogenic variants (primarily Xq28 copy number variations), indicating clinical significance though no specific monogenic disease is yet defined. The interleukin-like fold (shared with FAM3C/ILEI, a known EMT inducer) suggests FAM3A may regulate epithelial-mesenchymal transition or metabolic reprogramming via PPAR-gamma signaling. The nuclear localization combined with cytokine domain architecture proposes a dual mechanism: nuclear chromatin modulation during cell division plus extracellular metabolic signaling. X-chromosome location (Xq28) places it in a gene-dense region associated with immune and metabolic disorders.
