# CLN8 (C8orf61) - Lysosomal Ceramide Transporter in Neuronal Ceroid Lipofuscinosis

**Date:** 2026-03-17T04:22:25.074111

## Description
CLN8 encodes a 286 amino acid transmembrane protein localized to the endoplasmic reticulum and lysosomes. The protein contains a conserved TRAM/LAG1/CLN8 homology domain (IPR006634, residues 62-262) and a TLC (Tram-Lag-CLN8) domain (PF03798, residues 67-251) characteristic of lipid-binding/transport proteins. AlphaFold predicts a well-folded structure with very high confidence (pLDDT 90.1), indicating stable tertiary structure. STRING analysis reveals high-confidence interactions with 10 protein partners, notably other neuronal ceroid lipofuscinosis (NCL) disease proteins: CLN5 (0.978), PPT1 (0.969), CLN3 (0.966), MFSD8 (0.963), CLN6 (0.774), plus ceramide synthase CERS1 (0.818) and lysosomal proteases TPP1 (0.593) and CTSD (0.514). ClinVar documents 290 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants including nonsense mutations (p.Gln100Ter, p.Trp83Ter), frameshifts (p.Val41fs, p.Asp94fs), and missense changes (p.Arg70Leu, p.Leu207Arg), confirming strong disease association. HPA expression data unavailable. FUNCTIONAL HYPOTHESIS: CLN8 functions as an ER-to-lysosome ceramide transporter essential for lysosomal lipid homeostasis. Loss-of-function disrupts ceramide trafficking, causing lysosomal storage of autofluorescent lipofuscin material in neurons, leading to progressive neurodegeneration characteristic of NCL8 (Northern epilepsy phenotype). MECHANISTIC PROPOSAL: The TLC domain mediates lipid binding while the transmembrane topology enables vesicular transport; interaction with CERS1 suggests coordination of ceramide synthesis and trafficking; clustering with other CLN proteins indicates participation in a lysosomal protease-lipid clearance complex.
