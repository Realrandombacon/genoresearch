## Description
C9orf57 encodes a 161-amino acid uncharacterized protein (UniProt Q5W0N0) localized to cellular membranes with testis-enriched expression (11.3 nTPM in Human Protein Atlas). The protein contains a Domain of Unknown Function DUF4723 (Pfam PF15851, InterPro IPR031710) spanning residues 75-160, corresponding to an extracellular domain (CDD cd23610, aa 75-161). AlphaFold predicts a partially structured conformation (pLDDT 69.4), suggesting the protein has ordered domains with potential intrinsically disordered regions. STRING-DB identifies 4 medium-confidence interaction partners: ABHD17B (0.577, lipid metabolism enzyme), C9orf85 (0.527, uncharacterized chromosome 9 protein), ZFAND5 (0.507, zinc finger stress response protein), and CEMIP2 (0.448, extracellular matrix hyaluronan-binding protein). ClinVar reports 39 pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, predominantly copy number gains and losses across 9q21, indicating dosage sensitivity. The combination of testis-specific expression, membrane localization, DUF4723 extracellular domain, and interactions with lipid metabolism (ABHD17B) and extracellular matrix (CEMIP2) proteins suggests C9orf57 functions in germ cell membrane dynamics during spermatogenesis, potentially in acrosome formation or sperm-egg recognition. Pathogenic CNVs disrupting C9orf57 dosage may contribute to male infertility or reproductive developmental disorders.
